# Share by default — the goods that compound are non-rival

<!--me-->Sharing is about non-rival goods<!--/me-->: an idea costs me nothing and can
help unboundedly many. That asymmetry is why openness sits near the top of a
scope-sensitive ledger: most goods are used up when given away; ideas are copied, and
the copy is free. Publishing is one
of the few acts whose reach isn't bounded by my own effort.

The track record is the argument:

- **<!--me-->The scientific revolution<!--/me-->** ran on open publication. Making
  results public and checkable, rather than guarded, is what let knowledge accumulate
  instead of dying with each discoverer.
- **<!--me-->The literature<!--/me-->** is that accumulation — the compounding, shared
  record every new result builds on.
- **<!--me-->Growth<!--/me-->** — non-rival ideas at the center. Paul Romer's
  endogenous-growth work — the [2018 Nobel](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2018/romer/facts/),
  building on his 1990 *Endogenous Technological Change* — models <!--me-->ideas as a
  core component of technological development and economic growth<!--/me-->, and as
  nonrival. Sharing them is not charity; it's the mechanism.
- **<!--me-->Ideas spread locally<!--/me-->** too, not only to the frontier. Much of the
  value lands on whoever is near — the people I work with, the ones who find the repo —
  through diffusion, not just the one breakthrough.

So the rule, and why it earns its place on this list: **default to public, and share the
process, not just the result.** Save the workflow, the methodology, the dead ends beside
the output. Redact only what's genuinely about others. Everything non-rival, let it
compound.
