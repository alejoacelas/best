## 1. The idea (root identity, in my voice)

I'm a Toby Ord utilitarian. I take that to mean: there's a set of questions about
what's best — the best act, the best motive, the best trait, the best work, the
best life — that we can keep asking ourselves and each other, and that I want to
hold my best tentative answers to and act from them, to make outcomes as good as
possible.

Ord's *global* move is that "make outcomes as good as possible" applies not just
to **acts** but to *every* evaluative focal point: motives, character, work,
tools, relationships. For any category, "the right one" just means **"the best
one."** So the whole thing reduces to a practice: **pick a category, ask "what's
the best one?", give a tentative answer, act on it, keep asking.**

That's how this workspace runs:

- **Every folder is a standing question** — a category I keep re-asking "what's
  the best one?" about. Its question is the first line of its `README.md`.
- **Every leaf is a tentative answer**, not a question. (`work/tools/voice` isn't
  a question; it's my current best answer to "what's the best dictation tool?")

I say *utilitarian*, not *consequentialist*, on purpose: I want the axiology baked
in — that the good I'm maximizing is others' welfare — not just the machinery of
"best x." The word carries the caring, not only the method.

## 5. Two reusable sub-questions (in my voice, kept in `README.md`)

These aren't rules for an agent — they're two moves I keep making, so I want them
in my own words in `README.md`, not in the instructions file:

- **`other/`** — whenever a category starts to collapse into its current answers, I
  open an `other/` inside it and ask: *what other ways are there to do this — to
  relate to these people, to do this work?* It keeps the question bigger than my
  answers. (At the root, `other/` holds art, advice, places — the things without a
  sharper question yet.)
- **`later/`** — the things worth returning to, but not now. `later/` names a
  question I'm choosing to defer, not a parking lot. It lives at the root and can
  reappear nested (`work/later/`, `me/body/later/`) to hold deferred sub-questions.

*(Alternative considered: move these to the global `CLAUDE.md`. Decided against —
they're me describing how I think, not a cross-project agent rule.)*

## 7. The git model — *already done by me (Alejo), out of band*

`best` is now one mega-repo. Only repos that need to be their own GitHub repos stay
nested; everything else is a plain folder tracked by `best`. This half is **done**:

- **Still their own repos:** `friends/{advice,partner,people}`, `friends/places/visa`
  (private); `machine/dotfiles`, `make/art/meals`, `make/writing/blog` (public but
  standalone/deployed); `make/tools` (private) with external `gdoc/gdoc` + `gws/gogcli`.
- **Absorbed into `best`** (`.git` removed, now plain folders): `principles`,
  `priorities`, `learn`, `calls`, `email`, `slack`, `voice`, `writing`, `art`,
  `body`, `mind`, `stuff`, `places`.
- **`.workspace/private-paths`** — hand-maintained list of which nested repos are
  private (drives the visibility column).
- **`sync-repos.py`** — reworked: generates per-parent **"Sub-repositories" tables**
  (Folder | Visibility | Repository), `.gitkeep`s the path down to a nested repo, and
  regenerates `repos.yaml` + `.gitignore` blocks.

**What the reorg still has to do to this machinery:**
- After renaming parents (`friends/`→split into `people/`+`other/`, `make/`→`work/`,
  `becoming/`→`utilitarian/`, `machine/`→`ai/`), **update `.workspace/private-paths`**
  to the new repo paths, then rerun `.workspace/sync-repos.py` so `repos.yaml`,
  `.gitignore`, and the sub-repo tables regenerate.
- **Add the question map to `sync-repos.py`:** a second managed block that walks the
  folder tree and lists every folder + the first line of its `README.md`, written
  into `best/README.md` so it always mirrors the live worktree and its questions.
  (This is additive to the existing sub-repo-table generator.)
- Nested repos travel with a parent rename (their `.git` comes along); only
  `repos.yaml` paths change, not GitHub remote names.
- **Visibility unchanged:** `tools`, `partner`, `advice`, `visa`, `people/friend`
  stay private; `me/mind` stays public. Re-audit only the new `me/past/` before
  committing (about me → public).
