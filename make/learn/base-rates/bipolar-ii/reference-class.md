# Bipolar II — defining the reference class

The children question had a crisp class and a binary behavior. This one resists
that, and the resistance is informative. Three design moves make it tractable.

## 1. What we measure: impact, not stability

The outcome is **lifetime good done by leveraging one's nature (utilitarian
impact)** — *not* stability, and not suicide-avoidance for its own sake. A
foreshortened life counts for the impact it made; a suicide is a loss only insofar
as it left impact on the table. So we do **not** sort people into thrivers vs.
crashers. We sort by impact, then ask a counterfactual.

## 2. The question the class exists to answer

**Does the up-swing drive the impact — and is the impact-maximizing move to ride it
or damp it?**

The tension is real and personal (from the source doc): *sustained hypomanic
activation borrows against the crash* — allostatic load makes the depressive trough
that follows deeper and longer. So the live question is whether protecting
sleep / moderating the high **raises** net lifetime output (fewer, shorter crashes;
more productive years) or **lowers** it (flattening the engine that produces the
peaks). The reference class is evidence on that trade — it is not settled in
advance, even though the instinct in the source doc leans toward damping.

## 3. Membership: a phenotype signature, scored by confidence

Because the *functional* cases never get the BP-II label and historical diagnoses
are guesses, membership is defined by a **signature** and each candidate gets an
explicit **match confidence** — we never assert a diagnosis.

The signature (derived from Alejo's own presentation; fuller clinical detail lives
in the private clinical workspace):

- **Decreased *need* for sleep that drives activation** — ~5h + nap, sustained for
  weeks, from an 8h baseline; not insomnia. This is the engine, and the highest-
  leverage lever.
- **An elevated, driven *baseline* temperament** — not just discrete episodes; a
  stably-raised set-point between them.
- **Cyclicity** — high-output activation followed by long depressive troughs
  (a 4+ month crash, floor ~10–12 productive hrs/week, after the prior episode).
- **Disinhibition / impulsivity / emotional lability in the up phase** — "too
  intense" messages, "reckless" spending, lability over the elevated baseline.
- **Triggered by upheaval** — both episodes set off by major travel / relocation.
- **Family history** of affective / bipolar-spectrum episodes.
- **Sub-psychotic** — hypomania, not mania; no psychosis, no substance use. This is
  the BP-II discriminator, and the reason these people pass as merely "intense"
  rather than ill — which is exactly why the most functional ones are unlabeled.

Score each candidate against this (high / medium / low match), and keep the score
visible in the table. This is a reusable technique — *define a fuzzy class by a
personal phenotype signature instead of a formal label* — noted back in
`../reference-classes.md`.

## 4. The cuts we compare

- **Era** — an *all-time* cut (accepts speculative matches: Churchill's "black dog"
  over prodigious output; Robert Lowell; etc.) **and** a *living / recent (under
  ~40)* cut, which has a better chance of catching the functional, self-aware,
  sometimes self-disclosed cases the historical canon misses.
- **Spine** — high utilitarian impact through a compounding career (per
  `../reference-classes.md`); phenotype-match is the membership filter on top.

## 5. What we extract per person (not a rate — a pattern)

For each high-impact strong-match: how did the nature feed the output? Did they ride
it or damp it? What did the crashes cost? Would plausibly-more or plausibly-less
management have *raised* their lifetime impact? The keystone case remains **Kay
Redfield Jamison** — she wanted the highs, refused the guardrail (lithium) to keep
them, nearly died, and concluded the guardrail is what preserved the upside. She is
the clearest natural experiment on the ride-vs-damp question.

## Honest limits

- **Speculative diagnosis** — we score confidence, never assert; many matches will
  be medium-confidence at best.
- **Survivorship, both directions** — the impact-outcome sidesteps the usual
  survivorship *inversion* (we don't auto-penalize the crashers), but we still can't
  see the low-impact people with the same phenotype who never surfaced.
- **Privacy / ethics of the living cut** — reason only from public self-disclosure;
  don't diagnose living people from a distance.
