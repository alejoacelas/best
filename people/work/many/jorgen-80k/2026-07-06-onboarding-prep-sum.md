But turnaround on a justified request is fast: "we could have a quick turnaround" — the next working day. Alejandro's reasoning for caring: every slight friction "reduces the things you're willing to undertake" — the value comes from being able to "fire off a Claude" the moment an idea strikes. Next-day access: "I'll take it — it's much better than what I've seen."

## Part 6: Contract length and commitment structure

The open decision point: how long to sign for. Impact Ops' blocker is recouping what they invest in him. Alejandro's own position:

> "If after two weeks I see I have a reasonable shot at doing it and it takes me six months, I'll just spend the six months — I see no better way of using my time."

Jørgen raised "the exploration versus exploitation tradeoff": constantly reconsidering big changes is costly, so at least "soft commit to a certain horizon, and then at that checkpoint we check in and reconsider". Alejandro countered with something stronger: "a hard commit on exponentially increasing time horizons" — small first commitment, made larger after ~two weeks. Jørgen agreed, with flexibility on the margin for AIM and other things.

Jørgen then floated structuring the Impact Ops contract as **hours rather than months**, with an hourly charge from 80k to Impact Ops and a committed minimum equal to whatever recoups Impact Ops' investment. Alejandro noted Josh had said they don't do hourly contracts (incentive problem: hourly billing rewards slowness), and will raise it with Joshua right after. Jørgen left it to them: "I'm not too concerned about that consideration."

## Appendix 3: Underlying dynamics

**Jørgen as unblocker, not gatekeeper:** Jørgen repeatedly positioned himself as clearing hurdles in advance — the shared doc, the contractor idea, pre-onboarding on access, pulling in Sam while he's on leave. He also preemptively defused two potential frictions: access levels ("don't take the access levels as a sign of how much we value you") and exclusivity (non-80k work is fine).

**Alejandro selling speed as the core value proposition:** The through-line of his asks is removing friction between idea and action — Claude credits, Drive access, next-day permissions, frictionless grants. He framed it explicitly: connect "the will to do something with the capacity to do it".

**Mutual enthusiasm with a calibration gap:** Alejandro anchors on the ambitious end (10% productivity, six-month horizon if warranted); Jørgen consistently endorses the direction while gently moderating magnitude ("maybe dampen the level", soft-commit checkpoints). The "exponentially increasing commitments" compromise resolved this cleanly.
