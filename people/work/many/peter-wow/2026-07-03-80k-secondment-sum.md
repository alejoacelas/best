# Meeting summary: Peter Hartree and Alejandro Acelas — deciding on the 80k secondment

**Date:** 2026-07-03

**Participants:** Alejandro Acelas; Peter Hartree (AI uplift consultant, advisor at 80,000 Hours)

---

## Summary

First call in about three months, ahead of a joint call with Hugo (a funder pushing an uplift consulting org) half an hour later. The main threads:

1. Peter shared his summer plan: July–August fully on AI-wow public goods (blogging, newsletter, meetups, calls) plus leveling up his own Fable skills, with a possible org launch in autumn.
2. Alejandro laid out his three-month secondment at 80k via Impact Ops (target start 20 July, London, ~36k GBP to cover salary and visa), and Peter strongly endorsed going for it — top-10-percentile opportunity, "don't overthink it".
3. They weighed the alternatives (funder money for a consulting org, Austin Chen's cohort, Surplus, Longview) and dismissed them for now; Alejandro said he'd decide that same day.
4. They agreed on a light coordination setup — low bar for calls and doc comments, mutual visibility into plans — and on a joint stance for the Hugo call: not us, not now, but help shape what he funds.

---

## Part 1: Reconnecting and Peter's summer plan

Peter opened by apologizing for the three-month gap: "I feel like that's probably my fault. Sorry." His Q2 was, "surprisingly", not focused on uplift; it wasn't distance-keeping. He's been "pretty excited" by what he's seen of Alejandro's work and wanted to start by "cheering you on".

His context: everything that kept him busy in Q2 is now wrapped, so the summer is clear for uplift. His default plan, which he's "pretty strongly committed to":

1. July–August focused on AI-wow public goods — blogging, a more active newsletter, maybe software, organizing meetups, info sharing between communities and orgs, more Slack activity, "loads of random calls" offering quick help and connections. He resisted all inbound consulting requests for these months.
2. Leveling up personally — "as much as half, maybe even more" of his time playing with Fable and pushing ambitious delegation further. His reasoning: the biggest bottleneck to people getting good at uplift is the whirlwind of existing commitments; he can afford to drop contracting for a bit, and "it's pretty rare" to be in that position.
3. Exit strategies later in the year: better consultant, or starting an org — possibly consulting, but he's more drawn to object-level work by a tiny team, citing Peter McIntyre and Ben West's two-person setup for AI safety. The space of projects tractable for a small team delegating to AI "is going to grow a ton during this year". Timing guess: autumn, possibly late summer.

## Part 2: Alejandro's position — the 80k secondment

Alejandro summarized his recent months: coaching calls for AIM's executive team (mostly Samantha, the CEO — "great relationship"), thinking about what to do next, and traveling. He described his edge: "great coaching energy", getting people excited in a half-hour call, perceptiveness about what helps each person, and loving to help — "it just drives me really hard".

The plan he's set up: a **three-month secondment at 80k**. He wanted 80k specifically ("I love those guys", couldn't name an org he'd be happier at) and London in person ("I kind of want to be there. I love this place").

Status: Jørgen confirmed "yes, we need someone for this". Impact Ops would employ him for the skilled worker visa; target date **20 July**. Impact Ops needs the arrangement to bring in ~**36k GBP** (salary plus visa costs); Alejandro: "I can bring that in three months. Yeah, for sure" — and Peter noted 80k will pay that.

**Peter's verdict was immediate:** the plan is "comfortably inside the sort of 90th percentile" of things Alejandro could work on, and his rule of thumb is that once inside the top 10 percentile, "don't overthink it... just go for it and see how it goes."

---

## Part 3: The co-founder question and focus

Alejandro's one open question: how much time to spend looking for co-founders, given repeated advice to pair up with another extreme power user. He wanted at least to coordinate with Peter — "cover different sides of the thing" — and floated capping the search at "20% of my time", because "I kind of just want to have focus."

Peter probed his historical over-commitment problem. Alejandro reported it largely cleared: biosecurity commitments done, an EA-group community-health issue resolved by Wednesday, and everything except Samantha's coaching calls "will, in theory, be resolved by July 7th". Day-to-day he's "pretty impulsive... moment to moment" but coherent at the high level.

Peter's suggestion: go "pretty all in" on 80k for the first month. That yields signal on the real opportunities there, on full-time vs. partial, and on interest in a longer role — which in turn prices how urgent the co-founder search is.

---

## Part 4: Weighing the alternatives

Peter asked what could plausibly be "2x or more times valuable in expectation" than 80k.

**a. Funder-backed consulting org (Hugo's push).** Hugo argued Alejandro might get "a bunch of money and form a team fast" and be better placed to help 80k in 18 months. Alejandro's reply: "I just want to know what doing the work looks like." Peter backed the instinct with a general caution: funders read ecosystem needs well but are "very plausibly kind of wildly wrong about what particular individuals should do"; money offers are signal, but there's "a real risk of overweighting the signal", since funders rarely go deep on execution. Alejandro, on the funders' Google Docs: "I've thought more about this than you have." Peter added that less experienced grantmakers "don't realize how much they can push people around."

**b. Austin Chen's cohort (Manifund/Mox).** Interviewed the day before. Austin suggested Alejandro's thing could be "the first step for something more scalable". Alejandro vibes strongly with Austin and sees the cohort as a talent magnet, but it means San Francisco and the Mox offices: "San Francisco can wait." He flagged he may be undervaluing it.

**c. Fallbacks if 80k fell through:** Surplus first, if it materializes. Then Longview — Jürgen [?] there pitched "we're very talented"; Alejandro would likely join the AI team under Michael Aird [?], possibly mixing in active grantmaking.

Alejandro closed the deliberation: "I think I should take the decision today. I don't expect to get more information." Peter agreed — diminishing returns hit "uncomfortably hard, uncomfortably early", and the secondment structure (no two-year expectation) is "really the sweet spot".

---

## Part 5: The case for and against 80k

Peter articulated the case:

1. **For:** 80k is a big existing org, so even an incremental improvement multiplies over a big base; and the lower bound is unusually safe — even just one-on-one uplift calls with staff would raise org output somewhere "in the range of 1 to 10%" (his guess for calls alone: "nearer to one percent"). Alejandro adopted it as a target: "I'll try to shoot for the 10 percent."
2. **Against (generic):** uplift at any org over ~10 people fights inertia — the electricity analogy, where gains only came once you "completely rebuilt the factories". But Alejandro noted Luca keeps getting permission for his experiments, and Peter corrected himself: with Niel keen, 80k will be "way better than most orgs of their size in that reference class, by really a long shot."

Peter also disclosed his own neglected 80k advisory role ("I massively neglect this... and I don't really rationally endorse it"); Alejandro offered to "pull you towards doing more 80k", which Peter called plausible, with a discipline caveat.

---

## Part 6: Coordination setup and community infrastructure

They settled a working mode:

- Low bar for Alejandro to book calls or send Google Docs; Peter engages as best he can.
- Mutual visibility into strategic plans. Peter will spend hours-to-days (not more) supporting others only where it feeds the AI-wow public-goods remit; confidentiality is a non-issue since he's an 80k advisor already.
- Alejandro pushes information to Peter proactively. Peter named networking passivity as "one of my biggest weaknesses" (he missed Surplus, was late on Generator); Alejandro offered a Fable-automated "more Peter Hartree" report pipeline; Peter asked at minimum for things Slacked to him.

Community gaps discussed: too few meetups for power users (Chris's format has gotten "a little bit too inclusive"); Peter's fortnightly peer-mentoring with Matt Brooks as a model. Alejandro wants more visibility into what people at different orgs are doing, possibly automated with Claude Cowork [?]. Precedent celebrated: Pablo sharing Epoch material, and Chris publishing Coefficient's internal guidelines after Peter's hassling — "that kind of stuff is very high return." Alejandro has been telling orgs to "just do what Coefficient Giving does."

---

## Part 7: The Hugo call and the win condition

For the joint Hugo call (half an hour after this one), they aligned:

- Alejandro has landed: 80k, not the org — Hugo "doesn't seem" to have a case Alejandro missed. On CG's money: "once I know what to do with the money, I'll take it."
- Peter is out for July–August: "I'm definitely not going to do it in July and August."
- Neither wants to talk Hugo out of it. The useful move is to **help shape what he funds**: Peter would be "pretty excited" about a firm bootstrapped on tons of one-on-one uplift calls, branching later into other training and product lines. Best-fit founder profile: someone who's run a consulting firm and knows the change-management playbooks. Peter's open research question: how consulting firms achieve internal-entrepreneurship-style transformation (his naive guess: empowered parallel teams inside orgs) — learning that could revive his excitement.

Closing on the long game, Alejandro asked what the "absolute win condition" looks like — analogies to engineering as a discipline, or virtues and human rights as encoded knowledge. Peter's take: playbooks are needed to scale because "most people can't just keep improvising", which is why one-on-one calls appeal — "widgety and formalizable", especially for raising the floor. Both agreed it feels contradictory to encode playbooks amid transformative change, and that no EA org yet matches Shopify/Zapier-level seriousness about AI adoption.

## Appendix 3: Underlying dynamics

**Repair before business:** Peter opened with an unprompted apology for three months of silence and explicit cheerleading — re-securing the relationship before strategy. Alejandro's "maybe I can pull you towards doing more 80k" signals he wants more of Peter, not less.

**Advice as permission:** Alejandro arrived essentially decided; the call's function was validation and coordination, not deliberation. Peter recognized this and supplied a clean decision rule (top-decile → stop optimizing) rather than new options.

**Coordinated front for Hugo:** They pre-aligned a joint position for the funder call minutes later — decline for themselves, redirect the money toward the version they'd endorse. Both were wary of funder push dynamics while wanting the funding to exist for someone.

**Division of labor emerging:** Alejandro as the high-contact networker feeding intel; Peter as the public-goods producer and strategist asking to be fed. The "more Peter Hartree" report idea makes the asymmetry explicit and productive.
