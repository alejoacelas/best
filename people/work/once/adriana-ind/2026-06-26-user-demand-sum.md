## Summary

Alejo shared his updated strategy document with Adriana: he has moved from "ideas" to "prioridades", organized around two shifts:

1. Focusing on a very small number of VIPs (per advice from Adam Jones and Peter Hartree), likely by going deep with a single organization for ~3 months.
2. Filtering all projects by validated user demand — "is someone asking you to do this? No? Ok, then maybe don't."

While reading, they cleared up small confusions: the doc's "VIPs" term (Alejo had deleted the explanation; it traces to his friend Yoyo liking to be treated as "my most special person"), Adam Jones's trajectory (Palantir before BlueDot, then Anthropic — not the other way around), and what "head hunting for a cofounder" meant.

## Part 2: Alejo's strategy — few VIPs, one organization, user demand

The core recommendation Alejo received from Adam Jones (and in some form Peter Hartree): focus on a very small number of VIPs and accelerate their work as much as possible. The natural implementation is picking one organization and going as deep as possible for ~3 months. Combined with wanting to live in the UK (or US), that pushes toward two routes:

1. **Create an organization** — get incubated / fiscally sponsored by Ambitious Impact or Impact Ops, which could also solve the visa.
2. **Work for someone** — 80K is "mi top candidata"; Longview Philanthropy floated "maybe you can also do active grantmaking"; Rohan at Coefficient Giving is exploring active grantmaking in the uplift area. Alejo's read: money and people with ideas are likely coming to the space soon.

The second shift: his strategy previously looked diffuse because he wasn't taking user demand seriously. Of his many documented ideas, only "one of them, maybe two at most" have validated demand. His new rule for projects without a demand signal: can you get "some signal of user demand" in five hours or a week?

He gave two illustrations:

- **His course idea** — Adriana asked if it fell in "maybe don't". He had run the test: a signup form that got exactly one person (Cecilia, a team lead).
- **Jess Rapson (Oxford)** — the Uzbekistan government, which owns many private companies, asked her for a dashboard to oversee them and pick the best performers. Alejo's reaction: "that sounds really exciting... Isn't that the whole point of this area? Obviously, someone should be doing this."

His advice to Adriana: "you should basically find something that has" user demand, "or that you could easily find if it has user demand". Adriana said the framing "makes all the sense" and that she could run a cheap experiment (a form, like the Prague thing) once she internalizes it.

## Part 3: Reliability and responsiveness

Alejo raised a candid meta-point: when he considers recommending Adriana for things, "don't know how reliable you are" comes to mind. Being able to commit to a date and deliver matters if she wants to work with others.

Adriana's honest self-assessment: it depends on the period — these two weeks the answer is no, though she believes she's capable of not committing to things she can't do, and it will shift once her current phase ends. Her only current commitment is helping with a hackathon in London (~10 hours/week, wraps in ~2 months). When she does commit, "quiero decir noventa por ciento".

Alejo distinguished two levels:

- **Explicit commitments** and following through on them.
- **A subtler one** — his intuition partly comes from sending her things on WhatsApp and not getting fast responses: "that's a signal."

He offered "responsive" as the companion word to reliable: "I think you being responsive works really great for me." That alone would take her far.

## Appendix 3: Underlying dynamics

**Mentor-mentee structure crystallizing:** The call converts a peer chat into a light apprenticeship — Alejo assigns tasks, reviews output, gates introductions on quality, and receives weekly accountability updates. Adriana explicitly welcomed the structure; her stated problem is choosing, not capacity.

**Reliability as an open test:** Alejo told her plainly that her WhatsApp response rate is already a signal he tracks, and that the coaching-call critique will be judged. The relationship's next phase is effectively an evaluation period, and both know it.

**Humor calibration gap:** Adriana said she can't tell when Alejo is serious ("soy muy mala para detectar cuándo es algo serio"); he assumed it was obvious. Worth remembering when delivering judgmental-sounding feedback.
