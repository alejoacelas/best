## Part 2: Alejandro's consulting practice and Claude tag

Alejandro has been AI consulting for about a month. He tried a similar idea a year ago but "didn't have any coherent strategy" — Claude Code wasn't good enough then. Now "Claude Code is so good and people are still not using it."

He introduced Varun to **Claude tag** (released ~4 days earlier): tag Claude in Slack; "like Claude Code but better" for many things, with memory scoped per channel and per org, permission scopes, and the ability to borrow the user's permissions (e.g. Google Drive). Someone at Anthropic reportedly moved a bunch of their AI use to it — "a good reason to think it has very high potential." It landed mid-way through Alejandro writing his guide introducing people to Claude Code — "pretty good but also pretty frantic."

His coaching format: ~50 minutes building "a concrete vivid image in your mind of what their work looks like," then ~30 minutes doing things that make the person immediately more productive. "Maybe a third or a quarter of the calls go really, really great"; the rest are still positive, but he wants to make them "more consistently great" and suspects that's possible.

"You basically can get 10x better than whatever you guys are doing" on the use-AI side, and after that the offering becomes clear.

## Part 5: Coaching Varun — becoming cracked at AI

Asked how to become that person, Alejandro said he's "still trying to figure that out," but: a coaching call is a good first step, and beyond that, "think of the things that you care about most and then just try to solve them with AI."

His live example: that morning he was researching whether very successful people have children, to inform his own big life question — noting influential figures (Holden Karnofsky, Elon Musk, Patrick [Collison, likely], others [names unclear]) seem to have children above the base rate, so despite the time cost "maybe you should have children" even if optimizing for tail impact. The meta-point: "I'm figuring out how I can break it into pieces that I can delegate to Fable."

Key advice threads:
1. **Ask Claude itself** how to work with it better — Varun admitted he'd never thought of this.
2. Treat the AI as "much smarter than you" with "really weird personality defects" — a brilliant "maniac" colleague; "ask your genius colleague."
3. **Shape the question**: "should I have children?" gets "a cheap answer"; "what are the main considerations?" scopes it into empirical research Claude excels at, given the right affordances.
4. Hold "the absolute conviction that you could be doing 10x better if you wanted to" — ask "how do I do it 10x better instead of doing my work?" (with the caveat it "might throw some people off a cliff").
5. "Your growth rate is way more important than your current size."

## Part 6: Tool walkthrough

- **Wispr Flow** (Varun is on Mac, had used it but lapsed): "never type a prompt ever again" — talking feeds the AI far more context; converse rather than command, delegating the way you would to a colleague with context.
- **Superconductor**: where Alejandro runs all his agent sessions, many in parallel across projects.
- **GitHub**: "a Google Drive for programmers" with "very, very, very precise version control." Varun doesn't need to use it himself — "your Claude needs to use it." Alejandro shared his public repo (github.com/alejoacelas/best): "those are the important things in my life," published so others can ask their Claudes "what is Alejandro doing, what could I steal from there?"
- Caveat: Claude chat can't access GitHub (prompt-injection concerns) — use **Claude Code** (install docs sent) via Superconductor, or maybe Claude tag ("I haven't researched that yet").
- Alejandro is not a developer; he figured it out with Claude Code. His current website prompt: "make it like a Mac application — really minimalist, so that everything on the page earns its place."
