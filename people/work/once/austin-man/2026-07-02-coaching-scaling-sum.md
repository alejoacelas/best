# Meeting summary: AI coaching strategy and scaling with Austin Chen

**Date:** 2026-07-02

**Participants:** Alejandro Acelas (AI uplift coach, applying to Surplus), Austin Chen (Manifund; running the Surplus program)

---

## Summary

A Surplus application interview in which Alejandro pitched his AI uplift work: one-on-one coaching that "works really, really well" but is "pretty non-scalable". He recounted three weeks of coaching the Ambitious Impact executive team (six people plus two Samantha follow-ups) with a founder batch coming up, and admitted he doesn't yet "have a very scalable idea". Alejandro revealed his default plan is actually three months at 80,000 Hours as their AI uplift person, decided only the past weekend. Austin's steer: pure coaching belongs at 80k; Surplus only makes sense for a scalable, product-shaped version — possibly with Peter Hartree. Austin will get back within about a week; Alejandro talks to Peter tomorrow.

---

## Part 1: The pitch

After confirming the pronunciation of "Alejo" and that Alejandro was calling from Münster, Germany, Austin asked for a 30-second pitch. Alejandro's thesis: people who care about outcomes — EA and a broader group — could be doing much more with AI, and "I suspect they're not doing more for silly reasons that I don't quite understand." His known-working intervention is one-on-one coaching: "pretty non-scalable, but it works really, really well." The plan is to coach a small target audience first, then find something scalable, because the potential productivity gain "seems pretty big".

---

## Part 2: Track record with Ambitious Impact

Austin probed what he'd actually done. Ambitious Impact's leadership wanted uplift, reached out to Chris Webster and Peter Hartree, who suggested coaching; Alejandro was the only coach they rated as "both good at it and also enthusiastic about doing more". So far: six individual sessions plus two follow-ups with Samantha (CEO of AIM), all started "three weeks ago maybe" — which Austin called "pretty fast progress". AIM then asked him to "uplift the whole thing", so over the next three weeks he'll run a batch of coaching calls for charity founders.

Session anatomy: an intake form (things to delegate, current AI use), then the first 10–15 minutes of the call spent "trying to make a really vivid image in my mind of what it looks like to do their job day to day", then doing one immediately-valuable thing live, picking up affordances along the way (Wispr Flow, projects in Cowork, giving context to the AI). Calls go well "maybe half of them", really well "maybe a third" — those end with about three "whoa" wins; the rest get stuck but "still I think people like them anyway".

Pricing: first sessions at "$20 an hour"; the founder batch negotiated at "$100 an hour" — but split $50 founders / $25 AIM / $25 via "a weird deal". Austin approved: subsidized deals for founders are "very much in the Surplus spirit".

---

## Part 3: Scaling ideas and community

Asked how one coach scales, Alejandro offered:

1. An AI uplift consultancy org — scale by adding consultants (the idea he's floated most).
2. A baseline-then-broadcast model he's "not sure I quite believe": get people past a setup baseline (Claude Code, GitHub), then apply scalable interventions — currently coaching → excitement → a Slack channel he spams with AI ideas; possibly blog-post tips, though "I'm not sure what fraction of the value that captures".
3. Reduce the power-user/normal-person gap "to a small set of factors", build pockets of AI power users inside orgs, and standardize giving them "the affordances to act within the organization to bring about change".

He was candid that failures often look trivially fixable — a slightly different prompt would work — but "it's probably not as easy as tweaking the prompt" and "I really don't have a good model of" what separates power users.

On community: two Slack channels — a year-old builder-support one that "worked really well" as a filter (3–4 truly active people), and a newer catch-all channel for anyone enthusiastic, which also got dominated by power users. Traffic: 2–3 top-level messages a day, "triple or four times that" with thread replies.

---

## Part 4: The 80k plan

Asked about client pipeline after the AIM batch, Alejandro dropped the key reveal: "I mostly just plan to go do 80,000 Hours for three months, that's my default plan." 80k had been looking to eventually hire an AI uplift person; "I love 80k really hard"; he "only gained the determination to do this on the weekend" and started forming plans this week. 80k would pay for his services and sponsor a visa; the exact role is undefined ("we still haven't figured out the details of what I would do"). Prior 80k connection: footnotes for Benjamin Todd's book — a copy of which sits on Austin's shelf, with Alejandro's name in the acknowledgments.

Austin found the 80k route "quite reasonable": 80k has access to orgs and individuals who want coaching, plus institutional coaching/advising experience. Alejandro's call with Peter Hartree is tomorrow (maybe his fourth ever; he rations his outreach to Peter). Austin respects Peter and rates TYPE III AUDIO as "a very cool product".

---

## Part 5: Surplus fit, logistics, and Austin's steer

Logistics from Austin: he aims to reply to every video-interviewee "within like a week"; he'll keep Alejandro's possibly-urgent 80k timeline in mind; a US J-1 visa is probably doable but might force a remote first week.

Alejandro's own frame: Surplus is more appealing in the world where Peter is involved — but Peter "doesn't want to be my co-founder" (while still endorsing the idea), which would leave Alejandro co-founder-hunting. Otherwise he wants most of his work to be for 80k, perhaps from within Surplus.

Austin's clear advice:

> "I think if the thing that you're excited to do is coaching, you probably should just do that in 80k. I don't think it makes as much sense as a Surplus project."

Surplus fits a scalable-intervention version — "more software-shaped", though not necessarily software: "I can imagine like a book being a good project." Alejandro conceded he doesn't yet know what that product would look like. Austin closed the business half warmly: everyone's new to uplift, Alejandro is "moving really fast", and he should keep doing coaching calls for highly leveraged people.

Austin was game (though "extremely busy" for two weeks) and, notably, asked to become a coaching client himself — he uses AI heavily for coding and transcription but suspects there's more to gain.

## Appendix 3: Underlying dynamics

**An interview that self-resolved:** Alejandro volunteered mid-interview that his default is 80k, effectively arguing against his own application. Austin didn't fight it — he validated the 80k path and defined the narrow condition (a scalable product idea, plausibly with Peter) under which Surplus makes sense. Both left with a cleaner decision rule rather than a sales outcome.

**Peter Hartree as the pivot:** Peter recurs at every joint — he sourced the AIM coaching, declines to co-found while endorsing the idea, is respected by Austin, and has a call with Alejandro the next day. The Surplus option lives or dies mostly on Peter's involvement.

**Rapport beyond the application:** The marriage-and-Catholicism ask and Austin signing up as a coaching client turned a rejection-shaped conversation into a relationship — Austin is now a prospective client and ongoing contact regardless of the Surplus decision.
