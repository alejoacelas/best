# How to read the evidence here

Every claim in this folder tells you what backs it, in one word, before it tells you
the conclusion — especially the top-level sentences. The thing I keep needing to know
is not "is there evidence" but: **<!--me-->is this established by trials on the outcome I
actually care about, or patched together from indirect sources?<!--/me-->**

## Tiers, strongest to weakest

- **gold** — two or more RCTs measuring the outcome I care about (lifespan, energy,
  cognition). The only tier that proves the payoff.
- **RCT (surrogate)** — RCT evidence, but on an intermediate marker (LDL, ferritin,
  blood glucose). Shows a mechanism moves; not that it buys me anything I want.
- **cohort** — large observational studies on the real outcome. Directional only;
  healthy-user bias and confounding can produce the whole effect on their own.
- **patchwork** — triangulated from indirect studies, cross-sectional snapshots, or
  physiology. A reasoned guess.

## Rules

- A verdict sentence names its tier. If the tier is cohort or patchwork, say so
  *before* the conclusion, not in a footnote.
- Never let strong evidence on the wrong outcome — weight-loss RCTs, LDL RCTs — stand
  in for weak evidence on the right one: do I live longer, do I feel better.
- The tier summarizes the uncertainty; it doesn't replace the caveats. Keep both.
