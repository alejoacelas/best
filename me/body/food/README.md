What should I eat — and how much of it can a machine decide for me?

The food pillar of `body/`. Two diet decisions, each with a short verdict here and a
deep-dive file for the methodology and sources behind it. Long-term effects were
weighted most heavily; the short-term is mostly a transition cost.

## Verdicts

- **Meal replacements — up to ~25-50% of daily calories long-term.**
  [`meal-replacements.md`](meal-replacements.md). One to two of my eating occasions
  from a complete formula (soy-isolate or a proper pea+rice blend), keeping a
  whole-food base indefinitely for chewing, fiber diversity, and phytonutrients.
  Near-total replacement is fine only as a defined phase (up to ~12 weeks), never
  casually. This is an evidence-bounded default, not a safety line — no trial has
  compared long-term fractions head to head.

- **Veganism — longevity-neutral to mildly favorable, energy-neutral, *if* I supplement.**
  [`veganism.md`](veganism.md). The mortality signal tracks plant-food *quality*, not
  the vegan label, and never reaches significance for vegans specifically (US
  Adventist cohort shows benefit, UK EPIC-Oxford shows none), with real countervailing
  harms in stroke and fracture. The diet is won or lost on supplementation:
  [~52% of unsupplemented vegans are B12-deficient vs ~1% of omnivores](veganism.md),
  and B12 plus iodine, D, and algal DHA are cheap fixes. A junk-food or unsupplemented
  vegan diet forfeits the whole case.
