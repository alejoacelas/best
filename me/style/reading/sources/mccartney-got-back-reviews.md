# Paul McCartney on the "Got Back" tour

Short excerpts, quoted for study. Full articles at the links — copyrighted; read
them at Rolling Stone.

## Palm Springs tour opener (Rob Sheffield, Rolling Stone)
https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/paul-mccartney-live-review-palm-springs-tour-opener-1235438048/

Sheffield calls McCartney's "superhuman enthusiasm... terrifying to behold at
83," and argues that his "emotional urgency is what always sets McCartney apart,
and it makes him the greatest of rock & roll live performers." He names the
acoustic "Help!" the emotional climax of the night.

## Nashville (Joseph Hudak, Rolling Stone, Nov 7 2025)
https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-live-reviews/paul-mccartney-nashville-got-back-tour-1235461488/

Two moments the review singles out as what makes him endearing:
- He prefaced "Blackbird" with the story of the Beatles threatening to bail on a
  segregated 1964 Jacksonville concert unless Black and white fans could sit
  together.
- He opened George Harrison's "Something" alone on ukulele — "a gorgeous,
  understated rendition that hushed the audience, until the band joined in for
  its euphoric finish."
- The reviewer calls the Abbey Road couplet "in the end, the love you take is
  equal to the love you make" one of the evening's sweetest moments.

Note: the warmth people feel is built from small, deliberate things — the
between-song stories, the tributes to Lennon (a virtual duet on "I've Got a
Feeling") and Harrison, and the plainspoken sincerity. Compare against the
Antonakis tactics (stories, moral conviction, group sentiment) and the McCartney
biographies in the reading list.
