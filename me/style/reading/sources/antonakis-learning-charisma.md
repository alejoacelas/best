## Abstract (as indexed)

The authors challenge the idea that charisma is innate, presenting research that
managers can develop it through learnable techniques. They identify twelve
"charismatic leadership tactics" (CLTs) — nine verbal, three nonverbal — that
raise a person's influence and perceived leadership.

**Nine verbal tactics:** metaphors, similes and analogies; stories and
anecdotes; contrasts; rhetorical questions; three-part lists; expressions of
moral conviction; reflections of the group's sentiments; setting high goals;
and conveying confidence that they can be reached.

**Three nonverbal tactics:** animated voice, facial expressions, and gestures.

The authors report these tactics outperform generic presentation skills: in
their studies, executives who practiced them saw leadership ratings rise by
roughly 60%. The mechanism is emotional connection plus signaled competence and
trustworthiness, letting a leader unite followers around a shared vision.
