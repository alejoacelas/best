# Tyler Cowen's 12 Rules for Life

Posted January 24, 2018 on Marginal Revolution. Cowen framed these as thoughts
that resonate now, not universal principles. Condensed below; read the full
post at the source link for his exact wording.

1. Assume your temperament will stay somewhat childish and impatient; set your
   rules accordingly, and try to leverage that impatience toward long-run ends.
2. Study the symbolic systems of art, music, literature and religion, if only to
   understand alternative points of view.
3. Learn how prices work, and adjust your consumption when they rise.
4. Choose your spouse carefully.
5. Organize part of your knowledge of the world in terms of place — it makes
   almost every person interesting through their local expertise.
6. "When shooting the basketball, give it more arc than you think is necessary.
   Consistently."
7. Learn from those who offend or disagree with you.
8. Cultivate mentors, and be one.
9. "I don't know."
10. Heed Cowen's Three Laws.
11. Disregard Cowen's Three Laws.
12. Reread Erasmus, Montaigne, Shakespeare and the like, to keep perspective and
    not take rules too seriously.

# On his own practice

From David Perell's "Tyler Cowen: Production Function" interview (Dec 2020),
https://perell.com/podcast/tyler-cowen-production-function/ — short verbatim excerpts:

> "If you write every day, you don't even have to worry about how much you've
> written. It's going to add up."

> "Answering email is my business model. And I think it's how I end up well
> plugged in."

On reading fast yet retaining: he can read many times faster than peers, yet
after 100+ Conversations with Tyler interviews, "no one ever says anymore I
don't understand what I read."
