# Share by default — the goods that compound are non-rival

Sharing an idea costs me nothing and can help unboundedly many. That asymmetry —
non-rivalry — is why openness sits near the top of a scope-sensitive ledger: most goods
are used up when given away; ideas are copied, and the copy is free. Publishing is one
of the few acts whose reach isn't bounded by my own effort.

The track record is the argument:

- **The scientific revolution** ran on open publication. Making results public and
  checkable, rather than guarded, is what let knowledge accumulate instead of dying
  with each discoverer.
- **The literature** is that accumulation — the compounding, shared record every new
  result builds on.
- **Growth theory** puts non-rival ideas at the center. Paul Romer's endogenous-growth
  work — the [2018 Nobel](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/economic-sciences/2018/romer/facts/),
  building on his 1990 *Endogenous Technological Change* — models ideas as the nonrival
  input that drives technological progress and long-run economic growth. Sharing them
  is not charity; it's the mechanism.
- **Ideas spread locally too**, not only to the frontier. Much of the value lands on
  whoever is near — the people I work with, the ones who find the repo — through
  diffusion, not just the one breakthrough.

So the rule, and why it earns its place on this list: **default to public, and share the
process, not just the result.** Save the workflow, the methodology, the dead ends beside
the output. Redact only what's genuinely about others. Everything non-rival, let it
compound.
