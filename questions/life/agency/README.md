Why are people so helpless at solving their own problems?
<!--/ai-->

<!--ai-->
The provocative framing for a real puzzle: capable people leave *solvable* problems unsolved —
they under-attempt, quit at the first "no," never send the cold email, never even list the options.
And the puzzle is sharper now, because AI collapses the cost of the first attempt toward zero, so
what's left as the binding constraint is disposition, not capability. This folder tackles the
question from several angles at once and tries to state the candidate explanations precisely enough
to argue with.

Read it in two layers: skim the hypotheses for the argument, click into an angle file for the raw
sourced material behind any claim.

## The hypotheses ([full file](hypotheses.md))
The candidate explanations, located by *where in the chain* — perceive → generate → evaluate →
form intention → execute → persist — each one puts the break:

- **H1 · Non-representation** — the condition is never encoded as a problem-with-solutions; it's felt as fixed, like weather. Brainstorming works by *re-categorizing* it into a target.
- **H2 · Fixation** — the first idea captures attention and suppresses search; "stuck" is premature closure, not problem-space size. *(Best-evidenced mechanical account.)*
- **H3 · Option-generation ≠ akrasia** — the winning option never enters the choice set; the failure is upstream of willpower, where self-help never aims.
- **H4 · Efficacy-gating** — attempting is gated by a P(success) estimate biased low, because a failed attempt is legible to self-image and a non-attempt is deniable.
- **H5 · Social-cost mispricing** — the cheap first move (ask, cold-email) is overpriced; people underestimate stranger compliance by ~48%. *(Best-quantified instance.)*
- **H6 · Temporal discounting** — intentions form cool and distant, action happens hot and near; intention converts to behavior at a steep discount.
- **H7 · Over-ready revision** — the intention is formed but abandoned at first friction (Holton's weakness of will).
- **H8 · Agency is learned, domain-local control** — passivity is the wired-in default; *control* is what's learned, per domain. *(The strongest reframe: the Maier–Seligman self-reversal.)*
- **H9 · Environmental training** — institutions punish off-menu initiative, so not-trying may be *correct* learning about a low-agency environment.
- **H10 · AI as constraint-shift** — AI is a prosthesis for exactly the upstream stages, sharpening the residual down to the dispositional core. *(Hold loosely — forward-looking.)*

## The angles (raw sourced material)
- **[cate-hall.md](cate-hall.md)** — Cate Hall's six posts, the writer this started from: agency is learnable, *selectively* held, and bottlenecked by willingness not ability. "If you're only asking for things you get, you're not aiming high enough."
- **[psychology.md](psychology.md)** — learned helplessness and its 2016 reversal, self-efficacy, fixation/Einstellung, the intention–action gap, hope theory, the ~48% help-seeking underestimate. Flags what survived the replication crisis and what didn't.
- **[philosophy.md](philosophy.md)** — akrasia (Aristotle, Davidson, Holton), trying as the unit of action (O'Shaughnessy, Hornsby), and the grades-of-agency scale (Frankfurt's *wanton*, Bratman, Korsgaard) that makes "more or less an agent" rigorous.
- **[blogosphere.md](blogosphere.md)** — the rationalist/tech canon on agency: Soares, Yudkowsky's "heroic responsibility," PG's "relentlessly resourceful," Nabeel's "plant the flag," Salamon's "learned blankness."

## The two claims to build on first
Both rest on behavioral data, not self-report: **H8** — passivity is the default and agency is
*learned control* (Maier & Seligman inverted their own 1967 theory) — and **H5** — the systematic
~48% underestimate of how willing others are to help.

## Live questions
- Classify a real sample of *your own* under-attempted problems by which stage broke (never-represented / fixated / never-generated / bad-estimate / discounted / abandoned-early). Where does the mass actually sit?
- If H3 is right — the bottleneck is generation, not willpower — what's the lowest-friction personal "generate options" ritual, and does running it on a stuck domain move anything?
- Test H8 directly: does one vivid experience of control in a frozen domain bootstrap attempting there?
