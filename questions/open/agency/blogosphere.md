# The rationalist / tech blogosphere on agency

Self-published essays, fiction, newsletters, and tweets — blog opinion, not peer-reviewed
philosophy. The folk concept throughout is *agency*: treating your problems as solvable and
actually trying, rather than accepting the default. Quotes verified by fetching the source
except where noted; several attributions commonly gotten wrong are corrected inline.

---

## Nate Soares ("So8res") — [*Replacing Guilt*](https://mindingourway.com)
- **[Half-assing it with everything you've got](https://mindingourway.com/half-assing-it-with-everything-youve-got/)** — aim at the actual target, not at effort; find the minimum quality that hits the goal and spend full strength getting there. "If you're trying to pass the class, then pass it with minimum effort." / "Always deploy your full strength, in order to hit your quality target as fast as possible."
- **[Detach the grim-o-meter](https://mindingourway.com/detach-the-grim-o-meter/)** — grimness is a local motivational tool; don't calibrate it against the aggregate darkness of the world. "The world is dark and gritty, but that doesn't mean that you need to be dark and gritty to match."
- **[Caring about something larger than yourself](https://mindingourway.com/caring-about-some/)** — "Caring isn't about being overwhelmed by emotion, it's about not having the emotional compulsion and doing the right thing anyway."

## Eliezer Yudkowsky — the community's foundational statements
- **[Something to Protect](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/SGR4GxFK7KmW7ckCB/something-to-protect)** (often misattributed to Soares): rationality needs a purpose outside itself. "Power comes from having something to protect." / "The Art must have a purpose other than itself, or it collapses into infinite recursion." / "No one masters the Way until more than their life is at stake."
- **[Heroic responsibility](https://hpmor.com/chapter/75)** (HPMOR ch. 75, Harry to Hermione): you own the *outcome*. "You could call it heroic responsibility, maybe… It means that whatever happens, no matter what, it's *always* your fault." / "Following the school rules isn't an excuse, someone else being in charge isn't an excuse, even trying your best isn't an excuse… you've got to *get the job done no matter what*."

## LessWrong — [the "Agency" tag](https://www.lesswrong.com/w/agency)
Agency defined: "the property of effectively acting with an environment to achieve one's goals… the
more agentic a being is, the more you can predict its actions from its goals." Contrasted with
"sphexishness, the blind execution of cached algorithms without regard for effectiveness." (The
"player-character vs NPC" framing is real community shorthand but *not* a verbatim tag quote.)
Canonical how-to posts: "Ways to be more agenty," "Seven ways to become unstoppably agentic," "You
can just do things," "Being a Robust Agent."

## Paul Graham
- **[How to Do Great Work](https://paulgraham.com/greatwork.html)** — "Many more people could try to do great work than do. What holds them back is a combination of modesty and fear." / "Great work usually entails spending what would seem to most people an unreasonable amount of time on a problem." / "Never let setbacks panic you into backtracking more than you need to. Corollary: Never abandon the root node."
- **[Relentlessly Resourceful](https://paulgraham.com/relres.html)** — relentlessness alone fails on novel problems: "In any interesting domain, the difficulties will be novel… you have to be resourceful. You have to keep trying new things." / "To be hapless is to be battered by circumstances — to let the world have its way with you, instead of having your way with the world."

## Nabeel Qureshi — [Principles](https://nabeelqu.co/principles), [Reflections on Palantir](https://nabeelqu.co/reflections)
Agency = refuse to wait for permission; resistance, not the task, eats the time.
- "Stop asking for approval and permission from others… figure out what you want to do, and plant the 'this is happening' flag."
- "Once you are ok with people telling you 'no', you can ask for whatever you want. (Make reality say no to you.)"
- "Things don't actually take much time…; resistance/procrastination does. 'Slow is fake'."

## Byrne Hobart — [Optionality is for Innumerate Cowards](https://byrnehobart.medium.com/optionality-is-for-innumerate-cowards-f2c5a759b607)
Chronically keeping options open is a disguised failure of nerve; agency means committing.
- "Many otherwise smart and well-adjusted people have talked themselves into being the Ebenezer Scrooge of optionality, always hoarding the ability to do something later, never actually doing anything when 'later' arrives, and giving up a lot in the process."

## Applied Divinity Studies — [No One is Even Trying](https://www.applieddivinitystudies.com/trying/)
Most people work far below capacity; escaping it takes self-generated meaning, not just more hours.
- "Escaping the bind takes more than hard work. You have to actually go out and generate your own sense of meaning."
- "Why does it feel like no one else is even close?"

## John Salvatier — [Reality has a surprising amount of detail](https://johnsalvatier.org/blog/2017/reality-has-a-surprising-amount-of-detail)
Problems look simple from afar and explode into detail up close; people "bounce off" hard tasks
because the important details are invisible until you engage.
- "Before you've noticed important details they are, of course, basically invisible… It's hard to put your attention on them because you don't even know what you're looking for."
- "It's really easy to get stuck. Stuck in your current way of seeing and thinking about things."

## Patrick McKenzie (patio11) / John Collison — the museum of passion projects
- patio11 ([tweet](https://x.com/patio11/status/1643665830360776704), reprising Collison): "The world is a museum of passion projects. A lot of what we know and love required a small group of people, surprisingly often literally one, to make it their singular mission for a long time."
- Collison's [original](https://x.com/collision/status/1529452415346302976): "things around you weren't just always there; people made them happen… That hotel, that park, that railway. The world is a museum of passion projects."

## Alexey Guzey — [guzey.com](https://guzey.com)
No single agency manifesto; see [Every productivity thought…](https://guzey.com/productivity/) and
[Advice](https://guzey.com/advice/). Notably his own *reversal* — that solo grinding doesn't sustain,
so he [co-works with friends](https://guzey.com/co-working/) — is itself an agency-relevant data point.

## Anna Salamon — [Learned Blankness](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/puhPJimawPuNZ5wAR/learned-blankness) (LessWrong, 2011)
The community's "learned helplessness" — people *trained* to believe they can't change their
situation — distinct from Seligman's clinical term.
- "I think we need a term for learned helplessness about thinking (in a particular domain). I'll call this 'learned blankness'."
- "Learned blankness means not just choosing to ignore a domain, but viewing that domain as inaccessible."
- (Disambiguation: Scott Alexander's "Epistemic Learned Helplessness" is a *different* usage — refusing to update on clever arguments — not the agency sense.)

## The common target
Where analytic philosophy fixes on akrasia (judged X best, did Y), the blogosphere targets the break
*before* judgment even resolves into intention: "I never planted the flag" (Nabeel), "I bounced off
the detail" (Salvatier), "I hoarded optionality" (Hobart), "I treated the domain as inaccessible"
(Salamon). Their shared prescription is the folk theorem behind Cate Hall's title — *you can just do
things* — and their shared diagnosis is that the binding constraint is disposition, not capability.
