Which innovation-funding models are worth copying for growing AI enablement at 80k?

The [Atlas of Innovation](https://atlasofinnovation.org/) — by the
[Institute for Progress](https://ifp.org) and the
[Market Shaping Accelerator](https://www.marketshapingaccelerator.org/), presented at
[NBER 2026](https://www.nber.org/books-and-chapters/entrepreneurship-and-innovation-policy-and-economy-volume-6/policymaker-centric-guide-selecting-innovation-funding-mechanisms)
— catalogs [13 funding mechanisms](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library), each with
when-it-works, failure modes, and real cases. I read all 13 and picked the cases most
useful for [what I read as your goal: growing AI adoption and capacity at 80,000 Hours
— an org and a field, not a product]. Ranked by how directly each transfers.

## Top picks

**1. Manne Economics Institute for Federal Judges** —
[fieldbuilding](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/fieldbuilding). Two-week
economics courses for sitting judges; by the 1990s nearly half of all federal judges
had attended, and their opinions and sentencing measurably shifted toward economic
reasoning. The cleanest evidence that short, high-status training changes how busy
professionals work — the exact shape of teaching AI to 80k staff and advisees.
[Program](https://masonlec.org/divisions/mason-judicial-education-program/) ·
[QJE paper on the effects](https://academic.oup.com/qje/advance-article/doi/10.1093/qje/qjaf042/8241352)

**2. Fast Grants** —
[research grants](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/research-grants). COVID-era
science funding with 48-hour decisions; $50M+ across 260 grants. The model for
low-friction internal money/time: tiny AI-experiment grants to staff, decided in days
not committees. Also the mechanism that best matches your "move fast, learn which
ideas pan out" plan. [fastgrants.org](https://fastgrants.org/)

**3. The ARPA model (ARPANET, stealth)** —
[coordinated research programs](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/coordinated-research-programs).
Empowered program managers on 2-year terms who mix funding tools, back unconventional
performers, and kill underperforming projects fast. Read it as the job description for
an AI-enablement lead running a portfolio of adoption bets. Its failure modes (wrong
problems, PMs unwilling to kill projects) are the ones to watch in your own portfolio.
[ARPANET](https://www.comsoc.org/node/19486) ·
[Stealth case](https://www.darpa.mil/sites/default/files/attachment/2025-03/stealth-advancing-national-security-through-fundamental-research.pdf) ·
[RAND on DARPA](https://www.jstor.org/stable/10.7249/mr1696osd.13) ·
[UK's ARIA](https://www.aria.org.uk/) (same model, includes a Safeguarded AI program)

**4. DARPA Grand Challenges** —
[prizes](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/prizes). $1–2M self-driving races:
no winner in 2004, Stanford's Stanley in 2005, and the veterans seeded Google's
self-driving team → Waymo. A prize that mainly bought a *talent pipeline and a field*,
not a product — the case for public AI challenges/hackathons whose real output is who
shows up. [grandchallenge.org](https://www.grandchallenge.org/) ·
[Ansari XPRIZE](https://www.xprize.org/prizes/ansari) (26 teams spent $100M chasing $10M)

**5. SF Ruby meetups → GitHub** —
[fieldbuilding](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/fieldbuilding). Informal
recurring gatherings where GitHub's cofounders met. The near-zero-cost end of
fieldbuilding: standing AI show-and-tells, in-office or London-scene, compound into
hires and projects.
[Tom Preston-Werner's account](https://tom.preston-werner.com/2011/03/29/ten-lessons-from-githubs-first-year)

**6. HHMI Investigators & Janelia** —
[research grants](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/research-grants) /
[intramural science](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/intramural-science).
Fund people, not projects: ~$11M over renewable 7-year terms produced more seminal
work than project grants. The argument for giving your most capable colleagues
standing AI-experimentation time instead of per-project approval.
[Investigator program](https://www.hhmi.org/programs/investigators) ·
[Azoulay–Graff Zivin–Manso study](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1756-2171.2011.00140.x) ·
[Janelia's 15-year model](https://www.janelia.org/about-us)

**7. NASA COTS** —
[milestone payments](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/milestone-payments) /
[procurement](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/procurement-contracts).
Fixed-price, milestone-staged contracts let an under-capitalized 2006 SpaceX in and
cut development costs ~10x vs cost-plus. For bigger internal AI builds: stage the
commitment, pay on demos, screen weak bets early.
[NASA lessons learned](https://web.archive.org/web/20251114164322/https://appel.nasa.gov/2022/11/21/spotlight-on-lessons-learned-lessons-learned-from-nasas-commercial-orbital-transportation-services-cots-program/) ·
[COTS history (NASA SP)](https://www.nasa.gov/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/sp-2014-617.pdf)

## Also noted

- **BlueDot Impact** ([fieldbuilding](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/fieldbuilding))
  — courses as the entry path into a field with no academic pipeline; nearest
  neighbor to 80k's own audience. [bluedot.org](https://bluedot.org/)
- **Frontier** ([AMCs](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/advance-market-commitments))
  — $1B pre-commitment to buy carbon removal that doesn't exist yet; demand signals
  pull supply into existence. Analogy: publicly pre-commit to adopting AI workflows
  that hit a spec. [frontierclimate.com](https://frontierclimate.com/) · the original
  [pneumococcal AMC](https://www.gavi.org/investing-gavi/innovative-financing/pneumococcal-amc)
  saved ~700k lives.
- **Longitude Prize (1714)** ([prizes](https://atlasofinnovation.org/library/prizes))
  — designed as a fixed prize, administered bespoke: partial rewards to 10+ people,
  Harrison paid in installments. Announced rules meet messy reality.
  [Nesta's history](https://www.nesta.org.uk/feature/guide-historical-challenge-prizes/british-longitude-prize/)
