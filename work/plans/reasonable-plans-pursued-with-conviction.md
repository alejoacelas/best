# Reasonable plans, pursued with conviction

* It's great to be on a field that moves fast. I've had ideas that I didn't pursue, and 3 months (and once 3 weeks\!) later someone else implements it and you get to learn if it was a good idea. Also it's great if *you* personally move fast. You'll get to know much more quickly which of your ideas pan out  
* After I've seen some of my 3-month project ideas come up again in the future and be a bit more validated as a worthwhile endeavour, I've gotten more confident that I can actually figure out things that would be worth me investing a year or more working on. Specially if I'm proactive at formulating clearly and getting feedback on the high-level plan  
* The conviction part is important. An important mission pursued with conviction is one of the main factors for attracting talent. The other is being able to recognize the talent's strengths and create a narrative for how they fit in that mission
