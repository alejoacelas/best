# Jargon audit — 04-now-what.md (2026-07-04)

## Changes

- **"a second model critique the first" → "a second AI model critique the first"**
  — "model" alone is field vocabulary; ordinary meaning ("a representation," "a
  fashion model") points elsewhere. Pairing it with "AI" once makes the noun
  self-carrying for a reader new to the field.

- **"a second opinion from a second model" → "a second opinion from a different AI
  model"** — same fix; second occurrence. "different" also sharpens the intended
  point (a distinct underlying system, not just another instance).

- **"decompose it until each piece does" → "break it into smaller pieces until
  each one does"** — "decompose" reads as rot/chemistry in plain use; the
  technical "break into parts" sense needs field priming. Also aligns with "Break
  the task into pieces" three lines above.

## Considered and kept

- **skill** ("turning into a skill," "turn it into a skill so I can rerun it") —
  the product-feature sense (a saved, rerunnable procedure) is not the plain
  meaning of "skill," but this is doc 04 of a series and skills are introduced in
  03-power-ups. The reader arrives with the term defined.

- **agent** ("Agents are slow," "point a fresh agent") — plain meaning (something
  that acts on your behalf) maps well enough onto the technical sense, and the
  term is load-bearing throughout the series.

- **throughput** ("Your throughput isn't one agent's speed; it's how many useful
  things you have in flight") — a word a smart reader knows, and the sentence
  defines it in place via the contrast that follows.

- **churn** ("estimate our churn next quarter") — business jargon, but it sits
  inside an illustrative quote where the exact metric is not load-bearing; even a
  reader who doesn't know the SaaS sense gets that it's a hard analytical ask.

- **buckets** ("which of three buckets it's in") — everyday English for a
  category; the metaphor is dead and reads plainly.

- **in flight / let it ride** — common idioms, clear from context, and part of the
  author's voice.
