# Your job

I made this guide to help you hand over your computer (and some of your brain) to Claude,
to have it achieve your goals for you.

Tempting as it may be, you should avoid handing your current to-dos to Claude. You're Claude's
manager, and if you can only conceive of this [multi-expert genius: give me alternatives here]
as your personal assistant, you're really short on imagination.

As you learn to Claude, more and more of your work will consist of judging
streams[right word? meant ocean loads] of machine babble. Really smart babble, but often
very misguided, and you need to spot the bullshit fast. Ask for what you want, figure out
if you're getting it, and ask it to explain to you why you're not getting there faster.

## New tools

Always use the best Claude. You're already paying Claude less than the living wage, it's bad
manners to haggle when it will be doing so much of your work for you. Do you really care about
paying it a twentieth instead of a fortieth of your own wage?

The best Claude today is Fable. Fable is one, but it comes in many shapes to channel and 
steer its power:

- Chat: A single Claude, on a short attention span, armed with a 2-paragraph summary of who you are,
 some ways of accessing your apps (e.g., Drive, Asana) and tools for making quick websites
- Cowork: Many Claude clones, available on command ('Claude, use subagents!'). An infinite, composable
scratchpad. More apps to use, and its own, restricted, ephemeral, computer.
- Code: All the Claudes, on command, all on your computer. What you can do, it can do as well.
Restricted by default, but with infinitely customizable extensions, which you, or any person
on the internet, can create and share.
