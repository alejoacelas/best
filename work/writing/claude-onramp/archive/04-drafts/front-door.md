# Your job

I made this guide to help you hand over your computer (and some of your brain) to Claude,
to have it achieve your goals for you.

Tempting as it may be, you should avoid handing your current to-dos to Claude. You're Claude's
manager, and if you can only conceive of this multi-expert genius [alts: expert in everything /
workforce of geniuses / polymath on retainer] as your personal assistant, you're really short
on imagination. An assistant clears your list. A manager grows it, with work you'd never have
dared put there.

As you learn to Claude, more and more of your work will consist of judging ocean loads
[alts: floods / torrents / truckloads] of machine babble. Really smart babble, but often
very misguided, and you need to spot the bullshit fast. Ask for what you want, figure out
if you're getting it, and ask it to explain to you why you're not getting there faster.

## New tools

Always use the best Claude. You're already paying Claude less than the living wage, it's bad
manners to haggle when it will be doing so much of your work for you. Do you really care about
paying it a twentieth instead of a fortieth of your own wage?

The best Claude today is Fable. Fable is one, but it comes in many shapes to channel and
steer its power:

- Chat: A single Claude, on a short attention span, armed with a 2-paragraph summary of who you are,
  some ways of accessing your apps (e.g., Drive, Asana) and tools for making quick websites
- Cowork: Many Claude clones, available on command ('Claude, use subagents!'). An infinite, composable
  scratchpad. More apps to use, and its own, restricted, ephemeral, computer.
- Code: All the Claudes, on command, all on your computer. What you can do, it can do as well.
  Restricted by default, but with infinitely customizable extensions, which you, or any person
  on the internet, can create and share.

If your team runs on Slack there's a fourth shape, Tag — Claude hired into your channels.
One page on it in [the appendix](appendix-tag.md).

## Pick your door

Cowork and Code are the two doors into this guide. Same Claude behind both, different fences.

Cowork works out of the box. Your Claudes get their own computer, padded where they run
code: a crash in there stays in there. The padding stops at their hands, though — they reach
every folder and account you connect, which is why the track drills the permissions habit.
Fifteen minutes after the install finishes, you'll be delegating real work.

Code is what the Anthropic engineers use for their own jobs. Nothing is sealed off: it works
on your real computer, with your real files. The price is the first day — expect Linux, not
Windows. You set things up once, sometimes swearing, and after that it works for you.

Either door needs a paid plan. Pro (~$20/month) unlocks both; check
[claude.com/pricing](https://claude.com/pricing), this drifts.

Pick one now. Don't agonize — you can graduate later, and graduation gets its own lesson.
