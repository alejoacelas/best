# If your team has Tag

*Appendix. For teams on Slack with a Team or Enterprise plan.*

Tag is Claude hired into your Slack as a teammate. Type @Claude in a channel and
it plans the work, does it, and reports back in the thread. It runs in
the cloud — no laptop of yours has to stay open — so it keeps going through your
commute, your meetings, your night. It launched in June 2026, still in beta,
only on Team and Enterprise plans; if you pay for Claude alone, this page is a
preview, not an invitation.

One Claude per channel, shared: everyone sees what it's working on, and anyone
can pick up where the last person left off. And it remembers
the channel — months of your team's decisions are already in its head — so in an
established channel you skip the briefing you'd give a new hire.

Your job here is the one from the front door: ask for what you want, spot the
bullshit. Only now your whole team watches you do it. Good. A correction made in
a thread teaches everyone at once, Claude included.

Three moves matter:

1. **Work in public channels.** Claude learns from what it can see, and a channel
   dedicated to one kind of work becomes its training on your team. Every task
   you take to a DM is a lesson you withheld from everyone else.
2. **One thread per task; correct with new replies, never edits.** Each top-level
   message starts a task, and editing a message does nothing to work already in
   flight. Say what was wrong in a reply and the correction sticks for the
   channel's future work.
3. **Give it something to own.** Graduate from tasks to a responsibility — "keep
   the questions in this channel answered" — then every few weeks ask it what's
   been raised repeatedly that nobody answered. Standing duties can run without
   anyone tagging it (that switch is called ambient mode), but earn the trust
   with tagged tasks first.

Your IT admin will have two questions. Can our departments' Claudes be kept
apart? Yes: admins can create separate Claude identities — sales,
engineering — whose memories and permissions never cross. And where's the
record? Every task, who requested it, and every call it made out to other tools
lands in an audit log in the org settings. One more habit, yours rather than
your admin's: don't assume a connection exists because it ought to. Ask Claude
what it can see from the channel before you blame it for missing data nobody
ever wired up.

The win, today: pick one task your team actually repeats — the report someone
rewrites every Friday, the question answered weekly in three separate DMs — and
tag @Claude with it in a public channel. The work comes back in the thread, where
everyone can see it, and where everyone can see you asking.
