# Now what — actually delegating

You have the tools. The skill now is spotting what to hand off and handing it
off well. Three habits carry most of the value — read them, then do the one
thing at the bottom.

## Find the work: the 5× rule

Anything you'll do five or more times, ask whether Claude can do it — or set it
up so Claude does it from now on. Recurring, fiddly, or boring is a feature, not
a disqualifier: those are the tasks worth turning into a skill once and never
doing by hand again.

And go after the tasks you currently *skip* — too technical, too small, too
slow, too vague to start. Those are where delegation changes what you're capable
of, not just how fast you are.

## Hand it off well: verify, trust, or don't care

You can't just say "estimate our churn next quarter" and trust the result. For
every piece of a task, know which of three buckets it's in:

- **Verify it** — you'll check the output. Ask for the source next to the
  answer, run the code, or have a second AI model critique the first. Break the
  task into pieces you *can* check.
- **Trust it** — you've seen Claude do this kind of thing well before.
- **Don't care** — the cost of it being wrong is near zero.

If a piece fits none of these, break it into smaller pieces until each one does.
That's the whole craft of delegation.

## Don't wait: run many at once

Agents are slow, and you are not idle. The moment you've set one going, start
another — a different task, a check on the first one's work, a second opinion
from a different AI model. Your throughput isn't one agent's speed; it's how many
useful things you have in flight. (On a Mac, [Superconductor](decisions/interface.md)
makes this the default way of working.)

## Do this now: your first real delegation

**Goal:** hand off one task that's actually yours — not a practice run.

Pick the one that looks most like your real work and go:

- **Inbox → action:** *"Read my unread email from this week, group it by what it
  needs from me, and draft replies to the three most urgent."*
- **Documents → answer:** *"Here are twelve PDFs. Pull out each
  invoice's total and due date and build me a spreadsheet, with a link back to
  the source for each."*
- **Recurring report:** *"Every Monday I write a status update from these three
  sources. Do it this week, then turn it into a skill so I can rerun it."*
- **The thing you've been avoiding:** the task you keep not starting because it's
  vague. Describe it out loud, mess and all, and ask Claude where to begin.

**✓ Done when** you've delegated something you'd otherwise have done yourself —
and gotten back something you can use.

**Now try →** while that one runs, point a fresh agent at the
same result and say *"critique this and tell me what you'd fix."* That's the
whole way of working, in two windows.
