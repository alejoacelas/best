As I've aged, I've found myself more interested in expressing my consequentialism at higher levels of abstraction. I used to be very worried about whether my individual acts were good (e.g., which classes should I take); now I'm much more worried about which virtues should I cultivate. I think part of that is a good move. You start by understanding the base level where you can get more feedback from reality, and build up to higher levels of abstraction.

That's why I think you can't simply copy the practices of extreme power users. It's like taking someone who's not worked in an org to be the manager. Managing all your projects using 2-sentence messages on Telegram is not something people can do if they don't have some understanding of the lower levels of abstraction.
