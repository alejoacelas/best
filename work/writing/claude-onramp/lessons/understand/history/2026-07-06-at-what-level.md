# How `at-what-level.md` was made — 2026-07-06

**Request.** From the second tab (`t.le1e2thkbbx5`) of the "think / understand / ending"
Google Doc: *"make a quick report that helps me think how the literature on utilitarian
focal points/decision procedures could map here."* The tab's "understand" section draws
the analogy itself — "you can choose at the level of acts, motives, character, etc … the
highest level covers more terrain but is less responsive to circumstances" — and names
Askell & Carlsmith choosing Claude's constitution.

**Anchor chosen.** Toby Ord's *global consequentialism* (DPhil thesis *Beyond Action*,
2009) — the exact literature that scores many focal points (acts, procedures, motives,
rules, character) by one criterion, evaluated independently. That independence is what
licenses "judge at the top, pull the lever low."

**Supporting sources pulled in** (all linked in the report): Bales 1971 (criterion vs
decision procedure), Railton 1984 (sophisticated consequentialism — be judged by results,
don't deliberate by them), Pettit & Smith 2000 (global consequentialism, earlier
statement), Hare's two-level utilitarianism (intuitive/critical switch + its trigger),
Sidgwick/Williams "Government House utilitarianism" (who sets the level).

**Mapping built.** Ethics focal points → Claude levers (output / prompt+CLAUDE.md /
persona+constitution / skills+permissions / model). Three original contributions beyond
restating the literature: (1) the *frequency heuristic* — one-off failures stay at the
act level, recurring ones get promoted up a level; (2) the *don't-over-fit* warning drawn
from focal-point independence — one bad act isn't evidence against a disposition; (3) the
link to tab 1's "cached thinking" — both tabs are one argument about at what level to
install the cache. Section 8 flags three places the analogy breaks (task-relative
criterion; direct write-access to motives; leaky level boundaries).

**Verification.** Toby Ord thesis title, year, and URLs confirmed via web search
(amirrorclear.net + Oxford ORA). Other citations are standard references stated from
knowledge; JSTOR/PhilPapers/Wikipedia links given for the reader to check. Not
independently re-verified page-by-page — treat the report as a thinking aid, not a
citation-audited paper.
