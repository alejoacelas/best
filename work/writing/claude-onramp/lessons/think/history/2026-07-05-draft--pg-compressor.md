# PG density pass — verbalize-what-you-want organized draft

Scope: only the first `<!--ai-->…<!--/ai-->` block (the "Organized draft"), lines under
`# Verbalize what you want` down to the `---` before `## Raw notes`. Raw notes and the
three stylist passes untouched.

Finding: the block was already close to the target. It's the deliberately "organized"
draft, so most restatement had already been squeezed out. Two cuts stood up to the test;
everything else earns its words.

## Cuts

- "Do what you'd do with a person: bring them the mess and let the conversation turn it
  into a plan. **You already do this — think back to** the last time you talked a work
  task through with a colleague." → "…**Think back to** the last time you talked a work
  task through with a colleague."
  Why: "You already do this" is fully implied by asking the reader to recall a time they
  did it. The example carries the reassurance.

- "I'd rather improvise **the whole thing as** a speech" → "I'd rather improvise **it as**
  a speech"
  Why: "the whole thing" duplicates the "a spare hour" framing (fill the time); "it"
  keeps the referent (the message) without the redundant scope word.

## Considered and kept

- "It comes out worse and more imprecise than if you'd written it — but Claude is
  forgiving of that, infinitely patient with your ramblings, and perceptive enough to
  notice the gaps you left." The three predicates look list-y, but forgiving (tolerates
  low quality), patient (tolerates volume), and perceptive (fills gaps) are distinct.
  Kept whole; also near-verbatim author phrasing from the raw notes.

- "Writing is hard — my primate brain heaves as it turns jumbled thoughts into straight
  prose." Tempting to drop "Writing is hard" as a topic sentence the image restates, but
  it's the plain fact-and-stop the author's voice is built on; the primate-brain clause
  carries a real image, not just decoration. Both stay.

## Qualifiers kept (load-bearing, not flagged as removable)

- "most of the time" — in "When you don't know what you want — most of the time —": sets
  the base rate the whole lesson rests on.
- "probably" — "you'd probably come away understanding more": encodes the author's actual
  confidence; removing it overstates.
- "Most people think best out loud" — "Most" is a real scope limit, not filler.
