### Resources for Chapter 5

## Its Favorite Things

This is the online resource for Chapter 5 of _If Anyone Builds It, Everyone Dies_. Some topics we _don’t_ cover on this page, because they’re addressed in the book, include:

  * What motive would AI have for wiping us out?
  * Won’t sufficiently intelligent AIs discover that the right thing to do is to help us all flourish together?
  * Won’t humans still be valuable to superintelligent AIs, e.g., as trading partners?
  * It’s a big universe. Why wouldn’t AI just leave us alone?
  * Would it be a meaningful end to humanity, to let ourselves be replaced by something smarter?



In the book, we said that we’ve heard a long list of hopes and copes about superintelligence might benefit humanity in spite of the issues described in Chapter 4. This is the place where we summarize and respond to a long list of them. Many of the answers overlap; two relatively central points we’ll be making are that humans [aren’t the most efficient solution](/5/humans-are-almost-never-the-most-efficient-solution) to almost any problem, and that AI is unlikely to care about us [even a small amount](/5/wont-ais-care-at-least-a-little-about-humans). This is also where we’ll dive into a discussion of [AI consciousness and morality](/5/effectiveness-consciousness-and-ai-welfare).
