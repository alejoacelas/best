# Focus Areas

Our mission is to help build a world guided by reason and compassion for all sentient beings. We make both grants to nonprofits and investments in socially beneficial companies. Our current focus areas are outlined below.

## Artificial Intelligence

#### AI governance

Advanced AI could have enormous benefits but could also pose catastrophic risks. We're particularly interested in preventing advanced AI from being misused or falling into the wrong hands, including through technical work on [compute governance](https://www.governance.ai/post/computing-power-and-the-governance-of-ai) and [information security](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA2849-1.html).

#### AI welfare

As AI systems advance, they may develop sentience and require [moral consideration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.00986). The [NYU Center for Mind, Ethics, and Policy](https://nonhumanminds.org/) and [Eleos AI](https://eleosai.org/) are some of our grantees in this area.

#### Cooperative AI

We support research on promoting [cooperation](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-021-01170-0) between advanced AI systems. The [Center on Long-Term Risk](https://longtermrisk.org/) and the [Cooperative AI Foundation](https://www.cooperativeai.com/) are among our grantees in this area. We are also interested in work that guides [AI character](https://www.forethought.org/research/the-importance-of-ai-character) towards cooperativeness and benevolence.

## Reason & Democracy

Some of history's worst catastrophes have followed the erosion of democratic institutions, the rule of law, and reasoned public discourse. We support work that strengthens these and other safeguards to reduce risks from authoritarianism and [stable totalitarianism](https://80000hours.org/problem-profiles/risks-of-stable-totalitarianism/). Advanced AI is a central concern here, since it could create new pathways for power to be highly concentrated and entrenched—in the worst case, in the hands of [fanatical](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/EDBQPT65XJsgszwmL/long-term-risks-from-ideological-fanaticism) or malevolent actors.

## Animal Welfare

Both [farmed](https://80000hours.org/problem-profiles/factory-farming/) and [wild animals](https://80000hours.org/problem-profiles/wild-animal-welfare/) likely undergo great suffering in huge numbers. We are interested in interventions to reduce animal suffering, including that of especially neglected populations such as farmed fish and invertebrates.

## New Directions

Improving the world—especially from a long-term perspective—is fraught with [extreme uncertainty](https://ora.ox.ac.uk/objects/uuid:c4216a81-288d-432f-b6fc-3047bf9567f0). It's entirely possible that our current efforts are misguided. For example, most of our grants so far have supported academic and independent research but we'd also like to support more practical and entrepreneurial projects.

We actively seek external input, including critiques of our current work, to refine our approach to reducing long-term risks. This may lead us to pursue different focus areas or projects.
