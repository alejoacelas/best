When you aim to do good, you face questions like: How should I compare different global problems, and how can I contribute to solving them?

The In-Depth EA Program helps you engage with complex questions like these to help you figure out how you can increase your impact. Each week, you'll complete a set of readings that focus primarily on helping you learn about global problems and strategies for solving them. You'll then have weekly 1.5-hour discussions with a cohort of 4-8 participants and one facilitator. This program is designed as a follow-up to our Introductory EA Program. [The full curriculum can be found here.](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1QsiRk7rwBNHxAdwVogu2zJaodsQM4B6GX1A3raJxEcw/edit?usp=sharing)

We think this course is a particularly good fit for people who:

  * Already have a good understanding of [core ideas in effective altruism](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/cN9Zu7dowefAfmNnH/the-effective-altruism-handbook).
  * Are excited by the topics covered in the curriculum.



Requirements

  * Can commit at least 3 hours a week to readings and exercises.
  * Can attend at least 7 out of the 8 weekly discussion sessions.
  * Are 18 years of age or older.



Like our Introductory Program, discussions are held virtually, allowing participants from anywhere in the world to join. The program is offered at no cost.

If you’re still unfamiliar with the aforementioned core ideas, we recommend that you start with the [Introductory EA Program](https://www.effectivealtruism.org/virtual-programs/introductory-program/). The [EA Handbook](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/cN9Zu7dowefAfmNnH/the-effective-altruism-handbook) is also great if you want to learn at your own pace.
