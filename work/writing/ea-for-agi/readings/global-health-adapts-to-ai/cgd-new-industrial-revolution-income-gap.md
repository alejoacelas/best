# A New Industrial Revolution: Will AI Widen or Close the Income Gap Between Rich and Poor Countries?

The authors examine how AI deployment may affect global inequality between wealthy and developing nations. Drawing parallels to the Industrial Revolution's uneven benefits, they ask: will AI expand or narrow the prosperity gap?

**Methodology.** The researchers used occupational AI-exposure scores from Felten et al. (2021) mapped against countries' export baskets to estimate national-level AI exposure. Their analysis found a strong positive correlation between AI exposure and income per capita—wealthier nations currently engage more with AI-exposed industries—though with significant variation among similarly-developed countries.

**Competing scenarios.** AI could *increase* global inequality if developing nations lack infrastructure and capital for adoption, letting only wealthy countries capture productivity gains. Conversely, AI could *reduce* inequality if workers in poor countries gain affordable access to AI tools via internet connectivity, enabling skill development and previously unavailable competitive advantages.

**Critical infrastructure gap.** Broadband access remains essential but inadequate: only 20% of low-income-country residents access the internet versus 90.2% in high-income nations. The authors conclude that scaling digital-infrastructure investment is imperative for equitable AI benefits.
