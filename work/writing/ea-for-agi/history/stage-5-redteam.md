# Stage 5 Red-Team Findings

Red-team review of the EA-for-AGI course design, grouped by lens. Within each lens, findings are ordered high → medium → low severity.

## Pedagogy

### High

**Session 7 (criticism week) is a 4h+ session mislabeled "Core (~2h)."** The four core critics total 39,103 words, and two are full-length essays: ai-as-normal-technology (21,926 words — the complete Narayanan & Kapoor piece, ~2h on its own) and counterarguments-to-basic-ai-xrisk-case (10,351). This is self-defeating: the one session whose whole design depends on participants reading critics closely enough to referee them ("let the critics visibly win rounds") is the session most guaranteed to be skimmed. The exercise (skepticism-as-deliverable) sits on top of that.

*Fix:* Cut to two core critics, not four. Keep Srinivasan (5,718) as the hostile-outside voice and ONE of Grace/Narayanan, hard-excerpted to its central section (~3-4k). Move the other two to "More to explore" with a one-line "what this one attacks" gloss. Target ~9-10k core words.

**Session 5's "pick ONE deep-dive (~1h)" packets are 2-4h each.** Packet A is 32,266 words (80k-extreme-power-concentration 10,562 + ai-enabled-coups 19,152 + critic 2,552); the coups essay alone is ~1.5-2h. The shared core is also drawn from a 21,898-word essay marked only "excerpt: intro + grand-challenges section" with no word target or section anchors, and that file is a messy fetch full of empty "##" and "## Image" headers, so a facilitator can't reliably scope the excerpt. The author's open-questions flag worries about packet B (economics); packet A is far worse and unflagged.

*Fix:* Cap every packet at one keystone + one short critic and specify explicit word/section ranges so each is genuinely ~6-8k words. For A, drop 80k-extreme-power-concentration OR ai-enabled-coups (not both) — coups excerpted to its scenario + mitigations list. Give the shared-core excerpt a concrete section range and word count.

**The design rationale claims a "wow-density per minute" / "Brevity x wow-density" selection principle that its own core reading contradicts.** The Handbook exemplars for virtue 11 are 700-900 words, but the actual core is built on long-form essays: 11.7k (Carlsmith), 21.9k (Narayanan/Kapoor), 19.1k (coups), 10.6k, 10.4k, 9.8k. The course claims a virtue its own reading selection contradicts, which is why the length problems recur session after session rather than being isolated slips.

*Fix:* Either honor virtue 11 — excerpt aggressively to the 1-3k "one staggering artifact" unit the Handbook exemplars model, with per-reading word/minute counts shown (the AGISF move the analysis says to steal) — or drop the brevity/wow-density claim from the rationale and own that this is a long-form seminar with a much smaller reading count per session.

### Medium

**Session 3 is secretly ~2.5-3h behind a "~1.75h" label.** fake-thinking-and-real-thinking is 11,721 words of dense Carlsmith prose (realistically ~75-80 min at careful pace) and is NOT marked excerpt, inside a 19,324-word session that also carries the pre-registered-oops exercise plus a minimal-trust verification task. This is the session teaching the course's load-bearing skill, so an over-stuffed prep that forces skimming undercuts exactly the stance it means to install.

*Fix:* Mark fake-thinking as excerpt to its core distinction (~3-4k), or demote it to "More to explore" and let importance-of-saying-oops (765) + scout-mindset-review + epistemic-learned-helplessness carry the core. Keep the recurring facilitator callback to Carlsmith without assigning the full essay night-one.

**The on-ramp front-loads weirdness despite claiming the opposite.** Session 1 is framed "feeling, not argument... No one is asked to believe anything yet" and cited as honoring virtues 1/2/12 (awe before argument; values before causes), but its core includes three-types-of-intelligence-explosion — a 5,134-word feedback-loop mechanism argument — and its exercise (the sealed forecast) demands a newcomer commit numeric P(AGI-by-2040) and P(transition-goes-well) on night one, before any timelines or mechanism grounding. The Handbook deliberately delayed both the AI content (week 6) and any quantified weird commitment. The S8 re-rating then measures course-induced anchoring, not calibration, since the night-one numbers are uninformed guesses.

*Fix:* Move three-types-of-intelligence-explosion to Session 4 (where AI is staged behind conceded machinery) or the optional layer; keep Session 1 core to the two awe/agency readings. Make the S1 sealed forecast qualitative ("which way do you lean, and what would move you"), and introduce the numeric credences in Session 4 after grounding, so the S8 re-rating tracks a real update.

**Session 4 runs ~2.5h behind a "~2h" label and double-taxes one reading.** Core is 20,447 words: why-would-ai-aim-to-defeat-humanity assigned in full (9,788) plus the full MacAskill founding essay (6,919), plus Christiano and West — and the exercise (cause-menu cartography) requires re-reading the essay's ~16-cause menu to build a scale/neglect/tractability grid. The essay is thus read once for argument and mined again for the exercise, with no excerpt relief.

*Fix:* Excerpt why-would-ai-aim to its plain-language goal-origins core (~4k), and scope the MacAskill read so its cause-menu section is the exercise's object rather than a second full pass. Target ~12k core words.

### Low

**Session 6 links an uncurated raw-fetch file.** qntm-lena-mmacevedo.md is 19,734 words because the fetch appended a 229-comment discussion thread (the story itself ends ~line 88, ~1,500 words). A participant who clicks the assigned path lands on a 19k-word page and cannot tell where the story stops — precisely the "platform rot / raw dump" failure the project's own notes say to avoid by shipping key texts as self-contained text. (Session 6's actual reading load, story only, is a healthy ~11.5k words — this is a file-hygiene defect, not a length one.)

*Fix:* Trim the committed file to the story (through the "See also" section) or cite an explicit line range in the curriculum, so the assigned text is the ~1,500-word story and not the comment thread. Spot-check the other linked/ fetches (ai-as-normal-technology, preparing-for-intelligence-explosion) for the same trailing-cruft problem before piloting.

## Balance

### High

**Session 4 quietly re-centers loss-of-control — the exact failure the design claims to avoid.** The session is framed as 'the turn' (should a changed world change priorities?) with AI safety 'staged behind conceded things, never as a hobbyhorse.' But by word count the two loss-of-control readings (why-would-ai-aim-to-defeat-humanity 9,788w + what-failure-looks-like 2,595w = 12,383w) are 61% of the ~20,450-word core, dwarfing the two 'turn' readings (west-third-wave 1,145w + the MacAskill essay 6,919w). The conceded alignment mechanism is literally the longest thing a participant reads all session. Nothing is marked 'excerpt' despite the '~2h' label, so the session runs ~3h and the canon it means to concede briefly instead dominates it.

*Fix:* Cut the loss-of-control footprint below the 'turn' footprint. Swap Karnofsky's 9,788-word why-would-ai-aim-to-defeat-humanity for the shorter Cotra mechanism reading ai-safety-core/why-ai-alignment-could-be-hard.md (4,361w) OR assign an explicit excerpt of it; mark idea/macaskill-ea-in-the-age-of-agi.md as excerpt (assign the Reason #1 cause-menu section, not all 6,919w). Target: the pivot argument out-weighs the alignment mechanism, and total core lands near 9–10k words.

**The distinctive beyond-safety causes MacAskill headlines are delivered as mutually-exclusive pick-one packets, so per participant they are name-checked, not engaged.** In Session 5 each student reads exactly ONE of A–G, then hears the other six as 3-minute peer teaching. That means AI-enabled coups / power concentration — which MacAskill calls 'plausibly the highest-impact thing' — plus persuasion, s-risks, space governance, and post-AGI economics are, for any given student, never actually read. Meanwhile the digital-minds trio (welfare/character/rights) gets a guaranteed full session (6). The result: the human-power cluster MacAskill weights most heavily gets ~1/7 the guaranteed depth of digital minds, and the '30% beyond safety' target is met only in cohort aggregate — individual beyond-safety depth is one packet + Session 6, which is borderline under 30%.

*Fix:* Guarantee that the causes MacAskill ranks highest reach every student. Cleanest: promote the human-power cluster to its own shared-core session (gradual-disempowerment-full.md excerpt or ai-rights-economics/80k-gradual-disempowerment-profile.md 2,140w + forethought/ai-enabled-coups...md + power-concentration-democracy/80k-extreme-power-concentration.md), matching Session 6's treatment of digital minds. If holding at 8 sessions, make Session 5 two required shared cores (power/economics/epistemic-security as one, s-risks/space/classic-under-AGI as the other) rather than one pick-one, so no headline cause is left to peer-teaching alone.

### Medium

**The criticism session (7) disproportionately doubts the safety/x-risk frame and spares the novel causes the course is trying to sell.** All four core critics attack either the AI-risk case (Grace counterarguments; Democratising Risk) or the EA method / rapid-change premise (Srinivasan; Narayanan-Kapoor). None attacks AI welfare (anthropomorphism), AI character, AI rights/succession, power-concentration (speculative), or s-risks (Pascalian). Those critics exist only as in-packet Session-5 optionals most students skip. So the course's designated 'doubt' beat under-tests exactly the fresh 30% it wants to be its contribution, and by making x-risk the thing worth a whole session of scrutiny, it implicitly reaffirms loss-of-control as THE load-bearing claim — against the thesis that safety is one variable among many.

*Fix:* Rebalance Session 7 so at least one core critic targets the distinctive causes. Demote one x-risk critic (e.g. Grace) to optional and add a beyond-safety pathogen in core — joe-carlsmith/does-ai-risk-other-the-ais.md (2,667w, the successionist critique of welfare/rights) or linked/moral-argument-for-giving-ais-autonomy.md (3,256w, 'peaceful human disempowerment may be fine'), or an excerpt of ai-welfare-digital-minds/no-such-thing-conscious-ai-skeptic.md attacking the welfare frame. Keep one x-risk critic and one EA-method critic; retire the redundancy between Grace and Democratising Risk.

**AI character — the cause MacAskill footnotes by name and lists FIRST among the novel areas — is carried in core by an Anthropic corporate blog post.** MacAskill calls character unusually neglected and unusually EA-weighted, yet Session 6 core carries it via ai-character/claudes-character.md (1,862w), while MacAskill & Davidson's own rigorous cause-area statement (linked/forethought-importance-of-ai-character.md, 4,798w — the keystone for character in best-readings.md) is demoted to the optional layer. For a course whose entire premise is MacAskill's thesis, putting a practitioner's product-note in core and the author's own 'why this is a cause area' case in optional inverts the intended weighting and lets a flagship neglected cause read as one-third of a shared session.

*Fix:* Promote linked/forethought-importance-of-ai-character.md into Session 6 core (pathways to impact, the counterargument, rejoinders); keep claudes-character.md as its practitioner companion in the same session. This makes character carry its own weight rather than free-riding on the welfare/rights readings.

**Session 6 is over budget and its emotional keystone is a corrupted file.** Nominal core is ~29,450 words (long-we-should-take-ai-welfare 2,381 + claudes-character 1,862 + when-digital-minds-demand-freedom 5,477 + qntm-lena 19,734) for a '~2h' session. The Lena figure is real: linked/qntm-lena-mmacevedo.md is 19,734 words because lines ~100–1026 are 900+ lines of reader comments; the actual story ends around line 90 (~2,500w). Assigned as-is, a participant scrolls a wall of comment threads, and the session cannot fit 2h.

*Fix:* Trim the Lena file to the story proper (cut everything from the 'Categories:' line / first comment timestamp onward — keep ~lines 1–90), which restores it to ~2,500w. Then hold Session 6 to three core readings: drop or move one of when-digital-minds-demand-freedom (5,477w) / the added character cause-case to optional so core lands near ~11k words. Same comment-bloat check is needed on no-such-thing-conscious-ai-skeptic.md (16,908w) and the-void.md (17,803w) before either is assigned.

### Low

**The 'principles-first made concrete' move is never cashed out as guaranteed content.** MacAskill's point is that even classic causes (esp. global development) have 'enormous juice' once you take the AI transition seriously. Session 1 introduces the both/and ('bednets AND AGI', 300000-lives-100-million-hens.md) as a motif, but the only place classic-causes-under-AGI actually lands is Session 5 packet G, which is itself animals-OR-global-health, read by perhaps 1/7 of the cohort. So the concrete demonstration that principles-first ≠ same-old-causes, which is the essay's own rebuttal to the '(2) double down on classics' camp, reaches almost no one.

*Fix:* Seed one classic-cause-under-AGI reading into shared core — global-health-adapts-to-ai/algorithms-and-authority-power-global-health.md (1,643w, also the collection's sharpest Global-South voice, addressing the near-total non-Western gap the landscape flags) fits either Session 4 (as a concrete 'principles-first' exhibit) or Session 8's flourishing/plan. That cashes the Session 1 both/and and stops the classic-under-AGI thread from depending on a coin-flip packet.

## Intellectual Honesty

### High

**Session 7 (the criticism session) blows its own '~2h' budget by ~65%.** Its four core readings total 39,103 words (verified by wc): counterarguments-to-basic-ai-xrisk-case (10,351w) + ai-as-normal-technology (21,926w) + srinivasan (5,718w) + democratising-risk (1,108w). At 200 wpm that is ~3.25h of reading alone, before the exercise and discussion. Crucially, ai-as-normal-technology and counterarguments are the two longest core readings in the whole course yet neither is marked *excerpt*, while shorter readings elsewhere (PIE, Machines of Loving Grace, 80k-century) ARE flagged. So the most important session — the one that must let critics 'visibly win rounds' — is the one most likely to go unread, and the critics' case gets skimmed.

*Fix:* Mark ai-as-normal-technology as *excerpt* with a named cut (its 'AI as normal technology' thesis section + the labor/diffusion section, ~6-7k words) and counterarguments as *excerpt* (Grace's headline objections, skip the long appendices). Target ~15-16k core words. Alternatively demote ai-as-normal-technology to 'More to explore' and let Grace + Srinivasan + Democratising Risk carry the core.

**The course reproduces the collection's single largest documented bias — non-Western/Global-South absence — leaving most participants finishing with zero non-Western core reading.** landscape.md flags this in 18/22 clusters as 'the single largest gap.' Sessions 1-4, 6, 7, 8 have no non-Western voice in core OR optional. The only substantive one, Senanayake's 'Algorithms and Authority,' is offered as one of two alternatives inside packet G, which is one of seven optional deep-dive packets in Session 5 — so a participant only meets it if they (a) reach Session 5, (b) pick packet G over six others, and (c) pick the global-health option over the animals option. Audrey Tang is a single optional link. The open-questions section admits this but ships it unfixed. For a course whose thesis is navigating a transition that reshapes the global majority, the global majority has effectively no seat.

*Fix:* Promote at least one Global-South voice into a shared core slot: put Senanayake's algorithms-and-authority-power-global-health.md into Session 5's *shared* core (everyone reads it, ~1h slot) rather than burying it in packet G, or into Session 8's 'who is this future for' framing. Separately, packet G is the only packet offering a bare choice with no keystone+critic pairing — rebuild it so the global-health option carries both Senanayake AND the OpenPhil GHW RFP defense, matching packets A-F.

### Medium

**Session 7 mislabels a forum reception-post as the substantive x-risk critique.** It cites democratising-risk.md as 'the most serious internal-reform critique of the x-risk framing,' but the file (verified: 1,108 words) is NOT the Cremer & Kemp paper — it is CarlaZoeC's forum post ANNOUNCING the paper and narrating how painful its reception was. The actual methodology critique (the Techno-Utopian-Approach argument, the democratization case) sits behind an unfetched SSRN link. So a participant clicking through to referee 'the strongest internal critique' gets the reception-drama, not the argument. The curriculum half-admits this in discussion Q3 ('as much about how EA treated a critic as about the critique') while the reading blurb still promises the substance.

*Fix:* Either (a) relabel the reading honestly as a case study in EA's treatment of critics (which is what the text actually delivers, and a legitimate criticism-session artifact), dropping the claim that it hands the x-risk critique; or (b) fetch the actual paper (SSRN 3995225 / the published 'Democratising Risk' in Futures) and cite that as the substantive core, keeping the forum post as the reception companion.

**Session 4, where AI risk first enters the course, stages zero in-core skeptic of the basic AI-danger claim.** All four cores — West (waves), MacAskill (essay), Karnofsky (why AI would aim to defeat us), Christiano (what failure looks like) — accept AI is dangerous; the session's 'disagreement in the room' (takeover-first vs preparedness-first) is intramural and presupposes danger. The optionals are also all pro-risk (Cotra, gradual disempowerment, AI-2027, Koehler); the nearest thing to a skeptic, short-review-if-anyone-builds-it, argues only against extreme doom while granting takeover. This violates the very Handbook virtue the course claims (virtue 2/3: the Handbook put LeCun on-page IN the AI-intro week, not deferred). Deferring ALL AI skepticism to Session 7 means the danger claim goes unchallenged for three sessions.

*Fix:* Pull one skeptic into Session 4's optional layer at least, and ideally core: e.g. ai-safety-core/bengio-faq-catastrophic-ai-risks.md (a laureate answering skeptics point-by-point, reconciling near-term and catastrophic) or move counterarguments-to-basic-ai-xrisk-case.md forward as the Session 4 optional skeptic. Name the skeptic in the 'disagreement in the room' box, not just the takeover-vs-prep intramural split.

**Session 6 (character/welfare/rights — 'the strangest content in the course') hands no skeptic in core.** All four cores argue FOR taking patienthood seriously (Long: 'no cautious option'; Claude's Character; Caviola: rights-as-risk, which still treats minds as real; Lena: exploited-em heart piece). The one genuine skeptic — the peer-reviewed no-such-thing-conscious-ai-skeptic.md ('genuine AI consciousness is impossible') — is demoted to 'More to explore.' This is the sanded-off pattern the task warns about, and it breaks the course's stated virtue 3 ('name the best critic in EVERY core'). The Handbook's own moral-circle week kept doubt in core (Karnofsky funding welfare while doubting chickens matter); Session 6 has no in-core doubt on its weirdest claim.

*Fix:* Promote no-such-thing-conscious-ai-skeptic.md into Session 6 core (four cores is already a full load — swap it for Caviola or pair it against Long), so the required reading contains a live 'there may be no one home' voice, not just a named-and-optional one.

**The empirical/on-the-ground register that landscape.md names as 'nearly absent' stays thin in core.** The clearest case is Session 5 packet B (economics), the natural home for measured labor evidence: it is three opinion/theory essays (Smith's comparative-advantage optimism, Newman's rebuttal, capital-agi-and-human-ambition) with no empirical labor-market study and — notably — no Acemoglu, the serious economist-skeptic of AI labor hype that landscape.md explicitly names as a missing adversary. Across the course, scenarios and thought experiments dominate; measured findings appear only sporadically (RAND bio red-team, DebunkBot RCT in Session 5 packets). The open-questions section flags 'capability-snapshot rot' but not this deeper evidentiary thinness.

*Fix:* Add an empirical anchor to packet B and name Acemoglu as the missing skeptic (his 'Simple Macroeconomics of AI' is the on-the-ground counterweight to both Smith's optimism and the capital-eats-everything pessimism). More broadly, tag which core claims rest on scenario vs measured evidence, so facilitators can flag it rather than the course arguing 'century in a decade' entirely in models.

### Low

**Data-quality rot in a cited file.** qntm-lena-mmacevedo.md is 19,734 words (verified), but the 'Lena' story itself is only ~2,000; the remaining ~17k are a scraped reader-comment thread appended after the story, ending in stray moderator notes ('No roleplaying in this thread. Thanks.') and site footer links. The story sits clean at the top so the damage is limited, but a participant who scrolls hits a wall of forum cruft, and it undercuts the 'ship clean self-contained text' standard the curriculum's own open-questions demands for exercises.

*Fix:* Trim the file to the story plus author's note (through the '#### 2026...' comment marker), dropping the comment thread and footer. Cheap one-time edit; applies to any other html2text scrapes with trailing comment sections.
