# Cluster map: ea-intro-program

## What this cluster is

The canonical **EA Intro Fellowship / EA Handbook (4th ed., 2022)** reading set — ~117
curated files reconstructing an 8-week syllabus, plus a linked pile of the primary
sources those readings cite. It is the *pre-AGI* introductory canon this course is
built to succeed: three weeks of values and thinking tools (effectiveness mindset,
scope sensitivity / cause comparison, moral-circle expansion) before any cause area,
then x-risk → longtermism → AI → criticism → career planning. The full pedagogical
teardown lives in [handbook-virtues.md](../handbook-virtues.md); this map covers the
readings as a *corpus* — its traditions, its best texts, and what it conspicuously
lacks for an AGI-era rebuild.

Four sub-threads run through it: (1) **global health & development** as the tractable,
evidence-first entry point (GiveWell, GiveDirectly RCTs, cost-effectiveness); (2)
**moral-circle expansion** (Singer, Karnofsky, animal welfare, wild-animal suffering);
(3) **x-risk & longtermism** (Ord, MacAskill, Bostrom, 80k); (4) a **meta/epistemics
& self-criticism** layer that is the Handbook's real signature.

## Traditions and voices, and where they disagree

- **Actuarial / evidence-first EA** (GiveWell, 80,000 Hours, Owen Cotton-Barratt):
  measure, hedge, show the arithmetic in footnotes. Its internal fault line is
  **randomista global health vs. hits-based / longtermist giving** — surfaced directly
  by the growth-vs-randomista debate ([growth-case-against-randomista.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/growth-case-against-randomista.md))
  and GiveWell's own now-vs-later and fund-the-future pieces in the linked pile.
- **Philosophical utilitarian / longtermist** (Singer, Ord, Greaves & MacAskill,
  Bostrom): the moral-circle-and-far-future argument. Sharp *internal* disagreement:
  MacAskill argues **against** us being at the hinge of history
  ([are-we-living-at-most-influential-time.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/are-we-living-at-most-influential-time.md)),
  and Singer — a founding figure — turns **critic** of extinction-risk framing in the
  linked Hinge-of-History essay. Tarsney's GPI paper and Greaves on cluelessness attack
  longtermism's *tractability* from inside the tent.
- **The uncertain funder** (Holden Karnofsky, Open Phil): worldview diversification,
  radical empathy while *personally doubting chickens matter* — the head/heart-splitting
  voice that models moral uncertainty rather than resolving it.
- **Lyrical / narrative** (Jai's smallpox epic, Nate Soares/Julia Wise register, Vox
  and Asterisk journalism): awe and named human heroes (Zhdanov, Arkhipov, Petrov)
  carrying the emotional load the numbers can't.
- **Hostile & insider critics in core slots** (Angus Deaton, Ben Kuhn, the Boston
  Review symposium, Pascal's Mugging): the cluster deliberately platforms attacks on
  its own EV machinery — but, tellingly, on *global-health-era* EA, not on AI-risk
  framing.

## Standout readings

Relative links resolve from this file. These are the ~14 a curriculum actually uses.

1. [500-million-but-not-a-single-one-more.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/500-million-but-not-a-single-one-more.md)
   — Jai's ~900-word smallpox-eradication epic. **Anchor / awe opener**: "You are a
   member of the species that did that," no mention of EA or duty. Establishes
   tractability as a *feeling*.
2. [scope-insensitivity.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/scope-insensitivity.md)
   — the $80/$78/$88-for-2k/20k/200k-birds experiment. **Accessible on-ramp / numeric
   hinge**: one unforgettable result installs the whole cost-effectiveness apparatus in
   ~700 words.
3. [prospecting-for-gold.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/prospecting-for-gold.md)
   — Cotton-Barratt's EAG talk on heavy tails and the ITN framework. **Rigorous
   deep-dive**: closes by re-rating his own confidence claim-by-claim ("Disagreement is
   an opportunity to learn").
4. [radical-empathy.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/radical-empathy.md)
   — Karnofsky on who merits moral concern. **Anchor for the moral-circle week / best
   "uncertain funder" voice**: funds farm-animal welfare *while doubting chickens
   matter*; models moral uncertainty instead of preaching.
5. [case-against-speciesism.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/case-against-speciesism.md)
   — **Bounded advocacy**: the one frankly persuasive essay, pre-empts its own strawman
   ("does not require we give gorillas the right to vote") and stays short.
6. [can-one-person-make-a-difference.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/can-one-person-make-a-difference.md)
   — 80k on individual agency. **Accessible on-ramp / agency**: converts the awe of
   reading #1 into "you, specifically, can."
7. [case-for-reducing-existential-risk.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/case-for-reducing-existential-risk.md)
   — the 80k x-risk explainer. **Rigorous anchor for the danger week**: stages weird
   claims (bio, AI) behind conceded ones (asteroids, nuclear near-misses) and flags its
   own selection bias mid-argument.
8. [policy-research-ideas-reduce-existential-risk.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/policy-research-ideas-reduce-existential-risk.md)
   — 50+ mundane levers (renew New START, screen DNA synthesis). **Tractability list**:
   earns its slot by being *boring* — makes x-risk look like ordinary work, not doom.
9. [introduction-to-longtermism.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/introduction-to-longtermism.md)
   — Moorhouse's intro. **Anchor essay w/ built-in exit ramp**: quotes Alexander Berger
   *against* strong longtermism and tells you that you can care about the causes without
   the philosophy.
10. [are-we-living-at-most-influential-time.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/are-we-living-at-most-influential-time.md)
    — MacAskill against the hinge-of-history claim. **Best critic (self-critique)**: the
    course's own headline author arguing against a load-bearing premise one click under
    the week that assumes it.
11. [openphil-views-advanced-ai.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/openphil-views-advanced-ai.md)
    — Open Phil's background memo on advanced AI. **Rigorous data/funder anchor for the
    AI week**: the cautious-institutional voice beneath the accessible AI intro.
12. [critique-of-effective-altruism.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/critique-of-effective-altruism.md)
    — Ben Kuhn's insider apostasy (written under Bostrom's "hypothetical apostasy"
    protocol). **Best critic**: a whole core session where the movement's own EV
    machinery is stress-tested and visibly loses rounds.
13. [advice-on-how-to-read-our-advice.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/advice-on-how-to-read-our-advice.md)
    — 80k undermining its own authority ("hard to be more than ~70% confident").
    **Teaches the stance, not the material**: ships the error bars with the advice.
14. [more-than-one-goal.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/more-than-one-goal.md)
    — Julia Wise's permission to not be a totalizing maximizer ("Sunday morning is for
    making pancakes"). **Permission-to-be-human**: nothing in the adjacent AGI curricula
    resembles it; drops the register from civilizational stakes to a livable life.

Runners-up worth keeping in view: [reply-to-wait-but-why.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/reply-to-wait-but-why.md)
(Muehlhauser's point-by-point paired *with* the Wait-But-Why AI piece — the
referee-the-disagreement mechanism), [the-narrowing-circle-summary.md](../../readings/ea-intro-program/the-narrowing-circle-summary.md)
(Gwern attacking the week's own expanding-circle metaphor — the escalation-ramp
terminus), and the linked [asterisk-salt-sugar-water-zinc-ors.md](../../readings/linked/asterisk-salt-sugar-water-zinc-ors.md)
(ORS impact-history — a fresher, better-written global-health awe piece than several
core slots).

## What's conspicuously missing

This is the *legacy* canon, so the gaps split in two: holes in the classic set, and the
entire AGI-era layer the successor needs.

**Missing from the classic EA canon it represents:**
- **Piper's "The case for taking AI seriously as a threat to humanity"** — the
  Handbook's designated journalist-mediated AI on-ramp; referenced across the set but
  the file failed to download. Re-fetch.
- **Greaves & MacAskill, *The Case for Strong Longtermism*** (GPI) — the rigor anchor
  beneath the longtermism intro; present only as a 487-byte stub. Re-fetch.
- **Singer's original drowning-child argument** (*Famine, Affluence, and Morality* /
  *The Life You Can Save*) — the obligation frame is only gestured at; no full text.
- **A non-Western / Global-South voice on aid and development** — the entire
  global-health thread is narrated by Western funders and RCT economists; no recipient-
  side or Global-South critic (e.g. Dambisa Moyo's *Dead Aid*, or an African
  development economist) is present.
- **A rigorous animal-welfare *skeptic*** — the moral-circle week is advocate-heavy;
  no serious philosophical case *against* animal moral status to referee against Singer.

**Missing AGI-era content (MacAskill's cause menu, near-absent here):**
- **AI welfare / digital minds** — nothing beyond the linked utility-monster BBC essay
  and Fischer's welfare-range table. Missing: Sebo & Long / Anthropic's model-welfare
  work, "Taking AI Welfare Seriously," Chalmers on machine consciousness.
- **AI character** — flagged as a wanted area in the brief; zero readings. Search:
  Anthropic's Claude character / constitutional work, Amanda Askell.
- **Gradual disempowerment & AI-enabled human coups** — no readings. Search: the
  "Gradual Disempowerment" paper (Kulveit et al.), Davidson/Forethought on AI coups.
- **Economic & political rights of AIs, AI persuasion / epistemic disruption, AI for
  better reasoning & coordination** — all named in the brief, none represented.
- **Richard Ngo's worldview** — explicitly named in the collection philosophy as a
  distinct lens; no Ngo reading present at all.
- **Global-health orgs' own plans to adapt to AI** — the brief asks for these; none
  found. Search: GiveWell / Open Phil / GWWC writing on AI and global development.
- **Personal reflections grappling with AGI** — the "more-than-one-goal" register
  applied to living through AGI; a genre the brief wants and the cluster lacks.
- **Space governance and s-risks** — menu cause areas with no toehold in the corpus.

**Missing criticism, specific to the successor:** the criticism session attacks
global-health-era EA (Deaton on aid, earning-to-give). It needs critics of *AI-risk
framing and AGI-centric EA itself* — e.g. the near-vs-long-term-harms debate (the
linked [xrisk-attention-hasnt-distracted-from-current-harms.md](../../readings/linked/xrisk-attention-hasnt-distracted-from-current-harms.md)
rebuts one side but the other side's strongest statement, e.g. Gebru/Torres on
"TESCREAL," is absent), and a critic of longtermism's practical grip in a fast-takeoff
world.
