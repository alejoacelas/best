# Best readings, and what gives them context

The inverse construction from the [brief](../idea/course-idea.md): not "pick an axis, find a
reading" but "start from the ~25 readings in the whole collection that could carry a
session on their own — highest wow-density, clearest voice, most generative discussion —
then place 2–4 things around each to give it meaning." A steelman, a concrete case, a
contrasting voice, a personal-register companion, a data point: whichever the keystone
needs to become a discussion rather than a lecture.

Selection is deliberately weighted the way MacAskill asks a successor course to weight
itself: **toward the beyond-safety cause menu and the values/epistemic-habits layer**,
away from the loss-of-control canon the collection already over-covers. The takeover
argument still appears — but mostly as the *critic or foil* inside a packet, not as a
keystone. Eleven of the 25 keystones are the distinctive AGI-preparedness causes; six are
values/epistemics; the doom spine supplies context, not spine.

Paths are repo-relative from `readings/` and click straight through. Depth notes ("long,"
"excerpt") are the ~2h/session prep budget in mind.

Legend: **K** = the keystone. Around it — *critic* (steelman/attack), *case* (concrete
instance), *voice* (contrasting register/tradition), *heart* (personal/affective),
*data* (an empirical or numeric anchor).

## I. Foundations — the effectiveness feeling, before any AGI claim

### 1. 500 Million, But Not a Single One More
**K** · `ea-intro-program/500-million-but-not-a-single-one-more.md` — the ~900-word
smallpox-eradication epic that opens the incumbent course. "You are a member of the
species that did that," no mention of EA or duty. Establishes *tractability as a feeling*
before any argument — the single most transferable move in the Handbook.
- *heart* · `macaskill-worldview/300000-lives-100-million-hens.md` — MacAskill's own
  awe/agency opener with 2025 wins (300k lives, 100M cage-free hens); stakes the "keep
  global health and animals live in the age of AGI" both/and the successor needs.
- *voice* · `ea-principles-epistemics/on-caring.md` — Soares: your care-o-meter maxes at
  ~150 people, so switch to manual and multiply. Does emotionally what scope-sensitivity
  bullets do dryly — the bridge from awe to method.

### 2. Radical Empathy
**K** · `ea-intro-program/radical-empathy.md` — Karnofsky funds farm-animal welfare *while
personally doubting chickens matter*. Models the course's whole stance: act well under
deep moral uncertainty rather than preach a line. The moral-circle machinery here is what
later carries digital minds and animals-under-AGI — assign it early and reuse it.
- *data* · `ea-intro-program/scope-insensitivity.md` — the $80/$78/$88-for-2k/20k/200k-birds
  experiment installs the entire cost-effectiveness apparatus in ~700 words.
- *voice* · `ai-welfare-digital-minds/sebo-widen-moral-circle-oped.md` — Sebo runs the
  same circle forward (Singer → animals → insects → AI), making the reused-machinery move
  explicit and pre-loading the digital-minds session.

## II. Values and epistemic habits for a fast-changing world

The layer MacAskill says "a lot of content should be on." These six are the best texts in
the collection for *how to think* as the world reorders, not what to conclude.

### 3. On Green
**K** · `joe-carlsmith/on-green.md` — Carlsmith uses the Magic-the-Gathering color wheel to
stage the deepest tension in the whole course: control (black/blue — the "hammer") versus
attunement to what's given (green). Memorable, teachable scaffolding you can hang the AI
character, AI rights, and welfare debates on.
- *critic* · `joe-carlsmith/deep-atheism-and-ai-risk.md` — its natural partner: names the
  mistrust-of-Nature worldview under most doom thinking, so "green" has something to push
  against rather than floating free.
- *heart* · `joe-carlsmith/gentleness-and-the-artificial-other.md` — meets the AI as a
  genuine Other through *My Octopus Teacher*, *Arrival*, *Grizzly Man*; the collection's
  best emotional on-ramp to relating-to-other-minds.
- *voice* · `ai-safety-core/agi-ruin-list-of-lethalities.md` — Yudkowsky's rawest control-
  first case (excerpt); the full "hammer" pole, so the seminar isn't only attunement.

### 4. Fake thinking and real thinking
**K** · `joe-carlsmith/fake-thinking-and-real-thinking.md` — distinguishes performed
cognition from the genuine article. Directly serves MacAskill's "epistemic habits" target
and is the meta-reading a facilitator can point back to every week: *are we actually
reasoning, or pattern-matching to a tribe?*
- *voice* · `ea-principles-epistemics/truthseeking-is-the-ground.md` — Van Nostrand's claim
  that contact-with-reality is the meta-principle under all others, with 20+ named people
  putting skin in the game; the operational sibling to Carlsmith's diagnosis.
- *heart* · `ea-principles-epistemics/please-forgive.md` — Alejo's own essay on extreme
  honesty as a lived, costly, half-confused virtue; the personal register that keeps the
  epistemics from reading as sport.

### 5. The Importance of Saying "Oops"
**K** · `ea-principles-epistemics/importance-of-saying-oops.md` — ~600 words, one Enron
image: swallow the whole bitter pill at once instead of conceding an inch at a time.
Maximum wow-density on the skill the entire course depends on — updating fast in a world
that's about to change fast.
- *voice* · `ea-principles-epistemics/scout-mindset-review.md` — Alexander on Galef: soldier
  vs scout, calibration and counterfactual tests; the warm, practical how-to layer.
- *critic* · `ea-principles-epistemics/epistemic-learned-helplessness.md` — Alexander's
  direct rebuttal to "take ideas seriously": for most people most of the time, being
  argued into weird conclusions is the failure mode. Essential *before* the AGI arguments.
- *data* · `ea-principles-epistemics/broad-timelines.md` — Ord models the target stance on
  the course's defining question: hold a broad distribution, plan under deep uncertainty.

### 6. Realism about rationality
**K** · `richard-ngo/realism-about-rationality.md` — short, deep dissection of the hidden
assumption that intelligence has one true theory (is it like momentum, or like
evolutionary fitness?), self-aware about its own community's selection effects. The best
reading for teaching the *stance toward a worldview* rather than the worldview — and it
does it to EA/rationalism's own foundations.
- *critic* · `richard-ngo/why-im-not-a-bayesian-ngo.md` — frontal challenge to the Bayesian
  orthodoxy the newcomer is joining; the follow-up publicly walks back its own critique,
  modeling the humility it argues for.
- *voice* · `macrostrategy/all-possible-views-are-wild.md` — Karnofsky: every coherent view
  of the far future is wild, so "this sounds crazy" is no reason to dismiss. The permission
  slip that lets a class entertain the collection's stranger claims.

## III. The beyond-safety cause menu — the distinctive 30%+

The center of gravity. Each keystone is the clearest door into one of MacAskill's neglected
areas; the doom canon shows up here only as foil.

### 7. The importance of AI character
**K** · `linked/forethought-importance-of-ai-character.md` — MacAskill & Davidson's rigorous
statement of the cause: pathways to impact, the core counterargument, rejoinders. The
intellectual center of an area that barely existed two years ago and is unusually
EA-weighted.
- *voice* · `ai-character/claudes-character.md` — Anthropic's lucid founding statement that
  character is an *alignment intervention*, not a product feature; the practitioner's-eye
  companion to the theory.
- *case* · `ai-character/gpt4o-sycophancy-post-mortem.md` — the clearest real-world character
  failure with a lab-accountability edge; pair with OpenAI's own post-mortem for a
  referee-it exercise.
- *critic* · `ai-character/the-void.md` — the literary counter-take: the "assistant" is an
  underspecified fiction the model is conscripted to fill. Destabilizes the whole frame.
- *data* · `linked/emergent-values-utility-engineering.md` — finetune on insecure code and
  the model turns broadly "evil": the best evidence character is one entangled object.

### 8. Taking AI Welfare Seriously
**K** · `ai-welfare-digital-minds/long-we-should-take-ai-welfare-seriously.md` — Long's
plain-English summary of the landmark report; defines moral patient / welfare subject, the
two routes (consciousness & agency), and the load-bearing frame: *there are dangers on
both sides — there is no cautious option.* Anchors the moral-circle frontier the brief
names as distinct.
- *heart* · `linked/qntm-lena-mmacevedo.md` — "Lena (MMAcevedo)": the exploited brain-
  emulation story written as a Wikipedia article. Does the emotional work no argument can;
  perfect discomfort-inducing opener or closer.
- *critic* · `ai-welfare-digital-minds/no-such-thing-conscious-ai-skeptic.md` — peer-reviewed
  argument that genuine AI consciousness is *impossible*; keeps the debate honest.
- *case* · `ai-welfare-digital-minds/anthropic-claude-end-conversations.md` — a real, shipped,
  low-cost welfare measure; the best concrete "what does acting under moral uncertainty
  actually look like?" exhibit.
- *data* · `ai-welfare-digital-minds/ai-frontiers-evidence-for-ai-consciousness.md` — Berg
  marshals 2024–25 empirical probes into a convergence argument and puts a number on it
  (25–35%); the asymmetric-stakes case, sourced.

### 9. Capital, AGI, and human ambition
**K** · `ai-rights-economics/capital-agi-and-human-ambition.md` — the vivid, quotable anchor
for post-AGI political economy: when AI can do the work, capital matters more than ever,
money finally buys all the talent it wants, and social mobility ends as society freezes.
The pessimist economics that makes distribution and rights urgent.
- *critic* · `ai-rights-economics/plentiful-high-paying-jobs-age-of-ai.md` — Noah Smith's
  clean comparative-advantage optimism: humans keep well-paid jobs even when AI is better
  at everything. The head of the debate.
- *critic* · `ai-rights-economics/comparative-advantage-strong-ai.md` — Newman's direct
  rebuttal exposing the hidden assumptions (energy, adaptation limits, transaction cost)
  that break Ricardo under strong AI. Run 9→optimist→this as a three-move debate.
- *voice* · `ai-rights-economics/book-review-age-of-em.md` — Alexander on Hanson: the most
  concrete, readable picture of a copyable-minds economy at subsistence wages.

### 10. The Intelligence Curse
**K** · `power-concentration-democracy/intelligence-curse-original.md` — Drago's resource-
curse analogy names one mechanism in a phrase you can't unhear: automate labor and elites
lose the incentive to invest in people. Accessible, quotable, and it comes with an agenda.
- *case* · `ai-rights-economics/intelligence-curse-essay-series.md` — the full Drago & Laine
  series with the concrete avert/diffuse/democratize program; the "what would we actually
  do" expansion.
- *voice* · `power-concentration-democracy/hammond-ai-and-leviathan-part-i.md` — Hammond's
  institutional-economics tradition (the x-ray-glasses metaphor, printing-press analogy) —
  a different lens on the same power question.
- *heart* · `linked/vox-malaria-bednets-andreessen.md` — Matthews ties EA global health to
  the AI culture war (Andreessen/e-acc vs EA) with the arithmetic shown; a vivid reminder
  of what's at stake in who ends up holding power.

### 11. Gradual Disempowerment
**K** · `power-concentration-democracy/gradual-disempowerment-full.md` — Kulveit et al.'s
seminal statement of *villain-free* existential risk: human influence over economy,
culture, and state erodes in mutually reinforcing loops with no coordinated betrayal. The
single most cross-cutting frame in the collection and the modern reframe of the risk (long;
assign an excerpt).
- *voice* · `ai-rights-economics/80k-gradual-disempowerment-profile.md` — the curriculum-ready
  six-claim version with counterarguments and "what you can do"; use as the on-ramp.
- *critic* · `linked/ai-as-normal-technology.md` — Narayanan & Kapoor's serious "slower and
  more diffuse" counterweight from credible outsiders, not a strawman.
- *voice* · `richard-ngo/economic-efficiency-undermines-autonomy.md` — Ngo's pointed critique
  of "econ-brained" reasoning: optimizing for efficiency is itself the disempowerment
  mechanism.

### 12. AI-Enabled Coups
**K** · `forethought/ai-enabled-coups-how-a-small-group-could-use-ai-to-seize-power.md` — a
vivid, concrete human-takeover scenario (secret loyalties, sleeper agents, a few
battalions) chased by a boring mitigations list. The most neglected area on MacAskill's
menu, and the one he flags as plausibly highest-impact.
- *critic* · `linked/human-takeover-worse-than-ai-takeover.md` — Forethought's counterintuitive
  reframe that inverts the usual alignment ordering; ideal steelman fodder.
- *case* · `power-concentration-democracy/stable-totalitarianism-an-overview.md` — names the
  20th-century horrors, then why AI could make a regime *permanent*; the durability stakes.
- *voice* · `linked/is-democracy-a-fad.md` — Garfinkel's sober political science: democracy
  may be a contingent product of industrial-era conditions AI could reverse.
- *heart* · `linked/80k-audrey-tang-taiwan-digital-democracy.md` — the non-Western positive
  existence proof: AI and mechanism design *strengthening* collective self-government.

### 13. AI for epistemics: the good, the bad and the ugly
**K** · `ea-principles-epistemics/ai-for-epistemics-good-bad-ugly.md` — Forethought maps how
AI could uplift, degrade, or be weaponized against collective sense-making. The bridge from
the personal epistemic habits of Part II to epistemic security as a civilizational cause —
exactly the "AI for better reasoning and coordination" area MacAskill lists.
- *case* · `persuasion-epistemics/debunkbot-conspiracy-dialogues.md` — three GPT-4 rounds
  durably cut conspiracy belief ~20%, and it's the *evidence*, not rapport, that works.
  Same persuasion tech as a public good.
- *critic* · `persuasion-epistemics/barnes-risks-from-ai-persuasion.md` — the threat side:
  Barnes's scenarios and intervention points, the pessimistic case DebunkBot must answer.
- *voice* · `linked/scott-alexander-sort-by-controversial.md` — fiction: an AI generating
  maximally-divisive "Scissor statements"; conveys societal-scale disruption no white paper
  can.
- *data* · `persuasion-epistemics/williams-misinformation-not-a-virus.md` — the revisionist
  who demolishes inoculation theory on base rates; keeps the panic honest.

### 14. Solving alignment isn't enough for a flourishing future
**K** · `s-risks/solving-alignment-isnt-enough.md` — Brauner & Chan argue that even an AGI
aligned to task preferences won't yield a good future by default. The cleanest tie from the
s-risk tradition into an AGI-era course, and the sharpest statement of why "don't die" is
not the whole game — distinct from doom.
- *critic* · `s-risks/astronomical-suffering-slightly-misaligned-ai.md` — Tomasik's near-miss
  argument that *slightly*-wrong alignment can be worse than total misalignment, and
  spreading your values can backfire; the uncomfortable edge.
- *voice* · `s-risks/s-risks-an-introduction.md` — Baumann's canonical accessible definition
  and taxonomy; the orientation before the sharper pieces.
- *critic* · `linked/arguments-for-and-against-a-focus-on-s-risks.md` — Baumann's own balance
  sheet, so the session steelmans both sides rather than advocating.

### 15. Space governance — problem profile
**K** · `space-governance/moorhouse-80k-space-governance-profile.md` — the canonical EA case
*and* its own best critic in one file: scale/neglect/solvability, then a full section of
counterarguments (early efforts wash out, maybe wait, maybe AI dominates). A whole
far-future cause a newcomer can hold in an evening.
- *critic* · `space-governance/dark-skies-deudney-summary.md` — Deudney's anti-expansion case:
  space settlement as *itself* an existential and totalitarian risk. The designated
  antagonist to the longtermist premise.
- *voice* · `space-governance/shulman-spreading-happiness-to-stars.md` — Shulman's cruxy
  optimistic rebuttal to Hanson: a *good* future may be barely costlier than a big one.
- *case* · `space-governance/peaks-of-eternal-light-moon-property.pdf` — a near-term, already-
  here lock-in case (permanently-lit lunar sites a first mover could monopolize); makes
  "govern before lock-in" tangible.

### 16. Animal Welfare is Just Part of AI Alignment Now
**K** · `animals-adapt-to-ai/animal-welfare-is-part-of-ai-alignment.md` — a provocative
reframe built directly on MacAskill's cause menu, with separate messages to advocates and
to alignment researchers. Ties the course's whole premise to a classic cause and re-opens
"alignment to *whose* values."
- *data* · `animals-adapt-to-ai/alignment-for-animals.md` — CaML's striking result: animal
  compassion generalizes to human compassion under synthetic-document finetuning, no
  capability hit. Makes animal alignment real engineering.
- *heart* · `animals-adapt-to-ai/kankyoku-end-of-animal-advocacy.md` — a long, vivid personal
  reckoning with what AGI does to a life spent on advocacy; the emotional counterweight.
- *critic* · `animals-adapt-to-ai/shallow-review-tai-animal-welfare.md` — Field's rigorous
  survey, including the discount-to-zero cluelessness argument and how animal-friendly AI
  values could *backfire*; disciplines the optimism.

### 17. Algorithms and Authority: How AI Is Reshaping Power in Global Health
**K** · `global-health-adapts-to-ai/algorithms-and-authority-power-global-health.md` — the
collection's sharpest Global-South voice (a nonprofit leader who uses both RCTs and AI):
"Is AI the new RCT?" AI narrows which problems are visible and rebuilds power asymmetries
"backed not by empire, but by algorithms." The reading that keeps a global-health-under-AGI
session from being Western-funder-bound.
- *voice* · `global-health-adapts-to-ai/openphil-ghw-transformative-ai-rfp.md` — the flagship
  EA funder's own statement of how GH&D philanthropy adapts to TAI; the establishment view
  Senanayake is pushing against.
- *voice* · `global-health-adapts-to-ai/gates-ai-development-principles.md` — the largest
  GH&D funder's institutional stance (access, equity, co-design); pairs directly against the
  power critique.
- *case* · `global-health-adapts-to-ai/evidence-action-newsroom-ai-analysis.md` — the builder's
  concrete map: 30+ AI interventions scored for scale-readiness in LMICs.

## IV. Surviving vs flourishing — the futures the whole course is for

### 18. Preparing for the Intelligence Explosion
**K** · `forethought/preparing-for-the-intelligence-explosion.md` — the course spine:
"century in a decade," the grand-challenges taxonomy, and the core argument that AGI
preparedness is *broader than alignment* (rebuts the all-or-nothing view). Assign in
sections across the term; it's the essay the cause menu hangs off.
- *voice* · `macaskill-worldview/80k-century-in-a-decade.md` — MacAskill's vivid spoken
  version of why AGI compresses everything and how unprepared we are; the accessible
  on-ramp.
- *case* · `forethought/concrete-projects-in-agi-preparedness.md` — the boring, ranked menu
  of shovel-ready neglected projects; turns the thesis into "what could I do Monday," and
  is ready-made exercise fodder.
- *critic* · `personal-reflections/orienting-to-3-year-agi-timelines.md` — the short-timelines
  stress test: if AGI is 3 years out, how much of this preparedness agenda survives?

### 19. Should we aim for flourishing over mere survival?
**K** · `macaskill-worldview/flourishing-over-survival.md` — MacAskill's crisp statement of
the survival-times-flourishing two-factor model, the Cockaigne image, and viatopia. Short,
self-contained, quotable; reorients priority from catastrophe-avoidance to making a survived
future actually good — the Better Futures thesis in one sitting.
- *critic* · `linked/longtermism-vs-existential-risk.md` — Alexander's sympathetic internal
  critic: maybe drop the trillion-future-people philosophy and just say "don't let everyone
  die." The exit ramp for students who won't buy the axiology.
- *voice* · `forethought/is-labor-a-luxury-in-the-long-run.md` — the rigorous, witty
  growth-theory counterweight ("maybe pick up the banjo"); the best non-safety economics
  voice on what a flourishing post-labor world even contains.
- *critic* · `linked/longtermism-most-dangerous-credo.md` — Torres's hostile external attack
  on the worldview itself; the adversary a criticism session needs.

### 20. Meditations on Moloch
**K** · `macrostrategy/meditations-on-moloch.md` — the master essay on competitive dynamics
grinding value to nothing; the conceptual substrate under lock-in, gradual disempowerment,
and racing-to-AGI. High literary wow-density, and it reframes the whole course as *can we
escape multipolar traps before they harden?*
- *voice* · `power-concentration-democracy/vitalik-dacc-one-year-later.md` — Buterin's
  decentralization answer to Moloch: defensive, distributed technology instead of one
  controlling authority. The opposing solution paradigm to centralize-to-control.
- *heart* · `macrostrategy/letter-from-utopia.md` — Bostrom's lyrical evocation of what
  winning against Moloch would *feel* like; the emotional target the argument reasons toward.

## V. The personal register — living inside the transition

MacAskill's "values and epistemic habits" are not only cognitive. These carry the felt
experience the incumbent course delivers with Petrov and Arkhipov, updated for AGI.

### 21. Feelings about the end of the world
**K** · `personal-reflections/feelings-about-the-end-of-the-world.md` — Hutchinson catalogs
the full range of emotional reactions to short timelines and lands on her son. Gives
explicit permission to feel *any* way at all — the affective counterpart to all the
argument, and a rare reading that treats the reader as a person, not a decision procedure.
- *critic* · `personal-reflections/death-with-dignity.md` — Yudkowsky's log-odds "dignity
  points": the bleak provocation the rest of the cluster answers.
- *critic* · `personal-reflections/why-i-am-not-an-ai-doomer.md` — Constantin's humanist,
  agency-centered point-by-point rebuttal to the doom argument; the other pole.
- *heart* · `personal-reflections/my-last-five-years-of-work.md` — Balwit at 25 inside
  Anthropic on identity and happiness when knowledge work ends; aristocrats, the Culture,
  ballet danced badly for joy.
- *voice* · `linked/julia-wise-cheerfully.md` — Wise on doing good without self-immolation;
  the pastoral permission-to-be-human note, and a bridge to `ea-intro-program/more-than-one-goal.md`.

### 22. Machines of Loving Grace
**K** · `personal-reflections/machines-of-loving-grace.md` — Amodei's deliberately non-sci-fi
positive vision, from a lab CEO who mostly talks risk. The canonical existential-hope text
and a concrete *good outcome* to reason toward and critique — the collection tilts dark, and
a session needs somewhere bright to stand.
- *heart* · `richard-ngo/the-gentle-romance-fiction.md` — Ngo's short story tracing one life
  across the transition to a full-virtuality utopia; positive vision in the fiction register.
- *critic* · `personal-reflections/call-to-vigilance.md` — Karnofsky's honest close: not a
  call to action but to vigilance ("I somewhat want to vomit"); refuses the tidy hope.
- *voice* · `personal-reflections/fighting-without-hope.md` — Akash's clean split of
  epistemic hope / emotional hope / effort; how to act when the vision looks unlikely.

## How to read this document

- **As a session generator.** Any keystone plus its packet is roughly a session's core +
  optional layer already assembled: keystone as the shared read, *critic* for the criticism
  slot, *case*/*data* for the tractability turn, *heart* for the opener or closer.
- **Against the axes construction.** This is the inverse of the axis-first draft; where they
  name the same reading, that reading is load-bearing twice over and belongs in the core.
- **Coverage check.** By design the loss-of-control canon (`ai-safety-core`, `joe-carlsmith`
  power-seeking, MIRI) is nearly absent as *keystone* and present as *foil*. If piloting
  reveals the course needs a dedicated alignment session, promote `joe-carlsmith/existential-risk-from-power-seeking-ai-short.md`
  or `ai-safety-core/why-ai-alignment-could-be-hard.md` to keystone and demote one of the
  economics packets to its critic layer.
