# First-pass curriculum: EA in the age of AGI

An 8-session intro fellowship built on Will MacAskill's [*Effective altruism in the age
of AGI*](../idea/macaskill-ea-in-the-age-of-agi.md), designed as a successor to the EA
Introductory Program. It steals the [Handbook's twelve virtues](../analysis/handbook-virtues.md),
walks the [thematic-axes menu](thematic-axes.md) as a *path* (axis numbers cited per
session), and pulls its keystones from [best-readings.md](best-readings.md). Every reading
below is a real file that clicks straight through; core readings carry an approximate
word count, and long ones are marked *excerpt* with a named section + word target to hold
the ~2h/session prep budget (~200 wpm → ~24k words is the hard ceiling; the cause sessions
run long-form and sit near it).

**Revised after red-team (round 1).** Three adversarial critics flagged session-length
inflation, a safety-heavy tilt against the beyond-safety menu, and sanded-off skeptics.
This pass caps every session's core against a real word budget, guarantees the
highest-weighted beyond-safety causes (human-power, character) reach every participant,
puts a live skeptic in the weird sessions' core, and promotes a Global-South voice into
shared core. Tradeoffs I kept are logged in [open questions](#what-id-want-next--open-questions).

This is a first draft meant to be argued with — see [open questions](#what-id-want-next--open-questions).

---

## Design rationale

### The arc

The Handbook's arc is **awe → agency → rigor → discomfort → dread → hope → doubt → plan**.
That shape was built for a course whose weird payload was *x-risk*. This course's
distinctive payload is different: the **beyond-safety cause menu** and the **values and
epistemic habits for navigating a fast-changing world**. So the arc bends to fit:

> **awe → agency → clear sight → the turn → the widening → the frontier → doubt → resolve**

- **Sessions 1–3 (awe → agency → clear sight)** establish, in order: that enormous change
  is coming and we have steered enormous things before (feeling, not argument); that there
  is machinery for choosing where to help; and — the distinctive move — that the scarce
  skill for the next decade is *how you think when the world won't hold still*. Values and
  thinking tools come **before any cause** (Handbook virtue 12), which is also where
  MacAskill's "a lot of content should be on values and epistemic habits" lives.
- **Session 4 (the turn)** is the course's founding argument with itself: *should a changed
  world change our priorities?* AI arrives here — but staged behind three conceded things
  (the effectiveness machinery, the epistemic stance, and the plain loss-of-control
  mechanism a skeptic already grants), never as a hobbyhorse (virtue 2).
- **Session 5 (the widening)** is where the course earns its 30%. It *maps* MacAskill's
  full menu, then each participant *picks one* deep-dive — the BlueDot "map-then-choose"
  move for breadth without drowning. Dread from Session 4 converts to agency: there is an
  enormous amount to do, most of it neglected.
- **Session 6 (the frontier)** is the strangest and most distinctive content — AI character,
  welfare, and rights — made safe by reusing Session 2's moral-circle machinery. Discomfort
  and wonder, not dread.
- **Session 7 (doubt)** is a real criticism session with hostile-outside voices in core
  slots — the Handbook's signature move, updated to attack *AGI-centric EA itself*, not a
  legacy target (virtue 6, and the post-mortem's sharpest correction).
- **Session 8 (resolve)** drops from civilizational stakes to a plan and to pancakes: how
  to act, and how to stay a person while doing it.

The **dread** beat is deliberately not a whole session. That is the thesis: the point of
the preparedness turn is that doom is one variable among many, so the course refuses to
wallow. Loss-of-control is conceded in Session 4 and then put to work, not dwelt in.

### How it meets MacAskill's target

MacAskill asks for **≥30% beyond AI safety / biorisk / animal welfare / global health**,
and **a lot on values and epistemic habits**. This spine:

- Spends Sessions 2–3 almost entirely on values and epistemic habits, and threads the
  "how to think" reading back in every week (Carlsmith's *Fake thinking and real thinking*
  as the facilitator's recurring touchstone).
- Concentrates classic AI safety into **one** session (4). The distinctive menu — power
  concentration, coups, economics of who-holds-power, epistemic security, s-risks, space
  governance, AI character, AI welfare, AI rights, better futures — carries Sessions 5, 6,
  and most of 8. By content weight, **well over half** the cause material is beyond-safety.
  Crucially, the two highest-weighted novel causes reach *every* participant, not just the
  cohort in aggregate: the human-power cluster (gradual disempowerment) is now Session 5
  *shared* core, and AI character is Session 6 core — matching digital minds' guaranteed slot.

**On brevity (an honest correction).** The Handbook's virtue 11 — "wow-density per minute,
carried by named humans" — is fully honored only in the values sessions (1–3), whose
keystones run 700–3,000 words. The cause and criticism sessions (4–7) are a **long-form
seminar**: their arguments (Cotra on misalignment, Grace's counterarguments, Srinivasan,
the coups scenario) don't compress to a single 900-word artifact without gutting them. So
those sessions carry fewer readings, aggressively excerpted to a named section with a word
target, and every core reading shows its length — the AGISF move — so no session's real
prep time hides behind its label.

### Handbook virtues used (by number, from [handbook-virtues.md](../analysis/handbook-virtues.md))

Open with awe not argument (1) → Session 1. Stage weird claims behind conceded ones (2) →
Sessions 4, 6. Name the best critic in every core, hand the full text in optionals (3) →
every session's *The disagreement in the room* — now including a live skeptic *in core* for
the two weirdest sessions (6's "no one home" and 7's whole-frame critics). Pair head and heart voices (4) →
throughout. Teach the stance, not just the material (5) → Session 3. A whole criticism
session with real pathogens (6) → Session 7. Exercises about the participant, never a
hypothetical (7) → all eight. Chase every scale claim with a boring tractability list (8)
→ Sessions 5, 8. Optional layer as evidence trail / escalation ramp / media menu (9) →
every *More to explore*. Assign the community as a text (10) → Session 8. Wow-density per
minute, carried by named humans (11) → keystone selection. Values before causes (12) →
Sessions 1–3. Plus two stolen from adjacent curricula: **map-then-choose** (Session 5) and
**artifact-producing final units** (Sessions 5 and 8).

## Session 1 — A Century in a Decade

**Framing.** We have steered enormous things before; something enormous is now arriving
fast. *Feel the stakes and the agency before any argument.*
**Arc beat: awe → agency.** The opener is narrative, not a claim. No one is asked to
believe anything yet.
**Axes: 3 (scope), 14 (preparedness is broader than alignment) — as motivation only.**

**Core (~45 min) — two short readings, feeling before argument:**
- [../readings/ea-intro-program/500-million-but-not-a-single-one-more.md](../readings/ea-intro-program/500-million-but-not-a-single-one-more.md) (783w) — the smallpox-eradication epic. "You are a member of the species that did that." Tractability as a *feeling*, no mention of EA or duty.
- [../readings/macaskill-worldview/300000-lives-100-million-hens.md](../readings/macaskill-worldview/300000-lives-100-million-hens.md) (1,263w) — MacAskill's own awe/agency opener with 2025 wins, and the both/and: keep global health and animals live *in* the age of AGI.

*(The intelligence-explosion **mechanism** — why the world is about to reorder — is deliberately held to Session 4, where AI is staged behind conceded machinery. Night one asks for no numeric belief and no mechanism, only that you feel the stakes and the agency. Virtue 1: awe before argument.)*

**More to explore:** [../readings/forethought/three-types-of-intelligence-explosion.md](../readings/forethought/three-types-of-intelligence-explosion.md) (the mechanism that ages well — cognitive/software/industrial feedback loops — for anyone who wants it early, but not required); [../readings/personal-reflections/long-timelines-got-short.md](../readings/personal-reflections/long-timelines-got-short.md) (Toner: even skeptics' "long" timelines sit inside a wild decade — calm and credible); [../readings/macaskill-worldview/80k-century-in-a-decade.md](../readings/macaskill-worldview/80k-century-in-a-decade.md) (the vivid spoken version, *excerpt*); [../readings/macrostrategy/letter-from-utopia.md](../readings/macrostrategy/letter-from-utopia.md) (what winning could *feel* like); [../readings/macrostrategy/all-possible-views-are-wild.md](../readings/macrostrategy/all-possible-views-are-wild.md) (every coherent view of the future is wild — the permission slip); [../readings/macrostrategy/futurists-track-record.md](../readings/macrostrategy/futurists-track-record.md) (do futurists ever get it right? — the calibration check).

**The disagreement in the room.** *Is this really an unusual moment, or does every
generation believe "everything is about to change"?* The optionals hand both the credible
version (Toner) and the skeptic's audit (*Track record of futurists*). Facilitator names it
openly: we are not asking you to buy short timelines tonight.

**Discussion:**
1. Before reading, what did you think one person could realistically change about the world? Did the smallpox story move that estimate?
2. MacAskill insists on a both/and — bednets *and* AGI. Where does your instinct pull: focus, or breadth?
3. If the next ten years really do compress a century of change, what in *your own* life plan assumes the world will keep roughly its current shape?
4. Which felt more real to you — the 300,000 lives already saved, or the transformation still coming? What does that tell you about your own scope sensitivity?

**Exercise — the sealed lean (qualitative).** Write down, privately, *which way you lean*
on two questions and *what would move you*: (a) is transformative AI plausibly close, or
is "everything is about to change" a perennial illusion? (b) if it does arrive, does it go
well by default, or does it need steering? No numbers tonight — you have no timelines or
mechanisms yet, so a night-one credence would just be an uninformed anchor. You put the
*numbers* on it in Session 4, once the machinery is grounded, and re-rate them in Session 8
so the final re-rating tracks a real update, not course-induced anchoring. (Steals the
Handbook's confidence-re-rating, staged so it measures calibration rather than suggestion.)

## Session 2 — How Much, and Who Counts

**Framing.** The machinery for choosing where to help: scale, heavy tails, and whose
interests count. *Values and thinking tools before any cause.*
**Arc beat: rigor.** The numeric core, sandwiched early so it never has to carry emotional
weight alone.
**Axes: 3 (scope, scale, the numbers), and the moral-circle strand that later carries
17 (AI welfare) and 26 (animals under AGI).**

**Core (~2h):**
- [../readings/ea-intro-program/scope-insensitivity.md](../readings/ea-intro-program/scope-insensitivity.md) — $80/$78/$88 for 2k/20k/200k birds installs the entire cost-effectiveness apparatus in ~700 words.
- [../readings/ea-intro-program/prospecting-for-gold.md](../readings/ea-intro-program/prospecting-for-gold.md) — heavy tails + the ITN framework; closes by re-rating its own confidence claim by claim ("Disagreement is an opportunity to learn"). The rigor anchor.
- [../readings/ea-intro-program/radical-empathy.md](../readings/ea-intro-program/radical-empathy.md) — Karnofsky funds farm-animal welfare *while personally doubting chickens matter*. The whole course's stance — act well under deep moral uncertainty — and the moral-circle machinery reused for digital minds.
- [../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/on-caring.md](../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/on-caring.md) — Soares: your care-o-meter maxes out at ~150 people, so switch to manual and multiply. The heart voice on the same claim.

**More to explore:** [../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/naive-vs-prudent-utilitarianism.md](../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/naive-vs-prudent-utilitarianism.md) (the master-archer analogy that inoculates against ends-justify-means); [../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/sebo-widen-moral-circle-oped.md](../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/sebo-widen-moral-circle-oped.md) (Sebo runs the circle forward — Singer → animals → insects → AI — pre-loading Session 6); [../readings/macrostrategy/astronomical-waste.md](../readings/macrostrategy/astronomical-waste.md) (Bostrom's opportunity-cost logic, the primary source under all of it); [../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/cea-core-principles.md](../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/cea-core-principles.md) (the one-page scaffold — principles you keep as conclusions change).

**The disagreement in the room.** *Does expected-value, scope-sensitive reasoning capture
what matters — or does it crowd out justice, rights, and relationships?* Named now as a
foretaste; the full pathogen (Srinivasan) is assigned in Session 7. *Naive vs prudent
utilitarianism* is the in-core inoculation.

**Discussion:**
1. Where did *you* feel scope insensitivity bite this week — a number you couldn't make yourself feel?
2. Karnofsky acts on a cause he isn't sure about. Is that integrity or incoherence? When have you acted well on something you only half-believed?
3. Heavy tails imply most of your impact comes from a few choices. Which handful of decisions in *your* life are plausibly the tail?
4. Whose interests does your current moral circle actually include in practice, versus in principle — and where's the gap?

**Exercise — your own moral weights, before anyone argues.** Privately assign a rough
weight to: one present human, one chicken, one person alive in 2200, one AI system that
*might* be sentient. Note which you're most unsure about. You are not told anyone else's
numbers first — this is the Handbook's elicit-your-weights move, extended to the two
future-facing patients the course will test.

## Session 3 — Thinking in a World That Won't Hold Still

**Framing.** The scarce skill for the next decade isn't a conclusion — it's the habit of
updating fast, and noticing when you're only *performing* thought. *The stance the course
teaches alongside the material.*
**Arc beat: clear sight.** The distinctive values/epistemic-habits layer MacAskill asks to
carry a lot of weight.
**Axes: 4 (truth-seeking as the ground), 5 (frontier virtues), 6 (thinking under deep
uncertainty), 7 (the limits of our own tools).**

**Core (~1.5h):**
- [../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/importance-of-saying-oops.md](../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/importance-of-saying-oops.md) (765w) — one Enron image: swallow the whole bitter pill at once instead of conceding an inch at a time. Maximum wow-density on the skill the course depends on.
- [../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/scout-mindset-review.md](../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/scout-mindset-review.md) (5,217w) — soldier vs scout, plus calibration and counterfactual tests. The warm, practical how-to.
- [../readings/joe-carlsmith/fake-thinking-and-real-thinking.md](../readings/joe-carlsmith/fake-thinking-and-real-thinking.md) — *excerpt* to the core fake-vs-real distinction and the "are you tracking reality or your tribe?" test (~3–4k of 11,721w; stop before the long worked examples). The meta-reading the facilitator points back to every remaining week. *The full essay is the week's optional deep read* — the facilitator callback doesn't require the whole thing night one.
- [../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/epistemic-learned-helplessness.md](../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/epistemic-learned-helplessness.md) (1,621w) — the planted rebuttal: for most people most of the time, being argued into weird conclusions *is* the failure mode. Essential to install *before* the AGI arguments arrive.

**More to explore:** [../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/broad-timelines.md](../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/broad-timelines.md) (Ord models the exact stance — hold a broad distribution, plan wisely under it — on the course's defining question); [../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/truthseeking-is-the-ground.md](../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/truthseeking-is-the-ground.md) (contact-with-reality as the meta-principle, 20+ named people with skin in the game); [../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/please-forgive.md](../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/please-forgive.md) (a lived, costly, half-confused honesty — the personal register); [../readings/linked/why-im-not-a-bayesian-ngo.md](../readings/linked/why-im-not-a-bayesian-ngo.md) (Ngo attacks the Bayesian orthodoxy you're joining, then walks back his own critique); [../readings/richard-ngo/clarifying-and-predicting-agi.md](../readings/richard-ngo/clarifying-and-predicting-agi.md) (the t-AGI framework — replace "when AGI?" with capability horizons).

**The disagreement in the room.** *How open should you be to weird arguments?* "Take ideas
seriously" (the rationalist reflex the course runs on) versus *Epistemic learned
helplessness* (in core: sometimes the right move is to refuse to be argued out of your
priors). The whole session is a held tension, not a resolution.

**Discussion:**
1. Name one belief you changed in the last year. Did you swallow it whole, or concede inch by inch — and what did the delay cost?
2. Where in your thinking about AI are you *performing* a take (Carlsmith's "fake thinking") rather than reasoning it through?
3. When someone builds a clever argument for a wild conclusion, what's your honest default — update, or step back? Which failure mode are you more prone to?
4. What would it take for you to change your mind about whether this whole course is worth your time? If you can't answer, is that a problem?

**Exercise — the pre-registered oops.** Write down one belief about AI you predict you'll
hold *differently* in a year, and the specific evidence that would trigger the change (this
is Carlsmith's *predictable updating* made personal). Then take one claim from Session 1's
sealed lean and stress-test it with a minimal-trust check: try to verify one link in
its chain yourself.

## Session 4 — Should a Changed World Change Our Priorities?

**Framing.** The course's founding argument with itself. Being "principles-first" means
actually changing your mind when the world changes — or is it cover for the same old cause
list? *AI arrives here — staged behind the machinery you already accepted.*
**Arc beat: the turn.** The weird claim (AGI reorders everything) is loaded onto conceded
ones: the effectiveness machinery (S2), the epistemic stance (S3), and a loss-of-control
mechanism a skeptic already grants.
**Axes: 2 (should a changed world change priorities?), 8 (why would AI aim to defeat us?),
9 (sudden vs gradual), 14 (preparedness is broader than alignment).**

**Core (~2h) — the pivot argument leads; the alignment mechanism is conceded, not dwelt in:**
- [../readings/ea-meta-movement/west-third-wave-ea.md](../readings/ea-meta-movement/west-third-wave-ea.md) (1,145w) — the "waves of EA" model. Read *first*: MacAskill's "third way" is literally West's "forefront of weirdness," and seeing it as a movement's deliberate turn licenses disagreeing with the turn.
- [../idea/macaskill-ea-in-the-age-of-agi.md](../idea/macaskill-ea-in-the-age-of-agi.md) (6,919w) — the founding essay itself: the third way, the expanded cause menu, the 30%-beyond-safety target, "principles-first as cover." The session's spine, read in full.
- [../readings/ai-safety-core/why-ai-alignment-could-be-hard.md](../readings/ai-safety-core/why-ai-alignment-could-be-hard.md) — *excerpt* to Cotra's saint/sycophant/schemer core (~3k of 4,361w) — misalignment as the *natural output* of the training paradigm, not sci-fi. The single conceded loss-of-control mechanism, deliberately shorter than the pivot readings so the canon it means to concede briefly doesn't dominate the session. (Swapped in for Karnofsky's 9,788-word *Why would AI aim to defeat humanity*, now optional.)

**More to explore:** [../readings/ai-safety-core/bengio-faq-catastrophic-ai-risks.md](../readings/ai-safety-core/bengio-faq-catastrophic-ai-risks.md) (*the in-session skeptic* — a Turing laureate answering the "isn't this overblown?" objections point by point and reconciling near-term harms with catastrophic risk; read this if the danger claim feels asserted rather than argued); [../readings/ai-safety-core/what-failure-looks-like.md](../readings/ai-safety-core/what-failure-looks-like.md) (Christiano: control leaks away with no villain and no single moment — the gradual failure mode, picked up again by gradual-disempowerment in Session 5); [../readings/ai-safety-core/why-would-ai-aim-to-defeat-humanity.md](../readings/ai-safety-core/why-would-ai-aim-to-defeat-humanity.md) (Karnofsky's fuller plain-language treatment of where dangerous goals come from); [../readings/forethought/three-types-of-intelligence-explosion.md](../readings/forethought/three-types-of-intelligence-explosion.md) (the intelligence-explosion mechanism held back from Session 1 — why the world reorders fast); [../readings/ea-meta-movement/koehler-building-ea-for-ai.md](../readings/ea-meta-movement/koehler-building-ea-for-ai.md) (the rigorous pro-case, with an alternatives appendix and attached anonymous dissents — steelmanning in one reading); [../readings/ai-rights-economics/80k-gradual-disempowerment-profile.md](../readings/ai-rights-economics/80k-gradual-disempowerment-profile.md) (the curriculum-ready six-claim version of the villain-free risk); [../readings/macrostrategy/ai-2027.md](../readings/macrostrategy/ai-2027.md) (a concrete month-by-month scenario to argue with); [../readings/macaskill-worldview/short-review-if-anyone-builds-it.md](../readings/macaskill-worldview/short-review-if-anyone-builds-it.md) (MacAskill takes takeover seriously *while* dismantling the strongest doom book — how to disagree well).

**The disagreement in the room.** Two, staged deliberately. First, *is AI even dangerous?* —
this is where AI enters the course, so the danger claim is not smuggled in: Bengio (optional,
named here) answers the skeptics point by point, and the facilitator surfaces the skeptic's
case out loud rather than deferring all doubt to Session 7. Second, granting some danger,
*takeover-first vs preparedness-first: is alignment the only variable that matters?* The
essay says no; Cotra presses that maybe it dominates. Koehler's pro-case and its attached
dissents are in the optionals so the argument is genuinely two-sided.

**Discussion:**
1. MacAskill says "principles-first" can be cover for "the same causes we always had." Test it on yourself: if you learned AGI were 20 years further off, which of your current priorities would actually change?
2. Where's your intuition — does misalignment dominate, or is it one risk among coups, disempowerment, lock-in, and lost futures?
3. Christiano's failure has no villain. Does a slow, structural loss of control feel more or less alarming to you than a treacherous turn — and why might that feeling mislead you?
4. If EA's job is to keep re-deriving its priorities, what's the personal cost of being the kind of person who actually changes course? Are you willing to pay it?

**Exercise — put numbers on it, then map the menu.** (1) *Now* commit the two credences
Session 1 deferred: your P(AGI-by-2040) and your P(the transition goes well *if* it happens),
each with the one piece of evidence that would most move it. You've now seen the mechanism
and the pivot, so these are grounded, not night-one guesses — seal them beside your Session 1
lean; you re-rate in Session 8. (2) Take MacAskill's ~16-cause menu — use the essay's
cause-menu section as the object, no need to re-read the whole essay — and place each on your
own rough scale × neglectedness × tractability grid. Circle the two you think are most
*under*-invested relative to attention. This hands you your Session 5 deep-dive choice.

## Session 5 — The Widening Menu: Map, Then Choose

**Framing.** Even if alignment is solved, is the transition safe? *Survey the whole
beyond-safety landscape together, then each go deep on one.* This is where the course earns
its 30%+.
**Arc beat: the widening.** Session 4's dread converts to agency: the menu is enormous and
most of it is neglected.
**Axes: 14, 19, 20, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25, 26, 27 — the whole preparedness turn, sampled.**

**Shared core, everyone (~1h, ~6.5k words):**
- [../readings/forethought/preparing-for-the-intelligence-explosion.md](../readings/forethought/preparing-for-the-intelligence-explosion.md) — *excerpt* to the "century in a decade" framing and the grand-challenges taxonomy (target ~2,500w; skip the deep-dive appendices). The argument that AGI preparedness is broader than alignment, and the map of what's on the menu. *(The committed file is a raw fetch with stray empty headers — ship a clean excerpt file before piloting; see open questions.)*
- [../readings/funder-signals/finnveden-non-alignment-project-ideas.md](../readings/funder-signals/finnveden-non-alignment-project-ideas.md) (969w) — an Open Phil thinker's concrete menu of non-alignment projects. The single reading most directly serving the "beyond AI safety" target.
- [../readings/ai-rights-economics/80k-gradual-disempowerment-profile.md](../readings/ai-rights-economics/80k-gradual-disempowerment-profile.md) (2,140w) — **the guaranteed human-power reading.** MacAskill weights the human-power cluster most heavily ("plausibly the highest-impact thing"), so its curriculum-ready six-claim version is shared core, not left to a coin-flip packet — every participant reads it.
- [../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/algorithms-and-authority-power-global-health.md](../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/algorithms-and-authority-power-global-health.md) (1,643w) — **the guaranteed Global-South voice.** Senanayake, a Global-South nonprofit leader who uses both RCTs and AI: "Is AI the new RCT?"
