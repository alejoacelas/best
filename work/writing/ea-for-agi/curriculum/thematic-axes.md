# Thematic axes: a walkable menu

A deliberately overlapping menu of axes the curriculum could be organized around. Each
axis is a question or tension to put in the room, then a CORE reading or two (the spine)
and a few CONTEXT readings that surround it (a critic, a case, the data, a personal
register). Overlap is intended: an axis is a *lens*, not a week, and the same reading
earns its place under several. Pick 8 of these to become sessions; the rest stay as the
optional layer's map.

Organized under the five super-clusters from
[landscape.md](../analysis/landscape.md), with cross-cutting axes placed where they read
most naturally. The six recurring debates that thread through everything are tagged
inline: **[takeover vs prep]**, **[sudden vs gradual]**, **[survive vs flourish]**,
**[control vs otherness]**, **[virtue vs institution]**, **[centralize vs decentralize]**,
plus the meta **[inside vs hostile]**.

Reading roles use the Handbook's grammar (see [handbook-virtues.md](../analysis/handbook-virtues.md)):
*on-ramp* (accessible entry), *anchor* (rigor floor), *critic* (names its own best
adversary), *case/data* (concrete or measured), *heart* (personal/lyrical register).

## I. Foundations — how to think, and what EA is

The values-and-epistemic-habits layer MacAskill wants to carry a lot of the weight.
Seven axes, values-and-thinking-tools before any cause.

### 1. EA as a question, not an answer
*Is EA a fixed list of causes, or a method that keeps re-deriving the list?* The frame
that licenses everything the course does next.

- **CORE** `readings/ea-principles-epistemics/cea-core-principles.md` — the one-page scaffold (scope sensitivity, impartiality, scout mindset, tradeoffs) — principles you keep even as conclusions change.

- CONTEXT `readings/ea-meta-movement/reardon-12-theses-on-ea.md` — twelve quotable provocations for a big, pluralist EA; *discussion fuel*.

## How to walk this menu into 8 sessions

The axes are a superset; a syllabus is a *path* through them. Two design constraints from
the Handbook post-mortem:

- **Values before causes.** Axes 1–7 (Foundations) should seed the first two or three
  sessions before any cause lands — the structural signature the Handbook and MacAskill's
  target both point at.
- **Map-then-choose for the cause menu.** Sections II–III hold ~18 axes; no course teaches
  all of them. One "survey the landscape, then pick a deep-dive" session (the BlueDot AGI
  Strategy move) is how to get breadth without drowning.

A plausible 8-session spine, each session = one or two axes: **(1)** EA as a question +
scope [1, 3] → **(2)** truth-seeking + frontier virtues + uncertainty [4, 5, 6] →
**(3)** should a changed world change our priorities? [2, 14] → **(4)** loss of control,
sudden vs gradual [8, 9] → **(5)** map-then-choose across the beyond-safety menu
[15–25, pick one] → **(6)** the moral-circle frontier: character, welfare, rights
[16, 17, 18] → **(7)** criticism week, inside and hostile [7, 11, 30] → **(8)** living with
it + what to do [31, 28]. Every other axis becomes that session's optional layer.
