# What the EA Handbook does great

Design principles extracted from the EA Intro Program / EA Handbook (4th ed., 2022) for
the "EA in the age of AGI" successor course. Method: reconstructed the 8-session
syllabus from the downloaded reading set ([../readings/ea-intro-program/](../readings/ea-intro-program/),
117 files), read all core readings, skimmed the optional layer, and contrasted against
8 adjacent curricula ([../readings/adjacent-curricula/](../readings/adjacent-curricula/)).

## Transferable design principles

1. **Open with awe, not argument or obligation.** Session 1's first reading is a
   900-word smallpox-eradication epic — "You are a member of the species that did
   that" — with no mention of EA, charity, or duty. Guilt (Singer's drowning child) is
   demoted to a week-3 *optional*. Establish tractability as a feeling before any
   concept. Exemplar: [500 Million, But Not a Single One More](../readings/ea-intro-program/500-million-but-not-a-single-one-more.md).

2. **Stage weird claims behind already-conceded ones.** X-risk opens with asteroids
   and nuclear near-misses (risks a skeptic grants), builds the scale/neglect
   machinery on them, and only then loads bio and AI onto it. The same ordering runs
   across weeks: concrete danger (wk 4) → longtermist philosophy (wk 5) → AI (wk 6).
   By the time AI arrives it's one more row in an accepted table, not a hobbyhorse.
   Exemplar: [the 80k x-risk article](../readings/ea-intro-program/case-for-reducing-existential-risk.md).

3. **Every core case names its best critic; the optionals hand you the critic's full
   text.** The longtermism intro quotes Alexander Berger *against* strong longtermism;
   the pandemics profile links Thorstad's skeptic series; Wait But Why is assigned
   *with* Muehlhauser's point-by-point rebuttal; MacAskill's own essay against
   hinge-of-history sits one click under the week that assumes it. Exemplars:
   [Longtermism: An Introduction](../readings/ea-intro-program/introduction-to-longtermism.md),
   [A reply to Wait But Why](../readings/ea-intro-program/reply-to-wait-but-why.md),
   [Are we living at the most influential time in history?](../readings/ea-intro-program/are-we-living-at-most-influential-time.md).

4. **Pair head and heart voices on the same claim.** Each big claim gets an actuarial
   voice and a lyrical or personal one: 80k's hedged x-risk explainer beside Ord's "a
   vast and extraordinary adulthood awaits"; the confident animal advocate beside
   Karnofsky funding farm-animal welfare *while personally doubting chickens matter*.
   Two-voice pairing lets participants triangulate instead of receiving a line.
   Exemplars: [Radical Empathy](../readings/ea-intro-program/radical-empathy.md) +
   [The Case Against Speciesism](../readings/ea-intro-program/case-against-speciesism.md).

5. **Teach the stance toward the material, not just the material.** Core slots go to
   meta-readings: 80k undermining its own authority ("we've been wrong before…
   hard to be more than about 70% confident"), Julia Wise's permission to not be a
   totalizing maximizer ("Sunday morning is for making pancakes"), Cotton-Barratt
   closing his talk by re-rating his confidence in every claim he just made. Ship the
   error bars with the advice. Exemplars:
   [Advice on how to read our advice](../readings/ea-intro-program/advice-on-how-to-read-our-advice.md),
   [You have more than one goal, and that's fine](../readings/ea-intro-program/more-than-one-goal.md),
   [Prospecting for Gold](../readings/ea-intro-program/prospecting-for-gold.md).

6. **Give criticism a whole core session, with real pathogens.** One-eighth of the
   program is critics in *core* slots: Nobel-laureate outsiders (Deaton: "It is an
   illusion that lives can be bought like cars"), an insider apostasy written under
   Bostrom's "hypothetical apostasy" protocol, and Pascal's Mugging stress-testing the
   EV machinery the course itself taught. The founder's replies are included and
   visibly lose some rounds. No adjacent curriculum does this at all. Exemplars:
   [A critique of effective altruism](../readings/ea-intro-program/critique-of-effective-altruism.md),
   [Responses to "The Logic of Effective Altruism"](../readings/ea-intro-program/responses-logic-of-ea.md).

7. **Exercises are about the participant, never a hypothetical.** Estimate *your*
   lifetime income and what a fraction could do; elicit *your* moral weights before
   the readings argue for any; write a letter persuading a historical counterpart to
   expand their compassion, then imagine the letter a *future* counterpart writes to
   you; hand in what you're still skeptical of as an assigned deliverable. Exemplar:
   [Expanding Our Compassion exercise](../readings/ea-intro-program/expanding-our-compassion-exercise.md).

8. **Chase every scale claim with a boring tractability list.** Immediately after "1
   in 6 this century" comes 50+ mundane policy levers ("renew New START," "screen DNA
   synthesis"). The list earns its slot by being boring: it makes x-risk look like
   ordinary work, not doom-mongering. Exemplar:
   [Policy and research ideas to reduce existential risk](../readings/ea-intro-program/policy-research-ideas-reduce-existential-risk.md).

9. **Use the optional layer as evidence trail, escalation ramp, and media menu.**
   Optionals let a skeptic audit the core's numbers (five GiveWell pages behind
   "$3,500 per life"), let the curious walk further than the core dares (drowning
   child → ongoing moral catastrophe → wild-animal suffering → Gwern's *Narrowing
   Circle* attacking the week's own metaphor), and re-serve the material as podcast,
   film, or book. Core stays safe; optionals radicalize opt-in. Exemplar ramp ends at
   [The Narrowing Circle](../readings/ea-intro-program/the-narrowing-circle-summary.md).

10. **Assign the community as a text.** The final week's core includes raw forum
    threads of ordinary members reasoning publicly about their own donations, and
    profiles of deliberately non-heroic pledgers (a banker, a lab assistant). It shows
    the register of the community being joined and says: the forum is the ongoing
    curriculum, and you can join at the lowest tier this week. Exemplar:
    [Where are you donating this year and why?](../readings/ea-intro-program/where-are-you-donating-2019.md).

11. **Select readings on wow-density per minute, carried by named humans.** The
    program's best readings are short, cited, and built around real people — Zhdanov,
    Arkhipov ("Thank you Vasili Arkhipov." as a one-line paragraph), Petrov, Nalin,
    Greg Lewis quantifying his own medical career. Specification-gaming robots doing
    flips do more than abstractions about mesa-optimizers. Prefer mechanisms and
    thought experiments (which age well) over capability snapshots (which don't).
    Exemplars: [Scope Insensitivity](../readings/ea-intro-program/scope-insensitivity.md),
    [The case for taking AI seriously as a threat to humanity](../readings/ea-intro-program/case-for-taking-ai-seriously.md).

12. **Values and thinking tools before causes.** Three full weeks (effectiveness,
    scope sensitivity, moral circle) precede any cause area; the 2022 revision
    explicitly rebalanced toward "core EA concepts and thinking tools." Adjacent AI
    curricula start at the technology; only the In-Depth EA Program has a partial
    version, and only for the already-converted. This is the Handbook's structural
    signature — and the natural home for MacAskill's "values and epistemic habits"
    target.

## The syllabus, reconstructed

8 weekly sessions; per session ~1–2h of core readings + 30–60 min exercise, then a
1.5h facilitated discussion in cohorts of 4–8
([official program page](../readings/adjacent-curricula/introductory-ea-program.md),
[2022 announcement](../readings/adjacent-curricula/new-intro-fellowship-curriculum.md)).

| Wk | Session | Core | Exercise |
|----|---------|------|----------|
| 1 | The Effectiveness Mindset | smallpox epic; 80k "can one person make a difference" | — |
| 2 | Differences in Impact | Prospecting for Gold; Scope Insensitivity | your income → impact |
| 3 | Radical Empathy | Karnofsky's Radical Empathy; Case Against Speciesism | moral weights; letters across time |
| 4 | Our Final Century? | 80k x-risk article; Ord EAG talk; policy-ideas list | — |
| 5 | What Could the Future Hold? | Longtermism intro; Precipice intro+ch1; Greaves/MacAskill strong longtermism | future-people intuition tests |
| 6 | Smarter Than Us | Piper's Vox AI case; 80k pandemics profile; Open Phil views on advanced AI | — |
| 7 | What Do You Think? | Kuhn's critique; Boston Review responses; CEA objections FAQ; Pascal's Mugging | licensed skepticism |
| 8 | Putting It Into Practice | 80k key ideas + planning process + advice-on-advice + two "beyond our list" posts; more-than-one-goal; donation threads; events ecosystem | career workbook |

Each session also carries 8–20 "More to Explore" optionals. (Two core readings failed
to download and should be re-fetched: Greaves & MacAskill's *The Case for Strong
Longtermism* and Open Phil's *Some Background on Our Views Regarding Advanced AI* —
each is its week's rigor anchor beneath the accessible intro.)

## Sequencing: the emotional arc

The arc is **awe → agency → rigor → discomfort → dread → hope → doubt → plan.**

- Weeks 1–3 move the reader through feeling powerful (smallpox died; Petrov saved your
  grandparents), then getting the machinery (heavy tails, 10,000x spreads,
  scale/tractability/neglect), then having the machinery turned on their own values
  (Bentham's prescience, "the possibility of an ongoing moral catastrophe"). Stories
  carry weeks 1 and 3; week 2 is the numeric core, sandwiched so it never carries
  emotional weight alone.
- Concepts arrive exactly when needed: scope insensitivity *before* cost-effectiveness
  tables; heavy tails *before* cause comparison; moral uncertainty *before* x-risk.
- The x-risk-before-longtermism ordering (wk 4 before wk 5, reversing the Handbook's
  part order) grounds a potentially airy worldview in Cuban-Missile-Crisis concreteness
  first, so the "quadrillion future lives" arithmetic lands on prepared soil. The
  longtermism intro even offers an exit ramp: you can care about these causes without
  the philosophy.
- AI enters in week 6 through a journalist (Piper) and a cautious funder (Open Phil),
  not insiders — the journalist mediates the weirdness, quotes believers and skeptics
  (LeCun on-page), and pre-installs the objections. The vivid true believers
  (Yudkowsky, Bostrom, Wait But Why) live in the optional layer, alongside their
  corrections.
- Week 7 licenses the doubt the course has been accumulating; week 8 converts what
  survives into a plan, with the tone dropping from civilizational stakes to process
  documents and pancakes.

## What makes a reading earn its slot

- **Brevity × wow-density.** The opener is ~900 words with one staggering number
  ([500 Million](../readings/ea-intro-program/500-million-but-not-a-single-one-more.md));
  the bridge into week 2 is ~700 words with one unforgettable experiment
  ([Scope Insensitivity](../readings/ea-intro-program/scope-insensitivity.md) — $80/$78/$88
  for 2,000/20,000/200,000 birds).
- **A named, sympathetic human at the center.** Greg Lewis quantifying his own career;
  Arkhipov; the debunking never deflates because a hero (Petrov, Nalin) restores
  possibility right after the sting.
- **Myth-busting with the arithmetic shown.** The 80k pieces put estimates in
  footnotes with "could easily be off by an order of magnitude" — the reading performs
  the epistemics it preaches. [Prospecting for Gold](../readings/ea-intro-program/prospecting-for-gold.md)
  is the exemplar: it ends by re-deriving its own confidence claim by claim
  ("Disagreement is an opportunity to learn" is a section header).
- **Self-flagged bias buys trust.** The x-risk article notes that researchers' risk
  estimates suffer selection bias — *in the article arguing from those estimates*.
- **Disarming before persuading.** Piper's AI piece opens with what it's *not*
  claiming ("We're not talking about computers 'turning evil'"), uses funny
  specification-gaming examples, and lands its payload with Rutherford declaring
  atomic energy infeasible <24h before Szilárd conceived the chain reaction.
- **Bounded advocacy.** The one frankly persuasive essay (speciesism) pre-empts its
  strawman ("does *not* require that we give gorillas the right to vote") and stays
  short.

## Core vs optional

The core is a safe, two-voice, criticism-naming spine kept to ~1–2h/week. The
optional layer does five distinct jobs:

- **Evidence trail** — the primary sources behind the core's headline numbers, for
  auditors (GiveWell's criteria/process/ITN pages behind "$3,500 per life").
- **Planted rebuttals of the core** — the randomista-growth debate, Tomasik contra
  astronomical charity differences, hinge-of-history, Muehlhauser contra Wait But Why.
- **Escalation ramps** — staircases of increasing radicalism the core only gestures at
  (drowning child → moral catastrophe → wild-animal suffering → narrowing circle).
- **Depth anchors** — the academic floor (Vulnerable World Hypothesis, Greaves on
  cluelessness, Against the Social Discount Rate).
- **Media diversity** — film (Dominion), documentary (AlphaGo), podcasts (Ord on FLI),
  interactive tools, full books. Heavy material gets a human voice.

## Exercises and discussion

Recurring forms, in program order: quantified self-implication (your income → lives) →
elicit-your-weights (your moral parameters, before being told anyone's) →
perspective-shift letter (historical counterpart, then the future counterpart writing
to *you* — the program's single best exercise, and the one an AGI course should steal
with "someone in 2045" substituted) → intuition-testing thought experiments →
structured skepticism as deliverable → plan-production with a workbook. All wrapped in
permissive framing ("don't feel obligated") — low-pressure wrappers around
high-intensity content. The consistent principle: the exercise's subject is always the
participant's own life, money, values, or blind spots.

## Voice diversity and disagreement

Three mechanisms, at three depths:

1. **Within a reading** — authors quote their best critics (Piper staging believers vs
   LeCun; Moorhouse platforming Berger) and rate their own confidence (Cotton-Barratt).
2. **Within a week's core** — deliberate voice pairs (advocate + uncertain funder;
   actuarial + lyrical); week 7 pairs critics with official rebuttals and lets the
   reader referee.
3. **In the optional layer** — full texts of the critics the core named, plus three
   raw forum *tag pages* (criticism of EA / causes / orgs): an unfiltered firehose as
   a "we're not hiding anything" gesture.

The genre range does as much work as the opinion range: mythic narrative, talk
transcript, funder memo, FAQ, philosophy paper, magazine symposium, newspaper profile,
raw comment thread. The implicit lesson: this conversation happens across society, and
you can already join it.

## Against the adjacent curricula

What the Handbook does that none of BlueDot, AGISF, Alignment 201, In-Depth EA,
Precipice syllabus, or Sciences Po does:

- Emotional narrative openers (BlueDot's closest is quote carousels — social proof,
  not felt experience; though its AGI Strategy course does open with *positive
  visions*, an arc choice worth noting).
- A whole criticism session with hostile outside voices in core slots.
- Values-before-causes sequencing for newcomers.
- Exercises about your own life rather than about the material.
- Permission-to-be-human content (nothing in the adjacent set resembles
  *more-than-one-goal*; BlueDot's register is recruitment-intense).

What the adjacent curricula do that the Handbook doesn't — the shortlist to steal
(details in [../readings/adjacent-curricula/](../readings/adjacent-curricula/)):

- **Artifact-producing final units** ([BlueDot governance](../readings/adjacent-curricula/bluedot-frontier-ai-governance-course.md):
  a policy briefing for a named decision-maker; "a 6-month roadmap, with the
  expectation you'll act on it") vs the Handbook's read-about-careers landing.
- **Steelman assignments** as a *mechanism* for disagreement — "argue for and against
  proposals you didn't choose"; "read across the spectrum, then defend a position in
  writing" — where the Handbook only curates disagreeing texts.
- **Map-then-choose structure** ([BlueDot AGI Strategy](../readings/adjacent-curricula/bluedot-agi-strategy-course.md)
  Unit 3: survey the threat landscape, then pick one of four deep-dives). The natural
  answer to MacAskill's 16-cause menu: breadth without drowning.
- **Connective narration + surgical cuts** ([Ngo's AGISF](../readings/adjacent-curricula/ngo-agi-safety-fundamentals-curriculum.md)):
  each week's intro says what each reading is *for* and where they conflict; readings
  trimmed to sections and timestamps; per-reading minute counts.
- **Week-0 / prerequisite layering** (AGISF's optional ML week; BlueDot's separate 2h
  self-paced AI Foundations) — keeps AI basics out of the main arc.
- **Pipeline integration and pacing options** (BlueDot: intensive vs part-time, grants,
  advising, alumni Slack; [Bernardi](../readings/adjacent-curricula/bernardi-courses-build-fields.md):
  courses convert "interest overhang" into a field; ~75–80% completion in cohorts vs
  ~20% for MOOCs; "a curriculum is laying a stake in the ground" — and curriculum gaps
  reveal "scoping documents you wish existed," i.e. readings this project may need to
  write).

## Weaknesses worth not reproducing

- **Capability-snapshot readings rot.** The AI core is 2015–2018 vintage ("around 50
  people work full time on technical AI safety"); the silently-updated 2026 pandemics
  profile shows the fix. Prefer mechanisms, thought experiments, and honestly-hedged
  ranges; where currency is unavoidable, plan for refresh.
- **Platform rot.** Exercises live in GuidedTrack (the archived week-2 exercise is a
  386-char husk), core readings sit behind Google Drive links and YouTube stubs, and
  several "readings" are org navigation pages. Keep exercises and key texts as plain
  self-contained text in the curriculum.
- **Criticism pre-selected to be survivable.** Week 7's critics attack global-health-era
  EA (aid skepticism, earning to give); nothing engages the current commitments. The
  successor's criticism week must platform critics of AI-risk framing and of
  AGI-centric EA itself, not legacy targets.
- **One-org landing.** Six of week 8's core slots are 80,000 Hours documents, two of
  them link-index pages. Diversify the action theories and orgs in the final week.
- **AI quarantined to one week, no insider voice in core.** Right-sized for 2018,
  wrong for this course — but the deeper lesson stands: the reader never hears an
  alignment researcher (or now: a lab insider, a digital-minds researcher) speak
  undiluted in a core slot.
