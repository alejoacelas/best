# Cluster map: Richard Ngo

## What this cluster covers

Richard Ngo — DeepMind then OpenAI alignment researcher, now independent, writing the
Substack [Mind the Future](https://www.mindthefuture.info) — is the brief's named exemplar
of "some way of looking at the world" worth sampling. The cluster is one man's arc from
technical AI-safety insider to heterodox civilizational theorist, and it spans six threads
that he insists are the same theory viewed at different scales:

1. **The misalignment case, from ML first principles** — the second-species argument
   ([AGI safety from first principles](../../readings/richard-ngo/agi-safety-from-first-principles.md)),
   its canonical technical statement
   ([The Alignment Problem from a Deep Learning Perspective](../../readings/richard-ngo/alignment-problem-deep-learning-perspective.md)),
   inner misalignment, takeover scenarios, and what "alignment research" even means.
2. **Forecasting AGI** — the [t-AGI framework](../../readings/richard-ngo/clarifying-and-predicting-agi.md)
   (capability horizons instead of a binary threshold) and deep-learning-progress visualizations.
3. **Epistemic habits** — his anti-orthodoxy trilogy against "rationality realism" and
   Bayesianism, arguing scientific/theory-construction epistemology beats credence-updating.
   This is squarely MacAskill's "epistemic habits we'll need" target.
4. **A scale-free theory of agency (coalitional agency)** — his current research program:
   minds, companies, and civilizations are all coalitions of competing/cooperating subagents.
5. **Sociopolitics and civilizational decay** — coups, preference cascades, elite
   coordination, "pessimization," efficiency-vs-autonomy, and his own competing curriculum.
6. **Ethics, AI character, and fiction** — virtue ethics as an alignment target, plus a
   flagship short story about living through the transition.

The connective tissue is coalitional agency: internal conflict, commitment, robustness-over-
efficiency, and virtue-as-glue recur whether the "agent" is a psyche, a lab, or the West.

## Intellectual traditions and where the voices disagree

- **Friendly critic of MIRI/agent-foundations.** Ngo rejects the assumption of a clean
  formal theory of intelligence
  ([Realism about rationality](../../readings/richard-ngo/realism-about-rationality.md)) yet
  builds his own framework directly on MIRI's tools — Garrabrant induction, Demski's FixDT,
  Garrabrant/Critch bargaining. He steelmans Yudkowsky's inner-misalignment argument
  ([A simple case for extreme inner misalignment](../../readings/linked/simple-case-extreme-inner-misalignment.md))
  while disowning it. So the disagreement is *within* the rationalist tradition, not against it.
- **Against Bayesian/EA epistemic orthodoxy.**
  [Why I'm not a Bayesian](../../readings/linked/why-im-not-a-bayesian-ngo.md) and
  [Towards a formal scientific epistemology](../../readings/richard-ngo/towards-a-formal-scientific-epistemology.md)
  attack the credence-on-propositions worldview that EA/LessWrong treats as bedrock — and, in
  the follow-up, Ngo publicly walks back his own critique, modeling the epistemic humility the
  course wants to teach.
- **Against the "econ-brained."**
  [Economic efficiency undermines autonomy](../../readings/richard-ngo/economic-efficiency-undermines-autonomy.md)
  and its co-traveler [Four ways learning econ makes people dumber re: future AI](../../readings/linked/four-ways-econ-dumber-future-ai.md)
  (Steven Byrnes) sit against the market-optimist camp represented here by
  [Halperin et al. on AGI and interest rates](../../readings/linked/agi-emh-asset-prices.md)
  and Drexler's [Comprehensive AI Services](../../readings/linked/reframing-superintelligence-cais.md)
  (which also disputes Ngo's unitary-agent model of AGI). This is a live cause-relevant debate:
  gradual disempowerment vs. efficient-market reassurance.
- **The empirical-ML co-travelers.** Jacob Steinhardt's
  [More Is Different for AI](../../readings/linked/steinhardt-more-is-different-for-ai.md) and
  [ML Systems Will Have Weird Failure Modes](../../readings/linked/steinhardt-ml-systems-weird-failure-modes.md)
  supply the emergence-from-scale evidence base underneath Ngo's more philosophical claims.
- **The heterodox-right turn.** The sociopolitics thread is post-EA and Burja/Yarvin-adjacent:
  the [21st Century Civilization curriculum](../../readings/richard-ngo/21st-century-civilization-curriculum.md)
  is co-authored with Samo Burja; [Elite Coordination](../../readings/richard-ngo/elite-coordination-consensus-of-power.md)
  runs on Yarvin's "cathedral";
  [Power Lies Trembling](../../readings/richard-ngo/power-lies-trembling.md) on Kuran's
  preference falsification. This is the genuinely non-EA worldview the brief wanted sampled —
  and its politics will read as a provocation inside an EA fellowship, which is the point.

## Standout readings

- [AGI safety from first principles: Introduction](../../readings/richard-ngo/agi-safety-from-first-principles.md)
  — the "second species" argument in four crisp premises, derived rather than asserted.
  **Role: conceptual on-ramp to why AGI could be an x-risk.**
- [Clarifying and predicting AGI (t-AGI)](../../readings/richard-ngo/clarifying-and-predicting-agi.md)
  — replaces the unanswerable "when AGI?" with 1-minute/1-hour/1-day capability horizons; a
  mechanism that ages well (per the handbook's anti-rot rule).
  **Role: the thinking tool for forecasting, testable against the last two years.**
- [Realism about rationality](../../readings/richard-ngo/realism-about-rationality.md)
  — dissects the hidden assumption that intelligence has one true theory (momentum vs. fitness);
  short, deep, self-aware about its own community's selection effects.
  **Role: anchor epistemic-habits reading; teaches the stance toward a worldview.**
- [Why I'm not a Bayesian](../../readings/linked/why-im-not-a-bayesian-ngo.md)
  — frontal challenge to the epistemic orthodoxy of the movement the reader is joining.
  **Role: best in-house critic of EA's own thinking tools; pair with the formal follow-up.**
- [Aligning to Virtues](../../readings/richard-ngo/aligning-to-virtues.md)
  — argues virtue is a better alignment target than consequentialism, deontology, or
  corrigibility; the only reading that makes MacAskill's "AI character" cause concrete.
  **Role: AI-character cause anchor.**
- [On Pessimization](../../readings/richard-ngo/on-pessimization.md)
  — vivid, memorable taxonomy of how movements achieve the opposite of their goal (incl. "AI
  safety awareness founded the labs"). Self-implicating and widely applicable.
  **Role: self-critical lens on advocacy and EA itself; discussion dynamite.**
- [Lessons from my time in Effective Altruism](../../readings/richard-ngo/lessons-from-my-time-in-ea.md)
  — insider-turned-critic, naming his own overconfidence, personal fit, in-group bias.
  **Role: personal reflection / movement critique in exactly the handbook's trusted register.**
- [The Gentle Romance](../../readings/richard-ngo/the-gentle-romance-fiction.md)
  — flagship short story of one life across the transition to full-virtuality utopia.
  **Role: the heart/vivid-narrative reading and media diversity; positive-vision counterweight.**
- [Power Lies Trembling](../../readings/richard-ngo/power-lies-trembling.md)
  — coups as the "supernovae of sociology," preference-falsification threshold models, closing
  on a Kierkegaardian stance toward politics. Rigorous *and* gripping.
  **Role: sociopolitics deep-dive that also models a healthy emotional stance.**
- [Economic efficiency often undermines sociopolitical autonomy](../../readings/richard-ngo/economic-efficiency-undermines-autonomy.md)
  — the gradual-disempowerment case against optimizing for efficiency; a contrarian econ read.
  **Role: gradual-disempowerment cause anchor; foil to the market-optimist camp.**
- [Towards a scale-free theory of intelligent agency](../../readings/richard-ngo/scale-free-theory-of-intelligent-agency.md)
  — his single most original contribution: coalitional agency unifying EUM and active inference.
  **Role: rigorous deep-dive / the engine behind his whole worldview (optional-layer rigor anchor).**
- [AGI safety career advice](../../readings/richard-ngo/agi-safety-career-advice.md)
  — most-cited practical guidance: find a lever, choose by personal fit.
  **Role: the action/career reading for a final unit.**
- [21st Century Civilization curriculum](../../readings/richard-ngo/21st-century-civilization-curriculum.md)
  — his own competing curriculum with Samo Burja; both a design comparator and a compact
  statement of his civilizational-decay worldview.
  **Role: meta comparator + concentrated dose of the heterodox lens.**
- [Towards more cooperative AI safety strategies](../../readings/linked/towards-more-cooperative-ai-safety-strategies.md)
  — "the AI safety community is structurally power-seeking," written from inside OpenAI.
  **Role: sharpest self-directed critique of the AI-safety movement's methods.**

## What's conspicuously missing

- **The other four-fifths of "AGI safety from first principles."** Only the intro is here;
  the Superintelligence, Agency, Alignment, and Control sections (the actual argument) are absent.
- **Ngo's own AGI Safety Fundamentals / AI Safety Fundamentals curriculum** — his most
  influential pedagogical artifact, analyzed in the handbook-virtues doc but not in this cluster.
- **The rebuttal side of his debates.** No agent-foundations reply defending "rationality
  realism" (e.g. MIRI's *How intelligible is intelligence?*, or Garrabrant/Demski responses to
  coalitional agency), and no defense of Bayesian epistemology answering him. His arguments are
  present without their best critics.
- **The Burja/Yarvin primary sources** his sociopolitics rests on — Samo Burja's *Great Founder
  Theory* / "social collapse" essays, Yarvin's "cathedral" — needed as context (and as an
  actual non-EA political voice, handled carefully).
- **Ngo on AI persuasion / epistemic disruption and on digital minds / AI welfare** — two
  MacAskill cause areas he has touched only in passing; no canonical piece here.
- **Empirical validation of t-AGI** — METR's task-horizon / time-horizon measurement work,
  which operationalized exactly his framework and would let the forecasting reading be checked
  against data.
- **A market-optimist steelman in its own right**, beyond the two foils — e.g. Robin Hanson's
  *Age of Em* (which Ngo repeatedly cites approvingly) or a Tyler Cowen piece — so the
  efficiency-vs-autonomy debate has a strong opposing anchor, not just Ngo's framing of it.
- **Any woman or non-Western voice.** The entire cluster is Ngo plus male co-travelers
  (Steinhardt, Byrnes, Drexler, Burja, Yarvin). No one from outside that milieu engages his ideas.
