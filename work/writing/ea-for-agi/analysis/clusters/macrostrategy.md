# Cluster map: Macrostrategy

## What this cluster covers

Macrostrategy is the "zoom all the way out" cluster: given that this century may be
the most pivotal in history, what is the shape of the transition, how confident can we
be, and what future are we steering toward? Four sub-threads run through it. (1) **The
big-picture case** — growth history, scope-sensitivity, and the claim that we live at
an unusual, high-leverage moment (Karnofsky's *Most Important Century*, Bostrom's
*Astronomical Waste*, Roodman/Hanson growth models). (2) **Timelines and takeoff** —
how fast and how continuously AGI arrives, and how much warning that leaves (Christiano
on takeoff speeds, AI 2027, expert surveys, bio-anchors). (3) **Crucial considerations
and strategic levers** — differential technological development, lock-in, the
vulnerable-world hypothesis, coordination failures (Moloch), the long reflection. (4)
**Better vs. worse futures** — what a "solved world" is for, existential hope, and the
constructive question MacAskill puts at the center of the course (*Machines of Loving
Grace*, *Letter from Utopia*, *Deep Utopia*). The connective epistemic thread is how
much to trust any of this: forecasting track records, burden of proof, and the
hinge-of-history debate.

## Traditions and voices, and where they disagree

- **Oxford/GPI longtermist philosophy** (Bostrom, Ord, Greaves & MacAskill, Mogensen,
  Tarsney, Thorstad): rigorous, scope-sensitive, argues the future is astronomically
  large and our era possibly decisive. It disagrees *with itself* — MacAskill argues we
  are *not* at the hinge of history; Mogensen and the "time of perils" skeptics
  (Thorstad) push back on both the hinge claim and the standard x-risk math.
- **The Open Phil / Cold Takes empiricists** (Karnofsky, Cotra, Davidson, Roodman):
  translate the philosophy into growth curves, bio-anchors, and nearcasting. More
  hedged than the philosophers, obsessed with their own track record and burden of
  proof.
- **The SF / frontier-lab forecasters** (Aschenbrenner, Kokotajlo/AI 2027, Amodei,
  Altman): short timelines, concrete scenarios, geopolitical urgency, and — in Amodei
  and Altman — an explicit optimistic vision. They read as prophetic to some and
  overconfident to others.
- **The rationalist / intelligence-explosion lineage** (Yudkowsky, Vinge, Chalmers,
  Hanson, Christiano, Alexander): the singularity, fast vs. slow takeoff, and Moloch as
  the master metaphor for value erosion. Hanson–Yudkowsky is the foundational takeoff
  disagreement; Hanson also splits from MacAskill on whether value lock-in is even
  desirable.
- **The skeptics and counterweights** (Narayanan & Kapoor's "AI as normal technology,"
  Tyler Cowen, Epoch's Erdil/Besiroglu on longer timelines, "futurists' track record"):
  argue the transformation is slower, more diffuse, and more forecast-resistant than
  the core cluster assumes.
- **The accelerationists** (Andreessen, e/acc, partly Altman): a foil worldview — more
  technology faster is unambiguously good; x-risk framing is fear. Included as the
  argued-against position, not a peer.

The central live disagreements: **are we at the hinge?** (MacAskill vs. Mogensen/Ord);
**fast or slow takeoff?** (Yudkowsky vs. Hanson/Christiano); **how good can we expect
the default future to be?** (Amodei/Andreessen vs. Bostrom's evolutionary pessimism and
the Moloch lineage); and **how much should we trust any long-range forecast at all?**
(Karnofsky/Narayanan skepticism vs. Aschenbrenner confidence).

## Standout readings

Relative links resolve from this file (`analysis/clusters/`).

1. **[The Most Important Century (roundup)](../../readings/macrostrategy/most-important-century-roundup.md)** — Karnofsky.
   The hub. Best single entry point tying growth, timelines, and stakes together.
   *Role: anchor / course spine — the piece the whole cluster hangs off.*
2. **[This Can't Go On](../../readings/macrostrategy/this-cant-go-on.md)** — Karnofsky.
   The economic-growth argument that current trends are historically bizarre, told with
   charts a newcomer can feel. *Role: accessible on-ramp — open the unit here.*
3. **[All Possible Views About Humanity's Future Are Wild](../../readings/macrostrategy/all-possible-views-are-wild.md)** — Karnofsky.
   Reframes "this sounds crazy" as unavoidable: *every* coherent view is wild.
   *Role: epistemic keystone — trains taking strange-but-important claims seriously.*
4. **[Astronomical Waste](../../readings/macrostrategy/astronomical-waste.md)** — Bostrom.
   The foundational scope-sensitivity essay; the opportunity-cost logic under all
   longtermist macrostrategy. *Role: rigorous foundation — the primary source.*
5. **[Letter from Utopia](../../readings/macrostrategy/letter-from-utopia.md)** — Bostrom.
   A short, lyrical evocation of what a flourishing future could *feel* like.
   *Role: heart voice — "open with awe," pair against the actuarial readings.*
6. **[AI 2027](../../readings/macrostrategy/ai-2027.md)** — Kokotajlo et al.
   The flagship concrete month-by-month scenario to superintelligence.
   *Role: vivid narrative — makes takeoff legible; the artifact students will argue about.*
7. **[Machines of Loving Grace](../../readings/macrostrategy/machines-of-loving-grace.md)** — Amodei.
   A frontier-lab CEO's detailed optimistic vision of the post-AGI world.
   *Role: better-futures target — a concrete "good outcome" to reason toward and critique.*
8. **[Meditations on Moloch](../../readings/macrostrategy/meditations-on-moloch.md)** — Alexander.
   The master essay on competitive dynamics eroding value; substrate for lock-in and
   gradual disempowerment. *Role: conceptual keystone — names the core failure mode.*
9. **[Takeoff Speeds](../../readings/macrostrategy/takeoff-speeds-christiano.md)** — Christiano.
   The slow-vs-fast takeoff case; the single strategic variable that reshapes everything
   downstream. *Role: rigorous deep-dive on the cruxy parameter.*
10. **[The Hinge of History: Reply to MacAskill](../../readings/macrostrategy/hinge-of-history-reply-mogensen.md)** — Mogensen (GPI).
    Contests the course's own author on whether now is uniquely influential.
    *Role: best critic — stages the central debate as live, not settled.*
11. **[The Track Record of Futurists Seems... Fine](../../readings/macrostrategy/futurists-track-record.md)** — Karnofsky.
    Empirical audit of whether long-range forecasting works at all.
    *Role: ship-the-error-bars — the meta-reading that calibrates trust in the cluster.*
12. **[The Vulnerable World Hypothesis](../../readings/macrostrategy/vulnerable-world-hypothesis.pdf)** — Bostrom.
    Technology-lottery framing motivating differential development and governance.
    *Role: strategic-lever foundation — why the order/timing of tech matters.*
13. **[AI as Normal Technology](../../readings/linked/ai-as-normal-technology.md)** — Narayanan & Kapoor.
    The most serious "it'll be slower and more diffuse" counterweight to the whole
    cluster. *Role: staged skeptic — the accepted-adjacent critique, not a strawman.*
14. **[Crucial Considerations and Wise Philanthropy](../../readings/linked/bostrom-crucial-considerations-wise-philanthropy.md)** — Bostrom.
    Defines the macrostrategy *method* itself — the search for considerations that flip
    your sign. *Role: names the discipline — good framing reading to open or close on.*

Runner-ups worth a slot if the unit runs long: [Deep Utopia review](../../readings/macrostrategy/deep-utopia-review-acx.md)
(meaning in a solved world), [How we could stumble into AI catastrophe](../../readings/macrostrategy/stumble-into-ai-catastrophe.md)
(bridges macro to action), [Situational Awareness](../../readings/macrostrategy/situational-awareness.md)
(the confident-forecast foil), [Differential Technological Development](../../readings/linked/differential-technological-development.md),
and [Existential Risk Pessimism and the Time of Perils](../../readings/linked/thorstad-existential-risk-pessimism-time-of-perils.md)
(the rigorous x-risk-math skeptic).

## Conspicuously missing

- **MacAskill's own macrostrategy primaries.** His essay explicitly routes the reader to
  *Preparing for the Intelligence Explosion* (PrepIE, §6) and the *Better Futures*
  series / **viatopia** framing (Forethought, 2025) — neither is in the collection as a
  primary. This is the biggest hole: the course is built on MacAskill, yet his
  constructive "what work makes the transition go well" thesis is represented only by
  Amodei's vision.
- **The canonical x-risk primaries the philosophy assumes.** Bostrom's *Superintelligence*
  (decisive strategic advantage, singleton, takeoff) appears only via reviews/Drexler;
  Ord's *The Precipice* appears only through a *critical review*. The cluster teaches
  the derivatives without the source texts.
- **Ajeya Cotra in her own voice.** Bio-anchors is present only as Karnofsky's summary.
  Cotra's original *Forecasting TAI with Biological Anchors* and her "two-year update"
  are the actual timelines workhorse and are missing.
- **A mainstream-economics skeptic on explosive growth.** Acemoglu's "The Simple
  Macroeconomics of AI" (or similar) is the obvious counterweight to Davidson/Roodman —
  a credentialed economist arguing growth effects are modest. Absent.
- **Richard Ngo's macrostrategy writing.** The brief names Ngo as a distinct lens, yet
  his big-picture pieces (e.g. "clarifying and predicting AGI," "AGI safety from first
  principles," his takeoff/futures writing) sit in other clusters or are absent here.
- **A suffering-focused / s-risk voice on trajectory.** Tomasik/CLR on *why the future
  could be bad by default* exists in the collection but was filed under s-risks; the
  macrostrategy cluster's "better futures" thread is almost entirely upside, missing the
  downside-future counterpart.
- **An outside-the-tent critique of longtermism as ideology.** Beyond Cowen and
  Narayanan/Kapoor, there's no Torres-style or STS/critical-futures piece asking *whose
  future* and interrogating the politics of "the long-term future" — a genre the
  handbook virtues say to include in a core slot.
- **A personal-reflection / grappling essay.** The brief asked for "personal reflections
  grappling with how to deal with AGI." The cluster is all argument and forecast; a
  first-person, human-scale piece (e.g. Carlsmith's *Otherness and control in the age of
  AGI*, or a diary-style reckoning) would supply the missing register.
- **A non-Western / Global-South macrostrategy voice.** China appears only secondhand
  (Ding's *Deciphering China's AI Dream* is cited, not included), and no author outside
  the Anglo-American EA frame writes here about what a good post-AGI future means.
- **Hanson's *Age of Em* / whole-brain-emulation macrostrategy** as a primary — a
  fundamentally different transition scenario, present only as a review.
