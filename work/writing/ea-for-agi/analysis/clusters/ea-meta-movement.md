# Cluster map: EA as a movement (meta)

## What this cluster covers

This is EA thinking about *itself* — its identity, strategy, health, and future as a
movement in the age of AGI. The spine is [MacAskill's anchor essay](../../idea/macaskill-ea-in-the-age-of-agi.md)
and the strategic argument it stages: is EA a **legacy** movement being eaten by AI
safety, should it **return to classic causes**, or should it take a **third way** —
own the mission of making the transition to a post-AGI society go well? Four sub-threads
radiate from that question: (1) the **waves / third-way** framing and its rebuttals;
(2) **institutional strategy** — the primary-source statements of the orgs that steer
EA (CEA, 80k, BlueDot, Kairos, NEST) and their critics; (3) **movement mechanics** —
growth vs. steering vs. cultivation, absorptive capacity, field-building; and (4) the
**critique-and-reckoning** tradition — external hostile critiques, internal reform
proposals, post-FTX disillusionment, and the defenses that answer them.

## Traditions and where they disagree

- **The third-way / "second Enlightenment" camp** (MacAskill, [Koehler](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/koehler-building-ea-for-ai.md),
  building on [West's waves](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/west-third-wave-ea.md)):
  EA's comparative advantage is a flexible, explicitly-moral, truth-seeking culture, and
  the highest-leverage move is pointing it at neglected AGI-preparedness causes beyond
  technical safety. Disagreement *within* the camp is about degree: MacAskill wants
  **cultivation over growth**; Koehler defends EA-building from the 80k seat.
- **The institutional / principles-first camp** ([Robinson/CEA](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/cea-principles-first.md),
  [Stewardship strategy](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/cea-stewardship-strategy.md),
  [Freeman's big-tent](../../readings/linked/big-tent-effective-altruism.md)): EA is
  bigger than any cause; steward the principles and the brand. MacAskill's essay is
  partly a shot *across this bow* — he worries "principles-first" is a cover for
  cause-area inertia and that a post-FTX "PR mentality" is corrosive. [hbesceli's
  disagreements](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/disagreements-cea-stewarding.md) attack
  CEA from the other side.
- **The "just build it / ideas-first" camp** ([NEST](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/nest-building-ideas-first-ea.md),
  [Kairos](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/introducing-kairos.md), [Bentham's Bulldog on
  ambitious projects](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/philanthropy-needs-ambitious-projects.md),
  [Reardon](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/reardon-ea-will-be-great-again.md)): stop
  meta-strategising, spin out focused orgs, do the thing. Reardon's [12 Theses](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/reardon-12-theses-on-ea.md)
  is the manifesto for a big, pluralist, low-demandingness, opportunity-framed EA.
- **The steering / movement-mechanics tradition** (Karnofsky's [rowing-steering-anchoring](../../readings/linked/rowing-steering-anchoring-mutiny.md)
  → [jtm's rowing vs steering](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/rowing-and-steering-ea.md)
  → MacAskill's "cultivation") and the **capacity realists** ([Allardice's absorption
  problem](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/the-absorption-problem.md), [AI-safety field
  growth](../../readings/linked/ai-safety-field-growth-2025.md)): money and recruits are
  not the binding constraint; the ability to *use* them is.
- **[Ngo's sociopolitical third wave](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/third-wave-ai-safety-sociopolitical.md)**:
  a distinct, disagreeable rationalist-adjacent voice arguing that at scale EA/AIS must
  drop hits-based "black-swan farming," add deontology and sociopolitical thinking, and
  reckon with its own power.
- **The critics**, in three registers that disagree with each other as much as with EA:
  - *External-hostile*: [Srinivasan (LRB)](../../readings/linked/srinivasan-stop-the-robot-apocalypse.md)
    and [Wenar (Wired)](../../readings/linked/deaths-of-effective-altruism-wired.md) on
    EA's utilitarian hubris; [Torres](../../readings/linked/torres-longtermism-dangerous-credo.md)
    on longtermism as dangerous credo; [Deaton](../../readings/linked/deaton-response-to-ea.md)
    and [SSIR](../../readings/linked/elitist-philanthropy-of-ea.md) on aid/elitism;
    [deBoer](../../readings/linked/deboer-ea-has-a-novelty-problem.md), [Callard](../../readings/linked/beyond-neutrality.md).
  - *Internal-reform*: [Cremer & Kemp's Democratising Risk](../../readings/linked/democratising-risk.md)
    (and how EA treated its critics), [ConcernedEAs' Doing EA Better](../../readings/linked/doing-ea-better.md),
    [Leo's structural flaws](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/structural-flaws-ea-movement.md),
    [Sempere's unflattering aspects](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/unflattering-aspects-of-ea.md)
    and [why movements fail](../../readings/linked/why-do-social-movements-fail.md).
  - *Defenders*: [Scott Alexander](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/scott-alexander-continued-defense.md),
    [Bentham's Bulldog vs Wenar](../../readings/linked/response-to-leif-wenar-critique.md),
    [Belfield vs Torres](../../readings/linked/response-to-phil-torres-longtermism.md),
    [Labenz's "EA is good, actually"](../../readings/linked/ea-is-good-actually.md).
- **The affective / disillusionment register** ([leaving and returning](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/there-and-back-again-leaving-ea.md),
  [Helen on disillusionment](../../readings/linked/leaning-into-ea-disillusionment.md),
  [EA is bad for mental health](../../readings/linked/ea-is-bad-for-mental-health.md),
  [Reardon on EA-adjacency as FTX trauma](../../readings/linked/reardon-ea-adjacency-ftx-trauma.md)):
  the lived, first-person side of the same strategic questions.
- **Skeptics of the AGI pivot itself** ([JohanEA rethinking the value of AI-safety work](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/rethinking-value-ai-safety.md),
  [Reardon's Sadly, 80k](../../readings/linked/sadly-80k.md) on the 80k pivot,
  [Häberli on preparing EA](../../readings/linked/preparing-ea-for-ai-transformed-world.md)) —
  the loyal opposition to the very move the course is built on.

## Standout readings

The spine of any curriculum built here is MacAskill's essay; these are the pieces that
give it context, opposition, and texture.

- **[West — Third Wave Effective Altruism](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/west-third-wave-ea.md)**
  — The two-table "waves" model MacAskill's whole essay presupposes (his "third way" is
  West's "forefront of weirdness" option). *Role: conceptual scaffold / on-ramp — assign before the anchor.*
- **[Koehler — Why building EA is important for making AI go well](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/koehler-building-ea-for-ai.md)**
  — The companion pro-case, with an explicit alternatives-appendix (rationalists, AIS
  community, School of Moral Ambition) and other people's anonymous dissents attached.
  *Role: rigorous companion to the anchor; models steelmanning.*
- **[Robinson/CEA — Principles-first EA](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/cea-principles-first.md)**
  — The canonical institutional statement of the position MacAskill both praises and
  needles; names the four core principles. *Role: primary source — the establishment view.*
- **[Allardice — The Absorption Problem](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/the-absorption-problem.md)**
  — The best-written piece in the cluster: a two-time scaling CEO on why a historic wave
  of philanthropic capital can't be absorbed effectively, with the voltage-drop and
  scalability-profile framework. *Role: vivid data/evidence narrative — the reality check on "more inputs."*
- **[jtm — Rowing and Steering the EA Movement](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/rowing-and-steering-ea.md)**
  — Supplies the exact vocabulary MacAskill reframes as "cultivation"; argues steering
  (critique, red-teaming) is the neglected margin. *Role: conceptual vocabulary + bridge to the criticism thread.*
- **[Ngo — Third-wave AI safety needs sociopolitical thinking](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/third-wave-ai-safety-sociopolitical.md)**
  — A distinct, deliberately disagreeable voice mapping thinking→mobilization→scale waves
  onto required virtues (decoupling → deontology → sociopolitical realism). *Role: rigorous deep-dive from a non-MacAskill tradition.*
- **[Scott Alexander — In Continued Defense of Effective Altruism](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/scott-alexander-continued-defense.md)**
  — The high-wow-density accomplishments-vs-boardroom-drama defense; the morale/answer to
  the critics. *Role: accessible on-ramp / the affirmative case a newcomer can feel.*
- **[Reardon — 12 Theses on EA](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/reardon-12-theses-on-ea.md)**
  — Compact, quotable provocations for a big, pluralist, opportunity-framed EA; each
  thesis is a discussion prompt. *Role: discussion generator / provocations.*
- **[Sempere — Unflattering aspects of Effective Altruism](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/unflattering-aspects-of-ea.md)**
  — A candid insider catalog of failure modes that performs the truth-seeking it defends.
  *Role: best internal self-critic — the "name your own skulls" reading.*
- **[Cremer & Kemp — Democratising Risk](../../readings/linked/democratising-risk.md)**
  — The most serious internal-reform critique of the x-risk/longtermist framing, plus its
  own saga of how EA treated the critique. *Role: the rigorous critic + case study in movement epistemics.*
- **[Srinivasan — Stop the Robot Apocalypse](../../readings/linked/srinivasan-stop-the-robot-apocalypse.md)**
  — The sharpest external-philosophical critique (LRB review of *Doing Good Better*):
  EA's impartial calculus vs. structural/political change. *Role: best hostile outside voice — the criticism-week pathogen.*
- **[Wenar — The Deaths of Effective Altruism](../../readings/linked/deaths-of-effective-altruism-wired.md)**
  — The canonical post-FTX mainstream reckoning; pairs with the Bentham's-Bulldog rebuttal
  for a referee-it exercise. *Role: post-FTX reckoning / current-events anchor.*
- **[LotteG — There and back again: leaving EA (and returning)](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/there-and-back-again-leaving-ea.md)**
  — The lived "is EA dead or alive?" question from the inside. *Role: vivid personal narrative — the human register of the strategic debate.*

## Conspicuously missing

- **The Forethought primary sources the anchor leans on** — MacAskill & Moorhouse's
  *Preparing for the Intelligence Explosion* and *Better Futures* (esp. "How to make the
  future better"). The essay cites them as *the* substantive case for the new cause menu;
  neither is in the cluster.
- **A direct, strong rebuttal to MacAskill's essay itself.** Disagreement is proxied
  (JohanEA, Sadly-80k, hbesceli) but there's no single essay that takes the third-way
  argument head-on. The forum comment thread on the essay is the obvious quarry.
- **A Global-South / non-Western voice**, and **global-health orgs' own AI-transition
  plans.** The cluster is entirely Anglo-Oxford-Bay. The course brief explicitly wanted
  "organizations in the global health space… on how they plan to adapt to AI" — nothing
  here. No EA-in-India/Africa perspective; [why-not-to-rush-to-translate-EA](../../readings/linked/why-not-to-rush-to-translate-ea.md)
  is the closest, and it argues *against* going global.
- **The accelerationist / tech-right opposition** the essay names (SB1047 backlash, e/acc).
  A primary Andreessen/Beff-Jezos-style text, or a serious right-of-center critique of
  EA-in-the-age-of-AGI, would give the criticism thread a live contemporary antagonist
  rather than only 2015–2024 targets.
- **A rationalist/LessWrong take on the same identity question** — e.g. Habryka/Lightcone
  on EA vs. rationality, or Ryan Greenblatt's shortform on longtermist community-building
  (cited by MacAskill). Only the [Asterisk "rationalist cults"](../../readings/linked/why-so-many-rationalist-cults.md)
  piece represents that world.
- **Empirical EA data** — the Rethink Priorities **EA Survey** (who is actually in EA,
  demographics, growth, homogeneity). The only movement-size data is [Ben Todd's 2021 stock-vs-flow](../../readings/linked/80k-effective-altruism-growing-2021.md), now dated.
- **A School of Moral Ambition primary source** (Rutger Bregman). Koehler names it as the
  main alternative movement to EA for the AGI mission; no primary text represents it.
- **Field-building for a brand-new cause** — how you build a pipeline for AI welfare /
  digital minds from zero (the absorptive-capacity problem MacAskill flags). A Sentience
  Institute / Sebo-style piece on movement strategy for a cause with no ecosystem yet.
- **The constructive-governance half of the critique** — Cremer's actual institutional-reform
  *proposals* (democratization, decentralized funding) in full, not just the diagnosis;
  pairs with [decision-making-and-decentralisation-in-EA](../../readings/linked/decision-making-and-decentralisation-in-ea.md).
- **A movement-studies / historian's outside lens** on EA (beyond journalism) — situating
  it against the Quaker/abolition analogies MacAskill and West both invoke, or a
  sociologist on why moral movements ossify.
