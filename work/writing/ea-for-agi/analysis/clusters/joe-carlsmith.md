# Cluster map: Joe Carlsmith

## What this cluster covers

Almost entirely the work of one author — Joe Carlsmith — plus the interlocutors he
argues with. It spans two registers that rarely share a shelf: (1) the **rigorous,
decomposed analytic case** for AI risk (the Open Philanthropy power-seeking report, the
Scheming AIs report, the "How do we solve the alignment problem?" series, with explicit
probability estimates and taxonomies), and (2) a **literary-contemplative philosophy of
how to relate to a superhuman Other** (the eleven-essay "Otherness and control in the
age of AGI" series, which reaches for Herzog, Le Guin, Basho, Wendell Berry, the Magic:
the Gathering color wheel, and Daoist yin/yang). Bridging them are two further threads:
**AI moral status / welfare** (are the AIs we're building patients we're wronging?) and
**metaethics + epistemic virtue** (what "our values on reflection" even means, and the
habits of sincerity, real thinking, and predictable updating we'll need to navigate the
transition). The through-line of the whole cluster is a single question: how should a
civilization that mistrusts both Nature and raw intelligence seek control over the future
*without* becoming the very thing it fears.

## Intellectual traditions and where they disagree

- **Carlsmith himself** is the hinge. He is a trained analytic philosopher writing
  Open-Phil-grade reports, but his native idiom is confessional and literary. He takes
  the doomer case seriously *and* subjects its psychology to sustained ethical scrutiny.
  Most of the "disagreement" in the cluster is staged inside his own head.
- **Yudkowsky / rationalism** — the "deep atheism" pole: fundamental mistrust of Nature
  and bare intelligence, fragility of value, "the tails come apart," CEV, Fun Theory,
  Soares' "niceness is unnatural." Carlsmith is in deep, admiring, but critical dialogue
  with this lineage — he thinks its logic drives toward an ever-more controlling ("yang")
  relationship to the universe that should make us uneasy.
- **Robin Hanson** — the successionist/cosmist counter-pole: AI-risk folks "other" the
  AIs, the future is allowed to not look like us, control-seeking is the real vice.
  Represented directly (reasonable-ai-fears, mind-children) and mediated through
  Carlsmith's "Does AI risk 'other' the AIs?" This is the cluster's sharpest live axis:
  *how alien will AI values be, and how much control is wisdom vs. tyranny.*
- **The "green" / conservationist tradition** — C.S. Lewis (*Abolition of Man*, the
  "conditioners" who shape all future value), Le Guin, Aldo Leopold's land ethic. Carlsmith
  mines these for the values-lock-in worry and for an ethic of attunement-to-the-given as
  an alternative to domination.
- **The optimists / "alignment is easy"** — Pope's value-misspecification piece, Nintil's
  cozy futurism. The thin representation of the pole that says the pessimists are wrong.
- **Metaethics: subjectivism vs. realism** — Carlsmith's normative-realism-bot dialogues,
  "against the normative realist's wager," subjectivism and moral authority, alienation.
  He mostly argues *against* a realism that would let us offload the question to the
  universe — which is exactly what makes idealized values underdetermined and the
  becoming-wiser problem hard.
- **Multipolar/structural pessimism** — Critch on multipolar failure, adjacent to
  gradual-disempowerment framings, a different failure geometry than the single-agent
  takeover story.

## Conspicuously missing

- **A serious external critique of Carlsmith's own power-seeking case.** His report
  spawned a whole red-teaming literature (Open Phil AI Worldviews Contest winners; David
  Thorstad's "Existential risk pessimism" / "Mistakes in the moment of doom" series;
  Nuño Sempere's estimate critique). The cluster platforms *his* voice but never a
  rigorous rebuttal of it — violating the handbook's "name your best critic" virtue.
- **The AI-welfare cause area beyond Carlsmith.** The Eleos "Taking AI Welfare Seriously"
  report (Long, Sebo, et al.) is cited by him but absent; so are Jeff Sebo's own work,
  Bostrom & Shulman "Propositions concerning digital minds and society," and Carl
  Shulman's Dwarkesh interviews on digital minds. The cause area rests on one author.
- **The consciousness-science head-voice on moral status.** Carlsmith is a philosopher;
  no empirical/scientific counterpart. Missing: David Chalmers "Could a Large Language
  Model Be Conscious?", Anil Seth on why biology may matter, the Butlin & Long "indicator
  properties" paper. Needed as head/heart pairing on patienthood.
- **Successionism stated by its own proponents.** Hanson is here, but the frank "let the
  AIs inherit the future" view is not — Rich Sutton's "AI Succession," Larry Page-style
  digital-god cosmism, or an e/acc primary text. The cluster critiques successionism more
  than it lets it speak.
- **The fuller "alignment is easy" pole.** Only Pope's one piece. Missing Belrose & Pope
  "AI is easy to control," or Quintin Pope's "My objections to 'We're all gonna die.'"
  The optimist counterweight is under-represented.
- **The canonical gradual-disempowerment text.** Critch's multipolar-failure post stands
  in, but Kulveit et al. "Gradual Disempowerment" (2025) is the reference piece and is
  missing — it directly serves the democracy-preservation and gradual-disempowerment
  cause areas in MacAskill's menu.
- **Primary sources for the contemplative traditions Carlsmith borrows.** He runs on
  Daoism (yin/yang), Buddhism, and deep ecology by allusion. A short primary text — a
  Zhuangzi/Daodejing excerpt, or Arne Naess on deep ecology — would ground "green" and
  "attunement" in a non-Western voice rather than a Westerner's gloss.
- **A metaethical-realist reply.** Carlsmith argues *against* the normative realist's
  wager; a defender (Parfit's *On What Matters*, David Enoch's *Taking Morality Seriously*)
  would make the debate two-sided instead of a monologue.
- **Gender and cultural diversity.** Le Guin is the lone woman in a cluster of ~a dozen
  men. Ajeya Cotra on AI takeover, Katja Grace on the case against, or Amanda Askell on
  AI character would broaden it.
- **AI character as *practiced*, not just theorized.** The cluster theorizes the AI-as-
  Other beautifully but has no practitioner text — Amanda Askell / Anthropic on Claude's
  character and constitution, or an OpenAI model-spec excerpt, would show the theory
  meeting a shipped system.
- **A dedicated s-risk reading.** Deep atheism and wild-animal-suffering threads circle
  suffering-risk, but nothing from CLR / Tobias Baumann / Magnus Vinding states the
  s-risk case directly — a gap given MacAskill's explicit s-risk cause area.
