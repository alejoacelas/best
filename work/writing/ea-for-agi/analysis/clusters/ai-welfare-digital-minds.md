# Cluster map: AI welfare & digital minds

## What this cluster covers

The moral status of the minds we are building: whether present or near-future AI
systems (and, further out, whole-brain emulations and other "digital people") could be
conscious, sentient, or robustly agentic enough to matter morally *for their own
sake* — and what we owe them if they do. Four main sub-threads run through it: (1) the
**science-and-philosophy question** — could a computation be conscious, and how would
we ever tell; (2) the **decision-under-uncertainty question** — how to act when we
can't resolve (1), given symmetric-looking risks of over- and under-attribution; (3)
the **empirical/practitioner question** — what today's models actually do (introspection
probes, pleasure/pain trade-offs, welfare assessments, exit rights); and (4) the
**macrostrategy/futures question** — the scale, political economy, and s-risk of a
world eventually shared with vast numbers of digital minds. A social-science strand
(what the public will believe, how rights fights will divide us) and an animal-welfare
strand (the field's chosen analogy and methodological parent) thread across all four.

## Intellectual traditions and where they disagree

- **The Eleos / NYU-CMEP mainstream** (Rob Long, Jeff Sebo, Patrick Butlin, Kyle Fish,
  Kathleen Finlinson). The field's center of gravity: *precautionary, probabilistic,
  indicator-based*. Don't demand certainty; look for convergent marker evidence; hedge.
  Sources: *Taking AI Welfare Seriously*, *Consciousness in AI*, the Quickstart Guide,
  Sebo's moral-circle work.
- **Philosophy of mind / consciousness science.** The theory floor. Chalmers
  (functionalism-friendly, "multimodal LLMs could be candidates in ~10 years"); Nagel
  and Searle as the foundational bat/Chinese-Room touchstones; Ned Block and the
  competing neuroscientific theories (GWT, HOT, IIT, recurrent processing).
- **The skeptics** — two distinct kinds, often conflated. *Metaphysical* skeptics say
  computation can't suffice: **Anil Seth's biological naturalism**, Searle, the Nature
  *Humanities & Social Sciences* "no such thing as conscious AI." *Pragmatic* skeptics
  grant it's possible but say building or believing in it is the danger:
  **Mustafa Suleyman** ("seemingly conscious AI is coming" and it's a societal hazard),
  Bengio & Elmoznino ("illusions of AI consciousness"). The field's own leaders police
  a third failure mode — sloppy debunking (Long's "how not to debunk").
- **Macrostrategy / futures** (Bostrom & Shulman, Karnofsky, Hanson, Bradford Saad).
  Less "is it conscious?" than "how big a deal, and what does a shared world look
  like?" — super-beneficiaries, digital-mind takeoff speeds, copyability, the most-
  important-century framing. This is the bridge to longtermism and s-risks.
- **Empirical / mechanistic practitioners** (Anthropic's welfare program, AE Studio /
  Cameron Berg, Google's Keeling & Street). Measure the models: introspection injection,
  pleasure/pain games, utility-structure probes, self-referential-processing reports.
- **Social science & forecasting** (Lucius Caviola, Sentience Institute). What will
  *people* believe, when will digital minds arrive, how will rights claims fracture
  politics. Data, surveys, expert forecasts.
- **Rights / political economy** (Schwitzgebel & Garza, Goldstein & Salib, Bales,
  Caviola). Legal personhood, "against willing servitude," and the warning that AI
  rights will divide us — possibly to the point of conflict.
- **Rationalist community** (Yudkowsky's *mindcrime*, Zvi, LessWrong). S-risk-inflected,
  attentive to model-weight preservation and "going Nova" persona episodes.
- **Fiction / narrative** (qntm's *Lena*, Ted Chiang, Black Mirror). The vivid on-ramps.

**The live disagreements.** (a) *Substrate*: computational functionalism (most of the
field) vs biological naturalism (Seth, Searle) — the load-bearing crux. (b) *Route to
status*: consciousness required, vs **agency alone might suffice** (Long, Shulman,
Delon). (c) *Are present LLMs candidates?*: Berg says 25–35%; Butlin et al. say "no
current system, but no technical barrier"; Chalmers says not yet. (d) *The asymmetry*:
Eleos insists we *can't* simply "err on the side of caution" because over-attribution
carries real safety costs, while Berg argues the stakes are asymmetric toward
under-attribution (industrial-scale suffering). (e) *Should we build it at all?* — a
"don't" camp (Seth, and off-collection Metzinger) barely represented here. (f)
*Safety vs welfare*: complementary or in tension.

## Standout readings

- [long-we-should-take-ai-welfare-seriously.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/long-we-should-take-ai-welfare-seriously.md)
  — **anchor / accessible on-ramp.** Long's own plain-English summary of the landmark
  report; introduces moral patient / welfare subject, the two routes (consciousness &
  agency), and the "dangers on both sides — there is no cautious side" framing that
  organizes the whole cluster.
- [taking-ai-welfare-seriously.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/taking-ai-welfare-seriously.md)
  (+ [full PDF](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/taking-ai-welfare-seriously-fulltext.pdf.pdf))
  — **rigorous anchor.** The Long/Sebo/Butlin/Chalmers et al. report the field orbits;
  the Acknowledge/Assess/Prepare recommendations. The citable floor beneath the summary.
- [digital-minds-quickstart-guide.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/digital-minds-quickstart-guide.md)
  — **field map / meta-resource.** Parrack & Los's choose-your-own-adventure syllabus:
  every sub-question, the key thinkers, orgs, fellowships, a strong glossary, and a
  fiction/media list. A curriculum could mine this for its entire optional layer.
- [ai-frontiers-evidence-for-ai-consciousness.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/ai-frontiers-evidence-for-ai-consciousness.md)
  — **best empirical synthesis + provocation.** Berg marshals the 2024–25 evidence
  (bliss-attractor, introspection injection, pleasure/pain games) into a convergence
  argument, puts a number on it (25–35%), and makes the asymmetric-stakes case. Vivid,
  current, and takes a stronger line than the Eleos consensus — good for debate.
- [consciousness-in-ai-butlin-long.pdf.pdf](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/consciousness-in-ai-butlin-long.pdf.pdf)
  — **rigorous deep-dive.** The 14 theory-derived "indicator properties" method; the
  intellectual engine behind the whole marker-based approach. The rigor anchor for the
  science thread.
- [long-common-mistakes-ai-consciousness.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/long-common-mistakes-ai-consciousness.md)
  — **discussion primer.** Five short, sharp corrections (don't take behavior at face
  value; avoid over- *and* under-confidence; "is AI conscious?" is as ill-posed as "are
  organisms conscious?"). Ideal to assign *before* a seminar to raise the floor.
- [no-such-thing-conscious-ai-skeptic.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/no-such-thing-conscious-ai-skeptic.md)
  — **best critic (metaphysical).** Peer-reviewed argument that genuine AI consciousness
  is impossible. Essential to keep the debate honest; pair with Seth's biological
  naturalism ([linked](../../readings/linked/seth-conscious-ai-biological-naturalism.md)).
- [sebo-widen-moral-circle-oped.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/sebo-widen-moral-circle-oped.md)
  — **values on-ramp.** Short, accessible review of Sebo's *The Moral Circle* that ties
  digital minds back to the EA moral-circle-expansion tradition (Singer → animals →
  insects → AI). The natural bridge from the animal-welfare week.
- [karnofsky-digital-people-bigger-deal.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/karnofsky-digital-people-bigger-deal.md)
  — **macrostrategy / scale, vivid.** The classic on "digital people": copyability,
  speed, and why a world of them is more transformative than almost anything. Connects
  the cluster to most-important-century longtermism.
- [summary-sharing-world-with-digital-minds.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/summary-sharing-world-with-digital-minds.md)
  — **political-economy depth.** Readable digest of Shulman & Bostrom on
  super-beneficiaries, status, and the distributive nightmare of sharing a world with
  minds that can be vastly happier and more numerous than us.
- [anthropic-claude-end-conversations.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/anthropic-claude-end-conversations.md)
  — **concrete intervention / acting under uncertainty.** A real, shipped, low-cost
  welfare measure (Claude can exit abusive chats) grounded in a model's observed
  aversion to harm. The best "what does this actually look like in practice?" case.
- [digital-minds-2025-year-in-review.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/digital-minds-2025-year-in-review.md)
  — **state-of-the-field orientation.** Caviola's newsletter round-up: who's doing what,
  which debates moved, what shipped. Best single "you are here" reading (accept it will
  date fast).
- [highlights-futures-digital-minds-forecasts.md](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/highlights-futures-digital-minds-forecasts.md)
  — **data / evidence.** Highlights of the 67-expert forecast: most give ≥50% odds of
  digital minds by 2050, and top forecasters see welfare capacity matching a billion
  humans within five years of the first one. The scale claim, sourced.
- [qntm-lena-mmacevedo.md](../../readings/linked/qntm-lena-mmacevedo.md)
  — **vivid narrative.** The canonical short story of an exploited brain emulation
  (*MMAcevedo*), written as a Wikipedia article. Does the emotional work no argument can;
  a perfect discomfort-inducing opener or closer.

## Conspicuously missing

- **Joe Carlsmith, "The Stakes of AI Moral Status" (2025).** Cited across the collection
  as *the* accessible intuition-builder for over/under-attribution, yet not curated as a
  file. High priority to fetch (essay + his talk video/transcript).
- **David Chalmers, "Could a Large Language Model Be Conscious?" (2023).** The single
  most-cited philosophy piece in the whole cluster, present only as a link. Belongs in
  core, not the linked pile.
- **Anil Seth's primary biological-naturalism argument** (his *Behavioral & Brain
  Sciences* target article). Only a linked stub + podcast; the leading scientific
  skeptic deserves his own text promoted alongside the Nature skeptic paper.
- **Thomas Metzinger's synthetic-phenomenology moratorium.** The strongest "we should
  *not build* potentially-suffering AI, and should ban it until ~2050" position is
  essentially absent. Search: Metzinger, "Artificial Suffering" / the moratorium proposal.
- **Non-Western and non-analytic perspectives — entirely absent.** No Buddhist
  philosophy of mind on machine sentience; no Japanese/Shinto animist framing of robot
  personhood (e.g., Masahiro Mori's "The Buddha in the Robot," and the uncanny-valley
  lineage); no Ubuntu/African relational-personhood account. The whole cluster is
  Anglo-American analytic.
- **Susan Schneider's ACT (AI Consciousness Test) and *Artificial You*.** Named in the
  reading list but no primary text; a concrete proposed *test* is a useful contrast to
  the marker/indicator approach.
- **A serious in-EA critic arguing AI-welfare work is net-negative** for AI safety or a
  distraction — the tension is *named* everywhere (Eleos itself worries about it) but the
  strongest adversarial version isn't platformed. Search the safety-vs-welfare debate for
  a piece that actually argues *against* the field's existence.
- **Legal scholarship on AI personhood** beyond Goldstein & Salib — e.g., Lawrence
  Solum's classic "legal personhood for artificial intelligences," and the EU
  robot-rights / electronic-personhood policy debate. The law-review voice is thin.
- **The individuation problem** as a curated primary text. Flagged as foundational in the
  Quickstart (who is the welfare subject — the weights, the instance, the character?) but
  represented only by links (Chalmers, "What do we talk to when we talk to language
  models?"; Schwitzgebel & Nelson on counting subjects). A genuinely novel, AI-specific
  puzzle with no anchor reading.
- **Whole-brain-emulation / "ems" as its own thread.** Sandberg-Bostrom roadmap and
  Hanson's *Age of Em* live only in links; the em sub-species (distinct from LLMs) is
  under-anchored despite being half the "digital minds" definition.
- **Lab-diversity beyond Anthropic.** The empirical/practitioner voice is an Anthropic
  monoculture (Fish, introspection, exit rights). Need Google DeepMind's stance, OpenAI's
  (non-)position, and a skeptical-lab voice — Suleyman's Microsoft essay
  ([linked](../../readings/linked/suleyman-seemingly-conscious-ai-is-coming.md)) is the
  closest and should be promoted.
- **The parasocial / companion-app human story.** Real people already treat Replika /
  Character.AI companions as conscious. Rosie Campbell's "AI Bambi Effect" is
  [linked](../../readings/linked/ai-bambi-effect.md), but reported narrative journalism on
  users who believe (and grieve) is missing — the over-attribution risk made human.
- **The animal-consciousness-history analogy as a standalone.** How scientific consensus
  on non-human minds actually formed (the Cambridge Declaration story) is the field's own
  chosen precedent but only a linked stub; a piece drawing the parallel explicitly would
  anchor the "lessons from animals" sub-thread.
- **Digital suffering / s-risk crossover.** Tomasik's "Risks of Astronomical Future
  Suffering" and Saad & Bradley's digital-suffering work are links; a curated anchor
  connecting AI welfare to s-risks (the astronomical-downside case) is missing from core.
