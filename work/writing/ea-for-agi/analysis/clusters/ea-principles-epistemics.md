# Cluster map: EA principles & epistemics

## What this cluster covers

The values-and-thinking-tools layer that the Handbook puts *before* any cause area — and
that MacAskill wants to be the backbone of the AGI-era course ("the values and epistemic
habits we'll need to navigate a very rapidly changing world"). It runs from EA's
canonical principles (scope sensitivity, impartiality, scout mindset, tradeoffs) down
through the moral-philosophy foundation (utilitarianism, moral uncertainty, the moral-saint
objection) and up into the *practice* of thinking well: truth-seeking, calibration,
deep investigation, deference vs. first-principles, decoupling, and integrity for people
who take consequences seriously. Its live edge — the part unique to this course — is
**epistemics under AGI**: how to hold timelines, whether to defer, and how AI itself could
uplift or wreck our collective ability to track truth.

Main sub-threads:
1. **Core principles / "what EA is"** — the question-not-ideology framing and the CEA scaffold.
2. **Moral foundation & its discontents** — utilitarianism, scope sensitivity, and the
   moral-saint / demandingness pushback.
3. **Truth-seeking as the ground** — scout mindset, saying oops, epistemic legibility, calibration.
4. **Naive → sophisticated consequentialism** — integrity, prudence, virtues, half-assing it.
5. **How to actually think** — minimal-trust investigations, learning by writing, reasoning transparency.
6. **Deference, modesty, decoupling** — when to trust experts, when to think for yourself, and the cognitive style that lets you reason about weird propositions.
7. **Epistemics under AGI** — broad timelines, AI-for-epistemics, why-have-opinions-on-timelines.

## Traditions and voices, and where they disagree

- **LessWrong rationalism** (Yudkowsky, Nate Soares, Scott Alexander). Virtue-list
  rationality, "shut up and multiply," change your mind fast. Internally split: Yudkowsky's
  *Twelve Virtues* / *Saying Oops* push taking-ideas-seriously to the hilt, while
  Alexander's *Epistemic Learned Helplessness* is a direct rebuttal — for 99% of people,
  99% of the time, being argued into weird conclusions is a *failure* mode. This is the
  cluster's sharpest live disagreement.
- **EA-institutional** (CEA, GiveWell/Holden Karnofsky, 80k, Helen Toner). Principles over
  conclusions; deep empirical investigation; reasoning transparency. Karnofsky's
  minimal-trust / learning-by-writing / useful-vices trilogy is the practical spine.
- **Academic utilitarian** (Chappell, Schubert & Caviola, utilitarianism.net). Defends a
  *prudent, multi-level* consequentialism that converges with common-sense virtue —
  explicitly built to answer the naive-utilitarian caricature.
- **The internal critics** (Elizabeth Van Nostrand, Ben Kuhn, Linch). Truth-seeking turned
  on EA itself: advocacy corrupts epistemics, EA "pretends to actually try," motivated
  reasoning is endemic. Elizabeth vs. the rationalist mainstream on the *drowning-child
  parable* (she hates it as anti-epistemic emotional manipulation) is a nice fault line.
- **The philosophical opposition** (Susan Wolf). *Moral Saints* is the one voice arguing the
  maximizing life may not be admirable at all — the counterweight the whole cluster leans against.
- **The AGI-macrostrategy voice** (Toby Ord, Forethought, and newer forum writers). Epistemic
  humility applied to timelines; epistemics as a *cause area* now that AI can reshape it.
- **The decoupling meta-voice** (John Nerst, Sarah Constantin). Names the cognitive style
  the rest of the cluster runs on — and where it clashes with contextualizing thinkers.
- **A personal / frontier-virtue voice** (Alejo's own *Please Forgive*). Extreme honesty as
  a lived, costly virtue — the seed of the course's "frontier virtues" angle.

## Standout readings

- [cea-core-principles.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/cea-core-principles.md) —
  CEA's own list: scope sensitivity, impartiality, scout mindset, tradeoffs, plus the
  secondary tools (EV, ITN, crucial considerations). **Role: the anchor scaffold** — the
  one-page map every other reading hangs off; frames EA as principles you'd keep even as
  conclusions change.
- [ea-is-a-question-not-an-ideology.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/ea-is-a-question-not-an-ideology.md) —
  Helen Toner: EA *asks* "how can I do the most good?" rather than *answering* it. **Role:
  accessible on-ramp** and the framing that licenses the whole course's openness; short,
  quotable, non-threatening. (Toner's later CSET/AI-governance stature is a bonus hook.)
- [on-caring.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/on-caring.md) — Nate Soares: your
  care-o-meter maxes out at ~150 people, so switch to manual control and multiply. **Role:
  the head-and-heart scope-sensitivity primer** — vivid, story-driven, does emotionally what
  the CEA "scope sensitivity" bullet does dryly. Pairs with any actuarial reading.
- [scout-mindset-review.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/scout-mindset-review.md) —
  Scott Alexander on Galef: soldier vs. scout, the calibration and counterfactual tests.
  **Role: the truth-seeking on-ramp** — EA's top recruiting text, teaches concrete moves
  (status-quo test, selective-skeptic test), warm and funny.
- [truthseeking-is-the-ground.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/truthseeking-is-the-ground.md) —
  Elizabeth: contact with reality is the meta-principle under all others; 20+ live examples
  put skin in the game. **Role: rigorous deep-dive on epistemic *culture*** — the mature
  insider's version, including how advocacy and deference corrode it.
- [virtues-for-real-world-utilitarians.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/virtues-for-real-world-utilitarians.md) —
  Schubert & Caviola: six virtues (moderate altruism, moral expansiveness, effectiveness-focus,
  truth-seeking, collaborativeness, determination). **Role: the baseline-virtues framework** —
  the systematic backbone Alejo explicitly wants to build *past* toward frontier virtues.
- [naive-vs-prudent-utilitarianism.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/naive-vs-prudent-utilitarianism.md) —
  Chappell: the master archer adjusts for wind; prudent utilitarians treat rights as
  near-unthinkable-to-violate. **Role: the sophistication move** — inoculates against the
  ends-justify-means caricature; the pivot from naive to real-world consequentialism.
- [integrity-for-consequentialists.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/integrity-for-consequentialists.md) —
  Christiano: act as if choosing an action makes everyone know you're the kind of person who
  chooses it. **Role: the frontier-virtue rigor anchor** — a consequentialist definition of
  integrity you can actually keep; directly serves the course's integrity/honesty thread.
- [minimal-trust-investigations.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/minimal-trust-investigations.md) —
  Karnofsky: occasionally check one claim end-to-end yourself (the LLIN worked example).
  **Role: the how-to-think practicum** — turns "truth-seeking" from a value into a repeatable
  discipline; pairs with *Learning By Writing* as the exercise engine.
- [importance-of-saying-oops.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/importance-of-saying-oops.md) —
  Yudkowsky: swallow the whole bitter pill at once instead of conceding inches. **Role: short
  wow-density piece** — ~600 words, one Enron image, the emotional core of changing your mind fast.
- [epistemic-learned-helplessness.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/epistemic-learned-helplessness.md) —
  Scott Alexander: don't be argued into weird conclusions you can't evaluate. **Role: the
  best in-house counterweight** — the planted rebuttal to the cluster's own "take ideas
  seriously" reflex; essential before asking newcomers to swallow AGI arguments.
- [broad-timelines.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/broad-timelines.md) — Toby Ord:
  hold a broad distribution over AI timelines and plan wisely under deep uncertainty. **Role:
  the AGI-native rigor anchor** — models the exact epistemic stance the course wants, on the
  course's defining question, from a founder-level voice.
- [ai-for-epistemics-good-bad-ugly.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/ai-for-epistemics-good-bad-ugly.md) —
  Forethought: AI could uplift, degrade, or be weaponized against our collective sense-making.
  **Role: epistemics-as-a-cause-area framing** — bridges "personal epistemic habits" to
  "civilizational epistemic security," a genuinely new-to-2020s slot the old Handbook lacks.
- [please-forgive.md](../../readings/ea-principles-epistemics/please-forgive.md) — Alejo:
  extreme honesty as a lived, costly, half-confused virtue. **Role: the vivid personal /
  frontier-virtue seed** — the register the course wants for "someone grappling with how to
  live these values," and a native answer to Wolf's moral-saint worry.

## Conspicuously missing

- **Richard Ngo's distinctive epistemics.** The brief names Ngo as a whole way of seeing;
  the cluster has only his linked *scope-sensitive ethics* post. Search his writing on
  reasoning under uncertainty / "being a sensible person" / his AGI-strategy epistemic
  posts — a signature voice is under-represented.
- **MacAskill in his own voice on epistemic habits.** The course is built on his essay, yet
  his epistemics (Cause X / moral progress, crucial considerations, the "most influential
  century" self-skepticism) sit only in the linked pile. A core MacAskill reading on
  *staying open to unknown priorities* belongs here.
- **A serious hostile *outside* critic.** Every critique here is in-house (Kuhn, Linch,
  Elizabeth). Missing the academic/left critique of the whole EA-epistemics style — Amia
  Srinivasan's LRB review of MacAskill, Crary/Adams/Gruen's *The Good It Promises*, or an
  Emile Torres piece. The Handbook's "hostile voices in core slots" virtue is unmet.
- **A non-Western / Global-South voice.** The cluster is entirely Anglo-American analytic
  rationalism. No care-ethics, Ubuntu, Buddhist, or Confucian angle on doing good; no voice
  from the communities being *helped*. A concrete gap: a non-Western philosopher or a
  practitioner from a recipient country on impartiality and effectiveness.
- **The FTX epistemic post-mortem.** Alejo's own essay riffs on SBF; the cluster gestures at
  advocacy/ratchet failures but has no direct reckoning with how EA's naive-maximization
  epistemics failed around FTX. Search for the best forum retrospective (e.g. on
  "naive utilitarianism and FTX," or Ozzie Gooen / Elizabeth-style post-mortems).
- **Care ethics / the integrity objection as primary text.** Wolf is present, but Bernard
  Williams' integrity objection and Railton's "Alienation, Consequentialism, and the Demands
  of Morality" — the canonical anti-maximizing-alienation arguments the *prudent utilitarian*
  readings are answering — aren't in as readings.
- **Moral uncertainty as a *decision procedure* for AGI-era novelties.** The parliamentary /
  MEC approach (MacAskill-Ord-Bykvist; Greaves & Cotton-Barratt) is only linked. Given the
  course's digital-minds and AI-welfare causes, a core "how to act when you don't know the
  right moral theory" reading is warranted.
- **Concrete AI-epistemic-threat pieces.** The Forethought map is high-altitude; the course
  menu names "AI-driven persuasion and epistemic disruption," but there's no ground-level
  reading on chatbot sycophancy warping beliefs, AI persuasion experiments, deepfakes, or
  epistemic security. Search for a vivid specific case.
- **Forecasting/calibration as a practiced skill, updated for AI.** Only the linked
  *Ten Commandments* summary and a superforecasting mention. Missing a hands-on calibration
  reading and something on AI-assisted forecasting (Metaculus/OpenPhil work).
- **The empirical psychology of (in)effective altruism as a reading.** Caviola & Greene's
  research is cited throughout but never assigned. A standalone "why humans are bad at this,
  per the data" piece would ground the virtues in evidence rather than assertion.
- **Sequence vs. cluster thinking / against-quantification, promoted to core.** The strongest
  steelman of *not* running the naive EV calculation (Karnofsky's cluster thinking, GiveWell's
  "why we can't take EV estimates literally," the Optimizer's Curse) lives only in the linked
  pile despite being central to the naive-vs-sophisticated thread.
