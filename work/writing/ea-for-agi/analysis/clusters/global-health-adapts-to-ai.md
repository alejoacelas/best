# Cluster map: global health adapts to AI

## What this cluster covers

How the EA-adjacent global-health-and-development (GH&D) world is reacting to transformative
AI — both as a **tool** to deploy (AI diagnostics, tutoring, weather forecasting, and
research-workflow automation for the global poor) and as a **force that reshapes the terrain**
GH&D operates on (explosive-growth economics, labor displacement in LMICs, a shifting
biomedical R&D pipeline, and the collapse of "Big Aid"). Four sub-threads run through it:
(1) the **"AI for Good" deployment agenda** and its boosters — Gates, the World Bank, CGD,
Evidence Action, the AI Access Initiative; (2) the **macro / growth-and-inequality** question
of whether AI widens or closes the rich-poor gap and erases the development ladder; (3) a
**field-power critique** worrying that AI narrows global health the way RCTs did and rebuilds
colonial power asymmetries "backed not by empire, but by algorithms"; and (4) a
**progress-studies / abundance turn** arguing EA's measurement culture underweights the
systems and regulatory bottlenecks that stall life-saving vaccines. Underneath sits a base
layer of canonical GH&D readings (bednets, deworming, graduation, cash) that the AI arguments
now recontextualize.

## Intellectual traditions and where they disagree

- **GiveWell / randomista rigor** (GiveWell 2026 search, red-teaming, Banerjee graduation,
  Kremer, worm wars). Measurement-first, direct-intervention, incrementalist. Treats AI first
  as a *workflow* tool and a *new emerging cause area to vet*, not a paradigm shift.
- **Progress studies / abundance** (Olvera, Trefethen, Elton's FDA list, Statecraft, ProPublica
  TB, Gavi's 35-year vaccine). Argues the measurement culture has "systematic blind spots" —
  it scales bednets but ignores the regulatory and market-incentive failures that make
  poor-country vaccines *impossible*. Explicitly positions itself as EA's complement/critic.
- **Development-sector & decolonial critique** (Senanayake, Starr's "Big Aid Is Over", Nexleaf's
  "Scale That Lasts"). Power, ownership, wicked problems, country ownership over tools. The
  sharpest internal dissent: AI is "the new RCT," a powerful method that narrows ambition and
  crowns a new tech elite.
- **Growth economists** (Chad Jones, Klinova & Korinek, Brynjolfsson). Model AI's effect on
  long-run income and labor; split on whether LMICs leapfrog or get left behind.
- **Big-philanthropy / multilateral institutional voice** (Gates/Suzman, World Bank, CGD's AI
  initiative, the LIC "catch-up" academic paper). Access-and-equity framing, co-design,
  digital-infrastructure gaps. Optimistic-but-cautious; the establishment register.
- **EA-funder strategic** (Coefficient/Open Phil GHW-in-era-of-TAI RFP). The flagship attempt to
  *institutionalize* the question: what should GHW funders do now that AI might compress the
  21st century or shrink LMIC tax bases?

The live disagreements: **deploy-now optimism** (Gates, World Bank, Evidence Action) vs.
**power-critique skepticism** (Senanayake: AI is poorly suited to trust-and-legitimacy
problems). **Widen vs. close the gap** (the CGD pieces themselves split; Klinova stresses
displacement). **Direct health vs. systems/growth** (the reopened EA "global health vs.
development" question — does AI-driven growth make direct interventions look small?). And a
meta-fight over whether EA's **measurement discipline** is its great strength or its blind spot.

## Standout readings

- [Coefficient/Open Phil RFP: GHW in an Era of Transformative AI](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/openphil-ghw-transformative-ai-rfp.md)
  — The anchor. The flagship EA funder's own statement of how global-health philanthropy adapts
  to TAI: "compressed 21st century" biomedicine, R&D bottlenecks shifting to regulation and
  distribution, the development ladder losing a rung. **Role: anchor / framing text for the whole session.**
- [Algorithms and Authority: How AI Is Reshaping Power in Global Health (Senanayake)](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/algorithms-and-authority-power-global-health.md)
  — The best critic, and from a Global-South nonprofit leader who *uses* RCTs and AI. "Is AI
  the new RCT?" AI narrows which problems are visible and fundable, and rebuilds power
  asymmetries. **Role: the named best critic / counterpoint the curriculum argues against.**
- [Why EA and Global Health Needs More Progress Studies and Abundance (Olvera)](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/ea-global-health-progress-studies-abundance.md)
  — The accessible bridge: personal, concrete (the 35-year malaria vaccine, GSK's shelved TB
  vaccine), reframes EA's measurement blind spot. "Why can't I be both?" **Role: accessible
  on-ramp that reframes the cause and opens the systems-vs-direct axis.**
- [AI Red Teaming at GiveWell (Phillips)](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/givewell-ai-red-teaming.md)
  — A rigorous org putting AI into its own cost-effectiveness workflow and reporting the
  unglamorous truth (~85% of AI critiques useless, quantitative estimates unreliable). Models
  the epistemics the course preaches. **Role: concrete case study of honest adoption; ships its error bars.**
- [How We're Searching for the Best Ways to Help in 2026 (GiveWell)](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/givewell-searching-best-ways-2026.md)
  — The establishment baseline: the flagship org's live 2026 agenda, with "AI applications to
  global health" listed as one emerging area among malaria, nutrition, water. Shows how modestly
  the incrementalist tradition folds AI in. **Role: status-quo baseline the other readings push against.**
- [The First Principles Guiding Our Work with AI (Gates/Suzman)](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/gates-ai-development-principles.md)
  — The largest GH&D funder's institutional stance: access, equity, co-design, "beneficiaries
  participating in development." **Role: the establishment-philanthropy voice; pairs against Senanayake's power critique.**
- [Tipping the Scales: AI's Dual Impact on Developing Nations (World Bank)](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/worldbank-ai-dual-impact-developing-nations.md)
  — Accessible macro on-ramp with vivid stats (45 health workers per 10,000 recommended; poor
  countries have a quarter of that; ChatGPT to 100M users in two months). Leapfrogging hope vs.
  divide-widening risk in one frame. **Role: accessible dual-impact macro overview.**
- [Evidence Action Incubates AI for Good / Cross-Sector Analysis](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/evidence-action-newsroom-ai-analysis.md)
  ([full analysis](../../readings/linked/ai-access-initiative-ai-for-good-cross-sector.md))
  — The builder's concrete intervention map: 30+ AI interventions scored for scale-readiness in
  LMICs, advised by Kremer and Amodei. TB X-ray diagnosis, AI tutoring RCTs, early-warning
  systems. **Role: vivid "here's what actually gets built" deployment case.**
- [AI and Shared Prosperity (Klinova & Korinek)](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/klinova-ai-and-shared-prosperity.pdf)
  — Rigorous deep-dive on labor-displacement risk and how AI development choices bear on
  low- and middle-income workers. **Role: rigorous depth anchor on the inequality thread.**
- [AI and the Economic Future (Chad Jones)](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/chad-jones-ai-economic-future.pdf)
  — Growth-theory lens on what AGI does to long-run incomes, poor countries included. **Role:
  the academic/data floor beneath the "explosive growth ends poverty" claim.**
- [Why Would Anyone Be Against Lifesaving Malaria Bednets? (Matthews, Vox)](../../readings/linked/vox-malaria-bednets-andreessen.md)
  — High wow-density narrative tying EA global health directly to the AI culture war
  (Andreessen/e-acc vs. EA), with the arithmetic shown (282M nets → ~1.58M lives in one year).
  **Role: vivid motivational narrative; connects the cluster to the AI-accelerationism fight.**
- [10 Technologies That Won't Exist in 5 Years (Trefethen)](../../readings/linked/trefethen-10-health-technologies-5-years.md)
  — Life-or-death progress-studies framing from inside science funding: with $10B/decade we'd
  get 5–6 of them, and millions would live. **Role: vivid progress-studies exhibit; the "why R&D bottlenecks matter" hook.**
- [(How) Do the Poorest Use AI? (Bjorkegren, CGD)](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/cgd-how-do-poorest-use-ai.md)
  — Sharp, empirical, neglected question: usage reports track knowledge workers, not the billions
  whose queries are health/agriculture/government. **Role: the neglected-user perspective; grounds the access debate in data.**
- [Big Aid Is Over (Starr, SSIR)](../../readings/linked/big-aid-is-over.md)
  — The funding-landscape shock (2025 aid collapse) that recontextualizes the whole cluster:
  government as payer, country ownership, the end of Big Aid. **Role: sets the post-aid paradigm the AI question lands in.**

## Conspicuously missing

- **Dario Amodei, "Machines of Loving Grace"** — the RFP itself cites it as the canonical
  "compressed 21st century in biomedicine" optimist essay, yet it's not in the collection. The
  single most-needed deployment-optimist anchor.
- **A real LMIC-deployment RCT with a named human** — e.g. the **Penda Health / OpenAI clinical
  copilot trial in Kenya** (AI cutting diagnostic errors in real clinics), or Ada Health / PATH
  work. The collection has Western funders *talking about* LMICs but almost no on-the-ground
  builder from the Global South reporting hard outcomes.
- **Richard Ngo / Forethought-style macrostrategy on global poverty under AGI** — the course
  brief explicitly wants Ngo's lens; nothing here takes the "what happens to the global poor in
  a post-AGI transition" question at that altitude (gradual disempowerment, benefit-sharing).
- **A concrete "Gavi for AI" / global benefit-sharing institutional proposal** — the RFP gestures
  at "a Gavi- or Global-Fund-style institution ensuring AI benefits reach the poor," but no
  reading actually designs one.
- **Lant Pritchett** (or a comparable growth-over-RCTs development economist) — the definitive
  voice on "national development beats direct health interventions," which would anchor the
  reopened health-vs-development axis far better than the thin EA-forum question currently
  standing in for it ([why-eas-focus-global-health-over-development](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/why-eas-focus-global-health-over-development.md)).
- **The ICT4D / "tech for good" failure base rate** — Kentaro Toyama's *Geek Heresy*, One Laptop
  Per Child, or a history of over-promised development tech. The skeptic's empirical ammunition
  against AI-for-good hype is absent; Senanayake critiques in the abstract without the graveyard.
- **A WHO / multilateral-health governance voice** — e.g. WHO's 2024 guidance on large multimodal
  models in health. The World Bank and CGD speak; the actual global-health governance body does not.
- **The within-EA rebuttal to progress studies** — the collection platforms the abundance case
  (Olvera, Trefethen, FDA reform) without the randomista/measurement defense of it. Needs the
  other side of the Hauke-Hillebrandt / "growth vs. randomista development" debate stated in EA terms.
- **Cash transfers as the response to AI labor displacement** — given the displacement thesis, a
  piece connecting GiveDirectly-style cash / UBI-in-LMICs to an automated-labor future is a
  natural bridge that's missing (the livelihoods reading is pre-AI framed).
- **An awe / long-run-health-progress opener** — Charles Kenny's *Getting Better*, Our World in
  Data on child-mortality collapse, or a smallpox-style epic. Per the handbook virtues, the
  cluster lacks its emotional on-ramp; everything here opens in argument or funder-memo register.
