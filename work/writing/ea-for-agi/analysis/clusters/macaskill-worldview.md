# Cluster map: MacAskill worldview

## What this cluster covers

The intellectual world of Will MacAskill and Forethought: longtermism, existential
risk, population axiology, and — in the newer material — the pivot from "will we
survive AGI?" to "if we survive, will the future actually be good?" Three sub-threads
run through it. (1) **The longtermism case** (WWOTF-era): the future is vast, our
actions are pivotal, we stand at "the beginning of history." (2) **The values/axiology
engine underneath it**: population ethics, the repugnant conclusion, moral uncertainty,
the new Saturation View, moral public goods. (3) **The age-of-AGI turn**: flourishing
over survival, viatopia, AI character, deals with misaligned AIs, and a deliberately
measured stance in the takeover-risk debate. Around the MacAskill core sits a ring of
critics (Torres, Alexander) and the philosophical primary sources he builds on
(Bostrom, Parfit-via-SEP, Nordhaus).

## Traditions and voices, and where they disagree

- **MacAskill / Forethought (the center).** Scope-sensitive, truth-seeking, optimizing
  utilitarian-leaning consequentialism, but self-consciously hedged — moral-realist
  language with a "translate as you like" disclaimer, and an explicit turn toward
  *not* locking in any narrow utopia (viatopia). The recent voice is strikingly
  ecumenical and "both/and": keep global health and animal welfare live *and* work on
  AGI; take x-risk seriously *and* argue p(doom) is probably low.
- **The x-risk-first camp he argues against.** Yudkowsky & Soares (*If Anyone Builds
  It*) — discontinuity, "first try or die," catastrophic-by-default misalignment.
  MacAskill's review is the cleanest statement of the disagreement: gradualism,
  imperfect ≠ catastrophic misalignment, deals with risk-averse AIs. Paul Christiano
  and Ryan Greenblatt are cited as the better foils.
- **The longtermism critics.** Émile Torres ("world's most dangerous secular credo") —
  longtermism as a dangerous, fanatical, potentially atrocity-justifying credo. Scott
  Alexander plays a subtler role: his "*Long-Termism* vs. *Existential Risk*" argues
  the movement should drop the philosophy and just say "don't let everyone die," and
  his WWOTF review is the sympathetic-but-skeptical outsider read.
- **The axiology lineage.** Parfit (repugnant conclusion, via SEP), Bostrom (quantity
  of experience / duplication), Broome (uniting vs disuniting), Tarsney (Saturation
  co-author), with Yudkowsky's "value is fragile" as a foil. This is where the
  disagreements get technical: Total View vs Saturation, Pareto objections, whether
  variety has impersonal value.
- **Adjacent traditions gestured at but barely represented.** Burke (conservative /
  intergenerational-contract longtermism), Nordhaus (growth-economics framing of the
  "singularity"). These are the seeds of the breadth the course brief wants and the
  cluster mostly lacks.

Core fault lines: (a) **survival vs flourishing** as the priority; (b) **discontinuity
vs gradualism** in AI takeoff; (c) **Total View vs variety-based axiology**; (d)
**whether longtermism-the-philosophy is load-bearing** (Alexander/Ord say drop it,
MacAskill keeps it); (e) **is longtermism benign or dangerous** (Torres).

## Standout readings

Curriculum-usable, ranked by value to an intro fellowship.

1. [flourishing-over-survival.md](../../readings/macaskill-worldview/flourishing-over-survival.md)
   — MacAskill's own crisp framing of the Better Futures thesis (survival × flourishing
   two-factor model, the "Cockaigne" image, viatopia). **Role: anchor essay** for the
   cluster's distinctive new idea; short, self-contained, quotable.
2. [beginning-of-history.md](../../readings/macaskill-worldview/beginning-of-history.md)
   — the *Foreign Affairs* WWOTF essay: "we are the ancients," Fukuyama foil, nuclear/
   bio concreteness, differential technological development, the Yahaba future-robes
   experiment. **Role: accessible on-ramp** to longtermism written for a policy
   audience, not EAs.
3. [short-review-if-anyone-builds-it.md](../../readings/macaskill-worldview/short-review-if-anyone-builds-it.md)
   — MacAskill dismantling Yudkowsky/Soares point by point (evolution analogy,
   discontinuity, imperfect vs catastrophic misalignment). **Role: models the "both/and"
   epistemics** the course prizes — takes takeover risk seriously *while* rejecting the
   doom argument. Best single text for "how to disagree well."
4. [300000-lives-100-million-hens.md](../../readings/macaskill-worldview/300000-lives-100-million-hens.md)
   — the "EA is back" horn-toot: 300k lives, 100M cage-free hens, effective giving up
   40%. **Role: awe/agency opener** (Handbook virtue #1) and the "keep global health +
   animals live in the age of AGI" stance in one short piece.
5. [the-saturation-view.md](../../readings/macaskill-worldview/the-saturation-view.md)
   — MacAskill responding to comments on his new population axiology (variety has
   diminishing-marginal impersonal value; answers repugnant conclusion + fanaticism).
   **Role: rigorous deep-dive** into the values engine; the optional-layer axiology
   anchor for motivated readers.
6. [80k-ai-character-viatopia.md](../../readings/macaskill-worldview/80k-ai-character-viatopia.md)
   — 80k #242: AI character as a neglected lever, deals with risk-averse AIs, viatopia,
   moral public goods, EA post-SBF. **Role: the AGI-era survey interview** — one
   transcript that maps the whole current worldview. Excerpt the "nutshell" + character
   section rather than assigning whole.
7. [80k-century-in-a-decade.md](../../readings/macaskill-worldview/80k-century-in-a-decade.md)
   — 80k #213: "a century in a decade" and the grand challenges of the intelligence
   explosion. **Role: vivid narrative** of *why AGI compresses everything* — the
   preparedness case that motivates the cause menu.
8. [longtermism-vs-existential-risk.md](../../readings/linked/longtermism-vs-existential-risk.md)
   — Scott Alexander: maybe skip the trillion-future-people philosophy and just say
   "don't let everyone die." **Role: best internal critic** — a sympathetic reframing
   that gives students an exit ramp from the heavy philosophy (mirrors Handbook virtue #3).
9. [longtermism-most-dangerous-credo.md](../../readings/linked/longtermism-most-dangerous-credo.md)
   — Torres's canonical hostile critique. **Role: the adversary in the criticism week**

10. [acx-review-what-we-owe-the-future.md](../../readings/linked/acx-review-what-we-owe-the-future.md)
    — Alexander's long, fair, funny WWOTF review. **Role: accessible outside read** of
    the book without assigning the book; good discussion fuel.
11. [flourishing/moral-public-goods-good-future.md](../../readings/linked/moral-public-goods-good-future.md)

## Conspicuously missing

- **Toby Ord in his own voice.** Ord is cited on every page (the 1-in-6, the Precipice
  framing) but no Ord primary text is in the cluster. Need *The Precipice* intro/ch.1,
  or his recent essays (e.g. "The Precipice Revisited," his shift toward the post-AGI
  transition). He is the co-parent of this worldview.
- **Greaves & MacAskill, "The Case for Strong Longtermism"** (GPI) — the academic rigor
  anchor beneath the popular essays. Flagged missing in the Handbook analysis too; it's
  the single most important primary source not here.
- **The hinge-of-history primary essay.** MacAskill's "Are we living at the most
  influential time in history?" and the Buck/rebuttal exchange — the internal debate is
  only glancingly present via a transcript title.
- **Holden Karnofsky's "Most Important Century" series** — the other major, and more
  empirical, worldview framing of the AGI transition (PASTA, lock-in, "this can't go on").
  A glaring absence for a course on EA in the age of AGI.
- **Richard Ngo.** The course brief names him explicitly as a distinct lens; nothing of
  his is here — e.g. "AGI safety from first principles," his fiction, or his
  coordination/values essays.
- **The rest of the Better Futures series in full** — Fin Moorhouse & MacAskill's "No
  Easy Eutopia," "Convergence and Compromise," the "Basic Case" supplement with Trammell.
  Only MacAskill's forum summary is captured.
- **A rigorous academic critic of longtermism.** David Thorstad ("Existential risk
  pessimism and the time of perils," "Against the singularity hypothesis") and Christian
  Tarsney's "epistemic challenge to longtermism" — the *serious* critiques, as opposed
  to Torres's polemic. Also Kieran Setiya's philosopher's-eye WWOTF review.
- **A non-Western / non-analytic tradition of future generations.** The cluster is
  almost entirely white male analytic philosophers. Missing: Roman Krznaric's *The Good
  Ancestor*, the Iroquois seven-generations principle, Japanese "Future Design" (the
  Yahaba experiment is mentioned but has no dedicated reading), or Māori/indigenous
  intergenerational stewardship. Directly serves the brief's "different traditions" aim.
- **The growth-economics longtermist tradition.** Tyler Cowen's *Stubborn Attachments*
  and Phil Trammell's work — longtermism that prioritizes growth/institutions over
  x-risk, a live alternative to the MacAskill line. Nordhaus is here but only as a
  narrow "economic singularity" paper.
- **The TESCREAL / political critique.** Torres & Gebru's "TESCREAL" argument, and the
  eugenics-adjacency critique of the transhumanist lineage — the sharpest current
  external attack on this whole worldview.
- **A woman's voice on the core axiology/longtermism.** Almost none present. Candidates:
  Hilary Greaves (population axiology survey, cluelessness), Katja Grace (already cited
  as a foil to "value is fragile" — get the full piece), Christine Korsgaard on future
  beings and animals.
- **Parfit primary.** The whole axiology thread rests on *Reasons and Persons* Part IV;
  only the SEP summary stands in for it.
