# Cluster map: forethought

## What this cluster covers

This is the **Forethought Research corpus plus its macrostrategy neighbourhood** — the
body of work arguing that superintelligence will compress "a century of progress into a
decade" and force a rapid succession of hard-to-reverse "grand challenges" that are
*not* reducible to the alignment problem. Six sub-threads run through it: (1) the
**dynamics of the intelligence explosion** — its cognitive, software, and industrial
feedback loops and physical limits; (2) an **AGI-preparedness portfolio** of grand
challenges — human coups, value lock-in, space governance, epistemic disruption — with
concrete projects for each; (3) MacAskill & Moorhouse's **Better Futures** philosophy,
which reframes the goal from *Surviving* (avoiding catastrophe) to *Flourishing* (making
a survived future actually great); (4) **AI for epistemics and coordination** as a
near-term lever; (5) **AI character, deals-with-AIs, and digital minds** — how to build,
communicate with, and morally treat the systems themselves; and (6) the **economics of
AGI** — growth, labour share, abundance, and windfall-sharing.

## Intellectual traditions and where the voices disagree

The cluster is overwhelmingly **one house style**: Oxford/GPI-descended longtermist
macrostrategy, written by a tight Forethought roster (MacAskill, Moorhouse, Tom
Davidson, Rose Hadshar, Lukas Finnveden, Owen Cotton-Barratt, Lizka Vaintrob). Within
that consensus there are real, stageable disagreements:

- **"Grand challenges" vs "all-or-nothing alignment."** The flagship (PIE) explicitly
  argues *against* the dominant safety view that outcomes hinge on one variable
  (alignment) and everything else can be punted to aligned superintelligence. The view
  it rebuts is present only as a foil, never in its own strong voice.
- **Flourishing vs Surviving.** *Better Futures* claims improving good futures may be
  ~36–10,000× the scale of avoiding extinction — a direct challenge to the standard
  x-risc-first priority ordering. MacAskill concedes tractability is the weak point.
- **Accelerate vs pause.** Cotton-Barratt & Vaintrob's differential-AI-development
  thesis (accelerate risk-*reducing* applications, often unilaterally) sits in tension
  with "slow down / pause" instincts elsewhere in EA.
- **Economists vs lab intuitions.** Trammell (labour-a-luxury) and Aghion-Jones-Jones
  bring growth-theory rigour that both formalises *and* complicates the labs'
  "country of geniuses in a datacenter" picture; Trammell openly needles ChatGPT's
  consensus.
- **Deals-with-AIs / AI-as-counterparty.** The Redwood-adjacent thread (Finnveden's
  honesty policy, risk-averse AIs, proactive prosociality) treats misaligned AI as a
  bargaining partner, not just a hazard — a distinct frame from control/interpretability.
- **Population ethics under the hood.** *The Saturation View* is a Theory-X candidate
  supplying the axiology *Better Futures* leans on — the cluster's one deep-philosophy root.

The near-total absence of outside traditions (economists aside) is the cluster's
defining limitation — see Gaps.

## Standout readings

- **[Preparing for the Intelligence Explosion](../../readings/forethought/preparing-for-the-intelligence-explosion.md)** — MacAskill & Moorhouse. The
  cluster's keystone: "century in a decade," the grand-challenges taxonomy, and the case
  that preparedness ≠ alignment. *Role: course-spine anchor essay (assign in sections).*
- **[Three Types of Intelligence Explosion](../../readings/forethought/three-types-of-intelligence-explosion.md)** — Davidson, Hadshar, MacAskill. Clean,
  short taxonomy (cognitive → software → industrial feedback loops). *Role: accessible
  mechanism on-ramp to the whole IE thread.*
- **[AI-Enabled Coups](../../readings/forethought/ai-enabled-coups-how-a-small-group-could-use-ai-to-seize-power.md)** — Davidson et al. Vivid, concrete "human takeover"
  scenario (secret loyalties, sleeper agents, a few battalions). *Role: the dread-inducing
  narrative that makes power-concentration risk felt, with a boring mitigations list to
  chase it.*
- **[Introducing Better Futures](../../readings/forethought/introducing-better-futures.md)** — MacAskill. The Surviving-vs-Flourishing reframe and
  the *viatopia* concept in ~15 min. *Role: values/worldview anchor; the natural home for
  MacAskill's "epistemic habits" target.*
- **[AI Tools for Existential Security](../../readings/forethought/ai-tools-for-existential-security.md)** — Vaintrob & Cotton-Barratt. The
  differential-AI-development thesis: which tools to accelerate and how. *Role: the
  "there's tractable work today" turn; agency after the dread.*
- **[Concrete Projects in AGI Preparedness](../../readings/forethought/concrete-projects-in-agi-preparedness.md)** — MacAskill & Moorhouse. A ranked menu of
  neglected, shovel-ready projects. *Role: the boring-tractability list; project/exercise
  fodder for a final unit.*
- **[Is Labor a Luxury in the Long Run?](../../readings/forethought/is-labor-a-luxury-in-the-long-run.md)** — Trammell. Rigorous, witty growth-theory on
  whether the labour share survives full automation ("maybe pick up the banjo"). *Role:
  best non-safety voice; head-and-heart economics counterweight.*
- **[AI Impacts on Epistemics: the Good, the Bad and the Ugly](../../readings/forethought/ai-impacts-on-epistemics-the-good-the-bad-and-the-ugly.md)** — short, vivid
  trajectory map of how AI reshapes collective sense-making. *Role: accessible epistemics
  on-ramp (pair the deeper Finnveden piece as optional).*
- **[Risk-Averse AIs](../../readings/forethought/risk-averse-ais.md)** — the "give misaligned AI something to lose, then pay it not
  to rebel" argument, rigorously worked. *Role: rigorous alignment/AI-character deep-dive;
  a genuinely counterintuitive claim to stress-test.*
- **[Being Honest with AIs](../../readings/linked/being-honest-with-ais.md)** (Finnveden) + **[A Draft Honesty Policy](../../readings/forethought/a-draft-honesty-policy-for-credible-communication-with-ai-systems.md)** — the case for
  credible human-AI communication and a concrete policy draft. *Role: provocative
  discussion pair on the ethics of how we treat/deceive AI counterparties.*
- **[No Easy Eutopia](../../readings/forethought/no-easy-eutopia.md)** — MacAskill & Moorhouse. The rigorous core of Better Futures:
  eutopia is a narrow target even common-sense utopias miss. *Role: the philosophy
  depth-anchor beneath the accessible intro.*
- **[The Industrial Explosion](../../readings/forethought/the-industrial-explosion.md)** — Hadshar & others. Robot factories building robot
  factories; doubling in days. *Role: vivid physical-world mechanism; keeps the IE from
  feeling purely abstract-cognitive.*
- **[Intelsat as a Model for International AGI Governance](../../readings/forethought/intelsat-as-a-model-for-international-agi-governance.md)** — Hadshar & MacAskill.
  A concrete historical analogy for institutional design. *Role: governance case study;
  data/evidence-grounded counterweight to speculation.*
- **[Project Ideas: Sentience and Rights of Digital Minds](../../readings/forethought/project-ideas-sentience-and-rights-of-digital-minds.md)** — Finnveden. The digital-minds
  cause area, made actionable. *Role: opens the moral-circle-expansion frontier the brief
  names as a distinct cause.*

## Conspicuously missing

- **A skeptic of the intelligence-explosion framing, in-cluster.** No Tyler Cowen
  (growth-continuity skepticism), no "AI as Normal Technology" (Narayanan & Kapoor), no
  deep-learning-wall voice. The IE is assumed, never seriously contested — fatal for a
  criticism session.
- **The "all-or-nothing alignment" view in its own strong voice.** PIE and Better Futures
  both rebut it; students never hear a Yudkowsky/Soares-style maximalist state it
  first. Assign the position being argued against.
- **A critic of the Flourishing-over-Surviving reframe.** No reply defending
  survival-first priority (a strong-longtermist rebuttal, or Thorstad-style skepticism of
  the expected-value arithmetic that yields "36× the scale").
- **Gradual Disempowerment** (Kulveit, Duenas, et al., 2025) — the central
  no-single-coup, slow-erosion account of losing control. Sits exactly between coups and
  lock-in and is absent.
- **Carl Shulman's post-AGI economy/governance** (Dwarkesh interviews; "society/economy
  after AGI") — the deepest empirical macrostrategy voice on abundance and institutions,
  present only in the 80k pile, not here.
- **Foundational digital-minds philosophy** — "Taking AI Welfare Seriously" (Long, Sebo
  et al.), Schwitzgebel & Garza, or Bostrom & Shulman's "Propositions Concerning Digital
  Minds and Society." The cluster has project lists but no foundational moral-status case.
- **Cooperative AI foundations** — Dafoe et al., "Open Problems in Cooperative AI." The
  coordination thread cites a project-ideas note but not the canonical statement.
- **Non-Western / Global South voices on windfall and governance.** "Sharing the AI
  Windfall" is here, but no author *from* the Global South, no African/Indian AI-governance
  perspective, no digital-democracy voice (Audrey Tang / Collective Intelligence Project /
  Divya Siddarth) on participatory processes for "viatopia."
- **A left / political-economy critique of "abundance" and "windfall."** Who owns the
  robots; distribution as politics not just design (a Piketty- or critical-AI-studies-
  adjacent voice). The cluster treats distribution as an engineering problem.
- **A dedicated space-governance deep-dive.** Referenced repeatedly (PIE, concrete
  projects) as a top cause, but there's no substantive reading on offworld-resource law
  beyond passing mentions.
- **Global-health/development-org adaptation to AI.** The brief explicitly wanted how
  GiveWell/OP-style global-health actors plan to adapt; nothing here speaks from that world.
