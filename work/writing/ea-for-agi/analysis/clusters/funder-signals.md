# Cluster map: funder-signals

## What this cluster covers

This is the *revealed-preferences-of-the-money* cluster: what EA / longtermist funders
actually fund, how grant decisions get made, and what each strategy shift signals about
where the field now thinks the frontier of impact sits. It is the meta-layer where
abstract cause prioritization becomes concrete dollars — RFPs read as taxonomies of a
field, fund prospectuses read as worldviews packaged for donors, and rebrands read as
bets on the future. Main sub-threads: (1) the **funder-capacity bottleneck** — the
loud 2026 claim that AI safety is starved of grantmakers, not money; (2) **RFPs and
payout reports as maps** of what a funder thinks matters (Open Phil's 21-area technical
safety RFP, capability-evals, HEMs); (3) **grantmaker inside views and legible
thresholds** (what a marginal LTFF/EAIF grant looks like, what grantmakers actually
do); (4) the **beyond-safety turn** — funders explicitly widening past alignment into
growth, epistemics, cooperative AI, AI welfare, which is the sub-thread that most
directly serves MacAskill's "30%+ beyond AI safety" target; and (5) **timing and
mechanism of giving** — patient vs. urgent, centralized worldview vs. distributed
S-process vs. trust-based.

## Intellectual traditions / distinct voices

- **Open Philanthropy → Coefficient Giving (Karnofsky–Berger–Muehlhauser lineage).**
  The gravitational center. Hits-based giving, worldview diversification, strategic
  cause selection, and a GiveWell-style *rigor-and-transparency* ideal. Now
  repositioning as a multi-donor coordinator (the Coefficient rebrand) and visibly
  widening beyond safety (Abundance & Growth Fund).
- **Worldview-investigations epistemics (Carlsmith, Finnveden, Cotra, Davidson).** The
  "figure out carefully what we believe, write it down so a skeptic can spot-check it,
  then fund it" tradition. Carlsmith's departure for Anthropic is itself a signal that
  the frontier of leverage may be migrating from funder to lab.
- **Donor-advisory funds (Longview, Founders Pledge).** Package worldviews into named
  funds for high-net-worth donors; more comfortable being explicitly longtermist /
  catastrophist; introduce the patient-vs-urgent and worldview-diversification framings
  to donors directly.
- **EA Funds — LTFF / EAIF (Parikh, Linch).** Small-donor, transparency-of-thresholds,
  principles-first. Publishes its decision boundary and marginal grants to stay legible.
- **Mega-donor mechanism design — Jaan Tallinn / SFF.** The S-process (simulate
  delegation across many recommenders) is a structural alternative to Open Phil's
  centralized worldview.
- **New / non-safety-native funders (Astera, Macroscopic).** Startup-like, flexible
  capital; betting on intelligence foundations, cooperative AI, AI welfare — deliberate
  diversity from the safety mainstream.
- **Patient-philanthropy school (Trammell).** Invest-to-give; the intellectual
  antagonist of the "deploy billions now" urgency.
- **Trust-based philanthropy (SSIR, outside EA).** The paradigm critique: hand
  discretion to grantees, drop restricted grants and heavy metrics — a direct foil to
  EA's vetting-heavy, measurement-driven default.

**Where they disagree.** Four live fault lines a curriculum can teach off:
1. **Money-constrained vs. capacity-constrained.** lukeprog and Hazell say the binding
   constraint is grantmakers, not dollars (billions waiting); Wentworth's "alignment
   grantmaking is funding-limited" and the 2023 post-FTX landscape say the opposite.
   Partly reconciled by *time* (2023 scarcity → 2026 abundance) and *segment*
   (independent researchers funding-limited; large orgs advisor-limited).
2. **Give now vs. invest to give.** Founders Pledge's GCRF vs. its Patient Philanthropy
   Fund; Trammell's discounting math vs. "time of perils, act now."
3. **Alignment-centric vs. beyond-safety.** The $40M technical-safety RFP vs. Abundance
   & Growth, Finnveden's non-alignment menu, Macroscopic's welfare/cooperative bets.
4. **Centralized worldview vs. distributed/trust-based mechanism.** Open Phil strategic
   cause selection vs. SFF's S-process vs. SSIR trust-based unrestricted giving.

## What's conspicuously missing

- **The labs-as-funders story.** The biggest emerging funders are the frontier labs
  themselves — the OpenAI Foundation's multi-billion "AI resilience" commitment and
  Anthropic-linked philanthropy (both referenced in lukeprog's footnotes via the
  *Transformer* piece) — yet nothing here covers them directly. Search: OpenAI
  Foundation nonprofit commitment; Anthropic employees' philanthropy.
- **A critique of single-funder concentration.** The whole cluster is downstream of one
  donor (Good Ventures / Moskovitz); no reading reckons with the distortion, dependency,
  and Goodharting-to-RFPs that creates. Search: critiques of Open Phil / Good Ventures
  centralization; "the AI safety ecosystem is downstream of one funder."
- **The explicit rebuttal to "bottlenecked on grantmakers."** The claim is asserted
  from the funder side but never seriously contested here. Search: counter-take that
  the binding constraint is org/founder capacity, or that active grantmaking distorts
  the field.
- **A global-health / GH&D funder adapting to AI.** The brief explicitly wants this and
  the pile only has a stale GiveWell AI memo (`givewell-ai-iem`). Search: GiveWell or
  Gates Foundation current thinking on AI; how neartermist funders are updating.
- **Regranting and democratized funding mechanisms, evaluated.** Manifund, ACX Grants,
  Nonlinear Network, SFF speculation grants are name-checked in Skoglund but get no
  dedicated piece with *results*. Search: Manifund regranting retrospective; the EA
  regranting experiment.
- **Impact markets / retroactive funding.** An entire funding-mechanism thread (impact
  certificates, retro public-goods funding) is absent. Search: impact certificates for
  AI safety; retroactive funding debate.
- **A dedicated AI-welfare / digital-minds funding memo.** Longview's Digital Minds Fund
  and Macroscopic gesture at it, but no funder writes up *why and how* to fund AI
  welfare. Search: Longview Digital Minds Fund thesis; Eleos AI / NYU CMEP funding case.
- **A grantee-side / reflexive view.** Nothing on what funder signals *do* to the field
  — chasing RFP fashions, one-funder dependency, the incentives an RFP creates. Search:
  grantee perspective on EA funding dynamics; how RFPs shape research agendas.
- **Non-Western / non-US funders.** Entirely Anglosphere. No Chinese, Indian, Gulf, or
  Global-South philanthropy on AI, and no non-US institutional voice. Search: AI safety
  philanthropy outside the US/UK; Chinese foundations and AI governance.
- **Current quantitative "where the money goes."** No up-to-date allocation breakdown
  (Coefficient/Good Ventures spend across GHW vs. GCR vs. AI); `openphil-ghw-longtermism-portfolios`
  is dated. Search: Coefficient Giving 2026 grantmaking allocation; Good Ventures budget.
- **FTX as a named funding episode.** The collapse haunts the 2023 landscape reading but
  no piece reckons with what it did to longtermist funding, trust, and diligence norms.
  Search: FTX Future Fund aftermath; lessons for EA grantmaking post-FTX.
- **FRO / ARPA-style funding models as contrast.** `eghbal-science-funding` opens the
  door; missing is Focused Research Organizations / Convergent Research or metascience
  applied to AI safety funding structure. Search: Focused Research Organizations model;
  ARPA-style funding for AI safety.
