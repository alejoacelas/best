# Cluster map: AI persuasion & epistemics

*Cartographer's map of the `persuasion-epistemics` cluster for the "EA in the age of AGI"
curriculum. Curated readings live in [../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/);
linked-pile additions in [../../readings/linked/](../../readings/linked/).*

## What this cluster covers

Two of MacAskill's illustrative cause areas fused into one: **AI-driven persuasion and
epistemic disruption** (the threat side) and **AI for better reasoning and coordination**
(the opportunity side). The cluster asks whether a world saturated with superhuman
persuaders, sycophantic companions, and synthetic media degrades humanity's collective
ability to figure out what's true and act on it — and whether the same technology, pointed
the other way (forecasting bots, truthful AI, bridging algorithms, prediction markets,
debate), could instead *upgrade* our epistemics past anything humans have managed alone.
Four sub-threads run through it: (1) **the persuasion threat model** — how capable, how
soon, distinct from or subsumed by alignment failure; (2) **the misinformation-panic
debate** — a fierce revisionist camp arguing the whole "infodemic" frame is empirically
wrong and demand-side, not supply-side; (3) **AI-for-epistemics as a cause area** —
forecasting, truthful AI, collective-intelligence infrastructure, info-finance; and (4)
**the gradual/civilizational frame** — sycophancy, companion bots, and disempowerment as
slow erosion rather than a single takeover.

## Intellectual traditions and where they disagree

- **The EA/alignment persuasion-threat school.** Beth Barnes, Daniel Kokotajlo, Carl
  Shulman, Wei Dai. Treats persuasion as a natural, cheaply-trainable, hard-to-align ML
  application and a possible takeover path *short of* AGI. Internally hedged (Barnes rates
  it ~10x smaller than standard misalignment; Kokotajlo expects gradual not insane). Their
  crux with each other: is persuasion its own threat, a facet of alignment, or just a risk
  factor that shortens timelines?
- **The misinformation revisionists.** Dan Williams, Hugo Mercier, Dan Sperber, and the
  HKS *Misinformation Review* empirical crowd. The cluster's sharpest live disagreement:
  they argue misinformation is *rare*, *demand-driven* (a "marketplace of rationalizations"
  catering to what people already want to believe), that humans are already *overly*
  vigilant not gullible, that inoculation/"prebunking" is methodologically bankrupt, and
  that GenAI misinformation fears are overblown. This is a frontal collision with the
  inoculation-theory establishment (van der Linden, Lewandowsky) — and, obliquely, with the
  alignment school's assumption that persuasion is a rising tide. Note the tension *inside
  the cluster*: Anthropic's own benchmark shows Claude 3 Opus matching human persuasiveness
  and a 2026 benchmark shows frontier LLMs *beating* campaign ads — evidence the
  revisionists have to reckon with.
- **The empirical persuasion-measurement camp.** Anthropic's societal-impacts team, the
  Kalla/Hackenburg political-science lineage, DebunkBot (Costello/Rand/Pennycook). Runs
  actual RCTs. Splits the field: DebunkBot shows AI dialogue *durably reducing* conspiracy
  belief (persuasion as cure), while the benchmarks show rising persuasive capability
  (persuasion as threat) — same tool, opposite valence.
- **The AI-for-epistemics optimists.** Lukas Finnveden, Ozzie Gooen/QURI, Benjamin Todd,
  Owain Evans & Owen Cotton-Barratt (truthful AI), Ought/Elicit, Forethought. Bet that
  differentially advancing AI's *epistemic* capabilities — forecasting, truthfulness,
  decomposition, process supervision — is a tractable, neglected lever. Their internal
  debate: build public epistemic goods (Phillips: AI has "killed Moloch dead" for
  forecasting) vs. private decision-support; direct capability-boosting vs. indirect
  trust/diffusion work.
- **The crypto/mechanism-design coordination school.** Vitalik Buterin (info finance,
  Community Notes), Robin Hanson (futarchy, meta-jury markets), Alex Tabarrok. Prediction
  markets and info-finance as flagship social epistemic technologies. Scott Alexander is
  the in-house skeptic here (Polymarket was mispriced; markets are thinner than believers
  claim).
- **The collective-intelligence / digital-public-square school.** Collective Intelligence
  Project (Divya Siddarth, Deger Turan), Aviv Ovadya, Audrey Tang, Jonathan Stray's
  prosocial-ranking crowd, Landemore. Institution-and-platform designers: bridging systems,
  collective dialogue, proof-of-humanity, recommender alignment. Overlaps heavily with the
  power-concentration/democracy cluster.
- **The essayists on discourse and virality.** Scott Alexander (Scissor statements,
  Moloch, "Guided by the Beauty of Our Weapons," toxoplasma of rage), Orwell (political
  language), Erik Hoel (semantic apocalypse). Supply the vivid, teachable mechanisms and
  the aesthetic register.
- **The gradual-disempowerment / sycophancy-harm frame.** Kulveit/Douglas et al., plus the
  "AI psychosis"/spiritual-delusion reportage. Persuasion-as-slow-erosion: not a persuader
  hijacking you, but companion AIs, sycophancy, and displaced human participation draining
  our leverage over culture and truth.

## Standout readings

- [barnes-risks-from-ai-persuasion.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/barnes-risks-from-ai-persuasion.md) —
  Beth Barnes (from Carl Shulman's ideas): the canonical EA threat model, with feasibility
  probabilities, pessimistic/central/optimistic 5-and-10-year scenarios, and intervention
  points. **Role: the anchor essay** for the threat side — hedged, mechanism-rich, ages
  well because it argues in scenarios not capability snapshots. Long; assign trimmed.
- [williams-misinformation-not-a-virus.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/williams-misinformation-not-a-virus.md) —
  Dan Williams demolishes inoculation theory (base rates, no "fingerprints," synthetic test
  items). **Role: the best critic / the cluster's designated contrarian** — the reading that
  makes the whole panic-vs-revisionist debate land. Pair directly against the Anthropic
  persuasiveness benchmark. (Long; the shorter blog version is this file, the Boston Review
  original is in the linked pile.)
- [anthropic-measuring-model-persuasiveness.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/anthropic-measuring-model-persuasiveness.md) —
  Anthropic's RCT: persuasiveness scales with model generation; Claude 3 Opus ≈ human.
  **Role: the empirical anchor / data point** — concrete, first-party, method transparent
  (including a "deceptive" prompt condition). The evidence the revisionists must answer.
- [debunkbot-conspiracy-dialogues.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/debunkbot-conspiracy-dialogues.md) —
  Costello/Rand/Pennycook in *Science*: 3 rounds with GPT-4 durably cut conspiracy belief
  ~20%, and it's the *evidence* not the rapport that does it. **Role: the vivid two-voice
  flip** — same persuasion tech as a public good; the optimistic counterpart to Anthropic's
  threat framing. Short, wow-dense, named researchers.
- [buterin-info-finance.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/buterin-info-finance.md) —
  Vitalik: prediction markets are one case of "info finance"; Polymarket as a news site for
  everyone else (the Venezuela vignette). **Role: the accessible on-ramp to epistemic
  technology** — concrete, story-led, generalizes markets → social media, science,
  governance. Reviewed by Hanson and Tabarrok.
- [ai-forecasting-public-epistemics.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/ai-forecasting-public-epistemics.md) —
  Lawrence Phillips: build the next generation of public forecasting AI-first (coordination
  over competition), because "AI has killed Moloch dead" for forecasting. **Role: the
  forward-looking cause pitch** — the freshest (2026) articulation of AI-for-epistemics with
  a concrete experiment.
- [truthful-ai-evans-cotton-barratt.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/truthful-ai-evans-cotton-barratt.md) —
  Evans & Cotton-Barratt: standards, institutions, and training for AI that doesn't lie —
  plus the capture risk (truthfulness evaluators becoming censors). **Role: the governance
  rigor anchor** — the foundational proposal the whole "truthful AI" thread rests on; names
  its own failure mode.
- [gradual-disempowerment.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/gradual-disempowerment.md) —
  Kulveit, Douglas et al.: incremental AI erodes human leverage over economy, culture, *and
  epistemics* with no coordinated takeover. **Role: the civilizational-frame bridge** —
  connects persuasion/sycophancy to the course's disempowerment and power-concentration
  threads; "no one has a concrete plausible plan" is the sobering hook.
- [ovadya-digital-public-squares.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/ovadya-digital-public-squares.md) —
  Goldberg, Ovadya, Tang, DiResta + 23 others: four concrete LLM applications for better
  public squares (collective dialogue, bridging, community moderation, proof-of-humanity).
  **Role: the solutions menu** — the constructive institutional answer; a map-then-choose
  survey for a project unit.
- [cip-collective-intelligence-whitepaper.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/cip-collective-intelligence-whitepaper.md) —
  Collective Intelligence Project: the transformative-technology trilemma
  (progress/safety/participation) and CI R&D as the way out. **Role: the framing document**
  for treating epistemics-and-coordination as *the* meta cause area.
- [cset-liars-dividend.md](../../readings/persuasion-epistemics/cset-liars-dividend.md) —
  Goldstein & Lohn: deepfakes' real harm may be the "liar's dividend" — dismissing *true*
  evidence as fake. **Role: the short, non-obvious mechanism** — reframes the deepfake
  worry away from the naive "fooled by fakes" story; ~1 page.
- [linked/persuasion-tools-ai-takeover.md](../../readings/linked/persuasion-tools-ai-takeover.md) —
  Daniel Kokotajlo: six kinds of persuasion tool (analyzers, feeders, chatbots, coaches…)
  and why they may arrive pre-AGI and shorten timelines. **Role: the taxonomy / companion to
  Barnes** — more concrete on the *tools*, and self-aware about not being alarmist.
- [linked/80k-hugo-mercier-misinformation-persuasion.md](../../readings/linked/80k-hugo-mercier-misinformation-persuasion.md) —
  Mercier: gullibility and mass persuasion are overrated; humans run robust epistemic
  vigilance. **Role: the accessible voice of the revisionist case** (podcast/interview
  register) — the head-and-heart pair to Williams's rigor.
- [linked/whats-important-in-ai-for-epistemics.md](../../readings/linked/whats-important-in-ai-for-epistemics.md) —
  Lukas Finnveden: the cleanest taxonomy of the cause area (boost epistemic capabilities vs.
  enable trust/diffusion; durable vs. commercially-swamped interventions). **Role: the
  cause-area rigor anchor** for the opportunity side — the syllabus scaffold for a whole
  AI-for-epistemics session.
- [linked/scott-alexander-sort-by-controversial.md](../../readings/linked/scott-alexander-sort-by-controversial.md) —
  Scott Alexander's fiction: an AI that generates maximally-divisive "Scissor statements."
  **Role: the wow-density teaching parable** — dramatizes societal-scale epistemic
  disruption in a way no white paper can; the vivid opener a session could hang on.

## Conspicuously missing

- **A frontline empirical persuasion result in its own file.** The cluster cites
  Hackenburg, Kalla, and Lin secondhand through the benchmark abstracts. Get the actual
  Hackenburg/Costello 2024–25 papers on LLM political persuasion (and the "durability of AI
  persuasion" follow-ups) as standalone readings — they're the ground truth both camps argue
  over.
- **The inoculation/"prebunking" establishment in its own voice.** Williams and Mercier
  attack van der Linden and Lewandowsky, but neither is present. Add Sander van der Linden's
  *Foolproof* case (or his Cambridge Bad News Game papers) so the debate isn't one-sided —
  exactly the Handbook's "name your best critic and hand over their full text" virtue.
- **A non-Western / Global-South epistemic-security voice.** Everything is US/UK/EA-crypto.
  Audrey Tang appears only as a co-author; get her (or the vTaiwan/Taiwan digital-democracy)
  primary writing, and something on WhatsApp/Global-South misinformation dynamics where the
  base-rate story may differ.
- **AI sycophancy / companion-bot manipulation as its own rigorous piece.** The threat is
  everywhere by implication (Barnes's companion bots, the AI-psychosis reportage in the
  linked pile) but there's no core reading on sycophancy-as-epistemic-harm. Pull in the
  OpenAI GPT-4o sycophancy post-mortem or Anthropic's sycophancy research, or the
  MIT/Stanford companion-app studies.
- **A serious defense of prediction markets' limits / a futarchy critique.** Scott's
  Polymarket skepticism is in the pile, but the strongest structured critiques (why markets
  stay illiquid, manipulation, the "no important questions" problem) deserve a core slot
  opposite Buterin/Hanson. `why-prediction-markets-arent-popular` is a start; add a rigorous
  futarchy critique.
- **The neuroscience/cognitive-liberty frontier.** Nita Farahany (neurotech, cognitive
  liberty) sits in the linked pile but there's no core reading on the *next* persuasion
  substrate — brain data, affective computing, emotion recognition. A distinctive angle the
  course could own.
- **MacAskill / Forethought in their own voice on this pair of causes.** The course is built
  on MacAskill's essay, which names both "AI-driven persuasion and epistemic disruption" and
  "AI for better reasoning and coordination" — but his and Forethought's direct treatment
  isn't curated here. Get the Forethought "AI for epistemics — good, bad, ugly" piece as a
  core framing.
- **An adoption/political-economy reality check.** Lots of "here's a beautiful mechanism"
  (bridging, info-finance, truthful-AI certification); little on why these fail to get
  adopted at scale, who resists them, and the incentives against them. One hard-nosed
  political-economy reading would keep the solutions thread honest.
- **A digital-minds / persuadee-consent angle.** If persuasion tools can hijack values, and
  if some AIs are moral patients, the ethics of AI-on-human and human-on-AI persuasion is a
  live gap connecting to the AI-welfare cluster — currently untouched.
