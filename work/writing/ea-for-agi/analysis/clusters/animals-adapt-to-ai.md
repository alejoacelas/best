# Cluster map: animals adapt to AI

## What this cluster covers

How transformative AI reshapes the fate of non-human animals — and how the animal
movement should react. Three intertwined sub-threads: (1) **near-term dual use** — AI as
a tool that helps advocacy (welfare monitoring, alt-protein R&D, targeted outreach,
interspecies communication) versus a tool that entrenches factory farming (precision
livestock farming, cheaper meat); (2) **long-run trajectory / lock-in** — whether an
AI-transformed future contains vast animal suffering (farmed, wild, or novel "functional"
beings) or renders factory farming obsolete, and how contingent that is on values locked
in around the transition; (3) **animal-inclusive alignment** — the concrete project of
getting animal concern into frontier systems (model specs, constitutions, benchmarks,
synthetic-document pretraining) and the meta-question of whether that is even coherent or
wise. Running underneath: a methodological fight about whether we are *clueless* about
post-paradigm-shift worlds and should discount speculative long-run interventions to
zero, or whether lock-in makes acting now unusually high-leverage.

## Intellectual traditions and where they disagree

- **Grantmaker realism (Lewis Bollard / Open Phil).** Near-term pessimist (industry
  adopts efficiency tech, shuns welfare tech), longer-run cautious optimist (wealth +
  alt-protein make factory farming obsolete). Skeptical of dramatic lock-in and of
  space-faring factory farms. The movement's funding center of gravity.
- **Forethought skepticism / cluelessness (Lizka, Ben West).** The uncertainty in any
  post-transformation intervention builds up so fast that expected value should be
  discounted to ~zero absent a concrete, articulable, robust mechanism. Warns that
  animal-friendly AI values could *backfire* (perpetuating farming by nostalgia/sentiment
  or misfiring "wisdom"). Recommends focusing on wild animals for the long run and
  heavily discounting normal interventions that pay off in 10+ years.
- **Act-now urgency / animal-inclusive alignment (Andrew Critch, Max Taylor, CaML —
  Brazilek & Tidmarsh, Sentient Futures).** Values get locked in soon; build the
  benchmarks (AnimalHarmBench, ANIMA), get "sentient beings" into constitutions and model
  specs, use synthetic-document finetuning. Empirical claim that animal-compassion
  training *generalizes to human compassion* and doesn't hurt capabilities — a bid to
  make animals matter even to those who don't care about animals.
- **Moral circle expansion / s-risk (Jacy Reese Anthis, Sentience Institute; Tomasik in
  the linked pile).** The foundational 2018 argument: prioritize *quality* risk (what the
  future is like) over *population* risk (whether it exists); the far future's expected
  value may be near zero or negative; MCE is neglected relative to AI alignment. The
  suffering-focused wing worries about astronomical suffering and spreading wild-animal
  suffering to the stars.
- **Movement-strategy reframers (Aidan Kankyoku / Sandcastles, Jamie Harris).** Kankyoku
  collapses the debate: "animal welfare is *just part of* AI alignment now" — the same
  through-line runs through every neglected cause on MacAskill's menu. Vivid,
  personal-essay register ("The End of Animal Advocacy"). Harris issues the movement-wide
  call to take short timelines seriously.
- **Optimist vs. pessimist visioning.** Jess Masterson ("What Success Looks Like")
  and Wladimir Alonso ("AI helps advocacy more than industry") paint concrete bright
  futures; Dylan Richardson ("Pessimistic Perspective") and Stewart/Dupuis ("What Failure
  Looks Like," linked) supply the dark mirror — value lock-in, decadence, generational
  replacement stalling, ASI as a time constraint on moral progress.
- **Academic animal-AI ethics (Philosophy & Technology; Singer & Tse in the linked
  pile).** Peer-reviewed case that alignment "to human values" disregards the vast
  majority of moral patients; proposes a *basic* concern floor and concrete lab/government
  policies.

The sharpest disagreements: **discount-to-zero vs. act-now** (Forethought vs.
CaML/Critch); **is shaping AI values good?** (Forethought's backfire worry vs. the
alignment builders' confidence); **farmed vs. wild** as the long-run locus of suffering
(Forethought says wild; most advocacy energy is still farmed); **MCE vs. extinction-risk**
(Anthis) now partly dissolved into Kankyoku's "it's all one problem."

## Standout readings (the ones a curriculum would use)

- **[A shallow review of what TAI means for animal welfare](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/shallow-review-tai-animal-welfare.md)** (Lizka/West) —
  the field's best structured survey: the normal-era vs. transformed-era grid, the
  discounting argument, and the value-shaping backfire case. *Role: the cluster's
  rigor/skeptic anchor; the reading that disciplines the rest.*
- **[What could AI mean for animals?](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/bollard-what-could-ai-mean-for-animals.md)** (Bollard) —
  short, concrete, dual-use framing (optimist utopia vs. pessimist dystopia, "who's
  right? I don't know"), grounded in agriculture's actual tech-adoption track record.
  *Role: accessible on-ramp; the trusted-grantmaker two-hands voice.*
- **[Animal Welfare is Just Part of AI Alignment Now](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/animal-welfare-is-part-of-ai-alignment.md)** (Kankyoku) —
  provocative reframe built directly on MacAskill's cause menu; separate messages to
  advocates and to alignment researchers. *Role: the thesis-statement / debate-igniting
  core reading; ties the whole course's premise to this cluster.*
- **[The End of Animal Advocacy](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/kankyoku-end-of-animal-advocacy.md)** (Kankyoku) —
  long-form vivid personal reckoning with what AGI does to a life spent on animal
  advocacy. *Role: the heart/narrative voice; emotional counterweight to the memo prose.*
- **[Alignment for Animals](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/alignment-for-animals.md)** +
  **[CaML](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/caml-compassion-in-machine-learning.md)** (Brazilek & Tidmarsh) —
  the concrete technical intervention: synthetic-document finetuning, the striking
  "compassion generalizes to humans" result, no capability hit. *Role: the "boring
  tractability" exhibit — animal alignment as real engineering, not just ethics.*
- **[Why I Prioritize Moral Circle Expansion Over AI Alignment](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/moral-circle-expansion-vs-extinction-risk.md)** (Anthis, 2018) —
  the foundational far-future-values argument the whole cluster descends from; expected
  value of the future, s-risk, the option-value rebuttal. *Role: the philosophical
  headwater / depth anchor; historicizes the debate.*
- **[Animal advocates should respond to TAI maybe arriving soon](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/advocates-should-respond-to-tai.md)** (Jamie Harris) —
  the clean movement-wide call to action with a concrete 2030 goal. *Role: the "so what
  do we do" bridge from diagnosis to strategy.*
- **[What could AI mean for animals? — Dwarkesh × Bollard](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/dwarkesh-bollard-artificial-meat.md)** —
  long interview; "artificial meat is harder than artificial intelligence," the alt-protein
  reality check. *Role: media-diversity slot; the alt-protein-optimism steelman and its
  limits, in a named-humans conversation.*
- **[What Success Looks Like For Animals](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/what-success-looks-like-for-animals.md)** (Masterson) —
  deliberately optimistic, concrete catalogue of bright AI-for-animals futures across
  farmed/wild/companion. *Role: the positive-vision opener (per BlueDot's "open with
  positive visions" and the Handbook's "awe" virtue).*
- **[A Pessimistic Perspective on Animal Welfare Social Change](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/pessimistic-perspective-animal-social-change.md)** (Richardson) —
  ASI as a hard time-constraint on moral progress; the cultivated-meat theory of change,
  interrogated. *Role: the best in-cluster critic; pairs against Masterson.*
- **[AI Alignment: The Case for Including Animals](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/ai-alignment-case-for-including-animals.md)** (Philosophy & Technology, 2025) —
  peer-reviewed "basic concern floor" argument + policy recommendations. *Role: the
  academic legitimacy anchor / evidence trail.*
- **[What AI could mean for animals](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/what-ai-could-mean-for-animals.md)** (Max Taylor) &
  **[Bringing about animal-inclusive AI](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/bringing-about-animal-inclusive-ai.md)** (Max Taylor) —
  the broad map + the practical routes (model specs, constitutions, evals). *Role: the
  survey-then-levers pair for a facilitator building a session.*
- **[Steering AI to care for animals, and soon](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/steering-ai-to-care-for-animals.md)** (Critch) —
  ~600 words, the crisp time-sensitivity cri de coeur. *Role: short provocation / discussion
  spark.*
- **[List of ideas for improving animal welfare in light of TAI](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/ideas-improving-animal-welfare-tai.md)** (Michael Dickens) —
  concrete brainstormed intervention menu. *Role: exercise fuel — hand students the list,
  have them rank by robustness against the Forethought discount.*

## Conspicuously missing

- **Jeff Sebo's AI-welfare / moral-circle bridge.** The cluster treats animals but barely
  connects to digital minds; Sebo (*The Moral Circle*, "the rebugnant conclusion," moral
  weight of digital minds) is the obvious bridge author and is only in the linked pile via
  one drowning-ant piece. Get his explicit animals↔AI-welfare argument.
- **A Peter Singer anchor.** Singer & Yip Fai Tse's "AI ethics: the case for including
  animals" (2022) and Singer's recent *Animal Liberation Now* / AI commentary sit in the
  linked pile but no Singer voice is curated — the movement's founding utilitarian voice
  should be a named core reading.
- **A lab-insider on whether animals are in scope.** Kyle Fish / Anthropic AI-welfare, or
  any frontier-lab safety researcher speaking undiluted on whether "alignment to human
  values" leaves animals out. The handbook virtue "insider voice in a core slot" is unmet
  here.
- **The pragmatic "this is a distraction" critic from inside animal advocacy.** Someone
  arguing the movement should ignore AGI speculation and keep winning corporate campaigns
  — a stronger present-suffering-first critic than Richardson (who accepts the AI frame).
- **Non-Western / Global South voices.** The entire cluster is Anglophone EA Forum. Given
  that meat consumption growth is concentrated in the developing world, a Chinese or Indian
  perspective on livestock + AI, or a non-utilitarian (Buddhist, Indigenous, care-ethics)
  framing of animals under AI, is completely absent.
- **Invertebrate / shrimp / insect-farming scale-up under AI.** Rethink Priorities welfare
  ranges are linked but the specific "AI industrializes insect/shrimp farming at
  astronomical scale" argument — the likely dominant near-term animal-numbers story — has
  no curated anchor. A Shrimp Welfare Project or insect-farming voice.
- **Wild Animal Initiative's own institutional position on AI.** The wild-animal long-run
  locus is where Forethought points, yet WAI's strategic take isn't curated (only a
  general TAI-and-wild-animals exploration).
- **A cross-movement analogy anchor.** "Corporate campaigns as a lesson for AI safety" and
  the social-movement case studies (children's rights, environmentalism, abolition) are in
  the linked pile but none is curated — a movement-history analogy is exactly the kind of
  transferable-lesson reading the Handbook favors.
- **Suffering-focused / s-risk deep anchor (CLR, Tobias Baumann, Brian Tomasik).** The
  s-risk tradition is gestured at through Anthis but has no dedicated curated piece on
  animals-specific astronomical suffering or spreading wild-animal suffering to space.
- **Empirical "current LLMs are speciesist" evidence.** Jotautaitė et al. (speciesist bias
  in LLMs) and "existential risk of speciesist bias in AI" are linked, not curated; the
  benchmarks (ANIMA, AnimalHarmBench) are curated but the underlying bias-measurement
  finding that motivates them should be a core evidence exhibit.
- **A concrete forecast of farmed-animal numbers under AI.** RP's "forecasting farmed
  animal numbers 2033" is linked; the cluster asserts factory farming may or may not
  persist but has no curated quantitative projection to ground the trajectory debate.
