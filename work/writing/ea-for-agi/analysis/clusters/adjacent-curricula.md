# Cluster map: adjacent curricula

Meta-documents, not object-level readings: the syllabi, reading lists, and
course-design write-ups that show *how other people have structured a program*
on EA, longtermism, x-risk, or AGI. This is the cluster the curriculum designer
mines for structure — sequencing, unit shapes, facilitation, exercises — rather
than for texts to assign. (Object-level readings on the causes themselves live in
the other clusters.)

## What it covers

Four sub-threads:

1. **The EA intro-fellowship lineage** — the official [Introductory EA
   Program](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/introductory-ea-program.md) and its
   [2022 rebuild](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/new-intro-fellowship-curriculum.md)
   (values-and-thinking-tools before causes), the
   [In-Depth Program](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/in-depth-ea-program-syllabus.md)
   (a menu of pick-your-cause weeks), and the academic EA courses taught in
   philosophy departments (Manley, Pummer, Chappell, Kagan, the Brown/Harvard
   Arete lineage). This is the *baseline the successor course revises*.
2. **The AI-safety field-building lineage** — the BlueDot suite
   ([AGI Strategy](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/bluedot-agi-strategy-course.md),
   [Frontier Governance](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/bluedot-frontier-ai-governance-course.md),
   [Future of AI](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/bluedot-future-of-ai-course.md),
   Technical), Ngo's
   [AGISF](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/ngo-agi-safety-fundamentals-curriculum.md)
   and [Alignment 201](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/ngo-alignment-201-curriculum.md),
   ARENA, the [80k syllabi](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/80k-ai-safety-syllabus.md),
   aisafety.com self-study, and researcher-facing strategy curricula
   ([MATS](../../readings/linked/mats-ai-safety-strategy-curriculum.md),
   [Redwood's AI futurism list](../../readings/linked/ai-futurism-reading-list.md)).
3. **The x-risk / longtermism course lineage** — Ord's
   [Precipice syllabus](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/precipice-syllabus.md)
   (textbook-anchored, 10 topics), Kagan's Yale seminar, the
   [full longtermism syllabus](../../readings/linked/ea-forum-full-syllabus-longtermism.md),
   and Stafforini's [index of longtermism courses](../../readings/linked/stafforini-longtermism-courses-list.md).
4. **Design & facilitation meta** — Bernardi on
   [why courses build fields](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/bernardi-courses-build-fields.md),
   Tessa Alexanian on [running a high-energy reading group](../../readings/linked/eaforum-how-to-run-high-energy-reading-group.md),
   the [2026 BlueDot reuse guide](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/running-bluedot-courses-2026.md),

   [EA syllabi list](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/ea-syllabi-and-teaching-materials.md),
   Stafforini's bibliographies).

## Traditions and where they disagree

The cluster is a standing argument between **two schools of course design**, plus
two quieter academic/researcher registers.

- **CEA intro-fellowship school** (Handbook, university EA courses): open with
  *awe and values*, sequence thinking-tools before causes, give criticism its own
  core session, make exercises about the participant's own life, keep the register
  low-pressure ("Sunday morning is for making pancakes"). Breadth across many
  causes is a feature. Recruitment is implicit.
- **BlueDot field-building school** (AGI Strategy, Governance): open with
  *urgency and executive quotes* (von der Leyen, Amodei, Pichai), bias toward
  action, *map-then-choose* (survey the threat landscape, then pick one deep-dive),
  end every course with a **produced artifact** (a policy briefing for a named
  decision-maker; a 6-month roadmap "with the expectation you'll act on it") and a
  **steelman mechanism** ("argue for and against proposals you didn't choose";
  "read across the spectrum, then defend a position in writing"). Recruitment is
  explicit — the course is upstream of a fellowship-and-grants pipeline. Bernardi's
  essay is the school's self-conscious manifesto: a curriculum "lays a stake in the
  ground," converts "interest overhang" into a field, and its gaps reveal "scoping
  documents you wish existed."

  The two schools disagree most sharply on **opener affect** (felt awe vs. felt
  urgency), **breadth vs. focus** (16-cause menu vs. pick-one-threat), and
  **exploration vs. funnel** (permission to not be a maximizer vs. "if you're here
  to add a credential, this isn't for you").

- **Academic-philosophy register** (Kagan, Manley, Pummer, Chappell, the
  Stafforini/Greaves/MacAskill university syllabi): canonical primary texts
  (Singer's *Famine*, Parfit *Reasons & Persons* pp. 453–4, Bostrom *Astronomical
  Waste*), seminar disagreement as the pedagogy, rigor over accessibility. Chappell's
  own retrospective flags the failure mode: an all-seminar, no-lecture course let
  students get "complacent in their dismissals" of EV and animal interests — a live
  argument against the pure discussion-cohort model the fellowships rely on.
- **Researcher-strategy register** (MATS, Redwood/Alexa Pan): opinionated,
  thesis-testing reading groups for people who will *do* the research — "consider
  whether you agree with their theses and how their predictions have fared." Not
  built for newcomers, but the source of the sharpest *current* AGI canon.

A cross-cutting disagreement runs on **affect toward the future**: BlueDot's AGI
Strategy course deliberately *opens Unit 1 with positive visions* (Fist et al.
*Preparing for Launch*, Bregman's *Utopia for Realists*, optionally Amodei's
*Machines of Loving Grace*, [solarpunk](../../readings/linked/solarpunk-vision.md))
before any threat — a bet that hope, not dread, is the better on-ramp. The Precipice
and most x-risk syllabi bet the opposite way.

## Standout readings

- **[BlueDot AGI Strategy — Unit 1](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/bluedot-agi-strategy-unit1.md)**
  — the single closest existing analogue to the target course: an actual per-unit
  reading list showing the positive-vision opener → drivers → *map-then-choose*
  threat menu → defense-in-depth → make-a-plan arc. *Role: structural template for
  the whole course.*
- **[BlueDot AGI Strategy — course page](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/bluedot-agi-strategy-course.md)**
  — the pacing/logistics/pipeline layer (intensive vs. part-time, pay-what-you-want,
  grants, alumni Slack) around that curriculum. *Role: operational design reference.*
- **[Ngo's AGISF curriculum](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/ngo-agi-safety-fundamentals-curriculum.md)**
  — the canonical field-building syllabus; model of connective narration (each week
  says what each reading is *for* and where they conflict), per-reading minute counts,
  surgical section-level cuts, week-0 prerequisite layering, choose-one-of-two
  exercises. *Role: the gold standard for weekly-unit craft.*
- **[Bernardi, How courses build fields](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/bernardi-courses-build-fields.md)**
  — the design meta-text: why to build a course at all, "interest overhang,"
  ~80% cohort vs. ~20% MOOC completion, curriculum gaps as a map of documents you
  must write. *Role: the rationale/strategy brief behind the whole project.*
- **[BlueDot Frontier AI Governance](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/bluedot-frontier-ai-governance-course.md)**
  — clearest example of artifact-producing units and the *take-a-side / stress-test*
  mechanism for teaching disagreement. *Role: source for final-unit and steelman design.*
- **[The Introductory EA Program](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/introductory-ea-program.md)**
  + **[2022 rebuild announcement](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/new-intro-fellowship-curriculum.md)**
  — the baseline being reimagined; the 8-session shape, per-session reading-time
  budgets, and the explicit "rebalance toward core concepts and thinking tools" that
  the successor inherits. *Role: the incumbent to beat.*
- **[Precipice syllabus](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/precipice-syllabus.md)**
  — Ord's 10-topic x-risk structure with a shared central text, key discussion
  questions per topic, and a highlighted-vs-supplementary reading split.
  *Role: model for a textbook-anchored, question-driven cause week.*
- **[80k: top resources for understanding AGI risk](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/80k-10-essential-resources-ai-risk.md)**
  — the tightest *current* AGI canon (Preparing for the Intelligence Explosion, AI
  2027, Situational Awareness, Most Important Century, Gradual Disempowerment, Taking
  AI Welfare Seriously, Machines of Loving Grace). *Role: the shortlist of actual
  post-2024 texts the course will assign.*
- **[Redwood AI Futurism reading list](../../readings/linked/ai-futurism-reading-list.md)**
  — opinionated core/extended split of the frames working researchers actually use;
  the sharpest strategy-tradition counterpart to 80k's list. *Role: rigor anchor for
  the macrostrategy/futurism thread.*
- **[Kagan, Ethics and the Future readings](../../readings/linked/kagan-ethics-and-future-readings.md)**
  — the academic rigor floor: Beckstead's dissertation, Greaves & MacAskill strong
  longtermism, Parfit, the social-discount-rate literature, staged by page count.
  *Role: the optional-layer depth anchor for the ethics weeks.*
- **[Manley, Changing the World syllabus](../../readings/linked/manley-changing-the-world-syllabus.md)**
  — the best academic model of *breadth across four causes* (global health, animals,
  environment, long-term survival) with comparative moral-significance-vs-tractability
  as the spine. *Role: template for teaching a wide cause menu without drowning.*
- **[MATS AI Safety Strategy curriculum](../../readings/linked/mats-ai-safety-strategy-curriculum.md)**
  — a threat-model discussion series (one big question per week, core readings,
  facilitator summaries) plus a candid post-mortem on what to cut. *Role: model for a
  discussion-driven strategy unit and honest iteration.*
- **[How to run a high-energy reading group](../../readings/linked/eaforum-how-to-run-high-energy-reading-group.md)**
  — the facilitation mechanics the syllabi assume but rarely state: how to pick
  readings and structure discussion so cohorts don't die. *Role: the facilitation
  companion.*
- **[EA syllabi and teaching materials](../../readings/adjacent-curricula/ea-syllabi-and-teaching-materials.md)**
  (Julia Wise) — the master index; ~40 courses with links, the single best link-mine
  for further syllabi. *Role: the map to everything not yet fetched.*

## Conspicuously missing

- **A curriculum that actually teaches MacAskill's non-safety AGI causes.** Nothing
  in the cluster structures AI character, AI welfare / digital minds, economic &
  political rights of AIs, AI persuasion / epistemic disruption, AI-enabled human
  coups, or space governance as *teaching units*. The 80k list names some as
  readings; no syllabus sequences them. This is the cluster's biggest hole and the
  course's whole reason to exist.
- **A Forethought-designed course or reading structure.** Forethought's *Preparing
  for the Intelligence Explosion* is the most-cited object-level text, but there's no
  Forethought-authored syllabus/course map. Search for a Forethought "grand challenges
  of AGI" reading list or curriculum.
- **A digital-minds / AI-welfare syllabus.** Search: NYU Center for Mind, Ethics &
  Policy reading list; a Sebo/Long "moral status of AI" seminar; Rob Long's teaching
  materials.
- **An s-risk / suffering-focused course.** No Center on Long-Term Risk (CLR) reading
  group or suffering-focused-ethics syllabus, despite s-risks being on the cause menu.
- **An epistemics / forecasting / rationality track.** The brief centers "the values
  and epistemic habits we'll need"; the cluster has only the In-Depth Program's single
  "How do you form beliefs?" week and scattered forecasting references. Search: a CFAR
  handbook-based curriculum, a Tetlock/forecasting syllabus, a "scout mindset" course.
- **A global-health-and-development course adapted to AGI.** The brief explicitly wants
  "how global health orgs plan to adapt to AI"; no GH&D syllabus is fetched (Stanford
  One for the World and Global Development Fellowship are only referenced in Wise's list).
- **An animal-welfare fellowship curriculum.** EA UCLA's Effective Animal Advocacy
  Fellowship and New Roots-style materials are referenced but not fetched — needed
  since factory farming stays on the menu.
- **Non-Western / Global South curricula.** Everything is Oxford/Cambridge/Bay
  Area/US-anchored. The FHI *AI & China* syllabus (Ding et al.) and any Indian,
  African, or Latin American EA/AI course are absent. Search for AI-governance or EA
  syllabi from GovAI-China, Concordia AI, or African university groups; an Audrey
  Tang / Taiwan digital-democracy teaching unit.
- **Scenario-immersion pedagogy.** AI 2027 is assigned as a *reading* but no
  curriculum teaches *through* an unfolding scenario (role-play, tabletop, a "someone
  in 2045 writes to you" exercise — the Handbook's best exercise, un-adapted here).
- **Chappell's current syllabus.** Only his 2016 version is fetched; the updated
  "Effective Altruism and the Future of Humanity" (utilitarianism.net-linked) is newer
  and directly on-theme.
- **CERI / Stanford "Preventing Human Extinction" intro syllabi.** Named in
  Stafforini's longtermism-courses list, not fetched — the two most course-shaped
  x-risk intros after Ord.
- **A progress-studies / "build for good" teaching module.** Palladium and solarpunk
  sit as loose readings; no Roots of Progress or progress-studies syllabus to anchor
  the positive-vision thread as a *unit*.
- **A facilitator discussion-guide / exercise bank.** The CEA facilitator guide
  (Notion) and the Handbook's exercise designs aren't captured as reusable artifacts;
  only Tessa's energy-focused post stands in.
