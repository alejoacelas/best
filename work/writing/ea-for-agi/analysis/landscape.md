# Landscape: the shape of the collection

The high-level view across all 22 cluster maps — the fault lines, where the collection
is deep vs thin, and the deduplicated gap list to hand to download agents. Detail lives
in [clusters/](clusters/); this is the map of the maps.

## 1. The shape of the space

The 22 clusters fall into five super-clusters:

- **Foundations** — how to think and what EA is: `ea-principles-epistemics`,
  `ea-intro-program`, `ea-meta-movement`, `adjacent-curricula`, `macaskill-worldview`.
- **The loss-of-control spine** — will the transition kill or disempower us:
  `ai-safety-core`, `joe-carlsmith`, `richard-ngo`, `macrostrategy`.
- **The preparedness turn** — MacAskill/Forethought's bet that the transition is broader
  than alignment: `forethought`, and the distinctive beyond-safety cause menu:
  `ai-character`, `ai-welfare-digital-minds`, `ai-rights-economics`,
  `power-concentration-democracy`, `persuasion-epistemics`, `s-risks`,
  `space-governance`, `biorisk`.
- **Classic causes adapting to AGI** — `animals-adapt-to-ai`, `global-health-adapts-to-ai`.
- **The movement's plumbing** — `funder-signals`, plus the affective register in
  `personal-reflections`.

### The recurring cross-cutting debates

Six tensions surface in cluster after cluster. They — not the cause boundaries — are the
real teachable architecture of the collection.

1. **Takeover-first vs preparedness-first.** The whole point of MacAskill/Forethought is
   that alignment is *not* the only variable (`forethought`, `macaskill-worldview`),
   against the safety-core/Carlsmith/MIRI view that loss-of-control dominates
   (`ai-safety-core`, `joe-carlsmith`). This is the course's founding argument with
   itself.
2. **Sudden takeover vs gradual disempowerment.** The single most cross-cutting frame in
   the collection: *Gradual Disempowerment* (Kulveit et al.) anchors `ai-rights-economics`,
   `power-concentration-democracy`, and `persuasion-epistemics`, and is the named gap in
   `ai-safety-core`, `forethought`, `joe-carlsmith`, and `global-health`. Emergent,
   villain-free loss of control is the modern reframe of the risk.
3. **Surviving vs flourishing / suffering-focused vs flourishing-focused.** Better Futures
   (`forethought`, `macaskill-worldview`, `macrostrategy`) pulls toward making a survived
   future great; the s-risk tradition (`s-risks`, and the s-risk threads in
   `ai-welfare`, `animals`, `ai-rights`) pulls toward the astronomical downside. Both
   argue the standard x-risk-first ordering is wrong, from opposite directions.
4. **Control vs otherness/autonomy.** How should we relate to minds with other values —
   dominate (corrigibility, the "hammer") or attune (Carlsmith's green, AI rights, deals
   with AIs)? Runs through `joe-carlsmith`, `ai-character`, `ai-rights-economics`,
   `ai-welfare`.
5. **Individual virtue vs institutional design.** Do we navigate the transition by
   cultivating better thinkers and better AI character (`ea-principles-epistemics`,
   `richard-ngo`, `ai-character`) or by building institutions that survive it
   (`power-concentration-democracy`, `forethought`, `space-governance`)? Ngo's
   "sociopolitical thinking" thread straddles both.
6. **Centralize vs decentralize / optimist vs pessimist economics.** Concentrate AI to
   control the race vs distribute it to prevent capture (`power-concentration-democracy`);
   comparative advantage saves wages vs capital eats everything (`ai-rights-economics`,
   `global-health`). Same underlying question — who ends up holding power — in two registers.

A seventh, meta-level tension is **inside-the-tent vs hostile-outside**: almost every
cluster is argued among people who already accept the frame, and almost every cluster
flags the same missing adversary.

## 2. Where the collection is deep vs thin

**Deep** (well-covered, often over-covered):

- **Loss-of-control argumentation.** `ai-safety-core` + `joe-carlsmith` + `richard-ngo`
  + `macrostrategy` triple- and quadruple-cover the takeover case, its failure stories,
  and the optimist/pessimist split. The single best-served part of the menu.
- **Forethought's own corpus.** PIE, Better Futures, the grand-challenges taxonomy,
  deals-with-AIs, viatopia — an entire cluster in one house style.
- **AI character theory.** The simulator/persona/role-play ontology and the lab-practice
  (constitutions, model specs, persona vectors) are richly represented.
- **AI welfare / consciousness science.** The Eleos/indicator-properties mainstream, the
  decision-under-uncertainty framing, empirical probes — a mature cluster.
- **Movement self-examination.** `ea-meta-movement` + `ea-principles-epistemics` +
  `funder-signals` cover EA's identity, epistemics, and money exhaustively.
- **The economics debate** (comparative advantage, explosive growth, Age of Em) and
  **power concentration / lock-in** are both strong.

**Thin** (present but under-anchored):

- **The classic causes under AGI.** `animals-adapt-to-ai` and `global-health-adapts-to-ai`
  exist but lean on EA-forum voices and Western funders; the on-the-ground and skeptic
  voices are missing.
- **s-risks** and **space-governance** are coherent but rest heavily on a few
  organizations (CLR/CRS; a handful of GPI/FHI physics papers) and lack primary sources.
- **Biorisk** is strong on the defense portfolio but thin on the founding controversies
  (H5N1, lab-leak) and the AI-as-actor bridge.

**Nearly absent — the systemic holes (each flagged in 15+ clusters):**

- **Non-Western / Global South voices.** Flagged in ~18 of 22 clusters. The collection is
  overwhelmingly Anglo-American analytic. This is the single largest gap.
- **Hostile outside critics.** TESCREAL/Gebru-Torres, Acemoglu, Srinivasan, e/acc — the
  adversaries a criticism session needs are named-missing in most clusters.
- **The empirical/on-the-ground register.** Labor-market RCTs, LMIC deployment trials,
  clinical companion-AI evidence, interpretability *results* — the collection argues in
  scenarios and models far more than in measured findings.
- **Primary sources cited everywhere but only linked.** Chalmers, Bostrom's
  *Superintelligence*, Ord's *Precipice*, Ngo's full first-principles argument, Parfit,
  Greaves & MacAskill — the canon is present as citation, not text.
