# Topic: Global health & development

*MacAskill menu item: global health & development — the existing cause area in the age of AGI, including how the transition can be structured to benefit the global poor as much as feasible.*

## What this topic covers

How the EA-adjacent global-health-and-development (GH&D) world is reacting to transformative
AI — both as a **tool** to deploy (AI diagnostics, tutoring, weather forecasting, and
research-workflow automation for the global poor) and as a **force that reshapes the terrain**
GH&D operates on (explosive-growth economics, labor displacement in LMICs, a shifting
biomedical R&D pipeline, and the collapse of "Big Aid"). Four sub-threads run through it:
(1) the **"AI for Good" deployment agenda** and its boosters — Gates, the World Bank, CGD,
Evidence Action, the AI Access Initiative; (2) the **macro / growth-and-inequality** question
of whether AI widens or closes the rich-poor gap and erases the development ladder; (3) a
**field-power critique** worrying that AI narrows global health the way RCTs did and rebuilds
colonial power asymmetries "backed not by empire, but by algorithms"; and (4) a
**progress-studies / abundance turn** arguing EA's measurement culture underweights the
systems and regulatory bottlenecks that stall life-saving vaccines.

## Voices and where they disagree

- **GiveWell / randomista rigor** (GiveWell 2026 search, red-teaming, Banerjee graduation,
  Kremer, worm wars). Measurement-first, direct-intervention, incrementalist. Treats AI first
  as a *workflow* tool and a *new emerging cause area to vet*, not a paradigm shift.
- **Progress studies / abundance** (Olvera, Trefethen, Statecraft, Gavi's 35-year vaccine).
  Argues the measurement culture has "systematic blind spots" — it scales bednets but ignores
  the regulatory and market-incentive failures that make poor-country vaccines *impossible*.
  Explicitly positions itself as EA's complement/critic.
- **Development-sector & decolonial critique** (Senanayake, Starr's "Big Aid Is Over").
  Power, ownership, wicked problems, country ownership over tools. The sharpest internal
  dissent: AI is "the new RCT," a powerful method that narrows ambition and crowns a new
  tech elite.
- **Growth economists** (Chad Jones, Klinova & Korinek, Brynjolfsson). Model AI's effect on
  long-run income and labor; split on whether LMICs leapfrog or get left behind.
- **Big-philanthropy / multilateral institutional voice** (Gates/Suzman, World Bank, CGD).
  Access-and-equity framing, co-design, digital-infrastructure gaps. Optimistic-but-cautious;
  the establishment register.
- **EA-funder strategic** (Coefficient/Open Phil GHW-in-era-of-TAI RFP). The flagship attempt to
  *institutionalize* the question: what should GHW funders do now that AI might compress the
  21st century or shrink LMIC tax bases?
- **Frontier-lab techno-optimism** (Amodei's "Machines of Loving Grace"). The maximalist
  deploy case: AGI-compressed biomedicine plus intentional effort could close the rich-poor
  health gap within decades. The vision the critics and funders are all reacting to.

The live disagreements: **deploy-now optimism** (Amodei, Gates, World Bank, Evidence Action) vs.
**power-critique skepticism** (Senanayake: AI is poorly suited to trust-and-legitimacy
problems). **Widen vs. close the gap** (the CGD pieces themselves split; Klinova stresses
displacement). **Direct health vs. systems/growth** (the reopened EA "global health vs.
development" question — does AI-driven growth make direct interventions look small?). And a
meta-fight over whether EA's **measurement discipline** is its great strength or its blind spot.

## Best readings

1. **[Coefficient Giving RFP: Global Health and Wellbeing in an Era of Transformative AI](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/NAaZTyx54bLYzpZg8/coefficient-giving-rfp-global-health-and-wellbeing-in-an-era)** (Coefficient Giving / Open Philanthropy, EA Forum). The flagship EA funder's own statement of how global-health philanthropy adapts to TAI: "compressed 21st century" biomedicine, R&D bottlenecks shifting to regulation and distribution, the development ladder losing a rung. *Role: anchor essay.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/openphil-ghw-transformative-ai-rfp.md))

2. **[Machines of Loving Grace](https://www.darioamodei.com/essay/machines-of-loving-grace)** (Dario Amodei). The canonical deploy-optimist anchor — the "compressed 21st century" essay the RFP itself cites, with a full section arguing AI can close (not widen) the rich-poor health gap. Long; the "Biology and health" and "Economic development and poverty" sections are the assignable core. *Role: deploy-optimist anchor; pairs against Senanayake.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/machines-of-loving-grace.md))

3. **[Algorithms and Authority: How AI Is Re-shaping Power in Global Health](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/veSyRwTKrRDyhDmJH/algorithms-and-authority-how-ai-is-re-shaping-power-in)** (Tony Senanayake, EA Forum). The best critic, and from a Global-South nonprofit leader who *uses* RCTs and AI. "Is AI the new RCT?" AI narrows which problems are visible and fundable, and rebuilds power asymmetries. *Role: best critic.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/algorithms-and-authority-power-global-health.md))

4. **[AI-based Clinical Decision Support for Primary Care: A Real-World Study](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.16947)** (Penda Health & OpenAI; accessible [OpenAI writeup](https://openai.com/index/ai-clinical-copilot-penda-health/)). The on-the-ground case the rest of the list only talks about: an LLM copilot in real Kenyan clinics, ~40,000 patient visits, 16% fewer diagnostic errors and 13% fewer treatment errors. *Role: vivid, measured LMIC-deployment case.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/penda-health-ai-consult-kenya.md))

5. **[Why Effective Altruism and Global Health Needs More Progress Studies and Abundance](https://abio.substack.com/p/why-effective-altruism-and-global)** (Abi Olvera, Substack). The accessible bridge: personal, concrete (the 35-year malaria vaccine, GSK's shelved TB vaccine), reframes EA's measurement blind spot — the bottlenecks AI-accelerated R&D will slam into. "Why can't I be both?" *Role: accessible on-ramp; opens the systems-vs-direct axis.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/ea-global-health-progress-studies-abundance.md))

6. **[AI Red Teaming at GiveWell](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/7tXSRGJJ3FxhXjmfL/ai-red-teaming-at-givewell-what-we-ve-learned-and-where-we-d)** (Brendan Phillips, GiveWell / EA Forum). A rigorous org putting AI into its own cost-effectiveness workflow and reporting the unglamorous truth (~85% of AI critiques useless, quantitative estimates unreliable). Ships its error bars. *Role: concrete case study of honest adoption.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/givewell-ai-red-teaming.md))

7. **[The First Principles Guiding Our Work with AI](https://www.gatesfoundation.org/ideas/articles/artificial-intelligence-ai-development-principles)** (Mark Suzman, Gates Foundation). The largest GH&D funder's institutional stance: access, equity, co-design, "beneficiaries participating in development." *Role: establishment-philanthropy voice; pairs against Senanayake's power critique.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/gates-ai-development-principles.md))

8. **[Tipping the Scales: AI's Dual Impact on Developing Nations](https://blogs.worldbank.org/en/digital-development/tipping-the-scales--ai-s-dual-impact-on-developing-nations)** (World Bank blogs). Accessible macro on-ramp with vivid stats (45 health workers per 10,000 recommended; poor countries have a quarter of that; ChatGPT to 100M users in two months). Leapfrogging hope vs. divide-widening risk in one frame. *Role: accessible dual-impact macro overview.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/worldbank-ai-dual-impact-developing-nations.md))

9. **[Three Reasons Why AI May Widen Global Inequality](https://www.cgdev.org/blog/three-reasons-why-ai-may-widen-global-inequality)** (Schellekens & Skilling, Center for Global Development). The accessible pessimist counterpart to the World Bank's dual-impact frame: rich countries capture the gains, poor countries lack shock absorbers, and automation kicks away the export-manufacturing ladder (≈60% of Bangladesh garment jobs by 2030). *Role: accessible inequality-pessimist case.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/cgd-three-reasons-ai-widen-inequality.md))

10. **[Evidence Action Incubates AI for Good Project](https://www.evidenceaction.org/newsroom/evidence-action-incubates-comprehensive-analysis-of-ai-applications-in-global-development)** (Evidence Action; [full cross-sector analysis](https://theaiaccessinitiative.substack.com/p/ai-for-good-cross-sector-analysis) by the AI Access Initiative). The builder's concrete intervention map: 30+ AI interventions scored for scale-readiness in LMICs, advised by Kremer and Amodei. TB X-ray diagnosis, AI tutoring RCTs, early-warning systems. *Role: vivid "here's what actually gets built" deployment map.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/evidence-action-newsroom-ai-analysis.md), [analysis local](../../readings/linked/ai-access-initiative-ai-for-good-cross-sector.md))

11. **[AI and Shared Prosperity](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2105.08475)** (Katya Klinova & Anton Korinek, AIES). Rigorous deep-dive on labor-displacement risk and how AI development choices bear on low- and middle-income workers. *Role: rigorous deep-dive on the inequality thread.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/klinova-ai-and-shared-prosperity.md))

12. **[AI and the Economic Future](https://web.stanford.edu/~chadj/AIandEconomicFuture.pdf)** (Chad Jones, Stanford). Growth-theory lens on what AGI does to long-run incomes, poor countries included. *Role: data/evidence; the academic floor beneath the "explosive growth ends poverty" claim.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/chad-jones-ai-economic-future.md))

13. **[Why Would Anyone Be Against Lifesaving Malaria Bednets?](https://www.vox.com/future-perfect/2024/1/25/24047975/malaria-mosquito-bednets-prevention-fishing-marc-andreessen)** (Dylan Matthews, Vox Future Perfect). High wow-density narrative tying EA global health directly to the AI culture war (Andreessen/e-acc vs. EA), with the arithmetic shown (282M nets → ~1.58M lives in one year). *Role: vivid narrative; connects the topic to the AI-accelerationism fight.* ([local](../../readings/linked/vox-malaria-bednets-andreessen.md))

14. **[(How) Do the Poorest Use AI?](https://www.cgdev.org/blog/how-do-poorest-use-ai)** (Daniel Bjorkegren, Center for Global Development). Sharp, empirical, neglected question: usage reports track knowledge workers, not the billions whose queries are health/agriculture/government. *Role: data/evidence; grounds the access debate in the neglected-user perspective.* ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/cgd-how-do-poorest-use-ai.md))

15. **[Big Aid Is Over](https://ssir.org/articles/entry/big-aid-is-over)** (Kevin Starr, Stanford Social Innovation Review). The funding-landscape shock (2025 aid collapse) that recontextualizes the whole topic: government as payer, country ownership, the end of Big Aid. *Role: sets the post-aid paradigm the AI question lands in.* ([local](../../readings/linked/big-aid-is-over.md))

**Also strong:** GiveWell's [How We're Searching for the Best Ways to Help in 2026](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/LotTxJF3PiHw6iLDq/how-we-re-searching-for-the-best-ways-to-help-in-2026) ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/givewell-searching-best-ways-2026.md)) — the status-quo baseline, where "AI applications to global health" is one emerging area among malaria, nutrition, and water; assign only if you want the incrementalist register documented rather than described. Jacob Trefethen's [10 Technologies That Won't Exist in 5 Years](https://blog.jacobtrefethen.com/10-technologies-that-wont-exist-in-5-yrs) ([local](../../readings/linked/trefethen-10-health-technologies-5-years.md)) — vivid on R&D bottlenecks from inside science funding, but a general progress-studies argument; the AGI-specific version of its point is already in the Coefficient RFP. Dany Bahar & Amelia Michael's CGD counterpoint [A New Industrial Revolution: Will AI Widen or Close the Income Gap?](https://www.cgdev.org/blog/new-industrial-revolution-will-ai-widen-or-close-income-gap-between-rich-and-poor-countries) ([local](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/cgd-new-industrial-revolution-income-gap.md)) — the leapfrogging-optimist reply to reading 9.

## Conspicuously missing

- **Forethought-style macrostrategy on global poverty under AGI** — nothing here takes the
  "what happens to the global poor in a post-AGI transition" question at that altitude
  (gradual disempowerment, benefit-sharing).
- **A concrete "Gavi for AI" / global benefit-sharing institutional proposal** — the RFP gestures
  at "a Gavi- or Global-Fund-style institution ensuring AI benefits reach the poor," but no
  reading actually designs one.
- **Lant Pritchett** (or a comparable growth-over-RCTs development economist) — the definitive
  voice on "national development beats direct health interventions," which would anchor the
  reopened health-vs-development axis far better than the thin EA-forum question currently
  standing in for it ([why-eas-focus-global-health-over-development](../../readings/global-health-adapts-to-ai/why-eas-focus-global-health-over-development.md)).
- **The ICT4D / "tech for good" failure base rate** — Kentaro Toyama's *Geek Heresy*, One Laptop
  Per Child, or a history of over-promised development tech. The skeptic's empirical ammunition
  against AI-for-good hype is absent; Senanayake critiques in the abstract without the graveyard.
- **A WHO / multilateral-health governance voice** — e.g. WHO's 2024 guidance on large multimodal
  models in health. The World Bank and CGD speak; the actual global-health governance body does not.
- **The within-EA rebuttal to progress studies** — the list platforms the abundance case
  (Olvera, Trefethen) without the randomista/measurement defense against it. Needs the other
  side of the Hauke-Hillebrandt / "growth vs. randomista development" debate stated in EA terms.
- **Cash transfers as the response to AI labor displacement** — given the displacement thesis, a
  piece connecting GiveDirectly-style cash / UBI-in-LMICs to an automated-labor future is a
  natural bridge that's missing.
- **An awe / long-run-health-progress opener** — Charles Kenny's *Getting Better*, Our World in
  Data on child-mortality collapse, or a smallpox-style epic. Everything here opens in argument
  or funder-memo register; the emotional on-ramp is missing.
