# Topic: S-risks

*MacAskill menu item: s-risks.*

## What this topic covers

S-risks — "risks of astronomical suffering," futures that contain vastly more
suffering than has existed on Earth, sometimes framed as outcomes *worse than
extinction*. The topic runs along four braided threads: (1) the **concept and
taxonomy** — what s-risks are, how they're classified (agential / incidental /
natural), and why their probability isn't negligible; (2) the **value theory** that
motivates prioritizing them — suffering-focused ethics, negative utilitarianism,
tranquilism, and the objections to these; (3) the **s-risk-from-AI** mechanism —
near-miss alignment, suffering subroutines, mindcrime, and the claim that solving
alignment isn't enough; and (4) the **cooperation/conflict** research program — CLR's
multi-agent agenda, commitment races, surrogate goals, and evidential cooperation,
where s-risk reduction becomes a concrete technical field. A fifth practical thread
runs underneath: **risk factors and interventions** (malevolent actors, moral circle
expansion, better politics, movement-building).

## Voices and where they disagree

Three organizational "schools," plus a mainstream bridge and a popularizer layer:

- **CRS (Center for Reducing Suffering)** — Tobias Baumann, Magnus Vinding, Simon
  Knutsson. The values-and-advocacy wing: suffering-focused ethics as the moral
  foundation, broad interventions (moral circle expansion, better institutions),
  s-risks framed as robust across many value systems. Baumann's *Avoiding the Worst*
  is the field's first book.
- **CLR (Center on Long-Term Risk, formerly Foundational Research Institute)** —
  Lukas Gloor, David Althaus, Jesse Clifton, Caspar Oesterheld, Anthony DiGiovanni,
  Daniel Kokotajlo, Max Daniel. The AI-and-decision-theory wing: cooperative AI,
  game/decision theory, commitment races, downside-focused cause prioritization.
  More technical, more AI-conflict-centric.
- **Brian Tomasik / reducing-suffering.org** — the intellectual grandfather. Negative-
  leaning utilitarianism, wild-animal suffering, suffering subroutines, near-miss AI,
  values-spreading over extinction-risk. Idiosyncratic, foundational, cited everywhere.
- **Mainstream EA bridge** — 80,000 Hours problem profile, Cooperative AI Foundation
  (Hammond, Dafoe et al.). Legible framing, lower probability estimates, ties s-risk
  to accepted AI-safety and great-power-conflict work.
- **Popularizers** — Rational Animations (Allen Liu), LessWrong explainers. Vivid
  on-ramps.

Live disagreements:
- **Does the case depend on suffering-focused ethics?** CRS/CLR insiders (Baumann,
  DiGiovanni) argue s-risk reduction is robust under moral uncertainty; outside
  critics (Chappell) see it as leaning on a negative axiology that "valorizes the
  void." This is the load-bearing crux.
- **How probable?** 80k judges worst-case s-risks "very low, much lower than
  extinction"; CLR/CRS argue "non-negligible" and disjunctive. Same facts, different
  bottom line.
- **NU vs classical utilitarianism** — the Ord ↔ Knutsson exchange (world-destruction
  argument, procreation asymmetry, minimalist axiologies).
- **Should we even prioritize AI?** Vinding's lengthy critique argues values-spreading
  beats AI-specific work — an internal dissent.
- **Extinction vs quality of future** — DiGiovanni's critique of "the expected value
  of extinction-risk reduction is positive"; Tomasik's values-spreading-over-extinction.
- **Can moral circle expansion backfire?** A contrarian thread argues wider circles
  could *increase* suffering (more beings deemed worth simulating/creating).

## Best readings

The top five fit in roughly two hours; deep-dives are marked in their roles.

1. **[S-risks: An introduction](https://centerforreducingsuffering.org/research/intro/)** (Tobias Baumann, Center for Reducing Suffering). The canonical short intro — the one required reading on the lens. *Role: anchor essay.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/s-risks-an-introduction.md))

2. **[S-risks problem profile](https://80000hours.org/problem-profiles/s-risks/)** (80,000 Hours). The mainstream-EA framing, with explicit probability skepticism — how a careful outsider sees the field. *Role: accessible bridge / sympathetic skeptic.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/80k-s-risks-problem-profile.md))

3. **[S-Risks: Fates Worse Than Extinction](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/bDoGbZX7Jzjr2x3Aa/s-risks-fates-worse-than-extinction)** (Allen Liu, Rational Animations / LessWrong). Black-Mirror-style vividness; the gentlest entry point for newcomers. *Role: vivid narrative on-ramp.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/s-risks-fates-worse-than-extinction.md))

4. **[Arguments for and against a focus on s-risks](https://centerforreducingsuffering.org/arguments-for-and-against-a-focus-on-s-risks)** (Tobias Baumann, Center for Reducing Suffering). Baumann's own balance sheet — the steelman-both-sides piece. *Role: in-house balance sheet.* ([local](../../readings/linked/arguments-for-and-against-a-focus-on-s-risks.md))

5. **[Astronomical suffering from slightly misaligned artificial intelligence](https://reducing-suffering.org/near-miss/)** (Brian Tomasik, reducing-suffering.org). Near-miss AI could be worse than total misalignment, and spreading your values can backfire — the sharpest single s-risk-from-AI argument. *Role: core argument, short.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/astronomical-suffering-slightly-misaligned-ai.md))

6. **[Solving alignment isn't enough for a flourishing future](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/oEpGBqDCo2z5pbpvA/solving-alignment-isn-t-enough-for-a-flourishing-future)** (Brauner & Chan, EA Forum). Even AGI aligned to *task preferences* doesn't yield a good future — a distinct, non-doom argument that ties s-risks to the AGI transition. *Role: best tie to the fellowship's frame.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/solving-alignment-isnt-enough.md))

7. **[Reducing Risks of Astronomical Suffering: A Neglected Priority](https://longtermrisk.org/reducing-risks-of-astronomical-suffering-a-neglected-priority/)** (Althaus & Gloor, Center on Long-Term Risk). The 2016 founding paper: the s-risk-vs-x-risk distinction and the psychology of why the future looks better than it is. *Role: rigorous deep-dive.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/reducing-risks-astronomical-suffering-neglected-priority.md))

8. **[Beginner's guide to reducing s-risks](https://longtermrisk.org/beginners-guide-to-reducing-s-risks/)** (Anthony DiGiovanni, Center on Long-Term Risk). The long, careful CLR on-ramp — the single best deep-dive if you assign only one long piece; skimmable via its summary. *Role: rigorous deep-dive / CLR research tradition.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/beginners-guide-to-reducing-s-risks.md))

9. **[Introduction to suffering-focused ethics](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/wLEyoncFCmDyqPA5f/introduction-to-suffering-focused-ethics-1)** (Knutsson & Vinding, Center for Reducing Suffering). The value theory motivating the field, including Buddhist strands. *Role: values anchor.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/introduction-to-suffering-focused-ethics.md))

10. **[Don't Valorize the Void](https://www.goodthoughts.blog/p/dont-valorize-the-void)** (Richard Y Chappell, Good Thoughts). The sharpest outside-EA philosophical rebuttal of the suffering-focused axiology — read against #9 to run the debate with both sides. *Role: best critic of the core value theory.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/chappell-dont-valorize-the-void.md))

11. **[The Future Might Not Be So Great](https://www.sentienceinstitute.org/blog/the-future-might-not-be-so-great)** (Jacy Reese Anthis, Sentience Institute). The one defended quantitative case that the long-term future's expected value may be net-negative — the piece to cite against "purely speculative." *Role: data/evidence.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/anthis-future-might-not-be-so-great.md))

12. **[Reducing long-term risks from malevolent actors](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/LpkXtFXdsRd4rG8Kb/reducing-long-term-risks-from-malevolent-actors)** (Althaus & Baumann, EA Forum). The most concrete, tractable, mainstream-legible intervention, linking s-risks to power concentration. *Role: the "what you'd actually do" reading.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/reducing-long-term-risks-from-malevolent-actors.md))

13. **[Multi-Agent Risks from Advanced AI](https://www.cooperativeai.com/post/new-report-multi-agent-risks-from-advanced-ai)** (Cooperative AI Foundation; 50+ authors incl. Dafoe, Conitzer). Legible, current bridge from s-risk concerns to peer-reviewed AI safety. *Role: data/evidence; mainstream bridge.* ([local](../../readings/s-risks/multi-agent-risks-from-advanced-ai.md))

14. **[Why Altruists Should Perhaps Not Prioritize Artificial Intelligence: A Lengthy Critique](https://magnusvinding.com/2018/09/18/why-altruists-should-perhaps-not-prioritize-artificial-intelligence-a-lengthy-critique)** (Magnus Vinding). Point-by-point internal dissent: values-spreading beats AI-specific work. *Role: in-house dissent.* ([local](../../readings/linked/why-altruists-should-perhaps-not-prioritize-ai.md))

15. **[Tranquilism](https://longtermrisk.org/tranquilism)** (Lukas Gloor, Center on Long-Term Risk). Gloor's axiology — suffering matters, contentment suffices — the crux value-theory piece one level deeper than the SFE intro. *Role: philosophical deep-dive.* ([local](../../readings/linked/tranquilism.md))

## Also strong

[Avoiding the Worst: How to Prevent a Moral Catastrophe](https://centerforreducingsuffering.org/research/avoiding-the-worst/) (Tobias Baumann) is the field's first book and the natural next step after #1 for anyone who wants the full treatment ([local](../../readings/s-risks/avoiding-the-worst-book.md)). Magnus Vinding's [Suffering-Focused Ethics: Defense and Implications](https://magnusvinding.com/2020/05/31/suffering-focused-ethics-defense-and-implications/) is the book-length version of the value theory in #9 ([local](../../readings/s-risks/suffering-focused-ethics-defense-and-implications.md)). Jamie Harris's [Reasons for optimism about measuring malevolence](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/SFkRrs2BCa5ieeMej/reasons-for-optimism-about-measuring-malevolence-to-tackle-x) operationalizes the malevolence-screening thread of #12 ([local](../../readings/s-risks/measuring-malevolence-x-and-s-risks.md)).

## Conspicuously missing

Specific enough to go search for:

1. **Toby Ord's original "Why I'm not a negative utilitarian."** The collection has
   Knutsson's *reply* and the world-destruction argument, but not the canonical
   philosophical critique they answer. You can't run the debate with only one side.
2. **Nick Bostrom primaries on "mindcrime" and infinite/hyperexistential ethics.**
   Mindcrime, "astronomical waste," and infinite ethics are cited across nearly every
   reading but no Bostrom text is in the collection.
3. **A non-Western / Buddhist primary source.** The SFE intro *mentions* Shantideva
   and Mahayana suffering-focus, but every actual text is Western-analytic. A primary
   Buddhist-ethics reading (or a scholarly piece on Buddhism and suffering) would add
   the tradition the collection claims but doesn't show.
4. **A digital-minds / artificial-sentience primary tying s-risk to AI welfare.** The
   "suffering subroutines / mindcrime" link leans on this but there's no Jeff Sebo,
   Bostrom & Shulman ("Propositions Concerning Digital Minds and Society"), or NYU
   digital-minds primary here — it lives in the AI-welfare topic.
5. **Stable totalitarianism as an s-risk.** 80k's "risks of stable totalitarianism"
   profile and Bryan Caplan's "The Totalitarian Threat" are referenced as the
   canonical worse-than-extinction lock-in scenario but neither is collected.
6. **Great-power-conflict / international-relations primary.** Agential s-risks and
   the whole cooperative-AI agenda rest on conflict dynamics, but there's no primary
   on war/escalation as the driver (only CLR's abstract game-theory framing).
7. **A forecasting-platform estimate of s-risk probability itself.** Anthis (#11)
   defends a number for the future being net-negative, but no Metaculus-style or
   explicit-estimate piece exists for the probability of astronomical suffering.
8. **A voice outside the CRS/CLR/Tomasik triangle.** Jonathan Leighton (OPIS, *The
   Battle for Compassion*) or David Pearce (Hedonistic Imperative / abolitionist
   project) — suffering-focused thinkers with a different institutional lineage.
9. **A 2024–25 piece connecting s-risks to concrete frontier developments** (agentic
   AI deployments, AI-welfare policy at labs). The newest collected framing pieces
   are mostly pre-2024 except the multi-agent report.
