# Topic: Biorisk

*MacAskill cause-menu item: biorisk.*

## What this topic covers

Global catastrophic biological risk — engineered pandemics as the near-term
extinction-scale threat, and how AI changes the picture. The topic runs on four
sub-threads: (1) the **threat model** — why a deliberately-released engineered pathogen
could exceed any natural one, and whether that's imminent or overstated; (2) the
**AIxBio question** — do LLMs and biological design tools actually uplift a bad actor,
and by how much, measured now; (3) the **defense portfolio** — the concrete,
pathogen-agnostic megaprojects (metagenomic early warning, far-UVC, super-PPE, DNA
synthesis screening, refuges) plus the defensive-acceleration ("d/acc") case that make
civilization robust regardless of the pathogen; and (4) **governance, norms, and
field-building** — information hazards, the toothless Biological Weapons Convention,
dual-use research policy, and how a newcomer enters the field. The center of gravity is
the MIT/SecureBio "delay, detect, defend" program; almost everything else either feeds
it, funds it, or disputes its urgency.

## Voices and where they disagree

Seven distinct voices, with two live disagreements running through them.

- **The Esvelt / SecureBio "delay-detect-defend" school.** Threat model: within a
  decade tens of thousands can single-handedly ignite a pandemic, so the answer is
  technical defense plus strict information-hazard discipline (Esvelt won't name the
  candidate viruses). The organizing frame the rest of the topic orbits.
- **EA cause-prioritization / longtermism.** ITN analysis, scalable megaprojects,
  "biosecurity is ~10–100x less important than AI but far more tractable"
  (Snyder-Beattie & Alley say so explicitly).
- **The Johns Hopkins CHS / NTI / Bipartisan-Commission policy establishment.**
  All-hazards public-health framing, tabletop exercises, tech-priority roadmaps.
  Overlaps heavily with EA but predates and outsizes it.
- **AIxBio security / governance studies.** Sandbrink's LLM-vs-BDT distinction (lowers
  barriers vs. raises the ceiling) is the load-bearing concept; CSET and RAND are the
  sober "don't over-index on AI" wing.
- **The skeptics / "germy paradox."** Tacit knowledge and organizational failure — not
  information — have historically stopped bioweapons; Aum Shinrikyo had money and
  scientists and still failed.
- **Techno-optimist / d/acc.** Defensive acceleration: out-build the offense with
  better air, vaccines, and detection rather than restrict.
- **Frontier labs, self-reporting.** Anthropic activating ASL-3, OpenAI's bio
  early-warning system, and the evals they publish on their own models.

**Disagreement 1 — how much does AI uplift biorisk *right now*?** RAND's red-team found
"no statistically significant difference" and CSET says AI isn't needed for biorisk at
all; Esvelt, Sandbrink, the frontier labs, and the MIT classroom exercise say the
trajectory is alarming. The 2026 uplift evals adjudicate on current evidence and ask
whether the evals even measure the real thing.

**Disagreement 2 — are bioweapons hard?** The germy-paradox skeptics (Ben
Ouagrham-Gormley, Georgia Ray, Lentzos/Marris) say the tacit-knowledge barrier is real
and durable; the delay-detect-defend school says AI is precisely what dissolves that
barrier, so the historical comfort is obsolete. This is the topic's deepest fault line.

A third, quieter tension: **natural vs. engineered framing** — institutions built for
natural outbreaks are blind to deliberate, surveillance-evading design.

## Best readings

The top of the list is assignable in ~2h; deep-dives are marked.

1. **[Delay, Detect, Defend](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/3EvLvjfsujzjjXTJM/delay-detect-defend-preparing-for-a-future-in-which)** (Kevin Esvelt, EA Forum). The three-word framework — delay access, detect via untargeted sequencing, defend all-hazards; short, memorable, structures the whole field. *Role: anchor / organizing frame.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/delay-detect-defend.md))

2. **[Preventing catastrophic pandemics](https://80000hours.org/problem-profiles/preventing-catastrophic-pandemics/)** (80,000 Hours). The flagship problem profile and standard EA case; hedged, footnoted, links its own critics. *Role: cause-area anchor essay.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/80k-preventing-catastrophic-pandemics.md))

3. **[A beginner's introduction to AI-driven biorisk](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/RidKuyF6EdK8AEhYn/a-beginner-s-introduction-to-ai-driven-biorisk-large)** (EA Forum). Written by a non-scientist fresh out of BlueDot; glossary-first, walks LLM vs BDT and the MIT chatbot exercise for readers with zero life-science background. *Role: accessible on-ramp.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/beginners-intro-ai-driven-biorisk.md))

4. **[Artificial intelligence and biological misuse](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.13952)** (Sandbrink, arXiv). The canonical LLM-vs-BDT distinction (lower barriers vs. raise the ceiling); the single most-cited conceptual frame in the AIxBio thread. *Role: rigorous conceptual anchor.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/sandbrink-ai-biological-misuse.md))

5. **[SecureBio's AI Team: An Overview of Our Biorisk Evaluations](https://securebio.org/blog/securebios-ai-team-overview-biorisk-evaluations/)** (SecureBio). The concrete instrument behind the whole uplift debate — how the org actually measures a frontier model's bio-capability (virology troubleshooting, protocol tasks, weaponization pathways). Read this to know what "the evals" even are. *Role: data / primary source.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/securebio-ai-team-biorisk-evaluations.md))

6. **[The State of Bio-Uplift Research in Mid-2026](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/S6ydgTdTr8sXkfs9x/the-state-of-bio-uplift-research-in-mid-2026)** (EA Forum). What the actual 2026 uplift evals (virology test, ProtocolQA) show; grounds the disagreement in fresh evidence and asks whether the evals measure the real thing. *Role: data / currency.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/state-of-bio-uplift-research-2026.md))

7. **[Does AI Increase the Operational Risk of Biological Attacks?](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA2977-2.html)** (RAND). The empirical skeptic: a red-team RCT finding no measurable LLM uplift. The best counterweight to the alarm. *Role: best empirical critic / evidence.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/rand-operational-risks-ai-bio-attacks.md))

8. **[AI and Biorisk: An Explainer](https://cset.georgetown.edu/publication/ai-and-biorisk-an-explainer/)** (CSET, Georgetown). Neutral "biorisk is already possible without AI; don't let AI-focus crowd out foundational gaps." *Role: de-hyping balance.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/cset-ai-and-biorisk-explainer.md))

9. **[What would it take for AI to disempower us? (Ryan Greenblatt)](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/8mqbLdhyobHMaD4rd/what-would-it-take-for-ai-to-disempower-us-ryan-greenblatt)** (80,000 Hours, EA Forum). The AI-as-actor pathway the rest of the topic omits: a misaligned or autonomous AI *itself* pursuing a bioweapon as a takeover route. The bridge from biorisk to AI safety proper — the reason this topic sits in an AGI course. *Role: connects biorisk to AI safety.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/greenblatt-ai-disempower-bioweapons.md))

10. **[Concrete Biosecurity Projects (some of which could be big)](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/u5JesqQ3jdLENXBtB/concrete-biosecurity-projects-some-of-which-could-be-big-1)** (Snyder-Beattie & Alley, EA Forum). The canonical megaproject list — early-detection center, super-PPE, refuges, BWC strengthening. The "boring tractability list." *Role: tractability list / from-doom-to-work.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/concrete-biosecurity-projects.md))

11. **[My techno-optimism](https://vitalik.eth.limo/general/2023/11/27/techno_optimism.html)** (Vitalik Buterin). The defensive-acceleration counter-voice; a non-EA outsider arguing you build your way out. *Role: distinct voice / hopeful counterpoint.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/vitalik-my-techno-optimism-dacc.md))

12. **[The Germy Paradox: An Introduction](https://eukaryotewritesblog.com/2019/09/23/the-germy-paradox-an-introduction)** (Eukaryote Writes Blog). "If bioweapons are so cheap and deadly, where are they?" — the accessible skeptic on-ramp, vivid and disarming; walks the Aum Shinrikyo failure as evidence. *Role: best accessible skeptic.* ([local](../../readings/linked/eukaryote-germy-paradox-intro.md))

13. **[Synthetic Biology and Biosecurity: Challenging the "Myths"](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4139924/)** (Lentzos & Marris, *Frontiers in Public Health*). The sharpest structural critic of the whole framing: a sociology-of-science argument that the securitized "GCBR" narrative overstates risk and militarizes the life sciences. The best critic of the core thesis. *Role: best critic of the framing.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/lentzos-marris-synbio-biosecurity-myths.md))

14. **[Technical Report on Mirror Bacteria: Feasibility and Risks](https://stacks.stanford.edu/file/druid:cv716pj4036/Technical%20Report%20on%20Mirror%20Bacteria%20Feasibility%20and%20Risks.pdf)** (Stanford). The mirror-life scenario: a vivid, concrete, novel existential threat scientists themselves flagged and paused. *Role: rigorous deep-dive / novel-threat narrative.* ([local](../../readings/linked/mirror-bacteria-technical-report.md)) — paired with the accessible **[A whole new thing that could end the world](https://www.vox.com/future-perfect/393048/mirror-life-mirror-bacteria-existential-risk-science-biology-apocalypse)** (Kelsey Piper, Vox), the readable on-ramp to the same story. ([local](../../readings/linked/vox-mirror-life.md))

15. **[On-Ramps Into Biosecurity — A Model](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/QeEgktwh2FQyot9Jw/on-ramps-for-biosecurity-a-model)** (EA Forum). The hear-it → learn-it → project → full-time pathway with real orgs; the what-do-I-do-Monday reading. *Role: careers / action.* ([local](../../readings/biorisk/on-ramps-into-biosecurity-model.md))

**Also strong.** [Practically-A-Book Review: Rootclaim $100,000 Lab Leak Debate](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/practically-a-book-review-rootclaim) (Scott Alexander) is the careful, non-partisan probabilistic treatment of the COVID origins question — the most politically salient biosecurity event of the era and a live case in gain-of-function governance ([local](../../readings/biorisk/acx-rootclaim-covid-lab-leak-debate.md)). [Can large language models democratize access to dual-use biotechnology?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.03809) (Soice, Rocha, Cordova, Specter & Esvelt) is the actual MIT chatbot study the AIxBio readings keep alluding to ([local](../../readings/biorisk/soice-llm-democratize-dual-use-biotech.md)). [Existential Risk and Cost-Effective Biosecurity](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5576214/) (Millett & Snyder-Beattie) is the shown-arithmetic $/life-year estimate ([local](../../readings/biorisk/millett-snyder-beattie-cost-effective-biosecurity.md)). [Reasons to be pessimistic (and optimistic) on the future of biosecurity](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/hHTAYPFuxL38d7YXt/reasons-to-be-pessimistic-and-optimistic-on-the-future-of) (EA Forum) is a 2026 two-sided ledger ([local](../../readings/biorisk/pessimistic-optimistic-future-biosecurity.md)), and [Aum Shinrikyo](https://cnas.org/publications/reports/aum-shinrikyo-insights-into-how-terrorists-develop-biological-and-chemical-weapons) (CNAS) is the deep case study behind the germy paradox ([local](../../readings/linked/cnas-aum-shinrikyo.md)).

## Conspicuously missing

- **A non-Western / Global South voice.** The reading list admits its US-Berkeley bias
  and nothing corrects it. No African, Indian, or Chinese perspective on pandemic
  preparedness or on what AGI-bio means for countries without a Strategic National
  Stockpile. Concrete targets: Shruti Rajagopalan on India's COVID response; an African
  CDC / IFBA voice on LMIC biosecurity.
- **A vivid named-hero opener.** No bio equivalent of smallpox eradication as awe, or a
  Pardis Sabeti Ebola-genomics field narrative. The topic opens on threat, never on
  triumph.
- **The 2011–12 H5N1 gain-of-function controversy as a two-sided narrative.**
  Fouchier/Kawaoka, the NSABB decision, Inglesby–Relman vs. the pro-publication camp —
  the founding dual-use debate.
- **A benefits-side / cost-of-caution voice from within bioscience.** Missing: a
  synthetic-biology researcher arguing that screening mandates and infohazard norms
  carry large real costs to beneficial biotech (a progress-studies rebuttal sharper than
  Vitalik's).
- **A playable tabletop exercise.** Clade X and Event 201 are referenced as videos but
  no scenario is collected in a form a cohort could actually run.
- **DNA-synthesis-screening governance primary source.** IBBIS and the NTI
  common-mechanism work — the concrete institution behind "screen DNA synthesis."
