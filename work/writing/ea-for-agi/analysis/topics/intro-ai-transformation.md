# Topic: The transition — a century in a decade

*Opening week of the fellowship. Corresponds to the extra week MacAskill's cause menu implies: "the main Forethought articles that introduce the transition — the 'century in a decade' intelligence-explosion articles — and other things introducing the idea that AI will have lots of effects in the world, with a lens or narrative to prepare for those."*

## What this topic covers

The claim that advanced AI will transform nearly everything — science, the economy, war, government, the human condition — and will do it *fast*, compressing something like a century of progress into a decade or less. This is the frame the whole rest of the course rests on: if the transition is that big and that quick, then the cause areas in later weeks aren't distant hypotheticals but decisions arriving on a short clock. The week is deliberately about **scale and breadth**, not takeover risk (that is the ai-safety week's job) — the point is to make participants *feel* the size of what's coming and hand them a lens to prepare.

Four sub-threads run through it:
- **The mechanism.** Why AI could accelerate progress super-exponentially: an intelligence explosion (software, then AI-designed hardware, then a full industrial/robotic explosion), and the growth-theory case that ideas-producing labor becoming cheap breaks the historical growth trend.
- **The scale.** That this century, or this decade, is astronomically unusual — "all possible views about humanity's future are wild," current growth rates already "can't go on," and we may be at the hinge of history.
- **The concrete texture.** Scenario writing that turns the abstraction into a month-by-month story (AI 2027), and positive visions of what a compressed century actually buys us in health, wealth, and governance (Machines of Loving Grace).
- **The skeptic's brake.** The serious case that it will be slower, more continuous, or much further off — takeoff-speed debates, multi-decade timelines, and the economists who doubt explosive growth.

## Voices and where they disagree

- **Forethought (MacAskill, Moorhouse, Davidson).** The house view of the course: an intelligence explosion is plausible this decade, and the right response is to *prepare for the grand challenges now* rather than assume future AI handles them. Davidson supplies the dynamics (three types of explosion; software → hardware → industrial). Their live internal tension is between the explosive framing and their own limiting-factors work (adoption gaps, physical limits) that temper how fast diffusion can really go.
- **Cold Takes / Open Phil (Karnofsky, Cotra, Davidson).** The accessible on-ramp tradition — "most important century," bio-anchors, explosive-growth economics. Argues the scale claim from long-run history and growth theory rather than from recent capability trends. Less committed to a specific fast timeline than Forethought or Aschenbrenner.
- **The scaling-trends camp (Aschenbrenner, Kokotajlo, the AI 2027 team).** "Just count the OOMs": straight-line extrapolation of compute and algorithmic progress puts AGI mid-decade and superintelligence shortly after. The most aggressive on speed, and the most concrete/scenario-driven. This is where the vivid narratives live.
- **The positive-vision camp (Amodei).** Accepts the compressed timeline but insists the discourse under-weights the *upside* — Machines of Loving Grace sketches what going right looks like across biology, neuroscience, economics, and governance. The natural counterweight to a doom-only reading of "everything changes."
- **The continuous-takeoff camp (Christiano, Davidson-on-continuity, Cotra).** Not skeptics of transformation, but of *discontinuity* — progress will be fast yet smooth, with no sharp foom, because pre-AGI systems are already economically enormous. "Continuous doesn't mean slow" is the slogan; the disagreement with Aschenbrenner is about shape, not destination.
- **The economist skeptics (Acemoglu, Cowen, Thorstad).** Doubt >30%/yr explosive growth is coming at all: bottlenecks, Baumol tasks, hard-to-automate steps, and the "ideas are getting harder to find" drag. Acemoglu's ~1% GDP bump is the credentialed low-end estimate.
- **The long-timelines skeptics (Erdil & Besiroglu / Epoch; Sanders & Allyn-Feuer).** Agree transformative AI is possible but put it decades out — Epoch's "multi-decade timelines" and the "<1% by 2043" decomposition are the sharpest brakes on the whole week's premise. Essential as the course's honest opposition.

## Best readings

Curated, most-usable first. An intro participant has ~2h/week — items 1–3 are the assignable core; deep-dives are marked.

1. **[Preparing for the Intelligence Explosion](https://www.forethought.org/research/preparing-for-the-intelligence-explosion)** (Moorhouse & MacAskill, Forethought). The named anchor: the "century in a decade" thesis in full, plus the argument that the grand challenges can't all be delegated to future AI, so we must prepare now. This *is* the week's spine. Long — assign the summary and a couple of the challenge sections. *Role: anchor essay; the course's founding frame.* ([local](../../readings/forethought/preparing-for-the-intelligence-explosion.md))

2. **[Will MacAskill on AI causing a "century in a decade"](https://80000hours.org/podcast/episodes/will-macaskill-century-in-a-decade-navigating-intelligence-explosion/)** (80,000 Hours podcast #213). The same thesis in conversational, course-voice form — the most digestible on-ramp, and the piece that most explicitly ties the transition to the "what will AI do to the future?" question the fellowship is built around. *Role: accessible on-ramp; ties the week to the course thesis.* ([local](../../readings/macaskill-worldview/80k-century-in-a-decade.md))

3. **[This Can't Go On](https://www.cold-takes.com/this-cant-go-on/)** (Karnofsky, Cold Takes). The shortest accessible argument for *scale* without leading with takeover: current growth rates are historically bizarre and must end soon, one way or another — so a transition of some kind is coming regardless of your AI views. The rest of the "most important century" series (the roundup, "All Possible Views About Humanity's Future Are Wild") anchors the macrostrategy week; assign only this one here. *Role: accessible on-ramp; the scale-and-breadth core.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/this-cant-go-on.md))

4. **[Three Types of Intelligence Explosion](https://www.forethought.org/research/three-types-of-intelligence-explosion)** (Davidson, Hadshar, MacAskill, Forethought). The dynamics the definition asks for alongside the anchor: the software → hardware → industrial cascade that makes "century in a decade" mechanistically concrete rather than hand-wavy. *Role: rigorous mechanism; the "how could it be that fast" piece.* ([local](../../readings/forethought/three-types-of-intelligence-explosion.md))

5. **[AI 2027](https://ai-2027.com/)** (Kokotajlo, Alexander, Larsen, Lifland, Dean). The vivid concrete scenario named in the definition — a month-by-month story of the takeoff that turns the abstraction into something you can picture and argue with. The single best "narrative lens to prepare with." *Role: vivid narrative; the scenario that makes it real.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/ai-2027.md))

6. **[Machines of Loving Grace](https://www.darioamodei.com/essay/machines-of-loving-grace)** (Amodei, Anthropic). The positive-breadth vision: what a compressed century of progress could buy in health, neuroscience, poverty, and governance if it goes well. The essential counterweight to reading "everything changes" as pure threat. Long; assign the intro plus one or two of the five domains. *Role: positive vision; breadth without doom.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/machines-of-loving-grace.md))

7. **[Situational Awareness: The Decade Ahead](https://situational-awareness.ai/)** (Aschenbrenner). The "just count the OOMs" scaling case that AGI is near and fast. For a 2h week, assign **"From GPT-4 to AGI"** (the extrapolation) and skim "From AGI to Superintelligence"; the later geopolitics chapters are optional. *Role: the scaling-trends case; the most aggressive timeline.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/situational-awareness.md))

8. **[Measuring AI Ability to Complete Long Tasks](https://metr.org/blog/2025-03-19-measuring-ai-ability-to-complete-long-tasks)** (METR). The freshest empirical grounding for the pace claim: the length of tasks AI agents can complete has been doubling roughly every 7 months. Short, assignable, and the recent-trend check on the theory-heavy extrapolations in #4 and #7. *Role: data/evidence; the empirical anchor for "how fast."* ([local](../../readings/linked/metr-measuring-ai-long-tasks.md))

9. **[Transformative AGI by 2043 is <1% likely](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/ARkbWch5RMsj6xP5p/transformative-agi-by-2043-is-less-than-1-likely)** (Sanders & Allyn-Feuer). The week's best critic: a step-by-step decomposition (software, hardware, sociopolitical) that multiplies out to ~0.4%. Whether or not you buy the number, it forces participants to name where they actually disagree. *Role: best critic; the honest opposition to the whole premise.* ([local](../../readings/intro-ai-transformation/transformative-agi-2043-unlikely.md))

10. **[The case for multi-decade AI timelines](https://epoch.ai/gradient-updates/the-case-for-multi-decade-ai-timelines)** (Erdil, Epoch). The credible-insider brake: someone who models AI progress for a living arguing the transition is real but decades out, not this decade. The most useful skeptic to pair against #7. *Role: best critic (from inside the field); timelines counterweight.* ([local](../../readings/linked/case-for-multi-decade-ai-timelines.md))

11. **[Takeoff speeds](https://sideways-view.com/2018/02/24/takeoff-speeds/)** (Christiano). The canonical continuous-takeoff argument: transformation can be fast *and* smooth, with no sharp discontinuity, because pre-AGI systems are already economically huge. Reframes the whole fast/slow debate as about *shape*. *Role: rigorous deep-dive; the continuity counterweight.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/takeoff-speeds-christiano.md))

12. **[Could Advanced AI Drive Explosive Economic Growth?](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/LBFhfooCQhB6SJ9AD/report-on-whether-ai-could-drive-explosive-economic-growth)** (Davidson, Open Phil). The economics evidence base behind "century in a decade": growth theory plus economic history put real probability on >30%/yr growth. The rigorous backbone under the scale claim. *Role: data/evidence; the growth-economics case.* ([local](../../readings/ai-rights-economics/could-advanced-ai-drive-explosive-growth.md))

13. **[Explosive growth from AI: a review of the arguments](https://epoch.ai/blog/explosive-growth-from-ai-a-review-of-the-arguments)** (Erdil & Besiroglu, Epoch). The balanced survey: the case for explosive growth *and* nine counterarguments, from the group most trusted on both sides. The best single "here's the state of the debate" reading. *Role: data/evidence; the even-handed reference survey.* ([local](../../readings/ai-rights-economics/explosive-growth-review-of-arguments.md))

14. **[The Industrial Explosion](https://www.forethought.org/research/the-industrial-explosion)** (Davidson & Hadshar, Forethought). Completes the mechanism from #4: how AI-directed robotics could drive physical-economy output through the roof, and where the bottlenecks bite. The "does it escape the screen into the physical world" piece. *Role: rigorous deep-dive; the physical-transformation half.* ([local](../../readings/forethought/the-industrial-explosion.md))

Also strong, in supporting roles: **[Carl Shulman (Pt 1) on the economy after AGI](https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/carl-shulman)** (Dwarkesh — the most sustained walkthrough of what a fully-automated economy concretely implies; **[The Simple Macroeconomics of AI](https://economics.mit.edu/sites/default/files/2024-04/The%20Simple%20Macroeconomics%20of%20AI.pdf)** (Acemoglu — the credentialed ~1%-GDP skeptic, a sharper economic critic than Cowen) ([local](../../readings/hostile-critics/acemoglu-simple-macroeconomics-of-ai.md)); ## Conspicuously missing

- **A single short, breadth-focused, *positive* narrative that isn't 50 pages.** AI 2027 is the vivid scenario but tilts ominous; Machines of Loving Grace is the upside but is book-length. There's no crisp, assignable short story of a *good* compressed decade — the natural complement to AI 2027.
- **The mainstream-normie voice.** Everything here is EA/rationalist/lab-insider. No Ezra Klein, no economist-columnist, no "here's how a smart outsider first encounters this claim" piece to check the in-group framing.
- **A hard capabilities-skeptic** who argues LLMs are near a plateau and the whole extrapolation is a category error (a Gary Marcus / Marcus-style deep-learning-wall voice). The critics here doubt *speed and timing*; none doubt the *trajectory* itself.
- **The "so what do I do Monday" bridge.** The week conveys scale and hands over a lens, but there's no short piece translating "century in a decade" into how a participant should actually reorient — the emotional/practical on-ramp before the cause-area weeks begin.
