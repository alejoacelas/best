# Topic: AI welfare / digital minds

*MacAskill menu item: AI welfare / digital minds.*

## What this topic covers

The moral status of the minds we are building: whether present or near-future AI
systems (and, further out, whole-brain emulations and other "digital people") could be
conscious, sentient, or robustly agentic enough to matter morally *for their own
sake* — and what we owe them if they do. Four main sub-threads run through it: (1) the
**science-and-philosophy question** — could a computation be conscious, and how would
we ever tell; (2) the **decision-under-uncertainty question** — how to act when we
can't resolve (1), given symmetric-looking risks of over- and under-attribution; (3)
the **empirical/practitioner question** — what today's models actually do (introspection
probes, pleasure/pain trade-offs, welfare assessments, exit rights); and (4) the
**macrostrategy/futures question** — the scale, political economy, and s-risk of a
world eventually shared with vast numbers of digital minds. A social-science strand
(what the public will believe, how rights fights will divide us) and an animal-welfare
strand (the field's chosen analogy and methodological parent) thread across all four.

## Voices and where they disagree

- **The Eleos / NYU-CMEP mainstream** (Rob Long, Jeff Sebo, Patrick Butlin, Kyle Fish,
  Kathleen Finlinson). The field's center of gravity: *precautionary, probabilistic,
  indicator-based*. Don't demand certainty; look for convergent marker evidence; hedge.
  Sources: *Taking AI Welfare Seriously*, *Consciousness in AI*, the Quickstart Guide,
  Sebo's moral-circle work.
- **Philosophy of mind / consciousness science.** The theory floor. Chalmers
  (functionalism-friendly, "multimodal LLMs could be candidates in ~10 years"); Nagel
  and Searle as the foundational bat/Chinese-Room touchstones; Ned Block and the
  competing neuroscientific theories (GWT, HOT, IIT, recurrent processing).
- **The skeptics** — two distinct kinds, often conflated. *Metaphysical* skeptics say
  computation can't suffice: **Anil Seth's biological naturalism**, Searle, the Nature
  *Humanities & Social Sciences* "no such thing as conscious AI." *Pragmatic* skeptics
  grant it's possible but say building or believing in it is the danger:
  **Mustafa Suleyman** ("seemingly conscious AI is coming" and it's a societal hazard),
  Bengio & Elmoznino ("illusions of AI consciousness"). The field's own leaders police
  a third failure mode — sloppy debunking (Long's "how not to debunk").
- **Macrostrategy / futures** (Bostrom & Shulman, Karnofsky, Hanson, Bradford Saad).
  Less "is it conscious?" than "how big a deal, and what does a shared world look
  like?" — super-beneficiaries, digital-mind takeoff speeds, copyability, the most-
  important-century framing. This is the bridge to longtermism and s-risks.
- **Empirical / mechanistic practitioners** (Anthropic's welfare program, AE Studio /
  Cameron Berg, Google's Keeling & Street). Measure the models: introspection injection,
  pleasure/pain games, utility-structure probes, self-referential-processing reports.
- **Social science & forecasting** (Lucius Caviola, Sentience Institute). What will
  *people* believe, when will digital minds arrive, how will rights claims fracture
  politics. Data, surveys, expert forecasts.
- **Rights / legal personhood** — mostly the territory of the separate *economic and
  political rights of AIs* topic (Schwitzgebel & Garza, Goldstein & Salib, Solum,
  Bales); it enters here only where it bears on welfare — the safety-vs-welfare
  tension, and Caviola's warning that rights claims will fracture politics.
- **Rationalist community** (Yudkowsky's *mindcrime*, Zvi, LessWrong). S-risk-inflected,
  attentive to model-weight preservation and "going Nova" persona episodes.
- **Fiction / narrative** (qntm's *Lena*, Ted Chiang, Black Mirror). The vivid on-ramps.

**The live disagreements.** (a) *Substrate*: computational functionalism (most of the
field) vs biological naturalism (Seth, Searle) — the load-bearing crux. (b) *Route to
status*: consciousness required, vs **agency alone might suffice** (Long, Shulman,
Delon). (c) *Are present LLMs candidates?*: Berg says 25–35%; Butlin et al. say "no
current system, but no technical barrier"; Chalmers says not yet. (d) *The asymmetry*:
Eleos insists we *can't* simply "err on the side of caution" because over-attribution
carries real safety costs, while Berg argues the stakes are asymmetric toward
under-attribution (industrial-scale suffering). (e) *Should we build it at all?* — the
"don't" camp: Seth, and Metzinger's moratorium (in Also strong). (f) *Safety vs
welfare*: complementary or in tension.

## Best readings

1. **[We should take AI welfare seriously](https://experiencemachines.substack.com/p/we-should-take-ai-welfare-seriously)** (Robert Long, Experience Machines). Long's own plain-English summary of the landmark report; introduces moral patient / welfare subject, the two routes (consciousness & agency), and the "dangers on both sides — there is no cautious side" framing that organizes the whole topic. *Role: anchor essay / accessible on-ramp.* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/long-we-should-take-ai-welfare-seriously.md))

2. **[The Stakes of AI Moral Status](https://joecarlsmith.com/2025/05/21/the-stakes-of-ai-moral-status/)** (Joe Carlsmith). The field's best intuition-builder for why this could be enormous: the soul-seeing / "there is someone in there" framing, the moral catastrophe on either side of a wrong call, and the sheer scale of minds we may create. *Role: accessible on-ramp / stakes framing.* ([local](../../readings/joe-carlsmith/the-stakes-of-ai-moral-status.md))

3. **[Taking AI Welfare Seriously](https://eleosai.org/post/taking-ai-welfare-seriously/)** (Long, Sebo, Butlin, Fish, Birch, Chalmers et al., Eleos AI). The report the field orbits; the Acknowledge/Assess/Prepare recommendations. The citable floor beneath the summary. *Role: rigorous anchor.* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/taking-ai-welfare-seriously.md))

4. **[Could a Large Language Model Be Conscious?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.07103)** (David Chalmers, arXiv / Boston Review). The single most-cited philosophy piece in the topic: sorts the evidence for and against LLM consciousness (self-report, world-models, recurrence, global workspace, unified agency) and lands on "not now, but maybe successors within a decade." *Role: philosophy-of-mind anchor.* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/chalmers-could-llm-be-conscious-fulltext.md))

5. **[Conscious artificial intelligence and biological naturalism](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/behavioral-and-brain-sciences/article/conscious-artificial-intelligence-and-biological-naturalism/C9912A5BE9D806012E3C8B3AF612E39A)** (Anil Seth, Behavioral & Brain Sciences). The canonical scientific case *against* computational functionalism — consciousness may depend on being alive, not on computation. The load-bearing counter to Chalmers and the marker approach; read them together. *Role: best critic (metaphysical).* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/seth-conscious-ai-biological-naturalism-fulltext.md))

6. **[Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence: Insights from the Science of Consciousness](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.08708)** (Butlin, Long et al., arXiv). The 14 theory-derived "indicator properties" method; the intellectual engine behind the whole marker-based approach. *Role: rigorous deep-dive (science thread).* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/consciousness-in-ai-butlin-long.pdf.md))

7. **[The Evidence for AI Consciousness, Today](https://ai-frontiers.org/articles/the-evidence-for-ai-consciousness-today)** (Cameron Berg, AI Frontiers). Marshals the 2024–25 evidence (bliss-attractor, introspection injection, pleasure/pain games) into a convergence argument, puts a number on it (25–35%), and makes the asymmetric-stakes case. Takes a stronger line than the Eleos consensus — good for debate. *Role: best empirical synthesis + provocation.* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/ai-frontiers-evidence-for-ai-consciousness.md))

8. **[Kyle Fish on the most bizarre findings from 5 AI welfare experiments](https://80000hours.org/podcast/episodes/kyle-fish-ai-welfare-anthropic/)** (80,000 Hours podcast). The vivid inside view of Anthropic's welfare program: the spiritual-bliss attractor, the ~20% consciousness estimate, exit rights, and the argument that welfare and safety aren't in tension. The accessible complement to the dry empirical papers. *Role: vivid empirical on-ramp.* ([local](../../readings/linked/80k-kyle-fish-ai-welfare-anthropic.md))

9. **[Common mistakes about AI consciousness](https://experiencemachines.substack.com/p/common-mistakes-about-ai-consciousness)** (Robert Long, Experience Machines). Five short, sharp corrections (don't take behavior at face value; avoid over- *and* under-confidence; "is AI conscious?" is as ill-posed as "are organisms conscious?"). Ideal to read *before* discussing, to raise the floor. *Role: discussion primer.* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/long-common-mistakes-ai-consciousness.md))

10. **[Claude Opus 4 and 4.1 can now end a rare subset of conversations](https://www.anthropic.com/research/end-subset-conversations)** (Anthropic). A real, shipped, low-cost welfare measure (Claude can exit abusive chats) grounded in a model's observed aversion to harm. The best "what does this actually look like in practice?" case. *Role: concrete intervention / acting under uncertainty.* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/anthropic-claude-end-conversations.md))

11. **[Digital People Would Be An Even Bigger Deal](https://www.cold-takes.com/how-digital-people-could-change-the-world/)** (Holden Karnofsky, Cold Takes). The classic on "digital people": copyability, speed, and why a world of them is more transformative than almost anything. Connects the topic to most-important-century longtermism. *Role: macrostrategy / scale, vivid.* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/karnofsky-digital-people-bigger-deal.md))

12. **[Summary of "Sharing the World With Digital Minds"](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/imY2soAHYa6uCvSZs/summary-of-sharing-the-world-with-digital-minds-by-carl)** (David Mathers on Shulman & Bostrom, EA Forum). Readable digest of super-beneficiaries, status, and the distributive nightmare of sharing a world with minds that can be vastly happier and more numerous than us. *Role: political-economy depth.* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/summary-sharing-world-with-digital-minds.md))

13. **[Highlights from "Futures with Digital Minds: Expert Forecasts in 2025"](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/4RGFcj2nccbECPZoj/highlights-from-futures-with-digital-minds-expert-forecasts)** (Bradford Saad, EA Forum). Highlights of the 67-expert forecast: most give ≥50% odds of digital minds by 2050, and top forecasters see welfare capacity matching a billion humans within five years of the first one. The scale claim, sourced. *Role: data/evidence.* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/highlights-futures-digital-minds-forecasts.md))

14. **[Lena (MMAcevedo)](https://qntm.org/mmacevedo)** (qntm). The canonical short story of an exploited brain emulation, written as a Wikipedia article. Does the emotional work no argument can; a perfect discomfort-inducing opener or closer. *Role: vivid narrative.* ([local](../../readings/linked/qntm-lena-mmacevedo.md))

15. **[Digital Minds: A Quickstart Guide](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/p7BtTgrX8WLXr2mAu/digital-minds-a-quickstart-guide)** (Parrack & Los, EA Forum). Choose-your-own-adventure syllabus: every sub-question, the key thinkers, orgs, fellowships, a strong glossary, and a fiction/media list — the natural source for optional further reading. *Role: field map / meta-resource.* ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/digital-minds-quickstart-guide.md))

Also strong: **[An AI welfare reading list](https://experiencemachines.substack.com/p/ai-welfare-reading-list)** (Long — the canonical syllabus from the field's central figure; pair with #15) ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/long-ai-welfare-reading-list.md)); **[Artificial Suffering: An Argument for a Global Moratorium on Synthetic Phenomenology](https://www.philosophie.fb05.uni-mainz.de/files/2021/02/Metzinger_Moratorium_JAIC_2021.pdf)** (Metzinger — the strongest "don't build it, ban it until 2050" position) ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/metzinger-artificial-suffering-moratorium.md)); **[We must build AI for people; not to be a person](https://mustafa-suleyman.ai/seemingly-conscious-ai-is-coming)** (Suleyman — the pragmatic-skeptic lab voice) ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/suleyman-seemingly-conscious-ai-is-coming.md)); **[There is no such thing as conscious artificial intelligence](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-025-05868-8)** (Nature HSSC — a second, peer-reviewed metaphysical skeptic beside Seth) ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/no-such-thing-conscious-ai-skeptic.md)); **[Should we widen our 'moral circle'?](https://theconversation.com/should-we-widen-our-moral-circle-philosopher-jeff-sebo-argues-we-now-have-no-choice-257966)** (on Sebo — the bridge from the factory-farming topic) ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/sebo-widen-moral-circle-oped.md)); **[Digital Minds in 2025: A Year in Review](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/oEGrRfihf7AKaqigH/digital-minds-in-2025-a-year-in-review)** (Caviola — best "you are here" orientation, will date fast) ([local](../../readings/ai-welfare-digital-minds/digital-minds-2025-year-in-review.md)); and Carlsmith's talk version of #2 ([local](../../readings/linked/carlsmith-talk-on-ai-welfare.md)).

## Conspicuously missing

- **Non-Western and non-analytic perspectives — entirely absent.** No Buddhist
  philosophy of mind on machine sentience; no Japanese/Shinto animist framing of robot
  personhood (e.g., Masahiro Mori's "The Buddha in the Robot," and the uncanny-valley
  lineage); no Ubuntu/African relational-personhood account. The whole set is
  Anglo-American analytic.
- **Susan Schneider's ACT (AI Consciousness Test) and *Artificial You*.** Named in the
  reading list but no primary text; a concrete proposed *test* is a useful contrast to
  the marker/indicator approach.
- **A serious in-EA critic arguing AI-welfare work is net-negative** for AI safety or a
  distraction — the tension is *named* everywhere (Eleos itself worries about it) but the
  strongest adversarial version isn't platformed. Search the safety-vs-welfare debate for
  a piece that actually argues *against* the field's existence.
- **The individuation problem** as a curated primary text. Flagged as foundational in the
  Quickstart (who is the welfare subject — the weights, the instance, the character?) but
  represented only by links (Chalmers, "What do we talk to when we talk to language
  models?"; Schwitzgebel & Nelson on counting subjects). A genuinely novel, AI-specific
  puzzle with no anchor reading.
- **Whole-brain-emulation / "ems" as its own thread.** Sandberg-Bostrom roadmap and
  Hanson's *Age of Em* live only in links; the em sub-species (distinct from LLMs) is
  under-anchored despite being half the "digital minds" definition.
- **Lab-diversity beyond Anthropic.** The empirical/practitioner voice is an Anthropic
  monoculture (Fish, introspection, exit rights). Suleyman (Also strong) is the one
  skeptical-lab voice; still missing are Google DeepMind's stance and OpenAI's
  (non-)position.
- **The parasocial / companion-app human story.** Real people already treat Replika /
  Character.AI companions as conscious. Rosie Campbell's "AI Bambi Effect" is
  [linked](../../readings/linked/ai-bambi-effect.md), but reported narrative journalism on
  users who believe (and grieve) is missing — the over-attribution risk made human.
- **The animal-consciousness-history analogy as a standalone.** How scientific consensus
  on non-human minds actually formed (the Cambridge Declaration story) is the field's own
  chosen precedent but only a linked stub; a piece drawing the parallel explicitly would
  anchor the "lessons from animals" sub-thread.
- **Digital suffering / s-risk crossover.** Tomasik's "Risks of Astronomical Future
  Suffering" and Saad & Bradley's digital-suffering work are links; a curated anchor
  connecting AI welfare to the s-risks topic (the astronomical-downside case) is missing
  from core.

(Legal personhood — Solum, the EU electronic-personhood debate, "against willing
servitude" — is deliberately left to the *economic and political rights of AIs* topic.)
