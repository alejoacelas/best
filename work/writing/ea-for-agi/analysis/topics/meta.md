# Topic: Meta

*MacAskill menu item: meta.*

## What this topic covers

The machine that turns money and time into good — and whether it still works when
AGI compresses a century into a decade. MacAskill's essay is itself a meta piece:
its "third way" (EA as the movement for making the post-AGI transition go well,
against both "legacy movement" fatalism and a retreat to classic causes) is the
live strategic question every reading here answers or attacks. Sub-threads: (1)
**identity** — third-wave framings, principles-first vs. cause-first, whether EA is
dying, thriving, or being eaten by AI safety; (2) **cultivation vs. growth** — the
rowing/steering/cultivation debate about whether the marginal effort goes to more
inputs or a healthier culture; (3) **the machine's mechanics** — absorptive
capacity, talent pipelines, field-building from zero for the new cause areas; and
(4) **cause prioritization as a live ongoing project** rather than a settled menu.

## Voices and where they disagree

- **The third-way camp** (MacAskill, Koehler, Forethought): EA should own the
  post-AGI-transition mission, expand well beyond AI safety, and shift marginal
  effort from growth to cultivation. Anti-PR-mentality, pro "own your EA
  affiliation."
- **The institutional stewards** (Zach Robinson / CEA, EAIF): principles-first EA,
  brand rehabilitation, and a growth-centric funnel. MacAskill endorses the
  principles-first label while warning it can become cover for anchoring on the
  existing cause menu — the sharpest intra-family disagreement in the topic.
- **The AGI-pivoted institutions** (80,000 Hours): enacted cause prioritization —
  the movement's biggest career org concluding the answer is AGI and restructuring
  around it. The counterpoint to CEA's cause-neutrality.
- **The loyal opposition / ideas-first school** (Reardon, hbesceli, NEST): the
  growth-funnel model corrodes truth-seeking; EA is losing its question-nature;
  build communities around ideas and let careers follow. They critique both CEA's
  brand strategy and 80k's pivot, from inside.
- **The rationalist/AIS side** (Greenblatt, Shlegeris, Buck's replies): even with
  short timelines, *longtermist* community-building beats narrow AI-safety
  field-building, because scope-sensitive, impact-obsessed people are the scarce
  input. Agrees with MacAskill's conclusion from a different worldview.
- **The machine empiricists** (Allardice, Joey of AIM): whatever the strategy says,
  the sector cannot absorb the coming money and talent at quality — scaling impact
  is the binding constraint, not recruitment.
- **The external competitors and critics** (Bregman's School for Moral Ambition,
  Nuño Sempere): one shows what a rebranded EA-adjacent movement looks like built
  growth-first; the other catalogs the failure modes the stewards would rather not
  discuss.

Live disagreements: growth vs. cultivation on the current margin; whether
"principles-first" is openness or anchoring; whether campus EA groups should cede
ground to AI-safety groups; whether EA is a legacy movement, a question, or a
mission; and where the marginal talented person should go — safety, a classic
cause, or a neglected AGI-preparedness area with no ecosystem yet.

## Best readings

A realistic ~2h core: the MacAskill essay (#1, ~45 min), West (#3, ~10 min), and
one of Koehler (#2) or the CEA statement (#4); hbesceli (#5) is the first optional
add. Everything after is deep-dives and counterweights.

1. **[Effective altruism in the age of AGI](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/R8AAG4QBZi5puvogR/effective-altruism-in-the-age-of-agi)** (William MacAskill, EA Forum). The founding essay of this fellowship and the topic's agenda-setter: three futures for EA, the expanded cause menu, cultivation over growth, and the case against PR mentality. *Role: anchor essay.* ([local](../../idea/macaskill-ea-in-the-age-of-agi.md))

2. **[Why I think building EA is important for making AI go well](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/oPue7R3outxZaTXzp/why-i-think-building-ea-is-important-for-making-ai-go-well)** (Arden Koehler, EA Forum). The companion memo: making AGI go well needs a flexible, *explicitly moral* movement, and the alternatives (rationalists, professionalized AI safety, Moral Ambition) each lack an ingredient. The cleanest positive argument for the movement's continued existence. *Role: rigorous pro-case; pairs with the anchor.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/koehler-building-ea-for-ai.md))

3. **[Third Wave Effective Altruism](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/XTBGAWAXR25atu39P/third-wave-effective-altruism)** (Ben West, EA Forum). Ten minutes that supply the topic's shared vocabulary: EA's two completed waves and three candidate third waves — MacAskill's third way is West's "forefront of weirdness" scenario grown up. *Role: accessible on-ramp.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/west-third-wave-ea.md))

4. **[CEA will continue to take a "principles-first" approach to EA](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/jPDByBkEdPt2SF2LL/cea-will-continue-to-take-a-principles-first-approach-to-ea)** (Zach Robinson, EA Forum). The canonical institutional position — EA as four principles bigger than any cause — stated by the org that stewards the community. Read it asking MacAskill's question: openness, or cover for the same old menu? *Role: primary source; the position under debate.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/cea-principles-first.md))

5. **[My disagreements with CEA's approach to stewarding EA](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/xsffhcHoexJgH4h4X/my-disagreements-with-cea-s-approach-to-stewarding-ea)** (hbesceli, EA Forum). Ex-CEA/EAIF insider arguing EA is dying as a moral-intellectual project and that the growth funnel itself is why — the strongest statement that cultivation and growth genuinely trade off. *Role: best critic of the growth machine — note it contests CEA's tactics, not the third-way mission; no published piece does (see Conspicuously missing).* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/disagreements-cea-stewarding.md))

6. **[80,000 Hours is shifting its strategic approach to focus more on AGI](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/4ZE3pfwDKqRRNRggL/80-000-hours-is-shifting-its-strategic-approach-to-focus)** (80,000 Hours, EA Forum). Cause prioritization enacted rather than theorized: the movement's biggest talent org betting itself on AGI by 2030, with its reasoning shown. *Role: primary source / case study.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/80k-shifting-to-agi.md))

7. **[Sadly, 80k](https://frommatter.substack.com/p/sadly-80k)** (Matt Reardon, From Matter). The loyal-opposition reply from a former 80k advisor: pivoting *to* AGI means abandoning the first-principles audience that made 80k work. The concrete cost side of the pivot's ledger. *Role: best critic of the pivot; pairs with #6.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/reardon-sadly-80k.md))

8. **[High absorbency career paths](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/rjiGwvzBEbYj2Cth8/high-absorbency-career-paths)** (Joey Savoie, EA Forum). Short piece MacAskill hyperlinks for his biggest caution: the new cause areas can't yet absorb people. Defines absorbency and shows why movement-level and individual-level career advice diverge. *Role: key concept, quick read.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/high-absorbency-career-paths.md))

9. **[The Absorption Problem](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/uPe8hiHwzM2Yi4nKG/the-absorption-problem)** (Nick Allardice, EA Forum). The Change.org/GiveDirectly CEO on why the sector can't absorb the coming wave of AI-wealth philanthropy: voltage drop, low ceilings, orgs that break from growth — plus a framework for scalability profiles. *Role: rigorous deep-dive; data/evidence on the machine's limits.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/the-absorption-problem.md))

10. **[EA Meta Funding Landscape Report](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/xGqpQKf2FpjvwJe6q/ea-meta-funding-landscape-report)** (Joel Tan / CEARCH, EA Forum). The only map of where meta money actually flows: aggregate funding $109M→$193M→$117M over 2021–23; by intervention, community building ($193M) > talent ($121M) > prioritization ($92M) > effective giving ($13M); OP, EA Funds, and SFF are 82% of the total. The empirical backbone under every cultivation-vs-growth and absorptive-capacity argument above. *Role: data/evidence.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/ea-meta-funding-landscape-report.md))

11. **[Rowing and Steering the Effective Altruism Movement](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/XweBntieePnzQyLtK/rowing-and-steering-the-effective-altruism-movement)** (jtm, EA Forum). Applies Karnofsky's ship analogy to EA itself: rowers fail by growing too slowly, steerers by heading somewhere bad. The pre-FTX case for steering that MacAskill reframes as "cultivation." *Role: deep-dive; the conceptual frame the debate runs on.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/rowing-and-steering-ea.md))

12. **[Ryan Greenblatt on longtermist community building](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/FG54euEAesRkSZuJN/ryan_greenblatt-s-shortform?commentId=yqD9WdzRidD5Qwycg)** (Greenblatt et al., LessWrong shortform). Five minutes, cited by MacAskill: longtermist (not just AI-safety) capacity-building is underdone even on short timelines, with Buck's reply on why scope-sensitive people beat safety-only allies. *Role: the rationalist-side corroboration, quick read.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/greenblatt-longtermist-community-building.md))

13. **[The case of the missing cause prioritisation research](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/MSYhEatxkEfg46j3D/the-case-of-the-missing-cause-prioritisation-research)** (weeatquince, EA Forum). The 2020 complaint that EA's core intellectual activity had stalled — exactly the gap MacAskill says explains why no one is shouting about the new cause areas. *Role: deep-dive; cause prioritization as unfinished business.* ([local](../../readings/ea-intro-program/missing-cause-prioritisation-research.md))

14. **[Unflattering aspects of Effective Altruism](https://nunosempere.com/blog/2024/03/05/unflattering-aspects-of-ea/)** (Nuño Sempere). A truth-seeking insider's catalog of the machine's failure modes — unaccountable funding, soldier mindset, structural rot — written in exactly the no-PR register MacAskill calls for. *Role: best critic of the machine itself.* ([local](../../readings/ea-meta-movement/unflattering-aspects-of-ea.md))

## Conspicuously missing

- **A direct institutional response to MacAskill's essay.** CEA/Robinson's
  principles-first posts predate it; no one with budget authority has answered the
  third-way proposal in print.
- **Any critic of the third-way thesis itself.** Every critic in this collection
  contests tactics — the growth funnel (#5), the 80k pivot (#7), the machine's
  hygiene (#14) — while accepting that EA should own the transition. Neither
  rejection MacAskill argues against has a written defense: not "EA was a
  bootloader for AI safety and should gracefully wind down" (reportedly the
  Constellation view), and not "expanding into speculative new cause areas is
  overconfident; stay narrow."
- **Data on the campus shift from EA groups to AI-safety groups.** The claim
  drives MacAskill's local-groups section and Kairos's existence, yet every source
  is anecdote; no membership or funnel numbers.
- **An outside historian or sociologist of social movements** on what happens
  when a movement's flagship cause goes mainstream (temperance, environmentalism,
  feminism analogues) — everyone here reasons from inside.
- **A developed quantitative model of growth vs. cultivation under short
  timelines.** MacAskill sketches a production function in a footnote and asks for
  Cunningham's Law; nobody has answered.
- **The effective-giving / GWWC growth voice.** MacAskill assigns growth to
  "e.g. Giving What We Can" in one line; #10 shows effective giving as the
  machine's most starved limb ($13M of ~$420M), but no primary source from that
  side made the collection.
- **A non-Anglophone or Global South movement-building perspective** — the whole
  conversation happens between Oxford, London, and the Bay.
