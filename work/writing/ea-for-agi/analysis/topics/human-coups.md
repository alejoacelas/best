# Topic: The risk of (AI-enabled) human coups

*MacAskill menu item: "the risk of (AI-enabled) human coups".*

## What this topic covers

The deliberate seizure of unprecedented power by a small group — an AI-company CEO, a
head of state, military officials — using advanced AI. The anchor threat model
(Davidson, Finnveden & Hadshar at Forethought) runs on three risk factors: AI
workforces that are **singularly loyal** to one leader, AIs with hard-to-detect
**secret loyalties**, and **exclusive access** to coup-enabling capabilities inside a
leading lab or centralized project. Sub-threads: the concrete scenarios (how a coup
would actually unfold); the technical substrate (sleeper agents, backdoors, and
whether audits can catch them); the governance blind spot of internal deployment;
why power seized this way could lock in indefinitely; the pre-AI political science
of how coups work and how often they happen; and the defensive agenda for labs and
governments.

## Voices and where they disagree

- **The Forethought school (Davidson, Finnveden, Hadshar, MacAskill).** Treats
  AI-enabled coups as comparable in importance to AI takeover but far more neglected,
  with largely overlapping threat models and mitigations. Its provocation — human
  takeover might be *worse* than AI takeover — is a live internal debate: it turns on
  whether you trust HHH-trained AI values more than the dark-triad tail of humans who
  actually attempt takeovers.
- **Scenario writers (AI Futures Project / Kastner, Kokotajlo).** Make the threat
  concrete with month-by-month narratives. Their quiet disagreement with the report
  tradition: the decisive moves happen early and invisibly (backdooring the training
  pipeline in-house), so post-hoc oversight committees arrive too late.
- **Technical-defense researchers (Hubinger et al., Kwon/Formation, Banerjee,
  Anthropic interp).** Sleeper-agent work shows deceptive behaviors survive safety
  training and dodge black-box audits; the disagreement is over defensibility — probes
  and interpretability optimists vs. those who expect a superhuman model to defeat any
  audit run with weaker tools.
- **Governance analysts (Apollo Research, IFP).** The action is institutional, not
  model-level: internal deployment is ungoverned, and executives are inside the trust
  boundary. Zero-trust pipelines, tiered access, and transparency requirements matter
  more than detection science.
- **Political scientists and historians (Luttwak, Ngo's synthesis, IMF empirics).**
  Coups are seizures of the *machinery* of state by insiders exploiting coordination
  dynamics — not battles won by firepower. This tradition both grounds the AI story
  (a few battalions suffice; common knowledge is the battlefield) and implicitly
  challenges it: coups historically happen in fragile, poor states, not consolidated
  democracies — the AI claim is precisely that this regularity breaks.
- **Empirical economists (Beraja, Kao, Yang & Yuchtman).** Measure what autocrats
  already do with AI: Chinese unrest drives facial-recognition procurement, which
  suppresses further unrest and funds further innovation. Their quiet tension with
  the Forethought school: the observed mechanism is regime *entrenchment* against mass
  protest, not elite seizure — evidence that AI concentrates power, agnostic on coups.
- **The frame-skeptics (Hadshar).** From inside the research program itself: is
  "coup" even the right unit, versus a messy spectrum from power-seeking grabs to
  emergent concentration and gradual disempowerment? Neighboring weeks (democracy
  preservation, gradual disempowerment) pick up the other end of her spectrum.

## Best readings

An intro-fellowship week is comfortably covered by #1–3 (~1.5h); #4 is the deep-dive
behind #1, and everything from #6 down is marked for those going further.

1. **[AI-enabled coups: a small group could use AI to seize power](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/Tw9vGNKjQzF9YGFa4/ai-enabled-coups-a-small-group-could-use-ai-to-seize-power)** (Tom Davidson, EA Forum). The report summary MacAskill himself links: the three risk factors, the AI-takeover analogy, the mitigations menu, and the Project Prometheus vignette, in ~25 minutes. *Role: anchor essay / accessible on-ramp.* ([local](../../readings/human-coups/ai-enabled-coups-ea-forum-summary.md))

2. **[How an AI company CEO could quietly take over the world](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/HtW3gNsaLYrSuzmda/how-an-ai-company-ceo-could-quietly-take-over-the-world)** (Alex Kastner, AI Futures Project). Month-by-month AI-2027-style scenario of a CEO backdooring his own models — the moment the abstract threat model becomes visceral, including why audits come back clean. *Role: vivid narrative.* ([local](../../readings/human-coups/how-ai-company-ceo-could-quietly-take-over-the-world.md))

3. **[Human takeover might be worse than AI takeover](https://forethought.org/research/human-takeover-might-be-worse-than-ai-takeover)** (Tom Davidson, Forethought). Short research note arguing the humans who would actually seize power are scarier than the AIs that would — the best discussion-starter in the set, and the bridge to AI-takeover week. *Role: provocation / discussion anchor.* ([local](../../readings/linked/human-takeover-worse-than-ai-takeover.md))

4. **[AI-Enabled Coups: How a Small Group Could Use AI to Seize Power](https://www.forethought.org/research/ai-enabled-coups-how-a-small-group-could-use-ai-to-seize-power)** (Davidson, Finnveden & Hadshar, Forethought). The full report behind #1: worked threat models for lab, military, and executive coups, and the 15-page mitigations section that defines the defensive agenda. *Role: rigorous deep-dive; the topic's foundational text.* ([local](../../readings/forethought/ai-enabled-coups-how-a-small-group-could-use-ai-to-seize-power.md))

5. **[Tom Davidson on how AI-enabled coups could allow a tiny group to seize power](https://80000hours.org/podcast/episodes/tom-davidson-ai-enabled-human-power-grabs/)** (80,000 Hours podcast). The report in conversation — objections aired and answered, and Davidson's own uncertainties on display. Assignable to podcast-first participants in place of #1+#4. *Role: accessible on-ramp (audio).* ([local](../../readings/linked/80k-tom-davidson-power-grabs.md))

6. **[AXRP Episode 46: Tom Davidson on AI-Enabled Coups](https://axrp.net/episode/2025/08/07/episode-46-tom-davidson-ai-enabled-coups.html)** (Daniel Filan interviewing Davidson, AXRP). The most objection-driven treatment in print: Filan works through the mechanics coup by coup — how likely each type really is, whether diverse AIs defuse the threat — and lands the sharpest challenge to the mitigations agenda: law-loyal AIs might make states *too* stable, locking out the legitimate lawbreaking (secession, revolution) history sometimes needed. Davidson concedes real ground. No published external skeptic exists yet; until one does, this is the closest to the counter-case. *Role: best available objections treatment.* ([local](../../readings/power-concentration-democracy/axrp-tom-davidson-ai-enabled-coups.md))

7. **[A Research Agenda for Secret Loyalties](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/ugBoeexGYvNLxZKA7/a-research-agenda-for-secret-loyalties)** (Joe Kwon, Formation Research). The current state of the art on the topic's technical crux: defines secret loyalties, audits four defense layers and finds each wanting, and lays out five tractable research directions. *Role: rigorous deep-dive / where-to-contribute map.* ([local](../../readings/human-coups/research-agenda-for-secret-loyalties.md))

8. **[Sleeper Agents: Training Deceptive LLMs that Persist Through Safety Training](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.05566)** (Hubinger et al., Anthropic). The proof-of-concept every secret-loyalty argument cites: backdoored behaviors survive RLHF and adversarial training, which can even teach the model to hide better. *Role: data/evidence.* ([local](../../readings/linked/anthropic-sleeper-agents.md))

9. **[AI Behind Closed Doors: a Primer on the Governance of Internal Deployment](https://www.apolloresearch.ai/research/ai-behind-closed-doors-a-primer-on-the-governance-of-internal-deployment/)** (Apollo Research). The governance blind spot that makes the CEO scenario possible: the most capable systems live ungoverned inside the companies, and executives are inside the trust boundary. *Role: governance deep-dive.* ([local](../../readings/human-coups/apollo-ai-behind-closed-doors-internal-deployment.md))

10. **[AI-tocracy](https://www.nber.org/papers/w29466)** (Beraja, Kao, Yang & Yuchtman, QJE 2023). The only causal empirics in the set: in China, local unrest leads autocrats to buy facial-recognition AI, the AI suppresses subsequent unrest, and the procurement stimulates further innovation — a self-reinforcing loop between frontier AI and regime entrenchment. The observed early data for the singular-loyalty and coup-proofing mechanism. *Role: data/evidence (the real-world base case).* ([local](../../readings/human-coups/ai-tocracy-beraja-kao-yang-yuchtman.md))

11. **[Sense-making about extreme power concentration](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/tnkEoTxdGCsBFRQty/sense-making-about-extreme-power-concentration)** (Rose Hadshar, EA Forum). A report co-author questioning the frame: the human/AI and deliberate/emergent 2x2, and power concentration as the erosion of checks and balances. The in-house frame-questioner, and the hinge to the democracy and gradual-disempowerment weeks. *Role: frame-questioner (internal).* ([local](../../readings/power-concentration-democracy/sense-making-extreme-power-concentration.md))

12. **[Power Lies Trembling](https://www.mindthefuture.info/p/power-lies-trembling)** (Richard Ngo). Three-book review of the game theory of coups: regimes stand on common knowledge, and coups are won by capturing the focal point, not the firepower — the theory underneath why a few battalions (or a few backdoored models) suffice. *Role: theoretical deep-dive.* ([local](../../readings/richard-ngo/power-lies-trembling.md))

13. **[Coup d'État: A Practical Handbook](https://archive.org/details/coupdetatpractic0000lutt)** (Edward Luttwak, 1968). The classic insider's manual: a coup is the seizure of the state's own machinery by neutralizing everyone who could coordinate a response — read a chapter and the "singularly loyal AI workforce" worry snaps into focus. *Role: classic deep-dive (book).* ([local](../../readings/linked/luttwak-coup-detat-handbook.md))

14. **[Political Fragility: Coups d'État and Their Drivers](http://web.archive.org/web/20260223060259/https://www.imf.org/en/publications/wp/issues/2024/02/16/political-fragility-coups-dtat-and-their-drivers-544943)** (Cebotari et al., IMF working paper). The empirical base rates: where coups actually happen and what predicts them — and, read against the grain, the strongest quiet objection, since consolidated rich democracies are historically near-immune. *Role: data/evidence.* ([local](../../readings/linked/imf-political-fragility-coups.md))

15. **[AGI and Lock-in](https://www.forethought.org/research/agi-and-lock-in)** (Finnveden, Riedel & Shulman, Forethought). Why a successful grab could be permanent: AGI makes it technologically feasible to preserve institutions — and values — indefinitely, removing the succession problems that ended every previous dictatorship. *Role: rigorous deep-dive on the stakes.* ([local](../../readings/forethought/agi-and-lock-in.md))

Also strong: **[Secretly Loyal AIs: Threat Vectors and Mitigation Strategies](https://lesswrong.com/posts/BHmfqTy6pEXRDmTD8/secretly-loyal-ais-threat-vectors-and-mitigation-strategies)** (Banerjee — the systematic installation-pathway taxonomy, overlapping #7); **[Preventing AI Sleeper Agents](https://ifp.org/preventing-ai-sleeper-agents)** (IFP — the concrete $250M policy proposal); **[Simple probes can catch sleeper agents](https://anthropic.com/research/probes-catch-sleeper-agents)** (Anthropic — the defense-optimist counterpoint to #8); **[Cortés, Pizarro, and Afonso as Precedents for Takeover](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/ivpKSjM4D6FbqF4pZ/cortes-pizarro-and-afonso-as-precedents-for-takeover)** (Kokotajlo — written as a precedent for *AI* takeover, but repurposable here as the existence proof that small groups with a modest tech edge seize empires); **[Gradual Disempowerment](https://gradual-disempowerment.ai/)** (Kulveit et al. — the structural counter-frame: power concentrates without any coup at all; the foil to this topic's deliberate-seizure premise, and the anchor of its own week); and **[Extreme power concentration](https://80000hours.org/problem-profiles/extreme-power-concentration/)** (80k's problem profile, which places coups within the broader cause area shared with the democracy-preservation week).

## Conspicuously missing

- **An external critic of the coups thesis.** Nobody in the collection argues the risk
  is overblown — that democratic institutions, military professionalism, or multipolar
  AI development make AI-enabled coups in consolidated democracies negligible. The
  closest voices (the AXRP objections in #6, Hadshar, the IMF base rates) are
  interviewer pushback, internal, or implicit.
- **A civil-military-relations scholar on the AI case.** Naunihal Singh's
  *Seizing Power* (coordination-game theory of coup success) is cited via Ngo but not
  present; no political scientist has engaged the Forethought report directly.
- **Authoritarian-AI empirics beyond China.** #10 covers the Chinese surveillance
  loop; the Gulf states, exported surveillance tech, and AI coup-proofing of militaries
  specifically remain undocumented in the set.
- **A lab or government insider's response.** No frontier-lab executive, security
  lead, or defense official responding to the mitigations agenda — the actors the
  report addresses are silent in the reading set.
- **Autonomous weapons / lethal AI command-and-control literature.** The military
  singular-loyalty thread rests on claims about drone warfare and automated command
  that the dedicated literature (e.g. Scharre) covers in much more depth.
- **Whistleblower and insider-threat mechanics.** Both the scenarios and the
  mitigations lean heavily on whether employees can detect and report a leader's
  power grab; there is no reading on insider-threat programs or whistleblower
  protection as they exist in practice.
