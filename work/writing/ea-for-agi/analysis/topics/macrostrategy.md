# Topic: Macrostrategy

*MacAskill menu item: macrostrategy.*

## What this topic covers

Macrostrategy is the "zoom all the way out" topic: given that this century may be
the most pivotal in history, what is the shape of the transition, how confident can we
be, and what future are we steering toward? Four sub-threads run through it. (1) **The
big-picture case** — growth history, scope-sensitivity, and the claim that we live at
an unusual, high-leverage moment (Karnofsky's *Most Important Century*, Bostrom's
*Astronomical Waste*). (2) **The hinge-of-history debate** — whether now really is the
most influential time, argued between MacAskill and his critics. (3) **Crucial
considerations and strategic levers** — differential technological development,
lock-in, the vulnerable-world hypothesis, coordination failures (Moloch), the long
reflection. (4) **Better vs. worse futures** — what a "solved world" is for,
existential hope, and the constructive question MacAskill puts at the center of his
essay (*Better Futures* / viatopia, *Letter from Utopia*, *Deep Utopia*). The
connective epistemic thread is how much to trust any of this: forecasting track
records, burden of proof, and formal predictability challenges. Timelines and takeoff
— how fast AGI arrives — also belong to macrostrategy, but they are the core of the
[opening week](intro-ai-transformation.md), so this list points there rather than
repeating them.

## Voices and where they disagree

- **Oxford/GPI longtermist philosophy** (Bostrom, Ord, Greaves & MacAskill, Mogensen,
  Tarsney, Thorstad): rigorous, scope-sensitive, argues the future is astronomically
  large and our era possibly decisive. It disagrees *with itself* — MacAskill argues we
  are *not* at the hinge of history; Mogensen and the "time of perils" skeptics
  (Thorstad) push back on both the hinge claim and the standard x-risk math.
- **The Open Phil / Cold Takes empiricists** (Karnofsky, Cotra, Davidson, Roodman):
  translate the philosophy into growth curves, bio-anchors, and nearcasting. More
  hedged than the philosophers, obsessed with their own track record and burden of
  proof.
- **The SF / frontier-lab forecasters** (Aschenbrenner, Kokotajlo/AI 2027, Amodei,
  Altman): short timelines, concrete scenarios, geopolitical urgency, and — in Amodei
  and Altman — an explicit optimistic vision. They read as prophetic to some and
  overconfident to others.
- **The rationalist / intelligence-explosion lineage** (Yudkowsky, Vinge, Chalmers,
  Hanson, Christiano, Alexander): the singularity, fast vs. slow takeoff, and Moloch as
  the master metaphor for value erosion. Hanson–Yudkowsky is the foundational takeoff
  disagreement; Hanson also splits from MacAskill on whether value lock-in is even
  desirable.
- **The skeptics and counterweights** (Narayanan & Kapoor's "AI as normal technology,"
  Tyler Cowen, Epoch's Erdil/Besiroglu on longer timelines, "futurists' track record"):
  argue the transformation is slower, more diffuse, and more forecast-resistant than
  the core readings assume.
- **The accelerationists** (Andreessen, e/acc, partly Altman): a foil worldview — more
  technology faster is unambiguously good; x-risk framing is fear. Included as the
  argued-against position, not a peer.

The central live disagreements: **are we at the hinge?** (MacAskill vs. Mogensen/Ord);
**fast or slow takeoff?** (Yudkowsky vs. Hanson/Christiano); **how good can we expect
the default future to be?** (Amodei/Andreessen vs. Bostrom's evolutionary pessimism and
the Moloch lineage); and **how much should we trust any long-range forecast at all?**
(Karnofsky/Narayanan/Tarsney skepticism vs. Aschenbrenner confidence).

## Best readings

Curated, most-usable first. Items 1–4 are the assignable core; deep-dives are marked.

1. **[The Most Important Century (series roundup)](https://www.cold-takes.com/most-important-century/)** (Karnofsky, Cold Takes). The hub for the scale claim. Read the two short flagships rather than the whole series: **[This Can't Go On](https://www.cold-takes.com/this-cant-go-on/)** (current growth rates are historically bizarre and must end) and **[All Possible Views About Humanity's Future Are Wild](https://www.cold-takes.com/all-possible-views-about-humanitys-future-are-wild/)** (you can't dodge the weirdness by being a moderate). Also the on-ramp of the opening week — participants who did that week can skip straight to #2. *Role: accessible on-ramp — the scope warm-up.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/most-important-century-roundup.md), [This Can't Go On](../../readings/macrostrategy/this-cant-go-on.md), [All Possible Views](../../readings/macrostrategy/all-possible-views-are-wild.md))

2. **[Are we living at the most influential time in history?](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/XXLf6FmWujkxna3E6/are-we-living-at-the-most-influential-time-in-history-1)** (MacAskill, EA Forum). The original hinge-of-history essay: the priors-based case *against* assuming our era is uniquely decisive, from the author the fellowship is built on. *Role: anchor essay — one side of the topic's central debate.* ([local](../../readings/ea-intro-program/are-we-living-at-most-influential-time.md))

3. **[The Hinge of History Hypothesis: Reply to MacAskill](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/idp4GEqWp24ocgfes/the-hinge-of-history-hypothesis-reply-to-macaskill-andreas)** (Mogensen, GPI). Contests #2 directly on whether now is uniquely influential — read as its pair. *Role: best critic — stages the central debate as live, not settled.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/hinge-of-history-reply-mogensen.md))

4. **[How to Make the Future Better](https://www.forethought.org/research/how-to-make-the-future-better)** (MacAskill, Forethought). The concluding essay of the [Better Futures](https://www.forethought.org/research/better-futures) series and the constructive core of MacAskill's macrostrategy — why making the future *good* is a different problem from making it safe, and the concrete levers (viatopia) he proposes. His own essay routes the macrostrategy-curious here. *Role: anchor essay — the better-futures primary.* ([local](../../readings/forethought/how-to-make-the-future-better.md))

5. **[Astronomical Waste](https://nickbostrom.com/papers/astronomical-waste/)** (Bostrom, Utilitas). The foundational scope-sensitivity essay; the opportunity-cost logic under all longtermist macrostrategy. *Role: rigorous foundation — the primary source.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/astronomical-waste.md))

6. **[Crucial Considerations and Wise Philanthropy](https://effectivealtruism.org/articles/crucial-considerations-and-wise-philanthropy-nick-bostrom)** (Bostrom, EA Global talk). Defines the macrostrategy *method* itself — the search for considerations that flip your sign. *Role: names the discipline — good framing reading to open or close on.* ([local](../../readings/linked/bostrom-crucial-considerations-wise-philanthropy.md))

7. **[Meditations on Moloch](https://slatestarcodex.com/2014/07/30/meditations-on-moloch/)** (Alexander, Slate Star Codex). The master essay on competitive dynamics eroding value; substrate for lock-in and gradual disempowerment. *Role: conceptual keystone — names the core failure mode.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/meditations-on-moloch.md))

8. **[Letter from Utopia](https://nickbostrom.com/utopia)** (Bostrom). A short, lyrical evocation of what a flourishing future could *feel* like. *Role: heart voice — pair against the actuarial readings.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/letter-from-utopia.md))

9. **[Book Review: Deep Utopia](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/book-review-deep-utopia)** (Alexander, Astral Codex Ten). Bostrom's "what is left to do in a solved world" question, in its most readable form — the sequel worry once safety and abundance are granted. *Role: accessible deep-dive — meaning after the transition.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/deep-utopia-review-acx.md))

10. **[The Track Record of Futurists Seems... Fine](https://www.cold-takes.com/the-track-record-of-futurists-seems-fine/)** (Karnofsky, Cold Takes). Empirical audit of whether long-range forecasting works at all. *Role: data/evidence — the meta-reading that calibrates trust in the whole topic.* ([local](../../readings/macrostrategy/futurists-track-record.md))

11. **[The Epistemic Challenge to Longtermism](https://globalprioritiesinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/Christian_Tarsney_epistemic_challenge.pdf)** (Tarsney, GPI working paper). The formal version of the worry #10 treats empirically: are long-run effects predictable enough that far-future value can dominate decisions? The strongest rigorous statement of the "maybe we just can't know" position. *Role: rigorous deep-dive on the confidence question.* ([local](../../readings/linked/tarsney-epistemic-challenge-to-longtermism.md))

12. **[The Vulnerable World Hypothesis](https://nickbostrom.com/papers/vulnerable.pdf)** (Bostrom, Global Policy). Technology-lottery framing motivating differential development and governance. *Role: strategic-lever foundation — why the order/timing of tech matters.* ([local](../../readings/ea-intro-program/bostrom-vulnerable-world-hypothesis.md))

13. **[AI as Normal Technology](https://knightcolumbia.org/content/ai-as-normal-technology)** (Narayanan & Kapoor, Knight First Amendment Institute). The most serious "it'll be slower and more diffuse" counterweight to the whole topic. *Role: staged skeptic — the accepted-adjacent critique, not a strawman.* ([local](../../readings/linked/ai-as-normal-technology.md))

The timelines-and-takeoff readings this topic shares with the

Speeds, Machines of Loving Grace — are core there and deliberately not renumbered here;

## Conspicuously missing

- **Bostrom's *Superintelligence* in its own voice.** Decisive strategic advantage,
  singleton, takeoff — present only via reviews and derivatives. (Ord's *The Precipice*
  intro/ch. 1 *is* in the collection, under `ea-intro-program`, but its x-risk framing
  belongs to other weeks.)
- **Richard Ngo's macrostrategy writing.** His big-picture pieces (e.g. "clarifying and
  predicting AGI," his takeoff/futures writing) sit in other clusters or are absent here.
- **A suffering-focused / s-risk voice on trajectory.** Tomasik/CLR on *why the future
  could be bad by default* exists in the collection but was filed under s-risks; this
  topic's "better futures" thread is almost entirely upside, missing the
  downside-future counterpart.
- **A staged outside-the-tent critique of longtermism as ideology.** The Gebru–Torres
  TESCREAL piece is in the collection (`hostile-critics`) but nothing here asks *whose
  future* from an STS/critical-futures angle in a form worth assigning.
- **A personal-reflection / grappling essay.** The list is all argument and forecast;
  Carlsmith's *Otherness and control in the age of AGI* series sits in the
  `joe-carlsmith` cluster, and none of it was selected here to supply that register.
- **A non-Western / Global-South macrostrategy voice.** China appears only secondhand
  (Ding's *Deciphering China's AI Dream* is cited, not included), and no author outside
  the Anglo-American EA frame writes here about what a good post-AGI future means.
- **Hanson's *Age of Em* / whole-brain-emulation macrostrategy** as a primary — a
  fundamentally different transition scenario, present only as a review.
