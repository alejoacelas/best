# Topic: Factory farming

*MacAskill menu item: factory farming — the animal-welfare cause area in the age of AGI.*

## What this topic covers

How transformative AI reshapes the fate of non-human animals — and how the animal
movement should react. Three intertwined sub-threads: (1) **near-term dual use** — AI as
a tool that helps advocacy (welfare monitoring, alt-protein R&D, targeted outreach,
interspecies communication) versus a tool that entrenches factory farming (precision
livestock farming, cheaper meat); (2) **long-run trajectory / lock-in** — whether an
AI-transformed future contains vast animal suffering (farmed, wild, or novel "functional"
beings) or renders factory farming obsolete, and how contingent that is on values locked
in around the transition; (3) **animal-inclusive alignment** — the concrete project of
getting animal concern into frontier systems (model specs, constitutions, benchmarks,
synthetic-document pretraining) and the meta-question of whether that is even coherent or
wise. Running underneath: a methodological fight about whether we are *clueless* about
post-paradigm-shift worlds and should discount speculative long-run interventions to
zero, or whether lock-in makes acting now unusually high-leverage.

## Voices and where they disagree

- **Grantmaker realism (Lewis Bollard / Open Phil).** Near-term pessimist (industry
  adopts efficiency tech, shuns welfare tech), longer-run cautious optimist (wealth +
  alt-protein make factory farming obsolete). Skeptical of dramatic lock-in and of
  space-faring factory farms. The movement's funding center of gravity.
- **Forethought skepticism / cluelessness (Lizka, Ben West).** The uncertainty in any
  post-transformation intervention builds up so fast that expected value should be
  discounted to ~zero absent a concrete, articulable, robust mechanism. Warns that
  animal-friendly AI values could *backfire* (perpetuating farming by nostalgia/sentiment
  or misfiring "wisdom"). Recommends focusing on wild animals for the long run and
  heavily discounting normal interventions that pay off in 10+ years.
- **Act-now urgency / animal-inclusive alignment (Andrew Critch, Max Taylor, CaML —
  Brazilek & Tidmarsh, Sentient Futures).** Values get locked in soon; build the
  benchmarks (AnimalHarmBench, ANIMA), get "sentient beings" into constitutions and model
  specs, use synthetic-document finetuning. Empirical claim that animal-compassion
  training *generalizes to human compassion* and doesn't hurt capabilities — a bid to
  make animals matter even to those who don't care about animals.
- **Moral circle expansion / s-risk (Jacy Reese Anthis, Sentience Institute; Tomasik in
  the linked pile).** The foundational 2018 argument: prioritize *quality* risk (what the
  future is like) over *population* risk (whether it exists); the far future's expected
  value may be near zero or negative; MCE is neglected relative to AI alignment. Sentience
  Institute's trajectory work asks which farming-trajectory changes actually persist
  through the transition. The suffering-focused wing worries about astronomical suffering
  and spreading wild-animal suffering to the stars.
- **Movement-strategy reframers (Aidan Kankyoku / Sandcastles, Jamie Harris).** Kankyoku
  collapses the debate: "animal welfare is *just part of* AI alignment now" — the same
  through-line runs through every neglected cause on MacAskill's menu — then turns the
  question back on the movement: aligned *to what*, exactly? Vivid, personal-essay
  register ("The End of Animal Advocacy"). Harris issues the movement-wide call to take
  short timelines seriously.
- **Optimist vs. pessimist visioning.** Jess Masterson ("What Success Looks Like")
  and Wladimir Alonso ("AI helps advocacy more than industry") paint concrete bright
  futures; Dylan Richardson ("Pessimistic Perspective") and Stewart/Dupuis ("What Failure
  Looks Like," linked) supply the dark mirror — value lock-in, decadence, generational
  replacement stalling, ASI as a time constraint on moral progress.
- **Academic animal-AI ethics (Singer & Tse; Philosophy & Technology).** Peer-reviewed
  case that alignment "to human values" disregards the vast majority of moral patients;
  Singer & Tse founded the subfield in 2022, the 2025 follow-on adds a *basic* concern
  floor and concrete lab/government policies.

The sharpest disagreements: **discount-to-zero vs. act-now** (Forethought vs.
CaML/Critch); **is shaping AI values good?** (Forethought's backfire worry vs. the
alignment builders' confidence); **farmed vs. wild** as the long-run locus of suffering
(Forethought says wild; most advocacy energy is still farmed); **MCE vs. extinction-risk**
(Anthis) now partly dissolved into Kankyoku's "it's all one problem."

## Best readings

Most-usable first; the top of the list fits a ~2h week, deep-dives marked.

1. **[What could AI mean for animals?](https://farmanimalwelfare.substack.com/p/what-could-ai-mean-for-animals)** (Lewis Bollard, Open Phil farm-animal newsletter). Short, concrete, dual-use framing (optimist utopia vs. pessimist dystopia, "who's right? I don't know"), grounded in agriculture's actual tech-adoption track record. *Role: accessible on-ramp; the trusted-grantmaker two-hands voice.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/bollard-what-could-ai-mean-for-animals.md))

2. **[Animal Welfare is Just Part of AI Alignment Now](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/skdp9uB4AoyN2fnuu/animal-welfare-is-just-part-of-ai-alignment-now)** (Aidan Kankyoku, EA Forum). Provocative reframe built directly on MacAskill's cause menu; separate messages to advocates and to alignment researchers. *Role: anchor essay; ties the whole fellowship's premise to this topic.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/animal-welfare-is-part-of-ai-alignment.md))

3. **[A shallow review of what transformative AI means for animal welfare](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/tGdWott5GCnKYmRKb/a-shallow-review-of-what-transformative-ai-means-for-animal)** (Lizka & Ben West, EA Forum / Forethought). The field's best structured survey: the normal-era vs. transformed-era grid, the discounting argument, and the value-shaping backfire case. *Role: rigorous deep-dive; the skeptic anchor that disciplines the rest.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/shallow-review-tai-animal-welfare.md))

4. **[What AI could mean for animals](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/ZNcdt7eYWW7YXALvx/what-ai-could-mean-for-animals)** (Max Taylor, EA Forum). The broad map of AI's effects on animals; pairs with his **[Bringing about animal-inclusive AI](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/2cZAzvaQefh5JxWdb/bringing-about-animal-inclusive-ai)** ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/bringing-about-animal-inclusive-ai.md)) for the practical routes: model specs, constitutions, evals. *Role: the survey-then-levers pair.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/what-ai-could-mean-for-animals.md))

5. **[Steering AI to care for animals, and soon](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/35bfnGmsyrZkEnkLJ/steering-ai-to-care-for-animals-and-soon)** (Andrew Critch, EA Forum). ~600 words, the crisp time-sensitivity cri de coeur. *Role: short provocation / discussion spark.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/steering-ai-to-care-for-animals.md))

6. **[Alignment for Animals](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/aZBL5gBcQpqjDfth5/alignment-for-animals)** (Brazilek & Tidmarsh, EA Forum). The concrete technical intervention: synthetic-document finetuning, the striking "compassion generalizes to humans" result, no capability hit; the **[CaML project site](https://www.compassionml.com/)** ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/caml-compassion-in-machine-learning.md)) is the ongoing home. *Role: the "boring tractability" exhibit — animal alignment as real engineering, not just ethics.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/alignment-for-animals.md))

7. **[What would an animal-aligned AI be aligned to?](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/mnLqdvnpKiudivyfv/what-would-an-animal-aligned-ai-be-aligned-to)** (Aidan Kankyoku, Anima International / EA Forum). The target question the alignment run begs: aligned to welfare? rights? whose weighting? Probes whether "animal-aligned" is even a coherent optimization target before the movement asks labs for it. *Role: the alignment-target interrogation; completes #4-6.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/what-would-animal-aligned-ai-be-aligned-to.md))

8. **[Animal advocates should respond to transformative AI maybe arriving soon](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/TY2f2Ts2EdsDAP35y/animal-advocates-should-respond-to-transformative-ai-maybe)** (Jamie Harris, EA Forum). The clean movement-wide call to action with a concrete 2030 goal. *Role: the "so what do we do" bridge from diagnosis to strategy.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/advocates-should-respond-to-tai.md))

9. **[What Success Looks Like For Animals](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/CMSnn76h6tMf3epKH/what-success-looks-like-for-animals)** (Jess Masterson, EA Forum). Deliberately optimistic, concrete catalogue of bright AI-for-animals futures across farmed/wild/companion. *Role: the positive-vision opener.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/what-success-looks-like-for-animals.md))

10. **[A Pessimistic Perspective on Animal Welfare Social Change](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/2SwX3D49XFYmzqmHb/a-pessimistic-perspective-on-animal-welfare-social-change)** (Dylan Richardson, EA Forum). ASI as a hard time-constraint on moral progress; the cultivated-meat theory of change, interrogated. *Role: best critic; pairs against Masterson.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/pessimistic-perspective-animal-social-change.md))

11. **[Longtermism and Animal Farming Trajectories](https://www.sentienceinstitute.org/blog/longtermism-and-animal-farming-trajectories)** (Sentience Institute). The dedicated trajectory piece: how today's farming path shapes what gets locked in around the transition, and which trajectory changes are actually persistent. *Role: the lock-in anchor for sub-thread 2.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/longtermism-animal-farming-trajectories.md))

12. **[The End of Animal Advocacy](https://sandcastlesblog.substack.com/p/ai-end-animal-advocacy)** (Aidan Kankyoku, Sandcastles). Long-form vivid personal reckoning with what AGI does to a life spent on animal advocacy. *Role: vivid narrative; emotional counterweight to the memo prose.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/kankyoku-end-of-animal-advocacy.md))

13. **[AI ethics: the case for including animals](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s43681-022-00187-z)** (Peter Singer & Yip Fai Tse, *AI and Ethics*, 2022). The founding academic paper of the subfield — Singer's first on AI — arguing AI ethics has ignored the vast majority of moral patients. *Role: rigorous academic anchor; the movement's founding utilitarian voice on AI.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/singer-tse-ai-ethics-including-animals.md))

14. **[Lewis Bollard — What could AI mean for animals?](https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/lewis-bollard)** (Dwarkesh Podcast). Long interview; "artificial meat is harder than artificial intelligence," the alt-protein reality check. *Role: media-diversity deep-dive; the alt-protein-optimism steelman and its limits.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/dwarkesh-bollard-artificial-meat.md))

15. **[List of ideas for improving animal welfare in light of transformative AI](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/d3gaMea82DWCd6wwz/list-of-ideas-for-improving-animal-welfare-in-light-of)** (Michael Dickens, EA Forum). Concrete brainstormed intervention menu — useful to rank by robustness against the Forethought discount. *Role: intervention menu / discussion fuel.* ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/ideas-improving-animal-welfare-tai.md))

Also strong, in supporting roles: **[Why I Prioritize Moral Circle Expansion Over Reducing Extinction Risk](https://www.sentienceinstitute.org/blog/moral-circle-expansion-vs-reducing-extinction-risk)** ([local](../../readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/moral-circle-expansion-vs-extinction-risk.md)) — Anthis's 2018 far-future-values argument, the historical root the trajectory debate grew from (its general MCE-vs-x-risk case belongs more to the s-risks and moral-circle topics); ## Conspicuously missing

- **Jeff Sebo's AI-welfare / moral-circle bridge.** The topic treats animals but barely
  connects to digital minds; Sebo (*The Moral Circle*, "the rebugnant conclusion," moral
  weight of digital minds) is the obvious bridge author and is only in the linked pile via
  one drowning-ant piece. Get his explicit animals↔AI-welfare argument.
- **A lab-insider on whether animals are in scope.** Kyle Fish / Anthropic AI-welfare, or
  any frontier-lab safety researcher speaking undiluted on whether "alignment to human
  values" leaves animals out.
- **The pragmatic "this is a distraction" critic from inside animal advocacy.** Someone
  arguing the movement should ignore AGI speculation and keep winning corporate campaigns
  — a stronger present-suffering-first critic than Richardson (who accepts the AI frame).
- **Non-Western / Global South voices.** The entire list is Anglophone EA Forum. Given
  that meat consumption growth is concentrated in the developing world, a Chinese or Indian
  perspective on livestock + AI, or a non-utilitarian (Buddhist, Indigenous, care-ethics)
  framing of animals under AI, is completely absent.
- **Invertebrate / shrimp / insect-farming scale-up under AI.** Rethink Priorities welfare
  ranges are linked but the specific "AI industrializes insect/shrimp farming at
  astronomical scale" argument — the likely dominant near-term animal-numbers story — has
  no curated anchor. A Shrimp Welfare Project or insect-farming voice.
- **Wild Animal Initiative's own institutional position on AI.** The wild-animal long-run
  locus is where Forethought points, yet WAI's strategic take isn't curated (only a
  general TAI-and-wild-animals exploration).
- **Suffering-focused / s-risk deep anchor (CLR, Tobias Baumann, Brian Tomasik).** The
  s-risk tradition is gestured at through Anthis but has no dedicated curated piece on
  animals-specific astronomical suffering or spreading wild-animal suffering to space.
