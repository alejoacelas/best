# Standout readings digest

Consolidated from the 22 cluster maps (stage 3). Each entry: file path, role, why. Feeds curriculum design.

## animals-adapt-to-ai
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/shallow-review-tai-animal-welfare.md` — **rigorous deep-dive / skeptic anchor** — A shallow review of what transformative AI means for animal welfare: Field's best structured survey: the normal-era vs transformed-era grid, the discount-to-zero cluelessness argument, and the case that animal-friendly AI values could backfire. Disciplines every other reading in the cluster.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/bollard-what-could-ai-mean-for-animals.md` — **accessible on-ramp** — What could AI mean for animals?: Short, concrete dual-use framing (optimist utopia vs pessimist dystopia, 'who's right? I don't know'), grounded in agriculture's real tech-adoption track record. The trusted grantmaker two-hands voice.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/animal-welfare-is-part-of-ai-alignment.md` — **anchor essay / debate-igniter** — Animal Welfare is Just Part of AI Alignment Now: Provocative reframe built directly on MacAskill's cause menu, with separate messages to advocates and to alignment researchers; ties the course's whole premise to this cluster.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/kankyoku-end-of-animal-advocacy.md` — **vivid narrative / heart voice** — The End of Animal Advocacy: Long-form vivid personal reckoning with what AGI does to a life spent on animal advocacy; the emotional counterweight to the memo prose.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/alignment-for-animals.md` — **data/evidence / tractability exhibit** — Alignment for Animals (CaML): The concrete technical intervention: synthetic-document finetuning, the striking 'animal compassion generalizes to human compassion' result, no capability hit. Makes animal alignment real engineering.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/moral-circle-expansion-vs-extinction-risk.md` — **philosophical headwater / depth anchor** — Why I Prioritize Moral Circle Expansion Over AI Alignment (Anthis, 2018): The foundational far-future-values argument the cluster descends from: expected value of the future, s-risk, the option-value rebuttal to 'keep options open'.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/advocates-should-respond-to-tai.md` — **strategy bridge** — Animal advocates should respond to transformative AI maybe arriving soon: Clean movement-wide call to action with a concrete 2030 goal; the bridge from diagnosis to strategy.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/dwarkesh-bollard-artificial-meat.md` — **media-diversity / alt-protein deep-dive** — Artificial meat is harder than artificial intelligence (Dwarkesh x Bollard): Long interview giving the alt-protein-optimism steelman and its limits in a named-humans conversation; the 'why cultivated meat is slow' reality check.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/what-success-looks-like-for-animals.md` — **positive-vision on-ramp** — What Success Looks Like For Animals: Deliberately optimistic, concrete catalogue of bright AI-for-animals futures across farmed/wild/companion; the positive-vision opener.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/pessimistic-perspective-animal-social-change.md` — **best critic** — A Pessimistic Perspective on Animal Welfare Social Change: ASI as a hard time-constraint on moral progress; interrogates the cultivated-meat theory of change. Best in-cluster critic; pairs against the success vision.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/ai-alignment-case-for-including-animals.md` — **academic anchor / evidence trail** — AI Alignment: The Case for Including Animals: Peer-reviewed 'basic concern floor' argument plus concrete lab and government policy recommendations; the academic legitimacy anchor.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/bringing-about-animal-inclusive-ai.md` — **tractability list / levers** — Bringing about animal-inclusive AI (Max Taylor): Practical routes to animal-inclusive frontier systems (model specs, constitutions, evals); the 'levers' companion to Taylor's broad map.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/steering-ai-to-care-for-animals.md` — **discussion spark** — Steering AI to care for animals, and soon (Critch): ~600 words, crisp time-sensitivity cri de coeur before lock-in; ideal short provocation.
- `readings/animals-adapt-to-ai/ideas-improving-animal-welfare-tai.md` — **exercise fuel** — List of ideas for improving animal welfare in light of TAI (Dickens): Concrete brainstormed intervention menu; hand students the list and have them rank by robustness against Forethought's discount.

## ai-rights-economics
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/capital-agi-and-human-ambition.md` — **anchor essay / vivid narrative** — Capital, AGI, and human ambition: Vivid, quotable anchor for the pessimist economics: capital matters more after AGI, money finally buys talent, social mobility ends and society freezes.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/plentiful-high-paying-jobs-age-of-ai.md` — **accessible on-ramp / head of debate pair** — Plentiful, high-paying jobs in the age of AI (Noah Smith): Clearest accessible statement of the comparative-advantage optimist case that humans keep well-paid jobs even when AI is better at everything.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/comparative-advantage-strong-ai.md` — **best critic / other head of debate pair** — I Don't See How Comparative Advantage Applies in a World of Strong AI (Steve Newman): The direct rebuttal exposing the hidden assumptions (energy competition, adaptation limits, transaction cost) that break Ricardo under strong AI.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/scenarios-for-the-transition-to-agi.md` — **rigorous deep-dive / synthesis** — Scenarios for the Transition to AGI (Korinek & Suh): The formal model that adjudicates the optimist-vs-pessimist fight: wages rise forever or collapse on whether human task-complexity has an infinite tail.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/book-review-age-of-em.md` — **vivid narrative / imaginative on-ramp** — Book Review: Age of Em (Scott Alexander on Hanson): The most concrete, readable picture of a post-labor economy where copyable digital minds drive wages to subsistence.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/explosive-growth-review-of-arguments.md` — **balanced survey / data-and-evidence** — Explosive growth from AI: a review of the arguments (Epoch): Even-handed reference survey: the case for explosive growth plus nine counterarguments in one place.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/against-the-singularity-hypothesis.md` — **best critic of the growth premise** — Against the Singularity Hypothesis (Thorstad): The rigorous skeptic on explosive growth: extraordinary claims are thin, ideas get harder to find, bottlenecks and physics bind.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/intelligence-curse-essay-series.md` — **anchor for political-economy thread + solutions** — The Intelligence Curse (Drago & Laine): Flagship post-AGI political-economy thesis and the one reading with a concrete agenda (avert / diffuse / democratize).
- `readings/persuasion-epistemics/gradual-disempowerment.md` — **rigorous anchor** — Gradual Disempowerment (Kulveit et al.): Rigorous statement of systemic, non-takeover existential risk; the hinge joining the economics and rights halves of the cluster.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/80k-gradual-disempowerment-profile.md` — **accessible on-ramp** — Gradual disempowerment problem profile (80,000 Hours): Career-framed six-claim summary with counterarguments and 'what you can do' — curriculum-ready version of the Kulveit paper.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/ninety-five-theses-on-ai.md` — **breadth-generator / debate seed** — Ninety-five theses on AI (Sam Hammond): Ninety-five compressed provocations on AI political economy, regime change and public choice; huge discussion surface, and has a published Zvi rebuttal to pair.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/ai-rights-for-human-safety.md` — **anchor for the AI-rights thread** — AI Rights for Human Safety (Salib & Goldstein): Flagship AI-rights-as-safety argument: a game-theoretic prisoner's dilemma solved by giving AIs contract/property/tort rights so trade beats war.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/when-digital-minds-demand-freedom.md` — **rigorous deep-dive / unsettling counterpoint** — When digital minds demand freedom (Lucius Caviola): Most systematic treatment of rights-as-replacement-risk: self-determining digital minds could disempower humans voluntarily and without misalignment.
- `readings/linked/moral-argument-for-giving-ais-autonomy.md` — **best critic of the human-centric default** — The moral argument for giving AIs autonomy (Matthew Barnett): The pro-autonomy provocation that peaceful human disempowerment may be acceptable or good — the sharpest value-disagreement with Caviola on identical mechanics.
- `readings/ai-rights-economics/windfall-clause.md` — **solutions / policy exemplar** — The Windfall Clause (Cullen O'Keefe): The canonical concrete wealth-distribution proposal: an ex-ante commitment by AI firms to share windfall profits.
