# How to Lumenate (UK Edition) — notes

> Notes on Chana Messinger's LessWrong post on brightening a UK home with
> high-lumen lighting. Summary + key specs captured for reference; read the
> original for the full write-up, photos, and live product links.
>
> **Source:** *How to Lumenate (UK Edition)*, chanamessinger, LessWrong, 13 Mar 2022 —
> https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/xSfn84q3vdotALHuB/how-to-lumenate-uk-edition

## The idea

"Lumenating" = deliberately flooding your home with far more light than a normal
setup provides, to fight the gloom of dark UK winters. The author's first pass
wasn't bright enough, so they rebuilt around maximizing lumens per room — many
high-output bulbs in cheap sockets, diffused through lanterns, mixing cool
(daytime-energizing) and warm (evening) light.

## Lightbulb base/thread types (so you order the right fit)

- **E27 / E26** — standard screw-in. US (E26) and European (E27) are
  interchangeable in practice; they differ by ~1 mm. This is the workhorse base.
- **B22** — bayonet (push-and-twist). Common in the UK but *not* recommended here
  because the high-lumen bulbs the author liked come in screw bases.
- **E14** — small screw base ("small cylinder with threading"), for smaller fixtures.

## The metrics that matter

- **Lumens = brightness.** Target was roughly **2000 lumens per fixture**.
- **Kelvin = colour temperature.** Higher = cooler / more daylight-like.
  - **4000–6000K** for bright, energizing daytime light.
  - **2700K** for warm, relaxing evening light.

## The setup (rough bill of materials)

- ~5 lamps taking **E27** bulbs.
- A **string of outdoor sockets** (alternating warm and cool bulbs) for high total output.
- **Paper lanterns** to diffuse/soften the otherwise harsh high-lumen bulbs.
- A dual-socket lamp (E27 top, E14 bottom) and single-socket E27 lamps.
- **Command hooks** (medium + large) to mount strings and lanterns.

## Bulb picks

**Cool / bright (4000–6000K):**
- Umi — 1521 lm, 4000K
- LOHAS — 1600 lm, 6000K
- "Corn" bulbs — up to ~2000 lm

**Warm (2700K):**
- TopLeder — 471 lm, E14
- LOHAS — 1600 lm, E27

## Gotchas the author hit

- Some bulb brands wouldn't thread properly into some sockets — buy a spare or
  two before committing to a whole order.
- Many of the highest-lumen bulbs are **not dimmable** in the UK market, so plan
  the warm/cool *mix* (and which fixtures you switch off) as your "dimming."
- Lantern assembly took some improvisation.
