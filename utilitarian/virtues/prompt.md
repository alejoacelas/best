This folder is for optimizing things in my life, and I want to distill a set of
principles that we can follow to make things better. The principles I'm thinking
of currently are:

## Being open

Open includes being honest — giving as much information as you can to others,
because that kind of makes things easier. If you're all sharing the same goal,
more knowledge means more capacity for action.

But being open also involves modularity and being discoverable. When there's so
much to share in the world, we need to find ways of presenting information, of
gradually disclosing, such that people can choose. So you're open for their
choosing, and you're open to let them explore you.

## Kindness

Kindness is related to believing in people — partly just seeing the best of
them, but also maybe it's about finding ways of delighting them: being creative
and resourceful in finding ways of delighting others. There's probably more to
that.

## Being strong

Strength is about capacity for action — expanding the range of things you can
do. One clear example is physical strength: if you have more physical strength
you can carry heavier objects, you can persist at physical activities for
longer, so you can have more one-on-ones at EAG. Strength is about capacity —
the range of things you can do.

## Being fast

If something can be done now, you do it now. If something can be done faster, do
it faster. If something can be done with less friction, that's so much better.

## Being cooperative

(Not sure what's the right name for this.) It's related to integrity and being
law-abiding, and partly it's a form of making yourself predictable to others and
reining yourself in. If you're strong, you need a set of principles that hold
you back in some form, to avoid hurting others and to be predictable to others.

This connects to the Kantian notion: would I will everyone to behave as I do?
And to the fact that we are biased and self-serving in a lot of our intuitions.
So in a bunch of ways we should be restricting ourselves — tying ourselves to
the mast — such that even in the presence of such biases (many of which will
pass undetected to us), we still manage to act correctly.
